CHAPTER 16WINNERS AND LOSERS

PART FOUR: THE RESTORATION OF CAPITAL
The headline in the Washington Times on April 18 stirred considerable interest because the conservative newspaper was well connected at the Reagan White House. The President, the newspaper declared, has decided not to reappoint Paul Volcker as Federal Reserve chairman. White House aides promptly denied it, but the story was accurate. Treasury Secretary Donald Regan had persuaded the President to decide, first of all, that Volcker would not be given another four-year term as Fed chairman. Later, they could settle on the successor.1
1983年4月18日，《华盛顿时报》的头条激起了相当大的兴趣，因为这家保守派报纸与里根白宫关系密切。该报宣称，总统已决定不再任命Paul Volcker担任美联储主席。白宫助手们立即对此予以否认，但报道是准确的。财政部长Donald Regan已说服总统首先做出决定：Volcker将不再获得另一个四年的美联储主席任期。至于继任者，可以之后再定夺。
“The President did decide not to reappoint Volcker,” an Administration official explained, “but it didn’t mean anything.” As often happened in Washington, the newspaper leak was only the beginning of the argument, not the end. For the next two months, as the White House advisers cast about for a likely alternative, the powerful economic interests and other politicians all expressed their preferences. In the end, they chose Volcker.
"总统确实决定不再任命Volcker，"一位行政官员解释道，"但这并不意味着什么。"正如华盛顿经常发生的那样，报纸爆料只是争论的开始，而非结束。在接下来的两个月里，随着白宫顾问们四处物色可能的替代人选，强大的经济利益集团和其他政客纷纷表达了自己的偏好。最终，他们选择了Volcker。
Jim Baker was very reluctant to reappoint Volcker [David Stockman said]. Baker felt Volcker had prolonged the recession much longer than necessary. Volcker didn’t communicate with him very well. Baker would say: “How can you keep this misery going for five quarters?” Don Regan was adamant about Volcker. He said in private meetings many times: “There’s no way we’re going to put that guy back in there. He won’t take orders.”
Jim Baker非常不愿意再次任命Volcker [David Stockman说]。Baker认为Volcker将衰退拖得比必要的时间长得多。Volcker与他沟通不畅。Baker常说："你怎么能让这种痛苦持续五个季度？"Don Regan对Volcker的态度是坚决反对。他在私下会议中多次表示："我们绝不能再让那个人回到那个位置。他不听命令。"
The problem, however, was agreeing on the successor. Edwin Meese favored Preston Martin, the Fed vice chairman and a like-minded California businessman. Donald Regan liked Walter Wriston of Citibank and also Paul W. McCracken, CEA chairman under Nixon. Alan Greenspan, an outside economics adviser to President Reagan and also a former CEA chairman, was recommended as a candidate whom Wall Street would accept. Beryl Sprinkel was mentioned as a possibility and even Milton Friedman.
然而，问题在于就继任者达成共识。Edwin Meese倾向于支持Preston Martin，即美联储副主席，也是一位志同道合的加州商人。Donald Regan青睐花旗银行的Walter Wriston，以及曾在尼克松政府担任经济顾问委员会主席的Paul W. McCracken。Alan Greenspan，里根总统的外部经济顾问、前经济顾问委员会主席，被推荐为华尔街能够接受的候选人。Beryl Sprinkel被提及为可能性人选，甚至连Milton Friedman也在考虑之列。
The Federal Reserve chairman remained publicly aloof, but privately he expressed his desire for another term. “Volcker knew who his friends were and we talked about it,” Stockman said. “He said to me. ‘Now is not the time for discontinuity. I am now in the middle of the debt problems and these delicate negotiations. I’ve got all these deals going and they are based on trust and informal understandings.’” Stockman and Martin Feldstein, the new CEA chairman, lobbied strenuously in Volcker’s behalf.
美联储主席在公开场合保持超然姿态，但私下里表达了他对连任的渴望。"Volcker知道谁是朋友，我们谈过这事，"Stockman说。"他对我说：'现在不是中断的时候。我正处在债务问题和这些微妙谈判的中间。我手头有这么多交易在进行，它们都建立在信任和非正式谅解之上。'"Stockman和新任经济顾问委员会主席Martin Feldstein为Volcker进行了积极游说。
“I pecked at Baker like a hen,” Stockman said. “I told him the Fed was doing a pretty credible job and Volcker’s the only thing Ronald Reagan has going for him. He’s got credibility. The markets trust him. We need to keep him.”
"我像母鸡一样啄着Baker，"Stockman说。"我告诉他，美联储做得相当不错，而Volcker是Ronald Reagan唯一拿得出手的筹码。他有公信力。市场信任他。我们必须留住他。"
Baker and the President’s other advisers heard the same message from many quarters. Senator Jake Garn, chairman of Senate Banking, urged the White House to reappoint Volcker. “I wonder, if he is not reappointed, what kind of signals do you send to the marketplace?” Garn said.2 Howard Baker, the Senate Republican leader, delivered his own endorsement. So did Senator Robert Dole, the Finance chairman.
Baker和总统的其他顾问从许多渠道听到了同样的信息。参议院银行委员会主席Jake Garn参议员敦促白宫重新任命Volcker。"我想知道，如果他不被重新任命，你会向市场发出什么样的信号？"Garn说。参议院共和党领袖Howard Baker也表达了他的支持。财政委员会主席Robert Dole参议员同样如此。
The U.S. Chamber of Commerce was for reappointment as were the National Association of Manufacturers, the Business Council and The Wall Street Journal. The major interests overshadowed the dissenters. The National Association of Home Builders, remembering the devastation of Volcker’s recession, urged the President to appoint a more moderate leader for the central bank—Preston Martin, a former developer himself, who would be more sympathetic to the pressures on housing and small business. The home builders organized a coalition of twenty-four small-business organizations in support of Martin’s candidacy.
美国商会支持重新任命，全国制造商协会、商业委员会和《华尔街日报》也持同样立场。主要利益集团压倒了反对者。全国住宅建筑商协会则铭记着Volcker式衰退带来的破坏，敦促总统任命一位更为温和的央行领导人——Preston Martin，他本人曾是开发商，会对住房和小企业面临的压力抱有更多同情。住宅建筑商们组织了一个由二十四个小企业组织组成的联盟来支持Martin的候选资格。
The heavy thunder, however, came from Wall Street—scores of daily calls and letters from bankers and brokers to their contacts in the Reagan Administration, all urging Volcker. The more the financial markets read in the newspapers that the White House men wanted to pick someone else, the more intense the lobbying became. James Baker received a personal plea from an old friend in Houston, Ben Love of the Texas Commerce Bank, and lots of calls from Wall Street leaders. “Wall Street was bugging us all the time,” an aide to Baker said. “They really waged a campaign, just tons of them calling.”
然而，最强烈的呼声来自华尔街——每天都有大量银行家和经纪商致电或致信他们在里根政府中的联系人，全都力挺Volcker。金融市场在报纸上看到白宫人士想要另选他人的报道越多，游说活动就越激烈。James Baker收到了一位来自休斯顿的老友Ben Love（德克萨斯商业银行）的私人恳求，以及大量华尔街领袖的电话。"华尔街一直在烦我们，"Baker的一位助手说。"他们真的发动了一场宣传攻势，无数人在打电话。"
Lest there was still any doubt about what Wall Street wanted, a survey of 702 financial-market executives was released by the A. G. Becker Paribas brokerage—77 percent of them wanted Volcker. If he was not to be reappointed, Alan Greenspan was their distant second choice, but only 37 percent expressed special confidence in him. Milton Friedman was third with 11 percent. Preston Martin was sixth with only 7 percent.3
唯恐仍有人对华尔街的意愿存疑，A. G. Becker Paribas证券公司发布了一项对702名金融市场高管的调查——其中77%的人希望Volcker留任。如果他不能重新被任命，Alan Greenspan是他们远居第二的选择，但只有37%的人对他表示特别的信心。Milton Friedman以11%位列第三。Preston Martin以仅7%排在第六。
The White House advisers resented all the heat from Wall Street and insisted that it counted for little. This President was a “Main Street” Republican, they claimed, who paid little heed to the powerful voices of finance. Nevertheless, the small-business men from “Main Street” who wanted someone else did not get their way. The bankers and brokers from Wall Street did.
白宫顾问们对华尔街施加的巨大压力感到恼火，坚称这没什么影响。他们声称，这位总统是一位"主街"共和党人，不太理会金融界的强大声音。然而，那些希望另选他人的"主街"小企业主们并未如愿以偿。来自华尔街的银行家和经纪商得偿所愿。
“No one had a strong alternative,” said David Stockman, who had helped encourage the Wall Street lobbying. “There was a rising crescendo that failure to appoint Volcker would upset the markets. In the end, that was decisive.”
"没有人有一个强有力的替代人选，"David Stockman说，他曾帮助鼓励华尔街的游说活动。"呼声越来越高，说如果不任命Volcker会扰乱市场。最终，这一点起了决定性作用。"
“Volcker won by default,” an Administration official complained. “It was almost unanimous that it should be somebody other than Volcker, someone we can work with better and with the preferred ideology. But all the parties against Volcker were totally split. You couldn’t get three votes for any one person.”
"Volcker是默认获胜的，"一位行政官员抱怨道。"几乎所有人都认为应该选Volcker以外的其他人，一个我们能更好地合作、拥有更合意意识形态的人。但所有反对Volcker的派系完全分裂。你无法为任何一个人拉到三票。"
The discussions boiled down to a choice between Volcker and Greenspan—then Greenspan withdrew and endorsed Volcker. The President’s counselors were confronted with a choice that, for many months, they had promised themselves they would not make.
讨论最终归结为在Volcker和Greenspan之间做选择——然后Greenspan退出并转而支持Volcker。总统的顾问们面临着一个他们数月来一直承诺自己不会做出的选择。
At that point, the Federal Reserve chairman himself forced the issue. On June 6, Volcker abruptly phoned the White House and arranged for a private meeting with the President. None of the White House senior advisers was included. Volcker and Reagan were alone together in the Oval Office.
就在此时，美联储主席本人迫使问题得到解决。6月6日，Volcker突然致电白宫，安排与总统私下会面。白宫高级顾问无一参加。Volcker和Reagan单独在椭圆形办公室会面。
“This has dragged on too long and you ought to settle it one way or the other,” Volcker told the President. The rumors and speculations were adding to financial uncertainties. The President should make his choice and announce it.
"这件事拖得太久了，你该以某种方式解决它，"Volcker告诉总统。谣言和猜测正在加剧金融不确定性。总统应该做出选择并公之于众。
The Fed chairman raised another tantalizing point: if reappointed, he would not necessarily serve out the full four years. Volcker reminded the President that he supported the idea of making the Federal Reserve chairman’s tenure roughly coterminous with the President’s term, so that a new President could appoint his own Fed chairman within one year after taking office. Legislation to make this change was pending in Congress and Volcker had already endorsed it. A reasonable inference, the kind that politicians might draw from such conversations, was that Volcker was hinting at a compromise—if Reagan reappointed him now, Volcker could step down in midterm and the President, assuming he was re-elected, would be able to choose his own man in 1985 or early 1986.
美联储主席还提出了另一个耐人寻味的观点：如果重新被任命，他不一定非要做满整个四年任期。Volcker提醒总统，他支持让美联储主席的任期大致与总统任期同步结束的想法，这样新总统可以在上任后一年内任命自己的美联储主席。实现这一改变的立法正在国会待决，Volcker已经表示支持。一个合理的推论——政客们可能会从这种谈话中推导出的那种推论——是Volcker在暗示一种妥协：如果Reagan现在重新任命他，Volcker可以在任期中期辞职，而总统（假设他赢得连任）将能在1985年或1986年初选择自己的人选。
When Volcker got back to the Fed, Edwin Meese and James Baker came running over to his office. “What did you tell the President?” they asked. Volcker told them the same things he had told their boss. Later, when the substance of these conversations dribbled out into political gossip, it was said that Volcker had promised the White House that he would resign by 1986 and they could appoint their own Fed chairman. This was not the case, but every time this rumor surfaced, financial markets expressed their jitters and the principals denied there was ever such an agreement.
当Volcker回到美联储时，Edwin Meese和James Baker跑到他的办公室。"你告诉总统什么了？"他们问道。Volcker告诉他们的内容与他告诉老板的相同。后来，当这些谈话的内容逐渐渗入政治八卦圈时，据说Volcker曾向白宫承诺他会在1986年之前辞职，他们可以任命自己的美联储主席。情况并非如此，但每当这个谣言浮现时，金融市场都会表现出紧张情绪，而相关各方都否认存在过这样的协议。
The White House political managers, yielding reluctantly to the inevitability of Volcker’s reappointment, nevertheless attempted to establish some ground rules with him for the future course of monetary policy, particularly leading up to the crucial re-election season in 1984. “You don’t want a political whore as Fed chairman,” one of them explained, “but you don’t want someone who is philosophically opposed to your economic policy or partisan in the other direction. We assured ourselves that we could count on him being neutral as a policy maker.”
白宫政治管理者们尽管不情愿地屈服于Volcker连任的必然性，但仍试图与他建立一些关于未来货币政策走向的基本原则，特别是针对关键的1984年连任选举季。"你不想要一个政治娼妓式的美联储主席，"其中一位解释说，"但你也不想要一个在哲学上反对你的经济政策或偏向另一个党派的人。我们确保了自己可以指望他在政策制定上保持中立。"
The conversations were never so explicit that they took the form of negotiations. Volcker, for his part, vigorously denied that he had ever discussed the conduct of monetary policy in connection with his reappointment, either with the President or with any of the senior White House advisers. Still, the President’s men thought they had an understanding with the Federal Reserve chairman.
这些谈话从未明确到构成谈判的程度。Volcker则坚决否认他曾就自己的连任问题讨论过货币政策的执行，无论是与总统还是与任何白宫高级顾问。尽管如此，总统的人认为他们与美联储主席达成了谅解。
I certainly recall a meeting [an Administration official said] where the President would say to Volcker, “I’d like to know that we can count on you.” Or rather he’d say, “I’d like to know that your goals and mine are the same—sustained economic growth without inflation.” And Volcker agreed with that. We translated that into this understanding: the Fed would provide sufficient growth in the money supply to insure sustained economic growth, without inflation.
我当然记得一次会议 [一位行政官员说]，总统对Volcker说："我想知道我们可以指望你。"或者他会说："我想知道你的目标和我的是否一致——无通胀的持续经济增长。"Volcker对此表示同意。我们将其理解为：美联储将提供足够的货币供应增长，以确保在没有通胀的情况下实现持续经济增长。
Besides the President, the discussion with Volcker included Baker, Donald Regan, Edwin Meese and Michael Deaver, the official said. “There was no suggestion that I’m not going to reappoint you if you don’t agree to this, but we may well have discussed that the brakes were shoved on too hard in the recession,” the official recalled. “And we made the point that there were no signs of inflation returning now.” The supposed “understanding” meant a lot more to the White House than it did to Paul Volcker. The President’s advisers thought they had extracted operating terms from Volcker, an agreement they could hold him to later, if the Fed shifted course and threatened the recovery in the 1984 campaign season. But what exactly did “sufficient” mean? Or “sustained economic growth”?
这位官员说，除了总统，与Volcker的讨论还包括Baker、Donald Regan、Edwin Meese和Michael Deaver。"没有人暗示'如果你不同意这个，我就不重新任命你'，但我们很可能讨论过，刹车在衰退中踩得太狠了，"这位官员回忆道。"而且我们指出，现在没有任何通胀回归的迹象。"所谓的"谅解"对白宫的意义远大于对Paul Volcker的意义。总统的顾问们以为他们从Volcker那里榨取出了操作条件，一份他们以后可以要求他遵守的协议——如果美联储改变路线并威胁到1984年竞选季的经济复苏的话。但"足够"到底是什么意思？或者说"持续的经济增长"？
“It’s a perfectly good cliche,” a senior White House official complained, “but it’s meaningless. It’s not a coherent basis for asking the Fed to pursue a specific policy. Volcker can deliver on that commitment with 3 percent money growth and others can say it should be 6 percent. All the parties thought it had meaning, but Volcker could make it mean anything he wanted.”
"这是一个非常完美的陈词滥调，"一位白宫高级官员抱怨道，"但这毫无意义。这不是要求美联储执行特定政策的合理基础。Volcker可以用3%的货币增长来兑现这一承诺，而其他人可以说应该是6%。所有各方都认为它有含义，但Volcker可以让它意味着他想要的任何东西。"
The White House, nevertheless, expected the terms to be honored. As David Stockman said, “Jim Baker thought he had a commitment that nobody was going to screw up the economy in the election year.” On June 18, the President chose his weekly Saturday-morning radio broadcast to announce the news: he was reappointing Paul Volcker to a new four-year term. Volcker, the President declared, is “as dedicated as I am to continuing the fight against inflation.” Afterward, Treasury Secretary Regan added an important corollary: “Like the President, Volcker wants the recovery to be sustained and is committed to lower interest rates.”4
尽管如此，白宫仍期望这些条款得到遵守。正如David Stockman所说："Jim Baker认为他得到了一个承诺，即没有人会在选举年搞砸经济。"6月18日，总统选择在他每周六上午的广播讲话中宣布这个消息：他将重新任命Paul Volcker担任新的四年任期。Volcker，总统宣称，"和我一样致力于继续抗击通胀。"之后，财政部长Regan添加了一个重要的推论："像总统一样，Volcker希望复苏得以持续，并致力于降低利率。"
The Treasury Secretary, addressing the National Press Club, was perhaps entitled to a moment of I-told-you-so. For more than two years, the former chief executive of Merrill Lynch had been heckled by the press for his optimism, belittled for his stubborn faith in the principles of supply-side economics. Now it was his turn to cite the economic statistics. The bullish American recovery was redeeming the faith.
财政部长在全国新闻俱乐部的讲话中，或许有资格享受片刻的"我早告诉过你"。两年多来，这位美林证券的前首席执行官因其乐观主义而受到媒体的嘲讽，因其对供给学派经济学原则的固执信念而受到贬低。现在，轮到他说出经济统计数据了。强劲的美国复苏正在证明这种信念的正确性。
As custodians of the American Dream [Donald Regan boasted], we have nursed it through difficult days. We have brought it into the sunlight. And we will not surrender it to those who would go back to the old, failed dogmas of yesterday. We will now move from recovery to renewal. And we will disperse the dream until it is shared equally by millions who until now have known it only as an abstraction.
作为美国梦的守护者 [Donald Regan夸耀道]，我们悉心照料它度过了艰难的日子。我们已将它带入了阳光之中。我们不会将其拱手让给那些想要回到昨日陈旧失败教条的人。我们现在将从复苏走向复兴。我们将传播这个梦想，直到它被数百万此前只将其视为抽象概念的人们平等分享。
Industrial production had soared by 19 percent in the last three months. New orders for durable goods were up 36 percent. Employment in manufacturing jumped by 100,000 for two months running.
工业生产在过去三个月飙升了19%。耐用品新订单增长了36%。制造业就业连续两个月增加10万人。
Retail sales increased at a 27 percent rate over the last quarter. Autos were up 38 percent from the low point the year before, iron and steel up 44 percent, housing up nearly 50 percent. Consumer confidence, measured by regular polling at the University of Michigan, was soaring to its highest level in ten years.
零售额在上一季度以27%的速度增长。汽车销量比去年低谷增长了38%，钢铁增长44%，住房增长近50%。消费者信心——通过密歇根大学的定期民意调查衡量——飙升至十年来的最高水平。
Donald Regan declared victory and mocked those liberal skeptics who had opposed the President’s original doctrine, the program of massive tax cuts for business and individuals. The critics, Regan said, resembled comic-strip characters from Pogo, reduced to warning: “We are confronted with insurmountable opportunities.”
Donald Regan宣布胜利，并嘲笑那些反对总统最初信条（即对企业和个人大幅减税的计划）的自由派批评者。Regan说，这些批评者就像Pogo漫画中的人物，只能发出这样的警告："我们正面临着不可逾越的机遇。"
The recession did last longer than anyone expected [Regan continued]. But all the while, overlooked or simply denied by those who peddle pessimism for their livelihood, the market forces embodied in the Reagan program were gathering momentum. As inflation fell and worker productivity increased, private companies found themselves with more and more internally generated funds. . . .
衰退确实比任何人预期的都要持久 [Regan继续说]。但一直以来，那些以贩卖悲观情绪为生的人所忽视或干脆否认的是，体现在里根计划中的市场力量正在积聚动力。随着通胀下降和工人生产率提高，私营公司发现自己拥有越来越多内部产生的资金……
But our economic program is really aimed at Main Street. . . . What we are seeing is the creation of new opportunities from the grassroots up. We are building a bridge between today’s recovery and tomorrow’s lasting prosperity. And we are proving that quick fixes, while politically appealing, can never take the place of fundamental market forces and human motivations.5
但我们的经济计划实际上是针对主街的……我们正在看到的是从基层开始创造新的机会。我们正在建造一座桥梁，连接今天的复苏和明天的持久繁荣。我们正在证明，快速解决方案虽然在政治上吸引人，但永远无法取代基本的市场力量和人类动机。
The liberal critics were certainly wrong about one thing—the recovery was not weak, as they had predicted, but as robust and rapid as any of the previous postwar cycles. As the American economy regained its strength, it was clear that the abnormally high level of interest rates was not going to stunt a normal post-recession boom. Economists could worry about the implications, but the high price of money did not seem to inhibit consumers and businesses from rushing to join the action.
自由派批评者在一件事上肯定是错的——复苏并不像他们预测的那样疲弱，而是像战后任何一次周期一样强劲而迅速。随着美国经济恢复力量，很明显异常高的利率水平并不会抑制正常的衰退后繁荣。经济学家们可以担忧其影响，但高昂的货币价格似乎并没有阻止消费者和企业争相加入这场盛宴。
Alfred Zeller, who ran a fur shop in St. Johnsbury, Vermont, noticed that, on the contrary, his business improved when interest rates went up. “A lot of my customers are middle-aged, semiretired and retired,” Zeller said. “Some are widows whose husbands have left them quite comfortable. All they’ve got is liquid assets.” With higher returns on their money funds, they bought more expensive furs.6
Alfred Zeller在佛蒙特州St. Johnsbury经营一家皮草店，他注意到恰恰相反，当利率上升时，他的生意反而更好了。"我的很多顾客都是中年人、半退休和退休人员，"Zeller说。"有些是寡妇，她们的丈夫给她们留下了相当舒适的生活。她们拥有的只有流动资产。"随着货币基金回报率提高，她们购买了更昂贵的皮草。
William Hamilton, a political consultant for Senator John Glenn, who was seeking the Democratic presidential nomination, found that high interest rates simply did not “personally eat away” at voters. The issue, he concluded, could not be easily packaged by Democrats for the 1984 campaigns.
为正在寻求民主党总统候选人提名的John Glenn参议员担任政治顾问的William Hamilton发现，高利率根本不会"个人性地侵蚀"选民。他得出结论，民主党人无法轻易将这个问题包装成1984年竞选的议题。
President Reagan reminded voters repeatedly—and correctly—that nominal interest rates had been cut in half since he took office, from the inflationary frenzy of January 1981 when the prime rate hit 20.5 percent to the current level of 11 percent. What the President did not discuss, of course, was that real interest rates—the exchange of real wealth after discounting for inflation—were actually much higher now and still rising. Though far more significant, real interest rates were another element of money the public didn’t understand.7
里根总统反复且正确地提醒选民，名义利率自他上任以来已经下降了一半——从1981年1月通胀狂热时期优惠利率达到20.5%降至目前的11%。当然，总统没有讨论的是，实际利率——扣除通胀后的实际财富交换——实际上现在更高，而且还在上升。尽管意义更为重大，实际利率是公众不理解的货币的另一个要素。
Paul Volcker also blithely dismissed the impact of high interest rates on the different classes of citizens. The subject was too complicated, he claimed, to sort out winners and losers. “Lots of relatively poor people love high interest rates^it depends on how they borrow and save,” Volcker insisted. “A lot of older people love high interest. I even get mail, not very much but some, when interest rates go down.”
Paul Volcker也轻描淡写地否定了高利率对不同阶层公民的影响。他声称这个话题太复杂，无法区分赢家和输家。"很多相对贫穷的人喜欢高利率——这取决于他们如何借贷和储蓄，"Volcker坚持说。"很多老年人喜欢高利率。当利率下降时，我甚至会收到邮件，虽然不多，但有一些。"
The Federal Reserve Board’s own research on financial wealth had, of course, described the implications in starker terms. The Fed studies demonstrated that 55 percent of American families were net debtors in terms of financial assets and that a mere 10 percent of American families owned 86 percent of all the net financial wealth. Paul Volcker said he was unfamiliar with this research.
美联储理事会自己对金融财富的研究当然用更直白的语言描述了其影响。美联储的研究表明，55%的美国家庭在金融资产方面是净债务人，而仅仅10%的美国家庭拥有全部净金融财富的86%。Paul Volcker说他对此研究并不熟悉。
Keynesian economists, in any case, could claim vindication on a more fundamental point about the economy and they did. This was not a “supply side” recovery, they pointed out; it was Keynesian “demand side.” In broad outline, they were correct. Notwithstanding the gloating rhetoric from the President and Donald Regan and other Republicans, the expansion that developed in 1983 did not fulfill any of the supply-side premises articulated back in 1981. On the contrary, the recovery followed the standard Keynesian format for demand stimulus—it was led by consumers spending money on goods in the marketplace, not by savers investing in new capital formation.
无论如何，凯恩斯主义经济学家可以在一个关于经济的更基本观点上声称得到了证实，他们也确实这样做了。他们指出，这不是"供给侧"复苏；这是凯恩斯主义的"需求侧"。从大体轮廓上看，他们是正确的。尽管总统、Donald Regan和其他共和党人发表了洋洋得意的言论，1983年形成的扩张并未实现1981年阐述的任何供给侧前提。相反，复苏遵循了标准凯恩斯主义的需求刺激模式——它是由消费者在市场上花钱所驱动，而非由储蓄者投资于新的资本构成所驱动。
The Reagan tax cuts stimulated consumer demand by putting more cash in private hands, and this increased stimulus, combined with the Fed’s easing of interest rates, drove the expansion. After several quarters, consumer spending moderated and, as usually occurred, a boom in capital-goods spending developed, producers investing in new productive facilities and equipment.
里根减税政策通过将更多现金置于私人手中刺激了消费需求，而这种增加的刺激，加上美联储放松利率，推动了扩张。几个季度后，消费者支出放缓，并且像通常发生的那样，出现了资本品支出的繁荣——生产商投资于新的生产性设施和设备。
This was the standard pattern for economic recovery; the sequence and dimensions did not deviate appreciably from past cycles. The effects of the Reagan fiscal policy were not different from what liberal Democrats had done to manipulate economic growth in previous seasons—raising aggregate demand by running federal deficits—only with $200 billion deficits Ronald Reagan’s Keynesian push was much bolder than anything Democrats had dared attempt since World War II.
这是经济复苏的标准模式；其顺序和规模与过去的周期没有明显偏差。里根财政政策的效果与自由派民主党人之前为操纵经济增长所做的并无不同——通过运行联邦赤字来提高总需求——只是凭借2000亿美元的赤字，Ronald Reagan的凯恩斯主义推动比民主党人自二战以来所敢尝试的任何举措都更为大胆。
“The paradox,” said Charles Schultze, Jimmy Carter’s CEA chairman, “is it’s the opposite of supply-side economics.” The central premise of the new conservative doctrine had been that the across-the-board tax cuts would allow citizens to increase their personal savings—and these savings would flow into capital investment in new factories and equipment, expanding the productive capacity of the nation. Thus, broader prosperity would be created by enhancing the “supply side” of the economy. Nearly the opposite occurred: the savings rate declined, instead of increasing, during the Reagan recovery and fell to record lows the longer the expansion continued.8
"矛盾之处在于，"Jimmy Carter的经济顾问委员会主席Charles Schultze说，"这与供给侧经济学的理念完全相反。"这一新保守主义信条的核心前提是，全面的减税将使公民能够增加个人储蓄——而这些储蓄将流入对新工厂和设备等资本品的投资，从而扩大国家的生产能力。因此，通过增强经济的"供给侧"，将创造更广泛的繁荣。然而，几乎相反的情况发生了：在里根复苏期间，储蓄率非但没有增加，反而下降，并且随着扩张的持续而降至历史低点。
Rather than savings, it was more accurate to say Reagan’s economic revival was powered by debt—living on borrowed money. It was borrowed and spent by the federal government, by businesses and by consumers. As the recovery proceeded, an increasing portion of the accumulating debt was borrowed from abroad—capital supplied by foreign investors. For the first time since 1914, the United States would become a debtor nation again, borrowing more than it lent in international finance.
更准确地说，与其说里根的经济复兴是靠储蓄推动的，不如说是靠债务推动的——靠借钱过日子。联邦政府、企业和消费者都在借钱和花钱。随着复苏的进行，不断累积的债务中越来越大的部分是从国外借来的——由外国投资者提供的资本。自1914年以来，美国将首次再次成为一个债务国，在国际金融中借入多于贷出。
Ronald Reagan’s version of Keynes really was different, however, from what Democrats had done before him, despite the broad similarities. The President’s economic policy, combined with the Fed’s, effectively inverted the old liberal priorities—as if the principle of income distribution articulated by Marriner Eccles and the other New Dealers had been stood on its head. Instead of pushing money downward, in order to stimulate the consumption of goods by the broadest base of families, the federal government was now discreetly pushing money upward—concentrating income disproportionately in the upper economic brackets.
然而，Ronald Reagan版的凯恩斯主义确实与他之前的民主党人所做的有所不同，尽管存在广泛的相似之处。总统的经济政策，加上美联储的政策，实际上颠覆了旧的自由主义优先顺序——就好像Marriner Eccles和其他新政者所阐述的收入分配原则被颠倒了一样。联邦政府不再是向下输送资金以刺激最广泛家庭对商品的消费，而是谨慎地将资金向上输送——不成比例地将收入集中到较高的经济阶层。
The reversed redistribution of incomes was masked at first by the general prosperity, but the effects were already visible in 1983. According to the U.S. Census, only families on the top 20 percent of the economic ladder enjoyed real increases in their after-tax household incomes from 1980 to 1983. The others, the bottom 80 percent, actually lost. The highest fifth, families earning $38,000 or more, gained an average of $1,480 per household in real income, and the top 5 percent, earning more than $60,000, gained an average of $3,320. Families in the middle lost about $560 and the working poor lost about $250.
这种反向的收入再分配最初被普遍的繁荣所掩盖，但其效果在1983年已经可见。根据美国人口普查局的数据，从1980年到1983年，只有处于经济阶梯前20%的家庭在税后家庭收入方面实现了实际增长。其他人——底部的80%——实际上遭受了损失。收入最高的五分之一——年收入38,000美元及以上的家庭——每个家庭的实际收入平均增加了1,480美元，而收入最高的5%——年收入超过60,000美元的家庭——平均增加了3,320美元。中间层的家庭损失了约560美元，而工薪穷人损失了约250美元。
The progressive ladder that had guided liberal redistribution politics for two generations was turned upside down. The wealthiest got most and the least got less. The income share that went to the most prosperous families, the top fifth on the ladder, became even more disproportionate, increasing from 40.2 to 42 percent of all U.S. income.9
指导了两代人自由派再分配政治的累进阶梯被颠倒了。最富有的人得到最多，而最底层的人得到最少。流向最富裕家庭——阶梯上前五分之一——的收入份额变得更加不成比例，从占美国总收入的40.2%增加到42%。
The reversed redistribution flowed from several channels of government action, involving both fiscal policy and the Federal Reserve. The Reagan Administration’s budget cuts had modestly scaled back benefits for lower-income families, while the boom in defense spending naturally tended to favor high-salaried technical professions. The across-the-board reduction in tax rates delivered the largest benefits to taxpayers in the upper brackets. The business tax cuts rewarded the owners of capital and for corporations offset much of the impact of high interest rates. The process of liquidation had depressed economic activity generally, but the largest personal losses were absorbed by labor and small business. AD these influences flowed in the same direction.
这种反向再分配源自政府行动的多个渠道，涉及财政政策和美联储。里根政府的预算削减适度缩减了对低收入家庭的福利，而国防支出的繁荣自然倾向于惠及高薪技术职业。全面的税率降低为较高税级的纳税人带来了最大利益。企业减税奖励了资本所有者，并为公司抵消了高利率带来的大部分影响。清算过程普遍压抑了经济活动，但最大的个人损失由劳动力和小企业承担。所有这些影响都朝着同一个方向流动。
Finally, the continuing high level of real interest rates guaranteed a sustained, regressive redistribution of income upward. The rewards and penalties were steadily compounded between creditors and debtors, like compound interest itself. A larger share of incomes automatically flowed to the people who owned most of the net financial wealth, the well-to-do and the wealthy. The borrowers would owe more and more. The rentier had not only eluded the “euthanasia” that John Maynard Keynes had prophesied for him in The General Theory of fifty years before. The owner of capital was now receiving far greater rent for the use of his money than Keynes had ever seen.
最后，持续的高实际利率保证了收入向上的、持续的累退式再分配。奖励和惩罚在债权人和债务人之间不断累积，就像复利本身一样。更大比例的收入自动流向拥有大部分净金融财富的人——富裕和富有阶层。借款人将欠得越来越多。食利者不仅逃脱了John Maynard Keynes在五十年前的《通论》中为他预言的"安乐死"。资本所有者现在为其资金的使用所获得的租金远远超过Keynes所见过的任何水平。
In effect, the reward structure of American capitalism was gradually being altered—less for labor, more for capital; a smaller share for work, a larger one for money. This shift was visible in the shrinking portion of total U.S. personal income that was derived from wages and salaries. For many decades, from the 1930s onward, wages and salaries had accounted for about two-thirds of all personal income in the United States. In the 1970s, the wage share declined proportionately as government entitlement programs became a larger portion of incomes, mainly Social Security.
实际上，美国资本主义的奖励结构正在逐渐改变——给劳动的份额减少，给资本的份额增加；给工作的份额变小，给金钱的份额变大。这种转变可以从美国个人总收入中来自工资和薪水的比例不断缩小中看出。从1930年代起的几十年里，工资和薪水约占美国所有个人收入的三分之二。在1970年代，随着政府福利计划——主要是社会保障——成为收入的更大组成部分，工资份额比例下降。
In the era of Volcker and Reagan, the wage share shrank much further—to its lowest level in fifty years—as a larger and larger portion of total U.S. income was claimed by the incomes produced by money, dividends and interest payments. By 1983, wages and salaries accounted for only 60.7 percent of total personal income. The following year, the wage share would shrink further—to 59.5 percent—the lowest level since 1929.
在Volcker和Reagan时代，工资份额缩水得更多——降至五十年来的最低水平——因为美国总收入中越来越大的部分被货币产生的收入——股息和利息支付——所占据。到1983年，工资和薪水仅占个人总收入的60.7%。次年，工资份额进一步缩水至59.5%——自1929年以来的最低水平。
This was the restoration of capital—capital restored to a primacy it had not enjoyed since before the Great Crash. From 1979 to 1983, the personal incomes derived from interest grew by more than 70 percent, an increase of $158 billion, while wage incomes grew by only 33 percent in the same period. When income from stock dividends was included, the returns on capital would claim 20 percent of the whole in 1984, compared to 11 percent in 1979. Money’s share of the rewards generated by the American system and apportioned among its citizens had been virtually doubled—primarily by government action—in only five years’ time.
这是资本的回归——资本恢复到了自大崩盘以来未曾享受过的重要地位。从1979年到1983年，来自利息的个人收入增长了70%以上，增加了1580亿美元，而同期工资收入仅增长了33%。当股票股息收入计入后，资本的回报将在1984年占据总收入的20%，而1979年仅为11%。在短短五年时间里，货币在美国这个体系所产生的并在其公民中进行分配的奖励中所占的份额几乎翻了一番——这主要是通过政府行动实现的。
But did any of this matter? In the general euphoria, the effects of shifting income shares hardly seemed of consequence to the robust performance of the American economy. America was back and expanding vigorously again. The economy was demonstrating that neither high interest rates nor huge deficits would hold it back. After all, wealthy people spent their money too. The increased discretionary income available to the upper brackets from higher interest rates clearly invigorated the consumer demand.
但这有关系吗？在普遍的乐观情绪中，收入份额变化的影响似乎与美国经济的强劲表现关系不大。美国回来了，再次强劲扩张。经济正在证明，无论是高利率还是巨额赤字都无法阻挡它。毕竟，富人也花他们的钱。高利率使上层阶层可自由支配的收入增加，这显然刺激了消费需求。
The well-to-do, one could even say, led the nation to recovery. They bought expensive cars and luxury vacations, jewelry and watches and artwork, boats and bikes and pleasure aircraft, second homes at the beach or in the mountains. Home builders who had nearly gone broke a few years before building moderately priced subdivisions discovered a rich new market in resort condominiums. This upward tilt in the composition of consumer spending was fostering what some business economists called “a two-tier economy,” a subtle breakup in the mass-consumption patterns that had driven the American economy since World War II. Fortune magazine summarized the implications for its business readers:
甚至可以说，富裕阶层引领了国家的复苏。他们购买昂贵的汽车和豪华假期、珠宝、手表和艺术品、船只、自行车和娱乐飞机、海边或山里的第二套住房。几年前因建造中等价位住宅小区而几乎破产的住宅建筑商，在度假公寓中发现了一个富饶的新市场。消费支出构成的这种向上倾斜正在催生一些商业经济学家所称的"两级经济"——这是自二战以来驱动美国经济的大众消费模式的微妙破裂。《财富》杂志为其商业读者总结了其影响：
The mass market is splitting apart. Most businessmen don’t realize it yet, but the middle class—the principal market for much of what they make—is gradually being pulled apart. Economic forces are propelling one family after another toward the high or low end of the income spectrum. For many marketers, particularly those positioned to sell to the well-to-do, this presages good times. For others used to selling millions of units of their products to middle-income folks, the prospects are altogether darker.10
大众市场正在分裂。大多数商人尚未意识到这一点，但中产阶级——他们所生产产品的核心市场——正逐渐被撕裂。经济力量正将一个又一个家庭推向收入光谱的高端或低端。对许多营销人员来说，尤其是那些定位为向富裕阶层销售产品的人来说，这预示着好时光。对那些习惯于向中等收入人群销售数百万件产品的商家来说，前景则全然更加暗淡。
Liberal sensibilities were offended, but the liberal impulse for progressive redistribution of income had been discredited and very few were willing to speak for it now. The question of income shares was regarded as a political issue, a faintly old-fashioned argument about social equity, but not a question of great relevance to economic performance.
自由派的敏感神经受到了冒犯，但自由派累进式收入再分配的冲动已经名誉扫地，现在很少有人愿意为之发声。收入份额问题被视为一个政治问题，一个关于社会公平的略显过时的论点，而非与经济表现高度相关的问题。
Even many liberal Keynesian economists accepted that assumption.
即使是许多自由派凯恩斯主义经济学家也接受了这一假设。
So long as aggregate demand was sufficient to drive an expansionary cycle, they allowed, it did not really matter who got the money or what they bought. In gross economic terms, consumer demand generated by wealthy widows buying new furs with their increased interest income was indistinguishable from the consumer demand of a worker spending his pay raise on a new car. All of it would stimulate production and jobs. This was logical only if one assumed no significant difference between furs and cars.
只要总需求足以推动一个扩张性周期，他们说，谁拿到了钱或者他们买了什么其实并不重要。从宏观经济角度看，富裕的寡妇用增加利息收入购买新皮草所产生的消费需求，与工人将加薪用于购买新车所产生的消费需求并无区别。这一切都会刺激生产和就业。这只有在假定皮草和汽车之间没有显著差异的情况下才是合乎逻辑的。
The Democrats of the 1980s occasionally expressed the wish for a fairer distribution of the rewards, but they fashioned it into a political issue labeled “fairness,” not an argument about achieving better economic performance. Most Democrats no longer understood what Mar-riner Eccles had figured out back in the 1930s—that the progressive distribution of incomes was a necessary prerequisite for healthy economic growth. In a sense, Andrew Mellon, the great Republican theorist from the “roaring twenties,” had at last won the argument.
1980年代的民主党人偶尔表达过对更公平地分配奖励的愿望，但他们将其塑造成一个标榜为"公平"的政治议题，而非关于实现更好经济表现的论点。大多数民主党人不再理解Mar-riner Eccles早在1930年代就悟出的道理——收入的累进式分配是健康经济增长的必要前提。从某种意义上说，来自"咆哮的二十年代"的伟大共和党理论家Andrew Mellon最终赢得了这场争论。
Strangely enough, there was still one group nagged by private doubts about the long-term economic consequences of high interest rates—some of the senior policy makers of the Federal Reserve. Their fitful worrying seemed slightly out of character, since Federal Reserve governors tried to avoid questions of class conflict like the distribution of incomes. Furthermore, they themselves were implicated in the regressive redistribution through their own interest-rate policy, though they insisted this was the unavoidable consequence of the huge federal deficits enacted by Congress and the President. In any case, some at the Fed saw worrisome portents in the lopsided recovery. As Lyle Gramley explained:
奇怪的是，仍然有一群人私下对高利率的长期经济后果感到不安——美联储的一些高级决策者。他们断断续续的担忧似乎有点不合时宜，因为美联储理事们试图避免诸如收入分配之类的阶级冲突问题。此外，他们自己也通过自己的利率政策卷入了这种累退式再分配，尽管他们坚持认为这是国会和总统颁布的巨额联邦赤字不可避免的后果。无论如何，美联储的一些人在这不平衡的复苏中看到了令人担忧的征兆。正如Lyle Gramley所解释的那样：
With a sufficiently restrictive monetary policy, you might be able to maintain low inflation for a long period of time. In theory, as an economist,
通过足够紧缩的货币政策，你可能能够在很长一段时间内维持低通胀。理论上，作为一个经济学家，我可以想象这种情况持续相当一段时间。也许在某些理论世界中，你可以做到这一点。但是——从政治上说——这将是一颗炸弹。对经济中信贷敏感部门的影响将过于严重。
I can imagine that going on for quite a while. Maybe in some theoretical world, you can do that. But—politically—it’s going to be dynamite. The effects on the credit-sensitive sectors of the economy are going to be much too severe.
Charles Partee担心对收入的影响。"已经发生了相当多的向高收入群体的收入再分配，"他说。高收入的债权人阶层以所有借款人为代价稳步增加他们的收入——这些借款人必须借更多钱来维持他们的支出。
Charles Partee worried about the effects on incomes. “There’s been quite a lot of redistribution of income toward the higher-income groups,” he said. The high-income creditor classes increase their incomes steadily at the expense of all the borrowers—who must borrow more in order to keep up their spending.
Gramley指出了美国现在所追求的经济模式中的一个基本失衡：实际利率高于美国经济的实际增长率。从根本上说，这不可能永远持续下去。一个反复借入超过其劳动或企业产出所产生的盈余的债务人，将在债务上越陷越深，直到或早或晚，复利的无情压力将其击垮。这是经济学中的古老法则，至少和Adam Smith一样古老，并且它没有被废除。广义上说，它适用于整个国家，同样也适用于个人。
Gramley pointed out a basic disparity in the economic format that the nation was now pursuing: real interest rates were running higher than the real growth rate for the U.S. economy. In fundamental terms, that could not go on forever. A debtor who repeatedly borrows more than the surplus his labor or business enterprise produces will fall further and further behind in his obligations until, sooner or later, the inexorable pressures of compound interest defeat him. This was ancient law in economics, at least as old as Adam Smith, and it had not been repealed. In broad terms, it applied to an entire nation as aptly as it did to individuals.
"如果实际利率超过经济增长——如果内部债务增长快于GNP——那么我们的问题会越来越糟，"Gramley观察到。Henry Wallich就债务问题表达了同样的观点："只有当债务增长与创收资产的增长相匹配时，贷款人才能确保其资金正在被生产性地使用，并且债务偿还能够在未来几年内持续。"
“If real interest rates exceed growth in the economy—if internal debt is growing faster than GNP—then our problems get worse and worse,” Gramley observed. Henry Wallich made the same point about debt: “Only if growth in indebtedness is matched by growth of income-producing assets can the lender be sure that productive use is being made of his funds and that the debt service can be sustained in the years to come.”11
Wallich的警告实际上是针对商业银行向欠发达国家大量放贷的，但这个逻辑同样适用于那些借贷超过其未来收入偿还能力的美国式家庭，或者这样做的企业，乃至整个美国经济。
Wallich’s warning was actually directed at commercial banks when they were lending vast sums to less-developed countries, but the logic applied just as well to American families that borrowed more than their future incomes could pay back or businesses that did the same or, for that matter, the American economy as a whole.
这种病态效应——当实际利率高于产出的实际增长时发生的持续侵蚀——可能更容易在一个小企业中看到，比如一个农场或任何其他依赖信贷经营的企业。农民可能以6%的实际利率借入运营资本，期望企业能够覆盖成本并留有利润。然而，在季末，农民用这些资本生产的实际盈余只有4%。他必须以某种方式弥补差额——缺少的2%——要么从自己的个人利润中出，要么进一步削减生产成本，要么借更多的钱来弥补缺口。
The pathological effects—the steady erosion that occurs when real interest rates are higher than the real growth of output—were perhaps easier to visualize in a small enterprise, a farm or any other business venture whose operation depended on credit. The farmer might borrow operating capital at 6 percent real interest, expecting the enterprise to cover the cost and leave a profit. At the end of the season, however, the real earnings surplus that the farmer has produced with the capital amounts to only 4 percent. He must somehow make up the difference—the missing 2 percent—either from his own personal profits or by cutting production costs further or by borrowing still more money to cover the shortfall.
一位小镇银行家，堪萨斯州Luray的人民州立银行总裁John A. O'Leary Jr.，用具体的语言描述了这一困境：
A small-town banker, John A. O’Leary, Jr., president of the Peoples State Bank in Luray, Kansas, put the dilemma in concrete terms:
那些多年前买了地并且已经还清贷款的年长农民情况还不错。但年轻农民的处境很糟。我们一借钱给他就压垮了他。假设他需要10万美元。我们目前的最优惠利率是13.5%。他赚到的第一个1.2万美元需要用来养家糊口。经营三百英亩土地至少需要9000美元。另外三分之一的收入归地主。如果他从那块地里收获六千蒲式耳小麦，每蒲式耳3.50美元，他总共只收入21,000美元。这个等式根本行不通。如果是6%的利率，他还能维持。
The older farmers who bought their land years ago and have it paid off are doing okay. But the young farmer’s in bad shape. We break him as soon as we lend him some money. Say he needs $100,000. Our best rate now is 13.5 percent. The first $12,000 he earns he needs to feed his family.
关键在于，债权人使用其资本获得了13.5%的保证回报率——但资本本身并未产生13.5%的回报率。农民必须承担损失。下一季，他会再次尝试，除非他的产量奇迹般增长或小麦价格大幅上涨，否则他会进一步落后，他的负担会不断累积。食利者仍然会收取他的利息。最终，他会拥有这个农场。
It costs at least $9,000 to operate three hundred acres. Another one-third of his income goes to the landlord. If he gets six thousand bushels of wheat out of that land, at $3.50 a bushel, he brings in only $21,000. The equation just won’t work. With 6 percent interest rates, he could make it.12
联邦政府本身也陷入了类似的困境，尽管没有人预计政府会破产。鉴于其每年2000亿美元的赤字以及它为国债支付的高利率，政府的债务负担增长速度远快于实际经济——即它赖以获取收入的税基。政府的创收资产——它征税的私人经济——跟不上政府的借贷。
The essential point was that the creditor was guaranteed a 13.5 percent return for the use of his capital—but the capital did not produce a 13.5 percent return. The farmer must swallow the loss. The next season, he would try again, and unless his own output expanded miraculously or the price of wheat went up sharply, he would fall further behind, his burden compounded. The rentier would still collect his interest. Eventually, he would own the farm.
联邦利息支付从1980年的520亿美元增加到1986年的超过1420亿美元——从占全部联邦收入的10%增至19%。这是反向收入再分配的一个重要部分，因为增加的利息支付从所有美国纳税人流向选择性的少数债券持有者。可以说，食利者对纳税人的索取权几乎翻了一番。
The federal government itself was caught in a similar dilemma, though no one expected the government to fail. Given its $200 billion annual deficits and the high interest rates it was paying on Treasury bonds, the government’s debt burden was expanding much faster than the real economy—the tax base it depended on for revenue. The government’s income-producing asset, the private economy which it taxed, was not keeping up with the government’s borrowing.
从理论上讲，华盛顿当然可以通过将越来越多的纳税人的钱用于支付债券持有人——并在政府常规职能上花得越来越少——来维持债务。或者华盛顿可以提高税收来弥补缺口。对国会和总统来说，这两者似乎都不是可行的选择。相反，就像一个债务负担沉重的年轻农民试图维持下去一样，他们决定两者都做——继续支出，并继续借更多的钱。
Federal outlays for interest payments rose from $52 billion in 1980 to more than $142 billion by 1986—from 10 percent of all federal revenue to 19 percent. This was an important part of the reversed redistribution of income, since the increasing interest payments went from all American taxpayers to the select minority who were bondholders. The rentier s claim on the taxpayer, one could say, had nearly doubled.
当实际利率超过实际增长时，同样的破坏性效应也在整个经济中不那么显眼地起着作用。当然，许多家庭和企业超过了平均水平——他们的收入增长快于债务负担，尽管信贷成本高昂，他们仍能领先于他人。其他人在艰难维持，勉强持平。还有一些人则 steadily 落后。然而，平均而言，美国经济是落后的——其产出增长慢于债务增长。
Theoretically, of course, Washington could keep up with the debt simply by devoting more and more of the taxpayers’ money to paying the bondholders—and spending less and less on the regular functions of government. Or Washington could raise taxes to close the gap. Neither seemed like a practical option to Congress and the President. Instead, like a debt-burdened young farmer trying to keep going, they decided to do both—to keep spending and to keep borrowing more.
这种债务紧缩对私人借款人的压力，在很大程度上被美国税法的规定所抵消或至少缓解了。通过允许利息支付税前扣除，政府有效地降低了债务的实际成本，并通过免除一部分联邦税款来谨慎地补贴借款人。这缓和了高利率的影响，并使得许多企业和个人尽管增长缓慢仍能保持领先。
The same debilitating effects worked less obviously on the economy as a whole, when real interest rates exceeded real growth. Many families and businesses, of course, beat the average—their incomes grew faster than their debt burdens and they were able to come out ahead, despite the high cost of credit. Others bumped along and stayed even. Others fell steadily behind. On average, however, the U.S. economy was behind—its output growing more slowly than its debts were growing.
然而，对借款人的税收补贴也增加了一个反常的扭曲：最大的税收节省流向了按最高税率缴纳所得税的最富裕的借款人。对于处于50%税级的人来说，13%的利率在税后实际上只有6.5%。但对于那些处于较低税级、收入较少的人来说，补贴要小得多，或者根本不存在。利息扣除，最初是作为帮助债务人和促进宽松信贷的累进措施而设立的，在永久性高利率时代有了不同的含义。在应对困境的过程中，最强者得到了政府最多的帮助，而许多最弱者则完全没有得到帮助。
The pressures of this debt squeeze on private borrowers were offset or at least ameliorated in a substantial way by the U.S. tax code. By allowing tax deductions for interest payments, the government effectively reduced the real cost of debt and discreetly subsidized borrowers by forgiving a share of their federal taxes. That softened the effects of the high rates and allowed many businesses and individuals to stay ahead, despite slow growth.
尽管如此，人们还是继续借钱。随着复苏的蓬勃发展，信贷迅速扩张，速度远快于美联储的预期。对于一个苦苦挣扎的商人或农民来说，这个选择有时似乎根本不是选择——停止借贷就是停止做生意。对家庭来说，日益蔓延的乐观情绪和就业增长、个人实际收入的激增，似乎预示着他们能够跟上新的借贷步伐。情况正在好转，长期搁置的愿望现在得以实现——新车、新房，所有"美国梦"所承诺的美好事物。
Yet the tax subsidy for borrowers also added a perverse twist: the largest tax savings went to the most prosperous borrowers who paid income taxes at the highest rates. For someone in the 50 percent tax bracket, an interest rate of 13 percent was actually only 6.5 percent after taxes. But, for those in lower tax brackets with less income, the subsidy was much smaller or nonexistent. The interest deduction, originated as a progressive measure to assist debtors and foster easy credit, had a different meaning in an era of permanently high interest rates. In the struggle to cope, the strongest were helped most by the government and many of the weakest were helped not at all.
Charles Partee担心长期后果。债务人和债权人之间的分化，以及正在发生的收入的大规模再分配，都让他感到不安。这种情况让这位理事想起了他在大学里学到的一个古老而早已被遗忘的经济理论——关于复利的病理学。
People did keep borrowing, nonetheless. Credit expanded rapidly as the recovery blossomed, much faster than the Federal Reserve had intended. For a struggling businessman or farmer, the choice sometimes seemed like no choice at all—to stop borrowing was to stop doing business. For families, the spreading optimism and employment, the surging increase in real personal incomes, seemed to promise that they would be able to keep up with their new borrowing. Things were looking up and long-deferred desires were now fulfilled —new cars, new houses, all the good things that the “American dream” promised.
"我上大学时有一本小书，"Partee若有所思地说。"我记不起名字了，但这本书认为，复利的力量是收入再分配中如此强大和根本性的力量，你必须每隔一段时间打破复利利率的影响，核销债务——如果你不想压垮人们的话。"
Charles Partee worried about the long-term consequences. The divergence between debtors and creditors bothered him as well as the gross redistribution of incomes that was occurring. The situation reminded the governor of an old, long-forgotten economic theory he had learned in college about the pathology of compound interest.
典型的核销发生在衰退中，或者更壮观地说，发生在经济崩溃中。债务人大规模违约，债务被清算，这样每个人都可以重新开始。债务可以通过更微妙的方式——通过价格通胀来消除——通过使借款人必须偿还的美元贬值来减轻他们的负担。Partee并没有预测这些结果中的任何一种，但他对这些征兆感到不安。
“I had a little book in college,” Partee mused. “I can’t remember the name of it, but this book argued that the power of compound interest is such a strong and fundamental force in redistributing incomes, you have to break the impact of compound interest rates every so often and write off the debts—if you don’t want to crush people.”
已经发生了相当多的向高收入群体的收入再分配 [这位理事指出]，这削弱了经济中的总需求。它要求消费者积累越来越多的债务以维持支出。你会得到一个良好的消费繁荣——但你得借钱才能得到它。高收入者将增加的收入投资于金融资产——而这些钱又被其他所有人借走。
Typically, that write-off occurred in recession or, more spectacularly, in an economic collapse. Debtors failed massively and debts were liquidated, so everyone could start over again. The debts could be extinguished, more subtly, through price inflation—ameliorating the burden of the borrowers by depreciating the value of the dollars they must pay back. Partee was not predicting any of these outcomes, but he was bothered by the portents.
Charles Partee本质上是在阐述Mariner Eccles五十年前所表达的原则——公平收入分配的经济重要性。这是大多数国会中的自由派民主党人已经忘记、大多数凯恩斯主义经济学家不再认真对待的洞见。美联储的一些理事看到了正在发展的病理，但他们觉得这不是他们的职责范围。
There’s been quite a lot of redistribution of income toward the higher-income groups [the governor observed], and that saps the aggregate demand for the economy. It requires consumers to accumulate more and more debt in order to keep spending. You get a good consumption boom —but you have to borrow to get it. The high-income people invest their increased incomes in financial assets—and that money gets borrowed by everyone else.
"我毫不怀疑，如果利率更低，经济会更健康，"Gramley说。"它将把更多的储蓄分配给实际投资，让储蓄机构改善状况，帮助农业，为国际债务问题做出巨大贡献。但降低利率的方法是解决预算赤字问题。"
Charles Partee was describing essentially the same principles articulated by Marriner Eccles fifty years earlier—the economic importance of equitable income distribution. It was the insight that most liberal Democrats in Congress had forgotten and most Keynesian economists no longer took seriously. Some governors at the Fed saw the pathology developing, but they did not feel it was their place to do anything about it.
Robbin Craven，一位来自宾夕法尼亚州Homestead的28岁失业钢铁工人，尝试了一系列兼职低薪工作——为United Cerebral Palsy开面包车、在州立医院做保安、在当地的Sears商店做玻璃清洁演示员。在填写又一份工作申请表时，Craven拿自己的向下流动性开玩笑："我们在工厂里以前挣多少？我记不清了。我就写上：大钱。"

But the way to get lower interest rates is to do something about the budget deficits.”

Filling out yet another job application, Craven joked about his downward mobility: “What did we used to make in the mill? I can’t remember exactly. I’ll just put down: Big Bucks.”
不管你怎么说，所有人在找工作，却没有足够的工作，没有足够的好而稳定的工作。我们都必须相互竞争，失业人员为了一个越来越小的蛋糕彼此竞争。在这个经济中发生的事情是，高工资被压低了，每个人都被推向更低的工资，甚至是最低工资。是的，有些人重新开始工作了，但他们挣的是什么样的钱啊？
Carl Redwood, Jr., another young steelworker, had followed the same trail of disappointing jobs after he was laid off—security guard, “meter maid” issuing parking tickets, counselor at a day camp. The vast mills along the Monongahela River outside Pittsburgh had once employed twenty-eight thousand basic steelworkers at premium industrial wages; now there were only six thousand jobs left. Redwood described the big picture:
当经济复苏在其他地方蓬勃发展而工厂并未重新开工时，愤怒在Mon Valley变成了绝望，自杀的故事成了当地谈话的主要内容。似乎每个人都认识某个尝试过或成功自杀的人。县验尸官报告说，自杀率上升了11%，"原因是经济状况"。
No matter how you cut it, with all the people looking for jobs, there’s not enough jobs, in terms of good steady jobs. We all have to compete with each other, unemployed people against each other for a shrinking pie. What’s happened in this economy is that higher wages have been busted down, everyone pushed down to a lower wage, even a minimum wage. Yeah, there are people back to work, but what kind of money are they making?

As the economic recovery flourished elsewhere and the mills did not open, anger turned to despair in the Mon Valley and stories of suicide became a staple of local conversations. Everyone seemed to know someone who had tried it or succeeded. The county coroner reported that suicides were up 11 percent “due to economic conditions.”

After a while, I started blaming myself. The day I was committed, I went out on the bridge to jump. I figured this is it: why go on?” Friends “talked him down” and Leibowitz went to a state mental hospital for treatment.

One American’s loss, however, was another’s gain. While certain industries and certain workers suffered, every consumer benefited from the dollar-driven price competition. The import-export pressures were a major force in sustaining the low inflation rate that the Fed had established with the recession. It helped explain why the trade unions were still reeling, still giving up concessions even though the economy was expanding smartly again. It also explained why U.S. manufacturers did not dare raise their prices. Only businesses that did not have to compete with foreigners at home or abroad could safely do so.

The new situation, at first, confounded the economic assumptions of both liberal Keynesians and supply-side conservatives. Aggregate demand was increasing rapidly in the U.S. economy, the classic Keynesian formula for expanding output and employment. The import competition suppressed domestic prices and wages, and, Feldstein warned, if the Federal Reserve were to ease its control of money and bring down the dollar, its victory over inflation would be compromised. Licking inflation was one of the President’s proudest accomplishments, a benefit that seemed tangible to every American consumer, and no one in the White House wished to sacrifice it.
与住房建设一样，这些服务部门主要在国内市场竞争，在那里他们不需要面对外国竞争对手以及由不对称美元所产生的价格劣势。因此，这些企业可以通过提高自身价格（以及他们雇员的工资）来继续增加利润，而它们中的大多数也确实这样做了。在小麦、石油和汽车价格下跌的同时，服务价格指数继续稳步上涨——1983年上涨了超过5%。普遍通胀及其好处已经消失，但并非对所有人而言。
“The basic fact is that the value of the dollar can be changed only by modifying the goals for our domestic economy,” Feldstein advised. “A lower value of the dollar requires an expansion of the money supply that increases the rate of inflation.”29
就其自身的选民基础而言，共和党从强势美元战略中获益良多，风险很小。"白领"专业管理阶层——共和党选民的核心——将在此方案下享受收入的持续增长，从主街的零售商到华尔街的股票经纪人。"蓝领"工会成员将遭受痛苦，但他们无论如何都是压倒性的民主党选民。农民——最坚定的共和党人——可以指望他们无论怎样都会投票给共和党。
For the short run, it was an astute political strategy for the Reagan Administration to embrace. Despite the long-term economic damage that would be inflicted on America’s industrial base, the immediate benefits promised to please a large swath of the American electorate —an outcome sure to enhance Ronald Reagan’s re-election campaign in 1984. The President, whether he appreciated it or not, was pursuing a program designed to foster immediate gratification for a maximum number of electoral interests—a high-consumption, low-savings policy that would please both individual voters and economic interests. In another time, were Democrats in power, doing the same thing, Republicans would likely have denounced it as a cynical strategy of “buy now, pay later.” Ultimately, the nation would pay an enormous price for the self-indulgence arranged by the government.
另一个重要的产业部门是里根-沃尔克"强势美元"复苏战略的主要赢家——国防工业。军火生产是一个重要的特例，是一个不受强势美元影响的制造业部门，因为它不受国际竞争的威胁。美国政府根本不会从外国人那里购买很多武器。因此，国防公司及其配套的支持行业享有两个特殊优势：国防支出的巨额预算增长极大地扩展了他们的市场，同时，由于熟练产业工人的工资因就业不景气而普遍低迷，军火制造商受益于更便宜的劳动力。
But the political strategy, deliberate or otherwise, responded to new realities in the American economy. Manufacturing might still be thought of as the core of America’s economic strength, but it was not the dominant force it had once been, any more than farming. Manufacturing employment represented less than a quarter of the whole. Most Americans did not make things for a living. Most Americans sold intangible services, from school-teaching to computer programming, from trashmen to shopkeepers to lawyers and doctors. In relative terms, they would be winners.
克莱斯勒的Lee Iacocca徒劳地为其他制造业争取援助，试图辩称"国防"不仅仅是购买强大的军队。
The captains of heavy industry, the traditional locus of power within the Republican Party since the days of Lincoln, counted for less too. When the big corporate names from manufacturing lobbied the Republican White House, their failure to get results demonstrated how much their own political influence had declined. Manufacturers of steel, autos, textiles, machine tools and other basic goods were now outclassed in both economic girth and potential electoral strength. As an auto-company lobbyist explained:
事实是，没有强大的工业基础，我们就可以和国家安全吻别了 [Iacocca警告]。我们也可以和这个国家大多数高附加值的工作吻别了。是中产阶级首先使这个国家变得伟大。拿走美国每小时8到12美元的产业工作，你就削弱了这个国家。如果我们不小心，恐怕我们会发现自己武装到了牙齿——而剩下值得保卫的只有车道银行、电子游戏厅和麦当劳汉堡摊。
We still think of manufacturing as the “big mule” in politics, but it’s not true. The financial sector, the retailing sector, the service sector, are all so much bigger. The strong dollar was good for them and it was good for consumers. It kept prices down and guaranteed cheap goods, while the tax cuts and deficits meant there was plenty of money to spend. It was anti-manufacturing, anti-labor—but it was pro-consumption, proservices.
Iacocca令人感同身受的警告并未说服人。大多数美国人似乎过得不错，或者至少好多了。他们享受着恢复的繁荣，以稳定的价格再次购买东西，并不太关心这些东西是在哪里制造的。当然，里根政府的高级官员无意干扰复苏，既然它正如此强劲地向前推进。他们最大的担忧似乎是，美联储是否会独立地、出于自身原因决定把事情搞砸。
What was bad for Chrysler and independent oil drillers and Midwestern farmers was good for Sears, Roebuck and Toyota dealers, Citibank and McDonald’s and many others. An approximate sense of who the winners were was conveyed by a sample list of the domestic service sectors that benefited from cheaper prices on manufactured products and commodities, yet were themselves largely exempt from wage-price competition with foreign rivals. These included health services, banking and finance, insurance, real estate, retailing, hotels and restaurants, travel and transport, legal and technical professions, education, the media and communications.
到1983年11月下旬，再次听到了插话的声音。总统发言人Larry Speakes意味深长地向白宫记者团指出，美联储不应该让货币供应增长太慢。财政部长公开担心，鉴于近几个季度的强劲扩张，美联储可能正准备再次收紧信贷。
Like housing construction, these service sectors mainly competed in domestic markets, where they did not have to confront foreign competitors and the price disadvantage generated by the lopsided dollar. As a result, these enterprises could continue to increase profits by raising their own prices (and their employees’ wages too) and most of them did. While wheat and oil and auto prices sagged, the price index for services continued to advance smartly—up by more than 5 percent in 1983. General inflation and its benefits were gone, but not for everyone.
"我知道他们必须采取限制措施，"Donald Regan说。"我担心的是他们做得过火。"
In terms of its own constituencies, the Republican Party had much to gain from the strong-dollar strategy and little at risk. The “white collar” professional-managerial ranks, the core of Republican voters, would enjoy a continuing rise in their incomes under this scheme, from Main Street retailers to Wall Street stockbrokers. “Blue collar” union members would suffer, but they were overwhelmingly Democratic voters anyway. Farmers, the staunchest of Republicans, could be counted on to vote Republican, regardless.
Milton Friedman走得更远。他说，美联储已经做过头了。从7月到10月，M-1的增长几乎持平，因此Friedman预测，将会出现一个选举年的经济衰退。白宫政治顾问们不确定他们是否应该依赖这位货币主义者的分析，但Friedman的大胆预测肯定让他们更加紧张。
One other important industrial sector was a major winner under the Reagan-Volcker “strong dollar” recovery strategy—the defense industry. The production of armaments was an important special case, a manufacturing sector that did not suffer from the strong dollar because it was shielded from international competition. The U.S. government simply did not buy very many of its weapons from foreigners. The defense companies and their allied support industries, thus, enjoyed two special advantages: the massive budget increases for defense spending expanded their market enormously and, meanwhile, arms manufacturers benefited from cheaper labor, as wages for skilled industrial workers were depressed generally by the slack employment.
在国会，Jack Kemp众议员和另外四十九名成员致信总统表示抗议，指责Paul Volcker确实在与里根复苏"玩胆小鬼游戏"，并暗示地在与总统的连任玩同样的游戏。
Lee Iacocca of Chrysler, futilely campaigning for assistance to other manufacturing industries, tried to argue that “national defense” involved more than simply buying a strong military.
"货币政策被故意保持在不必要地紧缩的状态，经济扩张被挟持以推动增税，"Kemp和其他共和党人抱怨道。"Volcker先生提出了一种交换：以货币宽松和降低利率来换取他更喜欢的更高税收的财政政策。"
The fact is, without a strong industrial base, we can kiss our national security goodbye [Iacocca warned]. We can also kiss goodbye to the majority of this country’s high value-added jobs. It was the middle class that made this country great in the first place. Take away America’s $8-to-$12-an-hour industrial jobs and you undercut the country. If we don’t watch out, I’m afraid we’re going to find ourselves armed to the teeth—and with nothing left to defend but drive-in banks, video arcades and McDonald’s hamburger stands.
Volcker当然否认了这一点，但美联储主席一直在国会山游说，敦促立法者对赤字采取重大行动。他警告他们，如果赤字不尽快减少，利率可能会进一步上升。10月26日，Volcker与参议院财政委员会的成员私下会面，敦促他们考虑各种方式大幅增税。政治专栏作家Rowland Evans和Robert Novak报道说："财政委员会的成员离开会议时得到了这样的信息：如果他们想要Volcker的美联储放松货币，他们最好赶紧行动，通过增税。"
Iacocca’s evocative warnings did not persuade. Most Americans seemed to be doing fine or at least doing a lot better. They were enjoying the restored prosperity and buying things again at stable prices, not much concerned about where the things were made. Certainly, the high officials of the Reagan Administration did not intend to tamper with the recovery, now that it was rolling forward so energetically. Their largest worry, it seemed, was whether the Federal Reserve might decide—independently, for its own reasons—to spoil things.
不久之后，财政委员会主席、堪萨斯州的Robert Dole参议员确实提出了这样一项措施，一个将筹集550亿美元的增税方案。里根总统通过重复他此前多次说过的话来否决了这个想法——他会否决任何送到他办公桌上的增税法案。财政政策和货币政策之间的"胆小鬼游戏"仍在继续，到目前为止，双方都没有让步。
By late November 1983, kibitzing voices were heard again. The President’s spokesman, Larry Speakes, pointedly observed to the White House press that the Federal Reserve ought not to let the money supply grow too slowly. The Treasury Secretary worried aloud that the Fed might be getting ready to tighten credit again, given the robust expansion of recent quarters.
然而，里根政府误解了美联储的困境。批评者看着M-1，抱怨货币政策太紧。在美联储，政策制定者看着实际经济，担心他们太宽松了。按照自己的标准，美联储不是吝啬，而是过于慷慨。
“I know they have to be restrictive,” Donald Regan said. “My concern is that they not overdo it.”
一开始，Volcker发誓要抑制复苏，防止它发展成失控的繁荣。但他落后了。1983年初，联邦公开市场委员会同意，从1982年第四季度到1983年第四季度，实际经济增长应控制在适度的3.5%到4.5%。然而，这一年的实际增长率达到了6.3%——远比Volcker打算的更为强劲的扩张。
Milton Friedman went further. The Fed had already overdone it, he said. The growth in M-l had been nearly flat from July to October and, therefore, Friedman predicted, there would be an election-year recession. White House political advisers were not sure they should rely on the monetarist’s analysis, but Friedman’s bold forecast certainly made them more nervous.
尽管政府频繁抱怨，美联储似乎正在提供白宫所要求的东西——一种宽松的货币政策。华盛顿周围的 cynic 民主党人开始怀疑，双方之间是否确实存在一个"协议"：Paul Volcker被重新任命，经济繁荣，Ronald Reagan的支持率恢复，正好赶上1984年。
In Congress, Representative Jack Kemp and forty-nine other members sent a letter of protest to the President, charging that Paul Volcker was indeed “playing chicken” with the Reagan recovery and, by implication, with the President’s re-election.
政治背景比这些阴谋论所暗示的要微妙得多。美联储主席无疑受到周围政治预期的制约。他带领国家（和里根政府）经历了一场漫长而痛苦的衰退。既然国家正在享受复苏，Volcker打算加以约束——但他敢于走多远是有限度的。
“Monetary policy is deliberately being kept unnecessarily tight and the economic expansion held hostage to a tax increase,” Kemp and the other Republicans complained. “Mr. Volcker would offer a quid pro quo of monetary ease and lower interest rates in return for a fiscal policy of higher taxes which is more to his liking.”30
"Volcker不可能实施更紧缩的货币政策并全身而退，"预算主任David Stockman评论道。"政府会全力攻击他。他们本来就做了很多抨击美联储的事情。"
Volcker denied this, of course, but the Federal Reserve chairman had been lobbying on Capitol Hill, urging the legislators to take significant action on the deficits. He warned them that if the deficits were not promptly reduced, interest rates might go even higher. On October 26, Volcker met privately with members of the Senate Finance Committee and urged them to consider various approaches to a major tax increase. Political columnists Rowland Evans and Robert Novak reported: “Finance members left the meeting with this message: if they wanted Volcker’s Fed to ease money, they had better get cracking with tax increases.”31
无论白宫对美联储的抨击对Volcker产生了何种影响，他显然低估了复苏的强度——以及经济顶着高实际利率向前冲刺的能力。事实上，Volcker的货币政策似乎构成了一个反常现象：如果看M-1或利率，货币政策看起来非常紧缩，异常紧缩。但如果看经济的实际增长，货币政策看起来是宽松的。
Shortly afterward, the Finance chairman, Senator Robert Dole of Kansas, did introduce such a measure, a package of revenue increases that would raise $55 billion. President Reagan scotched the idea by repeating what he had said many times before—he would veto any tax-increase legislation that landed on his desk. The “game of chicken” continued between fiscal policy and monetary policy and, so far, neither side had blinked.
对于美联储官员来说，解释是清楚的——货币的含义再次发生变化（又一次将Milton Friedman引向错误）。在经历了近两年的异常下降之后，货币流通速度开始再次加速——回到其正常趋势线。这意味着美联储此前注入经济的大量货币供应现在将转化为更大的经济活动——因为M-1在不同交易中转手更快。美联储认为，也许是时候采取纠正措施了。
The Reagan Administration misunderstood the Fed’s predicament, however. The critics looked at M-l and complained that monetary policy was too tight. At the Federal Reserve, the policy makers looked at the real economy and worried that they had been too loose. By its own standard, the Fed was not being stingy, but too generous.
当联邦公开市场委员会于12月19日开会时，一些成员担心，鉴于其势头和刺激性的财政政策，1984年的经济将再次超出他们的预期。"特别是，有人提出，目前企业家的高度信心和企业的大量现金流有利于企业固定投资的相对快速扩张，"FOMC会议纪要记载。
At the outset, Volcker had vowed to restrain the recovery, to keep it from reaching a runaway boom. But he was behind. At the beginning of 1983, the Federal Open Market Committee had agreed that real economic growth should be held to a modest 3.5 to 4.5 percent, from the last quarter of 1982 to the last quarter of 1983. Instead, the real growth for the year was coming in at 6.3 percent—a much more robust expansion than Volcker had intended.
然而，其他人则不那么乐观。他们担心，现在提高利率可能会迅速抑制住房等利率敏感部门，并使美元在外汇市场上进一步走高，进一步损害制造业。
Despite the Administration’s frequent carping, the Federal Reserve appeared to be providing exactly what the White House had asked for —an accommodative money policy. Cynical Democrats around Washington began to wonder if there had, in fact, been a “deal” between the two. Paul Volcker is reappointed. The economy booms. And Ronald Reagan’s popularity is restored, just in time for 1984.
一位成员Preston Martin发出了更强有力的警告。Martin承认，目前经济看起来强劲，但商业活动和消费者支出很快就会放缓。FOMC应该考虑放松利率，而不是提高利率。
The political context was more subtle than these conspiracy theories suggested. The Federal Reserve chairman was, no doubt, inhibited by the surrounding political expectations. He had taken the country (and the Reagan Administration) through a long and punishing recession. Now that the nation was enjoying its recovery, Volcker intended to restrain—but there were limits on how far he dared go.
"对我来说，"Martin说，"通胀的压力不存在。来自工资和薪水的成本推动在任何地方都很难找到。在我看来，现在可能性在另一边——经济将会放缓，可能在利率方面需要一点帮助。"
“There was no way Volcker could have managed a tighter monetary policy and gotten away with it,” budget director David Stockman observed. “The Administration would have been all over him. They did a lot of Fed bashing as it was.”
Martin在美联储的年长同事们对这位副主席的话不以为然。他们知道Preston Martin与里根白宫中的加州人关系密切，是供给侧支持者的宠儿。他们假设Martin的经济分析深受政府确保在总统连任竞选期间经济繁荣的自然愿望的影响。当Martin回顾储蓄和贷款行业的疲软并描述潜在风险时，委员会的其他成员礼貌地听着，但他们并不买账。
Whatever influence the White House “Fed bashing” may have had on Volcker, he clearly had underestimated the strength of the recovery —and the economy’s ability to charge forward, despite the high real interest rates. Volcker’s monetary policy, in fact, seemed to pose an anomaly: if one looked at the M-l or interest rates, the money policy looked very tight, extraordinarily tight. But if one looked at the real growth of the economy, the monetary policy looked easy.
因为委员会成员自己对经济前景存在分歧，他们选择了一条谨慎的中庸之道。12月的指令要求"维持至少现有的限制程度"。但它允许"视情况发展略微增加此类限制的可能性……"
The explanation was clear to Fed officials—money’s meaning was changing again (and once again leading Milton Friedman into error). After nearly two years of abnormal decline, money’s velocity was beginning to accelerate again—returning to its normal trend line. That meant the swollen money supply that the Fed had already pumped into the economy earlier would now translate into greater economic activity—as M-l turned over faster in different transactions. Maybe, the Fed thought, it was time to take corrective action.
即使这个温和的步骤也让Preston Martin感到不安，他投下了作为美联储理事的第一次反对票。任何货币紧缩、任何利率上升，Martin警告说，"都将对经济扩张的可持续性构成威胁。必要的商业投资将变得更加昂贵，国际债务偿还更加沉重，对利率敏感的住房更加脆弱。"
When the Federal Open Market Committee met December 19, some members worried that the economy in 1984 was going to outrun their expectations again, given its momentum and the stimulative fiscal policy. “In particular, it was suggested that the currently high level of confidence among businessmen and large cash flows to business firms favored relatively rapid expansion in business fixed investment,” the FOMC minutes reported.
多数人则担心相反的情况。里根复苏才刚满一年，已经超出预期地蓬勃发展。美联储理事们认为他们正在接近困难的部分——他们必须决定多少繁荣才算太多的时候了。
Others were less sanguine, however. They worried that an increase in interest rates now could quickly depress interest-sensitive sectors like housing and send the dollar still higher in foreign-exchange markets, further damaging manufacturing.
One member, Preston Martin, sounded a stronger warning. The economy looked robust at the moment, Martin acknowledged, but business activity and consumer spending would soon be moderating. The FOMC should be thinking about relaxing interest rates, not raising them.
“To me,” Martin said, “the pressures of inflation weren’t there. The cost push from wages and salaries was very difficult to find anywhere. It seemed to me the probability was now on the other side—that the economy would be slowing down and it might need a little help on interest rates.”
Martin’s senior colleagues at the Fed discounted the vice chairman. They knew Preston Martin was close to the Californians in the Reagan White House and a favorite of the supply siders. They assumed Martin’s economic analysis was heavily influenced by the Administration’s natural desire to insure a roaring economy for the President’s reelection campaign. The other committee members listened politely as Martin reviewed the weakness in the savings and loan industry and described the potential risks, but they were not impressed.
Because the committee members were themselves divided on the economic outlook, they chose a cautious middle course. The December directive called for “maintaining at least the existing degree of restraint.” But it allowed for “the possibility of a slight increase in such restraint, depending on developments. ...”
Even this modest step bothered Preston Martin, who cast his first dissent as a Federal Reserve governor. Any tightening of money, any increase in interest rates, Martin warned, “would present a threat to the sustainability of the economic expansion. Needed business investment would be more expensive, international debt servicing more burdensome, and interest-sensitive housing more vulnerable.”32
The majority was worried about the opposite. The Reagan recovery was barely one year old and booming beyond expectations. The Federal Reserve governors figured they were approaching the hard part— the moment when they would have to decide how much prosperity is too much.
