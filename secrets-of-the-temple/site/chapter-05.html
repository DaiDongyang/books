CHAPTER 5THE LIBERAL APOLOGY

PART ONE: THE MONEY QUESTION
Wh en G. William Miller became chairman in 1978, he decided to break the deadlock on a long-festering problem that confronted the Federal Reserve System. Its membership was shrinking. Each year, a few dozen or more commercial banks would decide that the benefits of Fed membership were outweighed by the cost and they would withdraw. The total had fallen from six thousand to about fifty-six hundred and everyone understood why. The Fed paid no interest on the reserve balances that commercial banks were required to maintain at the twelve Federal Reserve Banks. If bankers withdrew and placed themselves under state banking regulation, the required reserve levels would be lower and, more important, the money would be kept at a correspondent bank, where it could be invested in income-producing securities.
当 G. William Miller 于 1978 年成为主席时，他决心打破困扰联邦储备体系的一个长期悬而未决的问题。其成员数量正在萎缩。每年，都有几十家或更多的商业银行认为美联储成员资格的好处抵不上成本，从而选择退出。总数已从六千家下降到约五千六百家，人人都明白原因所在。美联储不对商业银行被要求存放在十二家联邦储备银行的准备金余额支付利息。如果银行家们退出并接受州银行业监管，所需的准备金水平会更低，更重要的是，资金可以存放在代理行，在那里可以投资于产生收入的证券。
The Fed’s refusal to pay interest on reserves “constitutes a discriminatory tax,” the Federal Advisory Council complained, expressing a grievance long felt by the member banks. The “tax” on Fed banks was actually only lost opportunity—the interest income they did not earn on the idle reserve funds—but the burden of this “opportunity cost” naturally increased during the 1970s as inflation pushed up interest rates. The bankers’ distress increased proportionately.
美联储拒绝为准备金支付利息构成了"歧视性税收"，联邦咨询委员会抱怨道，表达了成员银行长期以来的不满。对美联储银行的"税收"实际上只是机会损失——他们未能在闲置的准备金资金上赚取的利息收入——但这种"机会成本"的负担在 1970 年代随着通胀推高利率而自然增加。银行家的痛苦也随之成比例增加。
Since Congress had failed to deal with the issue, Chairman Miller decided to take unilateral action. He informed the chairmen of the House and Senate banking committees that the Federal Reserve would henceforth begin paying interest to its member banks on their reserve balances. The response from Capitol Hill was swift and strong. Representative Reuss called the Fed’s congressional liaison with a message for the chairman: if Miller dared to do this, Reuss would introduce a resolution to impeach him.
由于国会未能处理这个问题，Miller 主席决定采取单方面行动。他通知众议院和参议院银行业委员会主席，联邦储备此后将开始对其成员银行的准备金余额支付利息。来自国会的回应迅速而强烈。众议员 Reuss 致电美联储的国会联络人，让其给主席带话：如果 Miller 敢这样做，Reuss 将提出决议弹劾他。
The bankers’ complaints about the “hidden tax” imposed on them by Federal Reserve membership left out several offsetting benefits they enjoyed. Thanks to Fed membership, these commercial banks enjoyed a safety net not available to any other business sector—the emergency borrowing available through the Fed’s Discount window. If they got into a corner, a temporary liquidity shortage or more serious problems, relief was only a phone call away. That protection from failure, though impossible to quantify, was worth quite a lot. The member banks also received free services from the Fed in their use of the check-clearing and payments system. And, from the congressional point of view, it took real chutzpa for banks to complain about “taxes” since they paid so little compared to other business sectors. In 1980, the effective federal income-tax rate on financial institutions was only 5.8 percent, compared with 34.1 percent for retailing, 24.5 percent for electronics and appliance manufacturing, 16.4 percent for aerospace, 10.9 percent for utilities. The only business sectors with lower effective tax rates than banks were two that were in serious trouble, airlines and wood products.
银行家们关于美联储成员资格强加给他们的"隐性税收"的抱怨，忽略了他们所享有的若干抵消性利益。得益于美联储成员资格，这些商业银行享有一个任何其他商业部门都无法获得的安全网——通过美联储贴现窗口获得的紧急借款。如果他们陷入困境——暂时的流动性短缺或更严重的问题——救助只需一个电话。这种免于失败的保护虽然无法量化，但价值相当可观。成员银行在使用美联储的支票清算和支付系统时还获得了免费服务。而且，从国会的角度来看，银行家们抱怨"税收"实在需要极大的勇气，因为他们与其它商业部门相比缴纳的税款如此之少。1980 年，金融机构的有效联邦所得税率仅为 5.8%，相比之下，零售业为 34.1%，电子和家电制造业为 24.5%，航空航天业为 16.4%，公用事业为 10.9%。有效税率低于银行的商业部门只有两个陷入严重困境的行业：航空公司和木制品行业。
Nevertheless, Miller won his point. Since 1913, membership had been voluntary, and now commercial banks were leaving the Fed at an accelerating rate. As interest rates rose, many more banks threatened to follow. If this continued, the Fed argued, its control over the money supply would be weakened. Miller acceded to the congressional objections and agreed not to pay interest on reserves, but Reuss and Proxmire, the two banking chairmen, committed themselves to finding a legislative solution.
尽管如此，Miller 还是赢得了他的论点。自 1913 年以来，成员资格一直是自愿的，而现在商业银行正以加速的速率离开美联储。随着利率上升，更多银行威胁要效仿。美联储辩称，如果这种情况继续下去，其对货币供应的控制将被削弱。Miller 接受了国会的反对意见，同意不支付准备金利息，但 Reuss 和 Proxmire 这两位银行业委员会主席承诺寻求立法解决方案。
The ultimate result was the Monetary Control Act of 1980, enacted just as the Fed was driving interest rates to historic peaks. The legislation required all depository institutions, member banks and nonmember banks alike, savings and loan associations, even credit unions, to maintain reserves with the Fed. The imposition of universal reserve requirements was presented to Congress as an arcane question of monetary management, the sort of issue few members of Congress cared much about or understood. In reality, the Fed membership issue was a political question, only marginally relevant to economics. Passage of the Monetary Control Act had very little to do with how effectively the Federal Reserve could control the supply of money. Its purpose was to protect the Federal Reserve’s political base.
最终结果是 1980 年的《货币控制法》，该法案恰好在美联储将利率推至历史高峰时颁布。该立法要求所有存款机构——成员银行和非成员银行、储蓄贷款协会、甚至信用合作社——都必须向美联储保持准备金。强制性统一准备金要求被呈递给国会，作为货币管理中一个晦涩难懂的问题，一种很少有国会议员关心或理解的问题。实际上，美联储成员资格问题是一个政治问题，与经济学的关联甚微。《货币控制法》的通过与联邦储备能多有效地控制货币供应几乎没有关系。其目的是保护联邦储备的政治基础。
The central bank, supposedly above politics, maneuvered as adroitly as any private interest group to secure this prize—a historic consolidation of its institutional power. It mobilized the lobbying support of its own infrastructure, the influential network of bankers and businessmen associated with the twelve Federal Reserve Banks. It reluctantly endorsed lucrative concessions to its own favored constituency, the largest money-center banks. It deftly pressured other financial interests to fall in line and accept the terms. As a government agency manipulating Congress, the Federal Reserve was as formidable as the Pentagon.
这家理应超然于政治的中央银行，像任何私人利益集团一样巧妙地操作，以争取这一战利品——其机构权力的历史性巩固。它动员了自身基础设施的游说支持，即与十二家联邦储备银行相关联的有影响力的银行家和商人网络。它不情愿地批准了对自身偏爱的支持群体——最大的货币中心银行——的有利可图的特许权。它灵巧地施压其他金融利益集团顺从并接受条件。作为一个操纵国会的政府机构，联邦储备与五角大楼一样令人生畏。
Still, it was extraordinary that the Fed could realize this long-sought goal at the very time it was imposing such drastic conditions on the private economy. Its legislative victory was possible because the Federal Reserve’s agenda was riding piggyback on a much larger cause whose time had finally arrived—the deregulation of finance. In companion legislation, Congress repealed virtually all of the remaining government limits on interest rates, political regulation of lending that had existed since the New Deal. The price of money was free at last —free to seek whatever level the marketplace dictated.
尽管如此，美联储能够在如此严峻的条件强加于私人经济之时实现这一长期追求的目标，确实非同寻常。其立法胜利之所以可能，是因为联邦储备的议程是搭上了一个终于到来的更大事业的便车——金融去监管。在配套立法中，国会废除了几乎所有自新政以来存在的政府对利率的剩余限制及对借贷的政治管制。货币的价格终于自由了——自由地去寻求市场所决定的任何水平。
The prime rate was already above 15 percent in the opening weeks of 1980 when the deregulation legislation reached final passage. The Democratic-controlled Congress went ahead, nevertheless, and voted overwhelmingly for the package, which, in other times, they would have denounced as Republican and retrograde. The social implications were obvious: borrowers, businesses and consumers would pay higher interest rates and creditors would enjoy higher returns on their wealth.
1980 年初，当去监管立法进入最终通过阶段时，优惠利率已经超过 15%。尽管如此，民主党控制的国会仍然以压倒性多数投票通过了这一揽子方案，而这些措施在其他时期他们本会谴责为共和党的倒退。其社会影响显而易见：借款人、企业和消费者将支付更高的利率，而债权人将享受其财富更高的回报。
Banks would henceforth be permitted to pay interest on checking deposits—with the so-called NOW accounts—in order to lure deposit money back from the money-market funds. The Fed’s Regulation Q, which for several decades had imposed arbitrary limits on how much interest banks and savings and loans could pay on savings deposits, was to be phased out. The S & L’s, given an interest-rate edge over commercial banks in order to encourage mortgage lending and broader homeownership, were stripped of their advantage—forced to compete on “a level playing field,” as the bankers liked to put it. State usury laws—which prohibited interest rates from rising above acceptable levels—were unilaterally suspended by act of Congress.
银行此后将被允许对支票存款支付利息——通过所谓的 NOW 账户——以便将存款资金从货币市场基金中吸引回来。美联储的 Q 条例，几十年来对数年来一直对银行和储蓄贷款协会可支付的储蓄存款利率施加任意限制，将被逐步取消。为了鼓励抵押贷款和更广泛的住房拥有而给予储蓄贷款协会相对于商业银行的利率优势，也被剥夺了——被迫在"公平的竞争环境"中竞争，正如银行家们喜欢说的那样。禁止利率上升到可接受水平以上的州高利贷法，被国会法案单方面暂停。
If the timing seemed odd, the politics was obvious to even the densest member of Congress. Financial deregulation had been gestating for more than a decade and passage was now assured by the convergence of powerful interests. Inflation had sufficiently damaged the holders of financial wealth, including “small savers” of modest means, that even liberal Democrats felt compelled to offer them relief. Commercial banks, led by the largest institutions, argued for the principle of free-market competition. Those who would be hurt—labor, small businesses, many S & L’s and smaller banks—protested feebly, but their voices were lost in the rush to consensus.1
如果时机看起来有些奇怪，其政治逻辑即使对最迟钝的国会议员也是显而易见的。金融去监管已经酝酿了十多年，而强大利益的汇聚确保了其通过。通胀已经严重损害了金融财富的持有者，包括财力有限的"小额储蓄者"，以至于即使是自由派民主党人也感到不得不为他们提供救济。以最大型机构为首的商业银行主张自由市场竞争的原则。那些将受到伤害的人——劳工、小企业、许多储蓄贷款协会和较小银行——软弱地抗议，但他们的声音在奔向共识的浪潮中消失了。
And the Federal Reserve joined in the lobbying with its own political objective—achieving the universal reach over banks that it had been denied since 1913. Volcker and other Fed officials actually had fundamental doubts about the wisdom of financial deregulation, knowing that the elimination of Washington’s interest-rate ceilings would probably weaken the Fed’s own control over the expansion of credit. In the heat of the legislative struggle, Volcker and others at the Fed kept these worries to themselves. For them, financial deregulation offered a rare opening and they seized it.
而联邦储备带着自己的政治目标加入了游说——实现自 1913 年以来一直被剥夺的对银行的普遍覆盖。Volcker 和其他美联储官员实际上对金融去监管的明智性抱有根本性的疑虑，知道取消华盛顿的利率上限可能会削弱美联储自身对信贷扩张的控制。在立法斗争的白热化阶段，Volcker 和美联储的其他人员将这些担忧藏在了心里。对他们来说，金融去监管提供了一个难得的契机，他们抓住了它。
Paul Volcker, like his predecessors, Miller and Arthur Burns, took up the cause of universal reserve requirements and repeatedly warned Congress that shrinking membership was atrophying the Fed’s “fulcrum for the conduct of monetary policy”—the base of bank reserves that it manipulated to control the money supply. “As attrition causes the total amount of reserves held at Federal Reserve Banks to decline,” Volcker warned, “the ‘multiplier’ relationship between reserves and money increases tends to become less stable. Consequently, fluctuations in the amount of reserves supplied—and these fluctuations inevitably have a range of uncertainty—can cause magnified and unintended changes in the money supply.”
Paul Volcker 和他的前任 Miller 及 Arthur Burns 一样，投身于普遍准备金要求的事业，并反复警告国会，成员数量的萎缩正在削弱美联储"执行货币政策的支点"——它用来控制货币供应的银行准备金基础。"随着成员流失导致在联邦储备银行持有的准备金总额下降，"Volcker 警告道，"准备金与货币增长之间的'乘数'关系变得不稳定。因此，准备金供应量的波动——而这些波动不可避免地存在不确定性范围——可能导致货币供应量被放大和意外的变化。"
By early 1980, Volcker portrayed the danger as imminent. In recent months, he testified in February, 69 banks with $7 billion in deposits had left the System. Another 320 banks were “considered certain or probable to withdraw,” he said, and 350 more were “actively considering withdrawal.” If all of those did indeed leave the Fed, the deposits covered by the Federal Reserve’s management would decline from 70 percent of the entire banking system to 64 percent. Eventually, the Fed claimed, its membership coverage would fall as low as 57 percent of U.S. deposits. Congress must act.2
到 1980 年初，Volcker 将危险描述为迫在眉睫。他在二月份作证说，近几个月来，拥有 70 亿美元存款的 69 家银行离开了体系。另有 320 家银行"被认为确定或可能退出"，他说，还有 350 家正在"积极考虑退出"。如果所有这些银行确实离开了美联储，由联邦储备管理所覆盖的存款将从整个银行体系的 70% 下降到 64%。最终，美联储声称，其成员覆盖率将低至美国存款的 57%。国会必须采取行动。
It sounded ominous to those who did not understand the complex hydraulics of the money supply. But to monetary economists, Volck-er’s claims were arguable at best and, to many of them, quite spurious. Logically, they pointed out, it did not matter whether the Fed’s “fulcrum” covered 70 percent of the banking system or 50 percent or even much less. Since the Fed was the only source that could originate money creation, its actions to inject or withdraw reserves would be transmitted automatically through every channel of the banking system, affecting credit conditions and interest rates for everyone, regardless of where banks kept their reserves.
对于那些不了解货币供应复杂的液压机制的人来说，这听起来不祥。但对于货币经济学家来说，Volcker 的主张充其量是有争议的，而对其中许多人来说，完全是站不住脚的。逻辑上，他们指出，美联储的"支点"覆盖银行体系的 70%、50% 甚至更少都没有关系。由于美联储是唯一能够启动货币创造的来源，其注入或提取准备金的行为将自动通过银行体系的每一个渠道传导，影响每个人的信贷条件和利率，无论银行的准备金存放在何处。
Everyone agreed, in any case, that the largest money-center banks would never give up their Fed membership, and they were the core of the banking system. The fifty or so largest banks held half of all U.S. deposits and were intimately linked to all others as their correspondent banks, the place where non-Fed banks held their reserves, where smaller banks parked their surplus funds or turned for help. The money-center banks needed the Fed membership both for the Fed’s payments system and because they, most of all, depended on the Fed’s Discount lending to manage their own liquidity. As long as these core banks were under the Fed’s supervision, it would have a reliable handle on the entire banking system.
无论如何，所有人都同意，最大的货币中心银行永远不会放弃其美联储成员资格，而它们是银行体系的核心。大约五十家最大的银行持有美国所有存款的一半，并且作为代理行与其他所有银行紧密相连——非美联储银行在那里持有准备金，较小的银行在那里存放盈余资金或寻求帮助。货币中心银行需要美联储成员资格，既是为了美联储的支付系统，也因为它们最依赖美联储的贴现贷款来管理自身的流动性。只要这些核心银行在美联储的监督之下，它就拥有对整个银行体系的可靠掌控。
Governor Henry Wallich, the most scholarly monetary theorist on the Board of Governors, was among the many economists who thought the Fed membership issue was a nonexistent problem, at least as a threat to monetary control.
州长 Henry Wallich，联邦储备委员会中最具学术性的货币理论家，是许多认为美联储成员资格问题根本不存在的经济学家之一——至少作为对货币控制的威胁而言是这样。
“I’ve always thought this was a red herring,” Wallich confessed. “Ultimately, I think we can run the economy without any reserve requirements. Some other central banks do it. When I was a director of a bank in New Haven [while teaching at Yale], I said to the managers: ‘Look, it’s profitable for us to get out of the Federal Reserve System.’ And they later did that.”
"我一直认为这是一个转移注意力的幌子，"Wallich 坦言。"最终，我认为我们可以在没有任何准备金要求的情况下运行经济。其他一些中央银行就这样做。当我在 New Haven 担任一家银行的董事时（在耶鲁教书期间），我对经理们说：'你看，退出联邦储备体系对我们来说是有利可图的。'后来他们确实这样做了。"
Even with the October 1979 shift in operating methods, Wallich argued, the Fed could still exercise adequate control over reserves with a much smaller number of member banks. During the congressional debate, Wallich did not broadcast his views. Instead, he deferred to the political position staked out by the Federal Reserve and its chairman. “It’s easier for me to say this as a theorist,” he explained, “than it is for Paul Volcker, who has to go before the congressional committees and talk to the banks and bear the brunt of the complaining.”
Wallich 认为，即使是在 1979 年 10 月操作方法转变之后，美联储仍然可以通过少得多的成员银行对准备金实施足够的控制。在国会辩论期间，Wallich 没有宣扬他的观点。相反，他遵从了联邦储备及其主席所设定的政治立场。"作为一个理论家，我这样说比 Paul Volcker 要容易得多，"他解释道，"他必须去国会委员会作证，与银行家对话，并承受抱怨的冲击。"
A senior executive of the New York Federal Reserve Bank shared Wallich’s skepticism about the threat of shrinking membership, as did many others within the System. “It was an article of faith in the System that it did matter,” the New York official said, “but a lot of people think it doesn’t. I’ve never been convinced it does myself. Logically, we could operate just as effectively if only a small number of banks had accounts with us or maybe even if only brokers had accounts with us.”
纽约联邦储备银行的一位高级执行官与 Wallich 一样对成员缩小的威胁持怀疑态度，体系内的许多其他人也是如此。"体系内的一种信条是这确实重要，"这位纽约官员说，"但很多人认为它不重要。我自己也从未信服过。逻辑上，即使只有少数银行在我们这里开设账户，或者甚至只有经纪商在我们这里开设账户，我们也可以同样有效地运作。"
The commercial banks, however, were also the core of the Federal Reserve’s political base, the clientele whom it served and who, in turn, defended the Fed’s independence. As more banks, large and small, resigned from the System, the System lost the supporters who helped to protect it from political attack. “There wasn’t any question,” the New York Fed official allowed, “that the membership provided us with a constituency we could ill afford to lose.”
然而，商业银行也是联邦储备政治基础的核心，是它所服务并反过来捍卫美联储独立的客户群体。随着越来越多的大小银行退出体系，体系失去了那些帮助保护其免受政治攻击的支持者。"毫无疑问，"这位纽约联储官员承认，"成员资格为我们提供了一个我们承担不起失去的支持群体。"
“It would destroy the Fed’s political base,” explained Kenneth A. Guenther, who was the Federal Reserve Board’s congressional liaison throughout the episode. Guenther compared the Fed’s political constituency of large and small banks to a naval deployment—“four hundred of the largest battleships surrounded by thousands of little PT boats.” The battleships were the essential element for monetary control, but the PT boats provided crucial protection. They were the smaller banks, scattered across the nation in small towns and cities where the bankers were typically much closer to hometown political leaders, including the local congressman, than a mega-banker like Citibank’s Walter Wriston or Chase Manhattan’s David Rockefeller would be.
"这会摧毁美联储的政治基础，"Kenneth A. Guenther 解释说，他在这整个事件期间担任联邦储备委员会的国会联络人。Guenther 将美联储由大小银行组成的政治支持群体比作海军部署——"四百艘最大的战舰，周围环绕着数千艘小型 PT 艇。"战舰是对货币控制至关重要的要素，但 PT 艇提供了关键的保护。它们是较小的银行，散布在全国各地的小城镇和城市中，在那里，银行家通常比 Citibank 的 Walter Wriston 或 Chase Manhattan 的 David Rockefeller 这类大型银行家更接近家乡的政治领袖，包括当地的国会议员。
Without the picket ships to protect them, the Fed and the money-center banks would be more vulnerable to political whim and the residual popular hostility directed at concentrated power (the political profile of banking resembled the oil industry: thousands of independent producers surrounded the major oil companies and were often more influential, whereas the “majors” like Exxon or Mobil were convenient symbols of corporate power and greed that political critics invoked when attacking the industry). “If you have four hundred of the largest battleships surrounded by thousands of PT boats,” Guenther observed, “then someone is really risking political trouble to tamper with them.”
如果没有哨兵艇的保护，美联储和货币中心银行将更容易受到政治任性和针对集中权力的残余民众敌意的攻击（银行业的政治概况类似于石油工业：成千上万的独立生产商围绕着主要石油公司，而且往往更有影响力，而像 Exxon 或 Mobil 这样的"巨头"则是政治批评者在攻击该行业时援引的企业权力和贪婪的便利象征）。"如果你有四百艘最大的战舰，周围环绕着数千艘 PT 艇，"Guenther 指出，"那么有人真的冒着政治风险去干预它们。"
The Federal Reserve’s self-interest in maintaining this broad base of support was obvious. But, in more subtle terms, liberal Democrats also saw an interest in preserving it. One of the damning complaints against the Federal Reserve, from the beginning, in 1913, was that it became the captive of the largest banks in Wall Street and the other financial centers. The Fed was too subservient to their demands, critics said, and too indifferent to the concerns of smaller institutions or competing interests in the American economy. Yet the Fed’s institutional bias toward bigness would likely become stronger if its constituency was narrowed to only the biggest and most important banks. Democratic cosponsors, in particular, were worried by this prospect.
联邦储备在维持这种广泛支持基础中的自身利益是显而易见的。但更微妙的是，自由派民主党人也看到了维护它的利益。从一开始，1913 年，对联邦储备的谴责性指控之一就是它成为了 Wall Street 和其他金融中心最大银行的俘虏。批评者说，美联储太屈从于大银行的要求，而对较小机构或美国经济中竞争性利益的关注过于漠不关心。然而，如果美联储的支持群体仅缩小到最大和最重要的银行，其机构层面对大型机构的偏袒可能会变得更强。民主党共同提案人尤其担心这种前景。
“I don’t think anybody in the Federal Reserve System felt you needed more than the four hundred largest banks to operate,” Guenther said, “but the Democrats were totally opposed to that. It would make the Federal Reserve the creature of the four hundred largest banks and destroy its democratic base.”3
"我认为联邦储备体系中没有人觉得运作需要超过四百家最大银行，"Guenther 说，"但民主党人完全反对这一点。那会使联邦储备成为四百家最大银行的造物，并摧毁其民主基础。"
To settle on the final terms for mandatory reserves, the sponsors engaged, over many months, in a three-sided dance of persuasion and bargaining—the Fed, the various banking interests and the congressional leaders, all negotiating with one another. To pass its legislation, the Fed had to assemble an enthusiastic coalition of bankers, and that required first Miller and then Volcker to offer some enticements to the various interests. As it happened, the final terms would resemble the outline first suggested by the Federal Advisory Council in the spring of 1979, though the banker-advisers did not get everything they wanted. When the legislation was finally enacted, the Federal Advisory Council was still complaining that it should have been more generous to the Fed’s member banks and more punitive for their rivals.
为了确定强制性准备金的最终条款，提案人花了数月时间进行了三方说服和讨价还价的舞蹈——美联储、各种银行业利益集团和国会领导人，都在相互谈判。为了通过其立法，美联储必须组建一个由银行家组成的热情联盟，这首先需要 Miller，然后是 Volcker，向不同利益集团提供一些诱惑。结果，最终条款与联邦咨询委员会在 1979 年春季首次提出的纲要相似，尽管银行家顾问们并没有得到他们想要的一切。当立法最终通过时，联邦咨询委员会仍在抱怨它本应对美联储的成员银行更慷慨，对其竞争对手更严厉。
The most important enticement offered to the Fed’s member banks was a substantial reduction in their reserves. The Federal Reserve agreed that if it won universal coverage for all banks, then the existing level of reserve requirements could be lowered for everyone. In the end, after much bargaining, the reserves that banks had to hold on demand deposits, the checking accounts that were the core of their funds, were reduced from 16.25 to 12 percent. The Fed’s reserve holdings actually shrank from $27 to $16 billion. In other words, the Fed member banks would enjoy a huge dividend in freed-up funds on which they could now earn interest income—while their competitors, the nonmember banks, would be subjected, for the first time, to the “tax” implicit in Fed reserves. (Bankers on the Federal Advisory Council argued for an even greater bonus—a reserve level of only 8 percent.)
向美联储成员银行提供的最重要的诱惑是大幅降低其准备金。联邦储备同意，如果它赢得了对所有银行的普遍覆盖，那么现有的准备金要求水平可以为所有人降低。最终，经过大量讨价还价，银行必须就需求存款（作为其资金核心的支票账户）持有的准备金从 16.25% 降至 12%。美联储持有的准备金实际上从 270 亿美元缩减到 160 亿美元。换句话说，美联储成员银行将享受巨额红利，释放出资金，现在它们可以赚取利息收入——而它们的竞争对手，非成员银行，将首次承受美联储准备金所隐含的"税收"。（联邦咨询委员会上的银行家主张更大的红利——准备金率仅为 8%。）
The reserves reduction, if nothing was done to offset it, would mean a substantial loss in revenue for the U.S. Treasury. This could be most embarrassing at a time when Volcker and the financial markets were urging Congress to reduce federal deficits to curb inflation. The Federal Reserve’s operating budget was entirely independent of congressional control, but each year the Fed paid a large dividend to the Treasury ($9.3 billion in 1979), which was its surplus income after operating costs. The Federal Reserve’s income came mainly from the interest on its holdings, including the reserve balances posted by banks, which were invested in U.S. government notes and bonds. The Fed held a little less than 15 percent of all the outstanding debt paper issued by the federal government. Thus, if its pool of bank reserves was reduced, the Federal Reserve’s annual income would be smaller and it would have less money to rebate to the Treasury.
如果不采取任何补救措施，准备金的减少将意味着美国财政部收入的重大损失。在 Volcker 和金融市场敦促国会减少联邦赤字以遏制通胀之时，这可能极其尴尬。联邦储备的运营预算完全独立于国会控制，但每年美联储都会向财政部支付大额股息（1979 年为 93 亿美元），这是其运营成本后的盈余收入。联邦储备的收入主要来自其持有资产的利息，包括银行存放的准备金余额，这些资金投资于美国政府票据和债券。美联储持有联邦政府发行的所有未偿债务票据中略低于 15% 的份额。因此，如果其银行准备金池减少，联邦储备的年收入将减少，能够返还给财政部的资金也会减少。
Actually, the Fed’s annual dividend to the Treasury seemed like money on a merry-go-round. Every year, like any private investor, the Fed collected interest payments from the Treasury on the U.S. securities it owned. Then at year’s end the Fed gave most of the money back to the Treasury. This was more than mere bookkeeping. In effect, as any commercial banker might bitterly point out, American taxpayers were saving money by using the bankers’ funds.
实际上，美联储每年向财政部支付的股息就像旋转木马上的钱。每年，像任何私人投资者一样，美联储从其拥有的美国证券中向财政部收取利息支付。然后在年底，美联储将大部分钱返还给财政部。这不仅仅是簿记工作。实际上，正如任何商业银行家可能会苦涩地指出的那样，美国纳税人通过使用银行家的资金来节省金钱。
The solution for the potential revenue loss was to require the Federal Reserve to start charging banks for its services—the daily checkclearing and allied processes. These banking services cost an estimated $400 million a year, and if the Fed priced them at the full cost, that would restore most of the loss. The final trade-off ended up costing the Treasury about $200 million, rising to nearly $600 million a year by 1985 when the new reserve rules would be fully phased in. The larger money-center banks, though they would pay more for Fed services, welcomed the change. Many of them were launching their own service systems for banks in competition with the Fed, and this would make it easier for them to lure away customers.
对潜在收入损失的解决方案是要求联邦储备开始对其服务收费——日常的支票清算和相关流程。这些银行服务每年估计耗资 4 亿美元，如果美联储按全成本定价，将弥补大部分损失。最终的权衡结果使财政部每年损失约 2 亿美元，到 1985 年新准备金规则完全实施时，这一数字上升到近 6 亿美元。较大的货币中心银行，尽管它们将为美联储服务支付更多费用，但欢迎这一变化。其中许多银行正在推出自己的银行服务系统，与美联储竞争，这将使它们更容易吸引客户。
At the core of the private bargaining, the Federal Reserve acquiesced to a deal with a delegation from the major New York banks. If the Fed would drop entirely its reserve requirement on time and savings deposits, the bankers proposed to Congressman Reuss, they would use their influence to win a legislative endorsement from the American Bankers Association. The concession on reserve requirements would mean a vast return for these bankers; their balance sheets depended heavily on large-denomination CDs. Reuss accepted and the Fed reluctantly went along—and the money-center bankers delivered on their end of the bargain. The ABA endorsed the legislation, despite many protests from regional banks and state-level affiliates.
在私下讨价还价的核心，联邦储备默许了与来自主要纽约银行代表团的交易。如果美联储完全放弃对定期存款和储蓄存款的准备金要求，银行家们向众议员 Reuss 提议，他们将利用其影响力赢得美国银行家协会的立法支持。准备金要求的让步将为这些银行家带来巨大回报；他们的资产负债表严重依赖于大额存单。Reuss 接受了，美联储不情愿地同意了——货币中心银行家们也兑现了他们的承诺。ABA 批准了该立法，尽管有许多地区银行和州级附属机构的抗议。
“We had to placate the small banks and the regional banks and the money-center banks—all of them,” Reuss explained. The Federal Reserve, he added, was simultaneously trying to protect its own interests while also looking out for the banks. “Axilrod [the Fed staff director] would throw new formulas into the hopper,” Reuss said. “Volcker, under Axilrod’s guidance, was always trying to get something more for the banks.”4
"我们必须安抚小银行、地区银行和货币中心银行——所有的，"Reuss 解释说。他补充说，联邦储备同时试图保护自身利益，同时也照看银行。"Axilrod（美联储工作人员主任）会不停地抛出新的方案，"Reuss 说。"在 Axilrod 的指导下，Volcker 总是试图为银行争取更多。"
In congressional combat, the Federal Reserve had what amounted to a secret weapon, an unseen political resource that it mobilized for every crucial roll call—the officers and directors of the twelve Federal Reserve Banks plus the board members for twenty-five branch offices in smaller cities. The central bank, as even Volcker acknowledged when pressed, could function perfectly well without any of these subsidiaries. But, in political terms, they provided a network of influential strategically scattered across America—available for discreet lobbying in behalf of the Fed.
在国会斗争中，联邦储备拥有相当于秘密武器的东西，一种看不见的政治资源，它在每一次关键投票中都会动员起来——十二家联邦储备银行的官员和董事，加上较小城市中二十五个分支机构的董事会成员。正如 Volcker 在被追问时承认的那样，中央银行完全可以在没有任何这些附属机构的情况下正常运作。但在政治层面上，它们提供了一个遍布美国各地的有影响力的网络——可以谨慎地为美联储进行游说。
Coast to coast, the Fed directors included an extraordinary cross section of important people: a sprinkling of academics and at least one labor leader, but mostly corporate executives and bankers. High-powered names in the Fed family included Filene’s and Connecticut General Life Insurance in the Boston district, Union Carbide, Allied Chemical and Union Pacific in New York, Westinghouse in Cleveland, Middle South Utilities in Atlanta, Boeing and Kaiser Aluminum in San Francisco, to mention a few. The Fed’s home office in Washington went through elaborate charades to convince the regional directors that their economic advice was vital to the System’s monetary decisions. In fact, it was not. What was important was their willingness to support the Fed in politics.
从东海岸到西海岸，美联储董事包括了一群非凡的重要人物：少数几位学者和至少一位劳工领袖，但主要是公司高管和银行家。美联储家族中的显赫名字包括波士顿地区的 Filene's 和 Connecticut General Life Insurance，纽约的 Union Carbide、Allied Chemical 和 Union Pacific，Cleveland 的 Westinghouse，Atlanta 的 Middle South Utilities，San Francisco 的 Boeing 和 Kaiser Aluminum，仅举几例。美联储在华盛顿的总部进行了精心设计的装模作样，以说服地区董事相信他们的经济建议对体系的货币决策至关重要。实际上，并非如此。重要的是他们愿意在政治上支持美联储。
“The banks don’t need boards of directors, but this is another way to get the movers and shakers on your side,” Daniel Brill, the Fed’s former research director, explained. “The chairman will put a lot of personal care into keeping them happy.”5
"银行不需要董事会，但这是让有影响力的人站在你这边的一种方式，"Daniel Brill，美联储前研究主任解释道。"主席会投入大量个人关怀来让他们开心。"
When each chamber of Congress prepared to vote on the deregulation legislation, the Fed enlisted members from its grassroots network. Pivotal senators or House members were targeted for personal phone calls and letters from the appropriate community leaders back home. Votes were secured and even switched in a most discreet manner. “It was quite effective,” Ken Guenther said. “We got the key players, whether directors or bankers, to move on the situation. They gave us contacts with the key congressional players at the moment of truth.”
当国会两院准备对去监管立法进行投票时，美联储从其基层网络中招募成员。关键的参议员或众议员被作为目标，由家乡的相关社区领袖进行个人电话和信件联系。以极其谨慎的方式争取选票甚至改变立场。"这非常有效，"Ken Guenther 说。"我们让关键人物——无论是董事还是银行家——推动局势。他们在关键时刻为我们提供了与关键国会参与者的联系。"
Congress went along with the Fed’s proposal, though many legislators probably did not grasp the true meaning of what they were enacting. In the midst of tight money and record-high interest rates, Congress effectively granted a huge windfall to the fifty-six hundred commercial banks that were members of the Federal Reserve System —especially the largest ones. This point was never mentioned in the congressional debates, but following the bankers’ own logic, the Monetary Control Act of 1980 represented a major tax cut for the banks, totaling many billions of dollars. When the new rules were fully phased in, the member banks would be freed of nearly $14.5 billion in reserve requirements—money they could now lend out or invest at market rates. How much was this worth to them? A conservative estimate, assuming the banks earned a return of 10 percent on these funds, suggested that the member banks would enjoy more than $5 billion in added income over the next five years, thanks to this arcane change at the Federal Reserve.6
国会同意了美联储的提案，尽管许多立法者可能并未完全理解他们所颁布的内容的真正含义。在紧缩货币和创纪录高利率的背景下，国会实际上向加入联邦储备体系的五千六百家商业银行——特别是其中最大的那些——授予了巨额意外之财。这一点在国会辩论中从未被提及，但按照银行家们自己的逻辑，1980 年的《货币控制法》代表了对银行的一次重大减税，总额达数百亿美元。当新规则完全实施后，成员银行将从近 145 亿美元的准备金要求中解脱出来——这些钱现在可以按市场利率贷出或投资。这对它们价值多少？一个保守的估计，假设银行从这些资金中获得 10% 的回报，表明成员银行将因美联储这一晦涩的变化而在未来五年内享受超过 50 亿美元的额外收入。
With all the other disorders in the economy, the raging inflation and the banks’ prime rate at 20 percent, members of Congress might have found it awkward to vote for the Federal Reserve’s measure if it had been advertised as a $5 billion tax cut for banks. The members of the two banking committees were intimately familiar with the implications, however. As the legislative negotiations proceeded, the Fed provided committee members with a running series of computer printouts, listing how much their hometown banks would gain or lose in the bargain.
在经济的其他各种混乱中——肆虐的通胀和银行 20% 的优惠利率——如果美联储的措施被宣传为对银行 50 亿美元的减税，国会议员们可能会发现投票支持它很尴尬。然而，两个银行业委员会的成员都熟悉其中的含义。在立法谈判进行过程中，美联储为委员会成员提供了一系列连续的计算机打印输出，列出他们家乡的银行在交易中将获得或损失多少。
The biggest winners were the largest banks. The fifty largest banks put up roughly half of the bank reserves, and they would get at least half of the $5 billion. The largest two hundred banks would get nearly three-fourths. The rest, $1.5 billion or so, would be divided among the remaining Fed members, more than five thousand banks.
最大的赢家是最大的银行。最大的五十家银行提供了大约一半的银行准备金，它们将获得至少 50 亿美元的一半。最大的二百家银行将获得近四分之三。其余约 15 亿美元将在剩余的五千多家美联储成员银行中分配。
“It was definitely a big-bank victory,” Guenther said, “that was foisted on the middle-level banks and the nonmember banks, who paid the freight.” The Federal Reserve advanced their big banks’ cause while it also served its own.
"这绝对是大银行的胜利，"Guenther 说，"强加给了中层银行和非成员银行，由它们买单。"联邦储备在服务于自身的同时也推进了其大银行的事业。
A banker and a congressman, typically, felt a natural affinity. Both were civic leaders in their community, and in different ways, both influenced the larger destiny beyond their own affairs. The banker knew the true condition of local commerce on a deep level. The congressman, naturally, consulted him. Despite occasional bombast between them, they needed each other’s favor. The relationship was so elementary and natural that it was seldom mentioned.
银行家和国会议员之间通常有一种天然的亲近感。两者都是社区的公民领袖，并以不同的方式影响着超越自身事务的更大命运。银行家在深层次上了解当地商业的真实状况。国会议员自然会咨询他。尽管偶尔在他们之间有夸夸其谈，但他们需要彼此的青睐。这种关系是如此基本和自然，以至于很少被提及。
Governor Nancy Teeters, coming from a liberal Democratic tradition, was surprised to discover this closeness when she served Congress as chief economist for the House Budget Committee. “On the Hill,” she recalled, “I was always amazed at the level of support for the Federal Reserve. It was partly because the way we operate is so arcane they don’t understand it, but also because the members of Congress are so close to the bankers. They see the bankers when they go back to their districts and they listen to them.”
州长 Nancy Teeters，出身于自由派民主党传统，当她作为众议院预算委员会的首席经济学家为国会服务时，对这种亲密关系感到惊讶。"在国会山，"她回忆道，"我总是对联邦储备所获得的支持程度感到惊讶。部分原因是我们的运作方式如此晦涩以至于他们不理解，但也是因为国会议员与银行家关系如此密切。他们回到选区时会见到银行家，并且倾听他们的意见。"
This mutuality—members of the governing elite relying on one another—was reinforced by more tangible forms of symbiosis. Commercial banks and other financial institutions, ranging from the savings and loan industry to major Wall Street brokerages, contributed generously to congressional campaigns, a major source of financing for the politicians. For the 1980 elections, incumbent senators and representatives would receive $2,664,000 in donations from the financial sector. The largest share of that money, nearly $590,000, was given to the members of the House and Senate banking committees, the people who drafted banking legislation.
这种互惠关系——统治精英成员相互依赖——受到了更具体形式的共生关系的加强。商业银行和其他金融机构，从储蓄贷款行业到主要的 Wall Street 经纪公司，都慷慨地向国会竞选活动捐款，这是政治家们的主要资金来源。在 1980 年的选举中，现任参议员和众议员从金融部门获得了 2,664,000 美元的捐款。这些钱的最大份额，近 590,000 美元，流向了起草银行业立法的众议院和参议院银行业委员会的成员。
In addition, many members of Congress were themselves involved in finance. More than one-fourth of the Congress owned a direct stake in financial enterprises. In 1980, 129 House members and 38 senators reported that they earned part of their income from stock shares in commercial banks, S & L’s and other financial institutions. As a private interest of congressmen and senators, ownership of financial institutions far exceeded their holdings in manufacturing, law firms, or oil and gas. Even some elected representatives were engaged in finance beyond the passive ownership of bank stocks. Forty House members and four senators were active as directors, officers or partners of commercial banks, S & L’s and investment companies.7
此外，许多国会议员本人也涉足金融业。超过四分之一的国会直接持有金融企业的股份。1980 年，129 名众议员和 38 名参议员报告说，他们部分收入来自商业银行、储蓄贷款协会和其他金融机构的股票。作为国会参众议员的私人利益，对金融机构的所有权远远超过其在制造业、律师事务所或石油和天然气中的持股。甚至一些当选代表也超越了被动持有银行股票而参与金融业。四十名众议员和四名参议员担任商业银行、储蓄贷款协会和投资公司的董事、高级管理人员或合伙人。
Finance was, therefore, in the front rank of political influence. As a general rule, if all the various interests in the financial sector reached agreement on banking legislation, then Congress would dutifully pass it. The long stalemate on deregulation essentially resulted from the intramural rivalry among the different financial institutions themselves, each holding out for competitive advantage. Once the contending interests in finance reached a fragile consensus on the terms, the legislation was passed.
因此，金融业处于政治影响力的前列。作为一般规则，如果金融部门的各种利益集团就银行业立法达成一致，那么国会就会尽职地通过它。去监管的长期僵局基本上是由于不同金融机构之间的内部竞争造成的，每一方都在争取竞争优势。一旦金融业中相互竞争的利益集团就条款达成了脆弱的共识，立法就通过了。
Interest rates were decontrolled and commercial banks were free to compete more aggressively for deposits. Small-town bankers resented giving up the “free money” of interest-free checking deposits, but larger banks insisted on it to preserve their competitive position. For years, bankers had portrayed themselves as embattled—surrounded by unregulated competitors like the money-market funds that siphoned off their loan customers and depositors. Their anxieties were no doubt sincere but, as Federal Reserve officers sometimes gently pointed out, not entirely consistent with the facts. Commercial banks were not losing market shares but actually gaining moderately (though perhaps not as fast as entrepreneurial bankers wished). The banks’ share of total credit-market debt claims was 30.6 percent in 1979, higher than in 1959, when it was 27.3 percent. Bank profits were down somewhat in the late 1970s but still looked quite strong compared to the long-run performance. The average return on equity was 11.5 percent in 1979, not an especially good year, compared to only 8.3 percent twenty years before. If bankers felt squeezed, they seemed to be coping admirably with their stress.
利率被解除管制，商业银行可以更积极地争夺存款。小镇银行家对放弃免息支票存款的"免费资金"感到不满，但较大的银行坚持要这样做以保持其竞争地位。多年来，银行家们将自己描绘成四面楚歌——被未受监管的竞争对手（如吸走他们贷款客户和存款人的货币市场基金）包围。他们的焦虑无疑是真诚的，但正如美联储官员有时温和地指出的那样，并不完全符合事实。商业银行并没有失去市场份额，实际上正在适度增长（尽管可能不如进取的银行家所希望的那样快）。银行在总信贷市场债务债权中的份额在 1979 年为 30.6%，高于 1959 年的 27.3%。银行利润在 1970 年代末略有下降，但与长期表现相比仍然相当强劲。1979 年的平均股本回报率为 11.5%，这算不上特别好的一年，而二十年前仅为 8.3%。如果银行家感到受压，他们似乎正在令人钦佩地应对他们的压力。
Most of the four thousand savings and loans were not coping as well. For many years, the S & L’s had used their own political clout to hold off deregulation, but inflation and rising interest rates had so weakened their balance sheets that they could no longer resist the bankers’ lobbying. “They’re going to go out of business anyway if they can’t adapt,” Senator Proxmire, the banking chairman, said.8 The thrifts were compelled to give up the interest-rate differential imposed by law that had made their savings accounts more attractive than the banks’. In exchange, they were given limited solace—the right to expand their lending into business ventures that were more profitable (and also more risky) than home mortgages. Most thrifts did not like it, but they did not have much choice. “They were reduced to a state of sullen neutrality,” Kenneth McLean, staff director of the Senate Banking Committee, explained.9
大多数储蓄贷款协会却没有应对得那么好。多年来，储蓄贷款协会利用自己的政治影响力来拖延去监管，但通胀和不断上升的利率严重削弱了它们的资产负债表，以至于它们再也无法抵挡银行家的游说。"如果不能适应，反正它们也会倒闭，"参议员 Proxmire，银行业委员会主席说。储蓄机构被迫放弃由法律规定的利率差，这种差异曾使它们的储蓄账户比银行的更吸引人。作为交换，它们得到了有限的安慰——将其贷款扩展到比住房抵押贷款更有利可图（也更风险更高）的商业领域的权利。大多数储蓄机构不喜欢这样，但它们没有太多选择。"它们被降为一种闷闷不乐的中立状态，"Kenneth McLean，参议院银行业委员会的工作人员主任解释说。
Traditionally savings and loans had been given a protected status and an implicit subsidy so they would provide adequate financing for homeownership—at affordable rates. Now, in effect, they were told to become more like their old rivals, the commercial banks, and to compete with them head to head. “It’s a matter of survival of the fittest,” Representative Reuss, the House Banking chairman, proclaimed.10 More than a thousand S & L’s would lose the struggle to survive—closed or merged within the next few years. Instead of improving their balance sheets, another thousand would sink further “under water.”
传统上，储蓄贷款协会被给予受保护的地位和隐性补贴，以便它们能够以负担得起的利率为住房拥有提供足够的融资。现在，实际上，它们被告知要变得更加像它们的老对手商业银行，并与它们正面竞争。"这是适者生存的问题，"众议员 Reuss，众议院银行业主席宣称。超过一千家储蓄贷款协会将在生存斗争中失败——在接下来的几年内被关闭或合并。另外一千家将不是改善其资产负债表，而是进一步"沉入水下"。
As these weaker lending institutions failed, the larger banking organizations would acquire the remains—a steady consolidation of financial power that enabled Citibank, Chemical, Chase Manhattan and others to build the beginning structures of nationwide banks, preparing for the day when Congress finally would make them legal. During the debates, A. W. Clausen, president of the Bank of America, largest in the nation, complained that America’s financial industry was still “dominated by mom-and-pop stores.” For better or worse, financial deregulation would hasten their demise.
随着这些较弱的贷款机构倒闭，较大的银行组织将收购残骸——金融权力的稳步集中使 Citibank、Chemical、Chase Manhattan 等银行能够构建全国性银行的初步架构，为国会最终使其合法化的一天做准备。在辩论期间，A. W. Clausen，美国最大的银行美洲银行的总裁，抱怨美国的金融业仍然"由夫妻店主导"。无论好坏，金融去监管将加速它们的消亡。
“Freeing the thrift and mortgage markets from government subsidy and guarantee,” Albert Wojnilower observed, “is like freeing family pets by abandoning them in the jungle.”11
"将储蓄机构和抵押贷款市场从政府补贴和担保中解放出来，"Albert Wojnilower 评论道，"就像通过将家养宠物遗弃在丛林中来实现解放一样。"
The Democratic majority, given its heritage, might still have resisted deregulation, despite the consensus among financial interests. Organized labor was opposed, as it had always been; so were the home builders and other interest groups allied with the housing industry. But the political resistance was worn down and finally overwhelmed by inflation.
鉴于其传统，民主党的多数派本来可能仍会抵制去监管，尽管金融界达成了共识。有组织的劳工一直是反对的；房屋建筑商和其他与住房行业结盟的利益集团也是如此。但政治阻力被通胀逐渐削弱并最终被压倒。
“If you save money, you lose,” Senator Proxmire observed caustically in the debate. “It is ridiculous for the savings rate to be a maximum of percent at a bank for a small saver or 5Vfe percent at a savings and loan, when the inflation rate, as we all know, is about 13 percent.” The Senate committee report also used strong language: “. . . Regulation Q now sanctions blatant discrimination against the small saver to justify its continued existence. It is grossly unfair. . . . Small savers should not be forced by a system of government rate controls to subsidize borrowers.”12
"如果你存钱，你就会亏损，"参议员 Proxmire 在辩论中尖刻地评论道。"一个小储户在银行最多得到 x% 的储蓄利率，在储蓄贷款协会得到 5 又 1/2%，而通货膨胀率，众所周知，约为 13%，这是荒谬的。"参议院委员会报告也使用了强烈的语言："……Q 条例如今认可对小储户的公然歧视，以证明其继续存在的合理性。这是极不公平的……小储户不应该被一种政府利率管制制度强迫去补贴借款人。"
Defending the “small saver” sounded like helping the very people the Democratic Party had always spoken for. This was not quite the case, as Senator Robert Morgan of North Carolina and the handful of other dissenters pointed out. “The majority of consumers, with or without savings, particularly families of low income, including one-half of the elderly families, would probably suffer a net economic loss,” Morgan predicted.13
捍卫"小储户"听起来像是在帮助民主党一直为之代言的那些人。但情况并非完全如此，正如北卡罗来纳州参议员 Robert Morgan 和其他少数持不同政见者所指出的那样。"大多数消费者，无论是否有储蓄，特别是低收入家庭，包括一半的老年家庭，可能会遭受净经济损失，"Morgan 预言道。
The “small savers” whom Democrats wished to help were actually a limited group, mostly above the median income. First of all, 37 percent of American families had no savings account at all. Another 29 percent had savings balances below $2,000. If these families received another 1 to 3 percent interest on their accounts, that would return at most only $60 a year in additional income. Meanwhile, as borrowers, they would of course pay higher interest rates. As consumers, they would also pay higher prices since business was a major beneficiary of the hidden subsidy of interest-rate controls. When businesses had to pay more for credit, the costs would be passed on to their customers.
民主党希望帮助的"小储户"实际上是一个有限的群体，大多在收入中位数以上。首先，37% 的美国家庭根本没有储蓄账户。另有 29% 的储蓄余额低于 2,000 美元。如果这些家庭在账户上多获得 1% 到 3% 的利息，那最多只会在每年增加 60 美元的额外收入。与此同时，作为借款人，他们自然要支付更高的利率。作为消费者，他们也将支付更高的价格，因为企业是利率管制隐性补贴的主要受益者。当企业不得不为信贷支付更多时，成本会转嫁给它们的客户。
During the debate, the elderly were frequently invoked as the victims of the government controls—widows and retired couples who depended on their savings and were embittered by inflation. The elderly had worked a lifetime to accumlulate their nest eggs and now they were being cheated out of a fair return.14 The Gray Panthers, among other groups, endorsed the legislation. Citibank put up posters in its lobbies blaming Washington for the bank’s inability to pay higher interest.
在辩论期间，老年人经常被援引为政府管制的受害者——依赖储蓄的寡妇和退休夫妇，被通胀所苦。老年人工作了一辈子来积累他们的养老钱，现在他们被剥夺了公平的回报。灰豹组织等团体支持该立法。Citibank 在其营业大厅张贴海报，将银行无法支付更高利息归咎于华盛顿。
Yet the elderly were also divided by their financial status into winners and losers. Half of the elderly households, sixty-five years old or older, had no savings account at all. Another 11 percent had less than $2,000 and would gain very little. Only 29 percent of the elderly had more than $5,000 in savings and stood to receive a significant increase in their incomes from deregulation. The group of older citizens who would benefit, in other words, was much smaller than the old folks who would not.
然而，老年人也按其财务状况分为赢家和输家。一半的老年家庭，65 岁或以上，根本没有储蓄账户。另有 11% 的储蓄不足 2,000 美元，几乎不会获得什么好处。只有 29% 的老年人有超过 5,000 美元的储蓄，将从去监管中获得显著的收入增加。换句话说，将受益的老年公民群体比不会受益的老年人群体小得多。
Like other forms of deregulation that eliminated hidden subsidies and assigned the true costs directly to customers, financial deregulation inevitably benefited the largest customers most and penalized smaller customers proportionately. Half of the families in America, for instance, had checking balances of $500 or less—too small to qualify for the interest payments from NOW accounts.15 But these families would begin paying more for their banking services. To make up the cost of paying interest on NOW accounts, banks introduced a bewildering variety of service fees on regular checking accounts. The consequences were not different from cost efficiencies large users gained from the deregulation of airlines or telephones. The little guys, once protected by government regulation, were free to pay more.
就像其他消除隐性补贴并将真实成本直接分配给客户的去监管形式一样，金融去监管不可避免地使最大的客户受益最多，并按比例惩罚较小的客户。例如，一半的美国家庭的支票余额为 500 美元或更少——太小了以至于没有资格获得 NOW 账户的利息支付。但这些家庭将开始为其银行服务支付更多费用。为了弥补支付 NOW 账户利息的成本，银行引入了令人眼花缭乱的各种普通支票账户服务费。其后果与大用户从航空或电话去监管中获得的成本效益并无不同。小人物，曾经受到政府管制的保护，现在自由地支付更多。
In a larger sense, however, financial deregulation was a contest between the generations, a choice between the old and the young, the claims of the past and the ambitions of the future. Though few focused on it, the issue pitted young people who were just starting out in life against their parents’ generation, older families that were already established, that already owned their own home and perhaps by middle age were accumulating financial savings. The old would lend their money to the young at higher prices than ever before. Young families forming in the 1980s, setting out to buy a car or their first home, would confront credit terms and interest rates that would have terrified their parents, double-digit mortgages and floating rates that left them vulnerable to default.
然而，在更广泛的意义上，金融去监管是世代之间的较量，是老年人与年轻人之间的选择，是过去的主张与未来的抱负之间的选择。尽管很少有人关注这一点，但这个问题将刚刚开始生活的年轻人与他们的父母一代、已经安顿下来的老年家庭对立起来，后者已经拥有自己的住房，或许在中年时已经积累了金融储蓄。老年人将以比以往更高的价格将他们的钱借给年轻人。在 1980 年代组建的年轻家庭，开始购买汽车或他们的第一套住房，将面临会吓坏他们父母的信贷条件和利率——两位数的抵押贷款利率和浮动利率使他们容易陷入违约。
Homeownership, the central component of what was described as the “American dream,” the main objective of the political controls imposed on finance, would gradually shrink among the population, most dramatically among younger families. By embracing financial deregulation, the Democratic majority was implicitly voting to protect the established interests of older Americans and to reduce life possibilities for the young.
住房拥有，作为被描述为"美国梦"的核心组成部分，也是施加于金融业的政治控制的主要目标，将在人口中逐渐萎缩，在年轻家庭中最为显著。通过拥抱金融去监管，民主党多数派实际上在投票保护年长美国人的既定利益，并减少年轻人的生活可能性。
Liberalism, it seemed, was turned upside down. Every important aspect of the 1980 legislation—repealing usury laws, authorizing interest on checking accounts, abolishing rate ceilings—involved the same exchange. The Democratic Party was abandoning the egalitarian agenda that it had embraced for nearly fifty years. The benefits would flow upward on the economic ladder and the costs would flow downward. The past was defended at the expense of the future. This transformation, overwhelmingly endorsed by Democrats after ten years of legislative struggle, could hardly be dismissed as the transient choices of a few party leaders or even explained by the political pressures from the financial industry. Something deeper was at work.
自由主义，似乎被颠倒了。1980 年立法的每一个重要方面——废除高利贷法、授权支票账户付息、取消利率上限——都涉及同样的交换。民主党正在放弃其已坚持近五十年的平等主义议程。利益将沿着经济阶梯向上流动，成本将向下流动。过去以牺牲未来为代价被捍卫。这种转变，在经过十年的立法斗争后被民主党以压倒性多数批准，很难被视为少数政党领导人的临时选择，甚至无法用来自金融业的政治压力来解释。某种更深层的东西在起作用。
The party of liberalism, one could say, was apologizing for inflation. The Democrats were offering redress to those who had been hurt most, the owners of financial wealth, large or small, whose assets were eroded by nearly fifteen years of price inflation. For years, the party of Roosevelt and Keynes had ducked the question or deflected blame, but as inflation worsened, the disorder was associated indelibly with the expansionary economic strategies of the Democratic Party. The political message implicit in financial deregulation was addressed to those who felt most injured by inflation, the people of relative means who had not been part of the Democratic Party’s traditional constituency. We know you have been hurt, the message said, but we will make it up to you. We will set aside our old agenda and help to restore some of your losses.
可以说，自由党是在为通胀道歉。民主党正在向那些受伤最深的人提供补偿——金融财富的所有者，无论大小，其资产都因近十五年的物价通胀而受到侵蚀。多年来，罗斯福和凯恩斯的政党一直在回避这个问题或转移指责，但随着通胀恶化，这种混乱不可避免地与民主党的扩张性经济战略联系在一起。金融去监管所隐含的政治信息是针对那些受通胀伤害最深的人——那些有一定财力但并非民主党传统支持群体的人。我们知道你受到了伤害，这条信息说，但我们会补偿你。我们将搁置我们的旧议程，帮助你恢复部分损失。
Senator Proxmire, who came closest to explicitly stating the new perspective, said:
参议员 Proxmire，最接近于明确陈述这种新视角的人，说道：
There is nobody in public office today who does not recognize that the number-one problem of this country is inflation. The way to meet inflation

参议院委员会报告同样表达了民主党人变化的思维："……储蓄已经成为一种无回报的经济体验。通货膨胀吞噬储蓄，侵蚀储蓄作为经济价值储存手段的功能。不公平的低储蓄回报抑制储蓄，鼓励当前消费。在通货膨胀是我们的头号经济问题的时候，这是错误的经济政策。"
The Senate committee report, likewise, expressed the changed thinking among Democrats: “. . . saving has become an unrewarding economic experience. Inflation eats away at savings and erodes savings as a store of economic value. Unfairly low returns on savings discourage savings and encourage current consumption. This is the wrong economic policy at a time when inflation is our No. 1 economic problem.”
粗略地说，该立法不仅奖励了辩论中如此频繁提到的传说中的"小储户"，而且奖励了所有债权人——包括拥有 86% 净金融财富的最富裕的 10% 美国家庭。去监管将帮助依赖小额储蓄账户的老年寡妇，但它也会改善所有其他拥有金融资产的人的利息收入。这一后果在辩论中只是间接讨论，毫无疑问，许多投票支持金融去监管的人并不理解他们实际上是在提高所有财富持有者的利率，无论他们将钱放在哪里。
In gross terms, the legislation would reward not simply the fabled “small saver” mentioned so often in debate but all creditors—including the wealthiest 10 percent of American families that owned 86 percent of the net financial wealth. Deregulation would help the elderly widow who relied on her small savings account, but it would also improve the interest income for everyone else who owned financial assets. This consequence was discussed only obliquely in debate, and, no doubt, many who voted for financial deregulation did not understand they were effectively raising interest rates for all wealth holders, regardless of where they kept their money.
这一解释对于金融市场的本质至关重要，在金融市场中，所有投资机会相互竞争。废除储蓄和支票账户的监管上限就像提高了货币的基本利率——其他未受监管的利率也必须上升以保持其竞争优势。提高底层储蓄回报将向上传导至其他金融工具，其方式类似于提高最低工资会将工资推高给其他已经远高于最低工资的工人。如果非技术工人得到更多，那么技术工人也可以要求更多。虽然经济学家将无法确定对利率的精确影响（可能在 1% 到 3% 之间），但去监管的效果将是明显的。整个贷款和投资领域的利率在未来几年内将保持在历史高位，无论通货膨胀水平如何。
The explanation was fundamental to the nature of financial markets, where all opportunities for investment compete with one another. Abolishing the regulatory ceilings on savings and checking accounts was like raising the base rate for money—other, unregulated interest rates must rise too in order to maintain their competitive edge. Increasing the bottom-tier savings return would ripple upward through other financial instruments, much the way raising the minimum wage will push up wages for other workers who are already paid much more than the minimum. If unskilled workers get more, then skilled workers can demand more too. While economists would find it impossible to determine the precise impact on interest rates (it might be anywhere from 1 to 3 percent), the effects of deregulation would be evident. Interest rates across the spectrum of lending and investment would remain historically higher in the years ahead, regardless of the level of inflation.
"就像原油价格大幅上涨时汽油价格被迫上涨一样……存款和贷款利率上限的去监管释放了比管制下高得多的利率水平，"Albert Wojnilower 解释说。实际上，更高的利率起到了收入再分配系统的作用——方向相反。不是像民主党传统上试图做的那样向下分配收入，而是利率将收入向另一个方向转移，从借款人到贷款人，从底层到顶层。
“Just as the price of gasoline is forced up when the crude-oil price increases materially ... so the deregulation of deposit and loan interest-rate ceilings has unleashed much higher levels of interest rates than prevailed under controls,” Albert Wojnilower explained.16 As a practical matter, the higher interest rates functioned as a system of income redistribution—in reverse. Instead of distributing income downward, as Democrats traditionally tried to do, interest moved incomes in the other direction, from borrowers to lenders, from the bottom to the top.
大致来说，这种分配将流向拥有个人所持全部净金融财富的 45% 的美国家庭。这笔钱将由净金融财富为零或为负的 55% 的美国家庭以及大小企业支付。表面上看，政治家们选择让多数人付费来奖励少数人似乎难以置信，但从选举角度来看，这更有道理。美国选民正越来越偏向高收入选民，正如 Thomas B. Edsall 在他的《不平等的新政治》一书中所解释的那样。随着过去二十年投票参与率的下降，在疏离的低收入公民中下降最为剧烈。民主党在 1980 年面临的萎缩的选民中，高收入、受过更好教育的选民占据了优势，他们能够表达自己的要求并期待回应。例如，收入低于 15,000 美元的家庭占人口的 40%，但在活跃选民中仅占 33%。相反，收入超过 25,000 美元的家庭占总人口的 29%，但他们行使了 35% 的投票力量。选民中的这种扭曲，随着它在 1970 年代日益增强，将民主党推向了选票所在之处——民主党更认真地倾听上层公民的抱怨。
The distribution, roughly speaking, would flow to the 45 percent of American families that owned all of the net financial worth held by individuals. The money would be paid by the 55 percent of American families whose net financial worth was zero or negative and by businesses, large and small. On its face, that seemed like an implausible choice for politicians to make, charging the majority to reward the minority, but in electoral terms it made more sense. The American electorate was becoming more and more skewed in favor of the upper-income voters, as Thomas B. Edsall explained in his book The New Politics of Inequality. As voting participation declined over the last twenty years, it fell most drastically among the alienated citizens with lower incomes. The atrophied electorate that the Democrats faced in 1980 was top-heavy with upper-income, better-educated voters who could articulate their demands and expect a response. Families that earned less than $15,000, for instance, made up 40 percent of the population, but only 33 percent of the active voters. Conversely, families over $25,000 were 29 percent of the population, but they exercised 35 percent of the voting strength. This distortion in the electorate, as it grew stronger through the 1970s, pushed the Democratic Party toward where the votes were—and Democrats listened more earnestly to the grievances of upscale citizens.
1980 年的银行业立法并非民主党向这些选民道歉的第一个姿态。政治价值观的深刻转变并非在一个季节或由一个问题开始。来自通货膨胀的抱怨已经积累了十年。民主党时而带着怨恨和不情愿，从他们旧的价值观中做出了一系列有限的退却，这些立法让步隐含地旨在恢复因通胀而损失的资本价值。1978 年，国会将联邦资本利得税减半，帮助那些在 1970 年代投资停滞的股东。同年，帮助穷人和受抚养者的国内社会项目的预算增长被叫停。在二十年的时间里，民主党控制的国会将企业的联邦税收负担份额从 26% 降至 12%，并主要通过累退的社会保障税将负担转移给了个人纳税人。
The banking legislation of 1980 was not the Democratic Party’s first gesture of apology to these voters. A deep transformation in political values did not begin in one season or with a single issue. The complaints from inflation had been building up for a decade. Fitfully, with rancor and reluctance, Democrats had made a series of limited retreats from their old values, legislative concessions implicitly intended to restore the value of capital lost through inflation. In 1978, Congress cut in half the federal income tax on capital gains, aiding the stockholders whose investments stagnated during the 1970s. The same year, the budget growth for domestic social programs that aided the poor and dependent was halted. Over two decades, the Democratic-controlled Congress had reduced corporations’ share of the federal tax burden from 26 to 12 percent and shifted the burden to individual taxpayers largely through regressive Social Security taxes.17
无论这样或那样，所有这些措施都逆转了自由主义传统的流向。每一项都试图向处于经济阶梯上半层的人提供一些缓解。这不足以满足他们对通货膨胀的不满。政治斗争将继续。这场角逐在许多战线上展开，从所得税到货币政策，都指向同一个基本目标——资本的恢复。
One way or the other, all these measures reversed the flow of the liberal tradition. Each attempted to give some relief to those on the upper half of the economic ladder. These were not sufficient to satisfy their grievances against inflation. The political struggle would continue. The contest arose on many fronts, from income taxes to monetary policy, addressed to the same fundamental goal, the restoration of capital.
金融去监管立法被证明是 1980 年代的合适序曲。去监管的政治准确地预测了将在新十年中盛行的政治偏好，哪些利益将得到服务，哪些将被推到一边。该措施建立的市场自由条件为金融业扫清了道路；不受管制的利率为金融财富的所有者开启了一个前所未有的繁荣时代。其他部门，那些最依赖借贷的部门——住房、农业、工业生产和劳工、消费者——将承受后果。民主党不情愿开始的事情，新的共和党政府将以热情继续。
The financial deregulation legislation proved to be an appropriate prelude to the 1980s. The politics of deregulation accurately forecast the political preferences that would prevail in the new decade, which interests would be served and which would be pushed aside. The free-market terms established by the measure cleared the way for finance; unregulated interest rates allowed an era of unprecedented prosperity to unfold for the owners of financial wealth. Other sectors, the ones most dependent on borrowed money, housing, agriculture, industrial production and labor, consumers, would absorb the consequences. What the Democrats had begun reluctantly, a new Republican Administration would continue with relish.
多年来，美国社会一直处于道德解放的时代，一个长期存在的宗教戒律和继承的社会禁忌在新的流行欲望面前倒下的时期。新教徒对赌博的反对被州和地方政府主办的数百万美元的公共彩票所取代。天主教对堕胎的罪孽被合法化。曾经被禁止和隐秘的色情内容变得自由可得。同性恋甚至卖淫都受到更宽容的重新审视。维持了几个世纪的道德约束被抛弃了。关于罪恶的旧观念被重新定义为主要是私事，不再受公共监管。
For some years, American society had been engaged in an era of moral liberation, a period when long-standing religious commandments and inherited social taboos fell before the new popular desires. Protestant disapproval of gambling was displaced by multimillion-dollar public lotteries, sponsored by state and local governments. The Catholic sin of abortion was legalized. Pornography, once forbidden and furtive, became freely available. Homosexuality and even prostitution were reconsidered in a more tolerant light. Moral inhibitions that had held authority for centuries were abandoned. Old notions of sinfulness were redefined as largely private matters, no longer subject to public regulation.
在这种道德变化的气候中，美国金融业也被解放出来去做曾经被禁止的事情。高利贷的罪恶被国会法案合法化了。反对高利贷的宗教禁令与《创世纪》一样古老，并在美国法律中得到了具体定义：以超过一定上限的利率放贷对不幸的借款人是毁灭性的，因此被禁止。当国会通过金融去监管时，它宣布，鉴于两位数的通货膨胀情况，高利贷法必须被搁置。州政府对住房抵押贷款设定的利率上限被联邦立法废除，对商业和农业贷款的限制被暂停三年。许多州立法机构，在相同的压力下回应，废除了它们自己对消费贷款的利率上限。
In this climate of moral change, American finance was also liberated to do what had once been forbidden. The sin of usury was legalized, by act of Congress. Religious injunctions against usury were as old as the Book of Genesis and given specific definition in American law: lending at interest rates above certain limits was ruinous to the hapless borrower and therefore prohibited. When it enacted financial deregulation, Congress declared that, given the circumstances of double-digit inflation, the usury laws must be set aside. The interest-rate ceilings imposed by state governments on home mortgages were abolished by federal legislation, and the limits on business and agricultural lending were suspended for three years. Many state legislatures, responding to the same pressures, repealed their own usury limits on consumer loans.
与其他道德禁忌的消退不同，高利贷被非刑事化时并未引起巨大争议。对私人性行为的新宽容引发了公众反应和政治运动的风暴，但高利贷几乎没有引起任何反应。以毁灭性的利率放贷现在将被视为，像性一样，一种"无受害者的犯罪"，是成年人之间自愿的私人行为。
Unlike the eclipse of other moral taboos, usury provoked no great controversy when it was decriminalized. The new leniency toward private sexual behavior inspired storms of popular reaction and political movements, but usury provoked little or no reaction. Lending money at ruinous interest rates would now be regarded, like sex, as a “victimless crime,” a private act between consenting adults.
高利贷法的暂停，虽然不如其他条款重要，却准确地反映了推动去监管一揽子方案的道德逻辑。美国精英阶层——无论是民主党还是共和党——所共享的政治价值观正发生深刻转变，其影响跨越了金融之外的许多领域。从新政继承下来的价值观——政府将在私人经济利益之间的原始斗争中充当监管者和保护者的理念——正在衰落。自由主义的承诺——为市场中较弱的竞争者提供庇护以确保某些社会目标——正在被取代。一种更古老的信仰正在复兴并重新获得其原始霸权，即认为不受束缚的自由市场最能实现社会正义。
The suspension of the usury laws, though less important than the other provisions, accurately reflected the moral logic that propelled the deregulation package. A deep transformation was under way in the political values shared by American elites, both Democratic and Republican, and it cut across many areas beyond finance. The values inherited from the New Deal—the idea that government would serve as regulator and protector in the raw struggles among private economic interests—were in eclipse. The commitments of liberalism— providing shelter for weaker combatants in the marketplace in order to insure certain social goals—were being displaced. An older faith was reviving and regaining its original hegemony, the belief that social justice was best served by an unfettered free market.
高利贷的圣经含义定义了那些已经积累财富的人对没有财富且处于需要中的人的责任。它限制了富人通过借贷对穷人行使的权力。到了 1980 年，道德论证被颠倒了。债权人在政治辩论中被描绘成受害者——被政治干预剥削的有德行的公民。借款人被描述为道德上可疑的人——自己不储蓄的人，其"投机性"支出受到有德行的储蓄者的"补贴"。高利贷概念所暗示的原始社会契约——财富对他人的需要的义务——被颠倒了。国会辩论描述了一种新的政治义务：储蓄者必须被解放，自由地追求其资金的最高回报率。
The biblical meaning of usury defined the obligations of those who had accumulated wealth toward others who had none and were in need. It imposed limits on the power that the wealthy could exercise, through lending, over the poor. By 1980, the moral argument was reversed. Creditors were now portrayed in political debate as the victims, the virtuous citizens who were exploited by the political interference. Borrowers were described as morally suspect—people who did not themselves save, whose “speculative” spending was “subsidized” by the virtuous savers. The original social contract implied by the concept of usury—the obligations of wealth toward the needs of others —was inverted. The congressional debate described a new political obligation: the savers must be set free, free to seek the highest rate of return on their money.
当然，国会并未辩论高利贷的道德含义，而是对高利贷法的实际后果做出反应。随着通胀将市场利率推得更高，高利贷上限实际上在一州又一州停止了贷款。没有人愿意以旧利率放贷，地方商业因缺乏信贷而陷入困境。然而，公民们仍然相信这个概念。在 Arkansas，选民两次拒绝废除州宪法中 10% 的高利贷上限，尽管该州已不再提供住房抵押贷款和汽车贷款。他们的抵抗最终是徒劳的。没有一个州能够在不付出经济停滞的不可承受代价的情况下退出国家金融体系。
Congress, of course, did not debate the moral meaning of usury, but was reacting to the practical consequences of usury laws. As inflation pushed market interest rates higher, the usury ceilings effectively shut down lending in state after state. No one would lend at the old levels, and local commerce was starved for credit. Citizens still believed in the concept, however. In Arkansas, voters twice refused to repeal the usury limit of 10 percent in the state constitution, despite the fact that home mortgages and auto loans were no longer available in the state. Their resistance ultimately was futile. No single state could opt out of the national financial system without paying the unbearable price of economic stagnation.
高利贷的道德概念始终与资本主义的动力存在根本冲突。高利贷意味着社会义务；资本主义依赖于个人收益。当它们在西方历史中碰撞时，资本主义的命令占了上风。直到中世纪晚期，天主教会仍然教导说，以任何利率放贷——无论利率多少——都是对上帝的犯罪，正如 1139 年第二次拉特兰会议所描述的，"可耻的"。高利贷者，尽管他们可能是富有的商人和教会事务中的显要人物，仍被逐出教会并拒绝葬在基督教墓地，与强盗、妓女和异教徒一同被谴责。他们永恒诅咒的描述是最为可怕的：蟾蜍和其他恶魔聚集在高利贷者的尸体上，从他的钱包中掏出银币，将硬币钉入尸体的心脏和嘴巴。道德上的冒犯是不劳而获。高利贷者出售时间，而时间只属于上帝。
The moral concept of usury was always in fundamental conflict with the dynamics of capitalism. Usury implied social obligation; capitalism depended on individual gain. When they collided in Western history, the capitalist imperative prevailed. Until the late Middle Ages, the Catholic Church still taught that lending at interest—any interest rate whatever—was a sin against God, “ignominious,” as the Second Lat-eran Council of 1139 described it. Usurers, though they might be wealthy merchants and prominent in Church affairs, were excommunicated and refused burial in Christian ground, condemned with robbers, prostitutes and heretics. Their eternal damnation was described in the most grisly terms: toads and other demons gathered on the usurer’s corpse, plucking silver from his purse and driving the coins into the heart and mouth of the cadaver. The moral offense was profit without work. The usurer sold time, which belonged only to God.
基督教神学最终屈服于新的现实。到了十三世纪，资本主义的原始网络——产生用于贸易的剩余商品的专业化生产——正在欧洲繁荣发展。即使以最简单的形式运作，资本主义也需要信贷所提供的跨越时间的联系——今天为未来完成的交易提供贷款。领导这场伟大转变的商人王子们被定为有罪者，然而他们的企业显然正在产生新的富足水平，使收入和财富倍增，超越了古代为生存而进行的不稳定斗争。一项神学创新为他们的罪孽赎罪开辟了道路——炼狱的详细阐述。死后，被诅咒的高利贷者的灵魂或许仍可以通过祈祷和其他考虑的干预而复活获得永生。许多原始资本家带着罪恶感向前冲，指望炼狱来获得最终的救赎。"炼狱的诞生，"法国历史学家 Jacques Le Goff 写道，"也是银行业的黎明。"
Christian theology eventually yielded to the new reality. By the thirteenth century, the primitive networks of capitalism—specialized production that yielded surplus goods for trade—were flourishing across Europe. To function, even in its simplest forms, capitalism required the linkage across time that credit provided—lending today for transactions that would be completed in the future. The merchant princes who led the great transformation were cast as sinners, and yet their enterprise was demonstrably generating new levels of abundance, multiplying income and wealth beyond the ancient, precarious struggle for subsistence. A theological innovation opened the way for absolution of their sins—the elaboration of purgatory. After death, the souls of condemned usurers might yet be resurrected for eternity through the intervention of prayer and other considerations. Many primitive capitalists plunged forward into sinfulness, counting on purgatory for their eventual salvation. “The birth of purgatory,” the French historian Jacques Le Goff wrote, “is also the dawn of banking.”18

处于"原始积累"阶段的典型资本家，Masenx 将利润再投资，并在其涉及的一切上赚钱。首先，他以短期和高利息提供谷物或货币贷款，每次为期一个月甚至一周，他还实行最原始形式的高利贷，"按日计息，随他高兴"。他借钱给他的佃农（他们甚至没有种子）来嫁女儿或姐妹，他还以信贷方式提供婚礼宴席上的银器、陈酒和羊腿。他用土地放贷，随着时间的推移，他负债累累的客户的田地会帮助扩充 Masenx 自己的财产。
A typical capitalist at the stage of “primitive accumulation,” Masenx reinvested his profits and made money on everything he turned his hand to. In the first place, he made loans of grain or money at short term and high interest for a month or for a week at a time, and he also practiced the crudest form of usury, “from day to day at his pleasure.” He lent his bordiers (who did not even have sowing seed) the money to marry off a daughter or a sister, and he also furnished, on credit, the silver, the old wine, and the leg of mutton for the wedding feast. He lent money on land, and in time the fields of his debt-ridden clients would help round out Masenx’s own properties.19
对银行家及其对他人的权力的道德蔑视历经数个世纪一直延续至今，但资本积累的过程建立了自己的正当性。其道德在 John Calvin 的新教伦理中得到提升；其机制由伴随工业资本主义兴起的古典经济学家详细阐述。银行家们被保证，做曾经被禁止的事情实际上是在为所有人做好事。
Moral contempt for bankers and their power over others would endure across the centuries and into the present time, but the process of capital accumulation established its own justification. The morality was exalted in the Protestant ethic of John Calvin; the mechanics were elaborated by the classical economists who accompanied the rise of industrial capitalism. Bankers were assured that by doing what had once been forbidden they were actually doing good for all.
利息的支付是资本主义动力的核心——它调动了闲置财富用于生产性企业。利息将储蓄吸引到新的风险、新的事业中，这些将倍增经济回报。投资与囤积相反——守财奴数着他仓库中的金币，对他人的需求视而不见。投资承诺给财富持有者带来回报，但它也为他人创造新的工作，为一般消费创造更多商品。如果成功，新的事业将产生自己的财富盈余，而这又反馈到增长和积累的过程中。即使考虑到失败和事物磨损的自然折旧，这种循环过程也会使财富倍增，使原始价值复合增长。支付利息的投资将过去与未来联系起来，而银行家是中介。
The payment of interest was the core of the capitalist dynamic—it mobilized idle wealth for productive enterprises. Interest lured savings into new risks, new ventures that would multiply the economic rewards. Investment was the opposite of hoarding, the miser counting gold in his storehouse and oblivious to the needs of others. Investment promised a return to the wealth holder, but it also created new work for others and more goods for general consumption. If successful, the new venture would produce its own surplus of wealth, which, in turn, was fed back into the process of growth and accumulation. Even allowing for failures and the natural depreciation of things wearing out, this recycling process multiplied wealth, compounding the original value. Interest-paying investment linked the past to the future and bankers were the intermediaries.
最终，高利贷的概念被提炼为一个更实际的标准：政治性地禁止毁灭性的利率。资本应得公正的回报，但它并不自由到可以收取会保证借款人失败的通行费。超过一定水平，利息实际上抑制了资本主义进程，产生了停滞和财富的进一步集中，因为债务人失败并将财产丧失给高利贷者。
Eventually, the concept of usury was refined to a more practical standard: a political prohibition against ruinous interest rates. Capital deserved a just return, but it was not free to collect a toll that would guarantee failure for the borrowers. Above a certain level, interest actually depressed the capitalist process and produced stagnation and further concentration of wealth as debtors failed and forfeited their property to the usurious lender.
John Maynard Keynes 本人也曾与这种道德矛盾作斗争。Keynes 强烈反对高利贷和财富与收入的不平等分配，但他仍然颂扬资本倍增的创造性可能性。"复利在二百年间的力量，"Keynes 写道，"足以让想象力震惊。"1930 年，在全球大萧条的最低谷，Keynes 写了一篇题为《我们后代的经济可能性》的预言性文章，它预言了人类的光明未来，这要归功于科学的节省劳动力的发明和复利的推动力。在日益加深的绝望中，Keynes 能够看到资本主义经济体正处于农业和其他技术的巨大突破的边缘——这些进步将极大地倍增生产潜力。
John Maynard Keynes himself wrestled with the moral contradictions. Amid the gathering despair, Keynes was able to see that the capitalist economies were on the brink of vast breakthroughs in agriculture and other technologies—advances that would dramatically multiply the productive potential.

Dense mathematical formulas were devoted to describing how x interest-rate increases would produce x restraint among borrowers and buyers—the behavior-modification model of monetary policy. The trouble was, people did not modify their behavior according to the model.
……没有任何程度的去监管和创新能使金融体系逃脱坚定的反通胀货币政策的熊抱。但是，如果经济中的关键参与者认为，凭借他们的聪明才智，他们作为个体能够逃脱，那就会使限制性货币政策更难成功。他们变得愿意承担更大的风险，并将利率推得更高，试图在竞争中胜过彼此和对手。每一次利率上升都使政府在政治上更难贯彻其限制性政策。而私营部门承担的风险越大，美联储的手就越可能被牵制，因为担心引发不可控制的破产浪潮。
Encouraged by expectations of inflation or driven by their own reckless optimism, borrowers were shattering the old assumptions about how much they would pay for money. People kept borrowing even despite the record rates—most dramatically in the early months of 1980 when the prime rate rose toward 20 percent and credit continued to expand explosively. Higher rates had bite, but not nearly as strong as the economists would have predicted. Household consumers were the most cautious, according to Albert Wojnilower’s analysis of the trend. Families were the first to pull back from borrowing at higher rates because they were risking their own well-being. Business executives, a naturally optimistic group, were less prudent than households. Financial managers, in Wojnilower’s judgment, were the most incautious of all—willing to continue lending at prices that some borrowers could not possibly pay. If enough borrowers eventually defaulted, then the generous bankers were in trouble too.
联邦储备不能简单地让鲁莽者自食其果，因为它还须对金融体系的"安全和稳健"负责。如果金融机构，特别是银行，因其自身轻率的放贷而受损，美联储将不得不收拾残局。作为最后贷款人，它应该拯救溺水的游泳者，正如 Wojnilower 所说。"去监管，"他警告道，"就像去掉绳索、深度标记和浮标，将所有安全责任都放在救生员身上。这是一场拿我们经济金融生存来赌博的懦夫游戏。"

用不那么生动的语言来说，许多高级联邦储备官员，包括 Paul Volcker，都部分认同 Wojnilower 的担忧。他们的忧虑从未在公开场合强烈表达过。毕竟，金融去监管在金融界是一个类似于母性的议题，所有美联储官员都祝福这个总体目标。然而，偶尔，Volcker 会暗示他的疑虑。
... no amount of deregulation and innovation can enable a financial system to escape the bear hug of a determinedly anti-inflationary monetary policy. But, if key participants in the economy believe that as a result of their ingenuity they as individuals can escape, they make it much harder for a restrictive monetary policy to succeed. They become willing to take greater risks and to bid up interest rates even higher in an effort to outlast each other and the competition. Every rise in interest rates makes it more politically difficult for the government to press home its restrictive policy. And the greater exposure taken by the private sector, the more the Fed’s hand may be stayed for fear of touching off an uncontrollable wave of bankruptcies.
"我们在过去十五年里投入了大量精力，无论对错……来解放市场，摆脱我们曾经拥有的那种约束，"Volcker 告诉众议院银行业委员会。"……在某种意义上，我们更加依赖于利率作为施加约束的成本因素。我们没有我们曾经拥有的可用约束，而且大多数人认为这值得庆幸。"Volcker 是否将其视为一种祝福？"哦，总的来说，"他回答。"我认为我们需要一些这里的解放。我偶尔会对此感到不安，因为它部分地导致利率高于它们本来的水平。"
The Federal Reserve could not simply let the foolhardy take their lumps, since it was also responsible for “the safety and soundness” of the financial system. If financial institutions, particularly banks, were undermined by their own carefree lending, the Fed would have to pick up the pieces. As lender of last resort, it was supposed to rescue the drowning swimmers, as Wojnilower put it. “Deregulation,” he warned, “is like removing the ropes and depth markers and buoys and putting all the responsibility for safety on the lifeguard. It is a game of chicken with the financial survival of our economy.”23
多年来，去监管问题使 Volcker 和他的前任处于尴尬的政治地位。对管制性截止阀的直言不讳的辩护将是最困难的，考虑到它们受到来自四面八方的攻击——失望的借款人、投资者以及失去存款的金融中介机构。相反，美联储拖延了。它赞同去监管的理念，但敦促在实践中谨慎行事。它尽可能长时间地坚守 Q 条例的各种上限，然后当变革压力变得过大时，一个接一个地放弃它们。
In less colorful terms, many of the senior Federal Reserve officials shared some of Wojnilower’s concern, including Paul Volcker. Their apprehension was never forcefully stated in public. Financial deregulation, after all, was an issue akin to motherhood in the financial community and all Fed officials blessed the general objective. Occasionally, however, Volcker would hint at his misgivings.
如果美联储是大银行的囊中之物，那么为什么在金融去监管上抵制它们？在许多方面，联邦储备确实像政治学家对"被俘获"监管机构的定义——对受其监管的行业感恩戴德，急于首先服务于其需求。然而，这里有一个对其核心支持群体——大银行——至关重要的问题——金融去监管——而中央银行却朝相反方向拉动，至少在其敢于坚持的时间内。
“We have devoted a lot of effort, rightly or wrongly, over the past fifteen years ... to freeing up the markets from the kind of restraint we once had,” Volcker told the House Banking Committee. “. . . We are more reliant, in a sense, on interest rates as that cost factor exerting restraint. We don’t have the available constraint that we once had and most people count that as a blessing.” Did Volcker regard it as a blessing? “Oh, by and large,” he answered. “I think that we needed some freeing up here. I get restive about it now and then because it comes out partially in interest rates higher than they otherwise would be.”24
解释是，金融去监管直接威胁到联邦储备自身的权力。它削弱了中央银行履行其最基本义务的能力——控制信贷的整体扩张。美联储最深层的机构目的是充当经济的调节者，即防止事情失控的聪明监管者。然而，Volcker 和其他美联储官员怀疑银行体系会自行做到这一点。他们担心，市场不会仅仅通过提高价格来缓和新的贷款步伐。
Over the years, the deregulation question had put Volcker and his predecessors in an awkward position politically. A forthright defense of the regulatory stop valves would have been most difficult, considering that they were under attack from all sides—the disappointed borrowers, the investors and the financial intermediaries that lost their deposits. Instead, the Fed stalled. It endorsed the idea of deregulation, but urged caution in actually doing it. It held the line on the various Reg Q ceilings as long as possible, then one by one discarded them when the pressures for change became too great.
没有供给控制，价格是美联储唯一的杠杆。但如果借款人愿意支付现行利率，无论多么昂贵，什么能阻止私人债务无限扩张——超出美联储的控制，也许远远超出新贷款能够偿还的任何合理预期？去监管给了私人市场决策更多自由，但联邦储备仍然负责维持秩序。如果私人债务负担变得膨胀并导致广泛的违约，过剩将被归咎于做出不可持续承诺的鲁莽贷款人和借款人。但联邦储备本身也将被指控玩忽职守。
If the Fed was in the back pocket of the major banks, then why did it resist them on financial deregulation? In many ways, the Federal Reserve did resemble the political scientist’s definition of a “captive” regulatory agency—one that was beholden to the industry it regulated, anxious to serve its needs first. Yet here was an issue of great importance to the Fed’s core constituency of major banks—financial deregulation—and the central bank pulled the other way, at least as long as it dared.
The explanation was that financial deregulation directly threatened the Federal Reserve’s own power. It undermined the central bank’s ability to deliver on its most basic obligation—to control the overall expansion of credit. The Fed’s deepest institutional purpose was to serve as the economy’s governor, the wise regulator who kept things from getting out of control. Yet Volcker and other Fed officials doubted that the banking system would do this for itself. The marketplace, they feared, would not moderate the pace of new lending simply by raising the price.
Without supply controls, price was the Fed’s only lever. But if borrowers were willing to pay the going rates, however punishing, what was to stop private debt from expanding indefinitely—beyond control of the Fed, perhaps far beyond any reasonable expectation that the new loans could be paid back? Deregulation gave more freedom to the decisions in the private marketplace, but the Federal Reserve was still responsible for maintaining order. If private debt burdens became swollen and widespread failures resulted, the excesses would be attributed to the reckless lenders and borrowers who had made the unsustainable commitments. But the Federal Reserve itself would also be accused of dereliction of duty.
