CHAPTER 3A PACT WITH THE DEVIL

PART ONE: THE MONEY QUESTION
The new chairman of the Federal Reserve Board sounded a lot like the old chairman and like the chairman before him. An immutable feature of the American political dialogue was that the Federal Reserve chairman always delivered the strongest, most eloquent sermons against the dangers of inflation. After Paul Volcker took the oath of office on August 6, 1979, he continued the tradition.
联邦储备委员会的新主席听起来和前任主席以及前前任主席如出一辙。美国政治对话中一个永恒不变的特征是，联邦储备主席总是就通货膨胀的危险发表最有力、最雄辩的布道。1979年8月6日，Paul Volcker宣誓就职后，延续了这一传统。
To be sure [Volcker told the House Budget Committee], the impact of inflation is uneven. Those on fixed incomes suffer, while some people who are well positioned—either by clever design or by good luck—do manage to increase their wealth. Even for the fortunate, however, such a result is at best precarious, frequently built on heavy indebtedness or highly speculative investments. . . .
"诚然，"Volcker告诉众议院预算委员会，"通货膨胀的影响是不均衡的。依靠固定收入的人遭受苦难，而一些处于有利位置的人——无论是通过精明的算计还是好运——确实设法增加了财富。然而，即便是对那些幸运者而言，这种结果充其量也是不牢靠的，常常建立在沉重的负债或高度投机的投资之上……"
It is not entirely a coincidence that we can observe in these recent inflationary years a declining tendency in the profitability of investment . . . one estimate indicates that the annual after-tax return on corporate net worth, measured, as it should reasonably be, against the replacement cost of inventories and fixed assets, has averaged 3.8 percent during the 1970s, a period characterized by rapid inflation, as compared to 6.6 percent in the 1960s. . . .
"并非完全巧合的是，我们可以观察到，在近年来的通货膨胀时期，投资盈利能力呈下降趋势……一项估算表明，公司净值的年度税后回报率——按照合理的方式，以存货和固定资产的重置成本衡量——在1970年代平均为3.8%，而这一时期的特征是高速通货膨胀，相比之下，1960年代则为6.6%……"
In other areas, inflationary expectations are reflected in a diversion of energies into essentially speculative activities—ranging from the “froth” of investing in art objects to the considered purchase, at the expense of heavy indebtedness, of larger or second homes as an inflation hedge.
"在其他领域，通货膨胀预期表现为精力向本质上的投机活动转移——从投资艺术品的'泡沫'，到以沉重负债为代价，审慎购买更大或第二套住房作为通货膨胀对冲。"
The public, if it was listening, was not persuaded. People kept borrowing and buying. The inflation rate was hovering around 14 percent. Consumer debt, the loans to buy cars and houses and other items, was growing faster than consumer incomes, approaching a historic peak. The prospect of continuing inflation seemed more reliable than any political promises to stop it.
公众即便在听，也未被说服。人们持续借贷和购买。通货膨胀率徘徊在14%左右。消费者债务——用于购买汽车、房屋和其他物品的贷款——增长速度超过消费者收入，接近历史峰值。持续通货膨胀的前景似乎比任何阻止它的政治承诺都更可靠。
Volcker appreciated the public skepticism. “We’ve lost that euphoria that we had fifteen years ago, that we knew all the answers to managing the economy,” he conceded. As he made the rounds of public speeches and testimony before congressional committees, he defined the problem in two parts: first, the tangible effects of inflationary pressures on the economy and, second, the psychological momentum of “inflationary expectations.” As long as people believed that rising prices were inevitable, then they would be. Labor unions would demand escalator contracts that protected their members’ real wages from erosion. Retailers would try to anticipate next month’s rising costs by raising this month’s prices. Producers would postpone new capital investment. Creditors would demand higher interest rates. In the long run, Volcker’s central challenge was to convince everyone they were wrong.
Volcker理解公众的怀疑。"我们已经失去了十五年前的那种 euphoria，那种我们掌握管理经济全部答案的感觉，"他承认。在他进行公开演讲和国会委员会听证时，他将问题分为两部分：第一，通货膨胀压力对经济的具体影响；第二，"通货膨胀预期"的心理 momentum。只要人们相信价格上涨不可避免，那么它就真的不可避免。工会会要求签订 escalator 合同，以保护其成员的实际工资不受侵蚀。零售商会试图通过提高本月价格来预期下月上涨的成本。生产商会推迟新的资本投资。债权人会要求更高的利率。从长远来看，Volcker的核心挑战是说服所有人他们是错的。
“For its part,” Volcker assured audiences, “the Federal Reserve intends to continue its efforts to restrain the growth of money and credit, a growth that in recent months has been excessive in terms of our own 1979 objectives.”1 The Federal Open Market Committee set annual target ranges for the rate of money growth it expected to allow. For M-l, the basic aggregate of spending money, the Fed wanted growth of only 1.5 to 4.5 percent in 1979. But, for the last three months, money had been growing at an annual rate of more than 10 percent—nearly three times higher than the upper limit the Fed had set for M-l growth.
"就其本身而言，"Volcker向听众保证，"联邦储备打算继续努力抑制货币和信贷的增长，近几个月的这种增长已超出了我们1979年自身的目标。"1 联邦公开市场委员会设定了其预期允许的货币增长率的年度目标区间。对于 M-1，即基本支出货币总量，Fed 希望在1979年仅有1.5%到4.5%的增长。但在过去三个月里，货币的年增长率超过了10%——几乎是 Fed 为 M-1 增长设定的上限的三倍。
Bland as Volcker’s remarks might have sounded to outsiders, for financial markets his words translated into a clear signal: he intended to tighten down, holding back the infusion of new reserves until— gradually—the rate of money growth subsided and came back within the target range. This meant raising interest rates—perhaps a lot.
尽管 Volcker 的言论在外人听来平淡无奇，但对金融市场而言，他的话转化为一个清晰的信号：他打算收紧政策，抑制新储备的注入，直到——逐步地——货币增长率下降并回到目标区间内。这意味着提高利率——可能大幅提高。
The question was how far Volcker was willing to push up rates. At the second board meeting where he presided as chairman, on August 16, the seven governors unanimously accepted a historic milestone, which in other circumstances might have jarred the markets. They raised the Discount rate from 10 to 10.5 percent—the highest level in the sixty-five-year history of the Federal Reserve System. The Discount rate applied to the hundreds of millions in daily loans that the twelve Federal Reserve Banks routinely made to commercial banks that were temporarily short of reserves. The Discount rate was the floor price of short-term credit. When the governors raised it, that meant other short-term interest rates would be going up too.
问题在于 Volcker 愿意将利率推高到何种程度。在他主持的第二场董事会会议上，8月16日，七位理事一致通过了一个历史性的里程碑，在别的情况下这可能会震动市场。他们将贴现率从10%提高到10.5%——这是联邦储备体系六十五年历史上的最高水平。贴现率适用于十二家联邦储备银行通常向暂时缺乏储备的商业银行提供的数亿美元的每日贷款。贴现率是短期信贷的最低价格。当理事们提高贴现率时，这意味着其他短期利率也会随之上升。
The financial markets were barely stirred by the action because they were expecting much more from the Hew chairman. The governors themselves were divided, however, on how tough the Fed should get. At two successive board meetings in early September, Volcker proposed additional increases in the Discount rate, but had to back off because it became clear he might not have a majority behind him.
金融市场对此行动几乎没有波澜，因为他们对新主席的期望更高。然而，理事们本身对 Fed 应强硬到何种程度存在分歧。在9月初的连续两次董事会会议上，Volcker 提议进一步增加贴现率，但不得不退缩，因为显然他可能无法获得背后的多数支持。
Most economists, including the Fed’s, were forecasting a recession, but then economists had been expecting one for many months and they had been wrong. Despite the aggravations of inflation, President Carter had presided over one of the longest and most expansive periods of economic growth in postwar history, four years of recovery starting in 1976. In confidential forecasts for the governors, the Federal Reserve senior staff predicted a deteriorating economy in the third quarter, then a recession by the Christmas holidays of 1979. The contraction of economic activity might last as long as nine months, the Fed staff thought.2
大多数经济学家，包括 Fed 的经济学家，都在预测衰退，但经济学家们已经预测了好几个月，而且他们一直错了。尽管通货膨胀带来烦恼，卡特总统主持了战后历史上最长、最 expansive 的经济增长时期之一，即从1976年开始的四年复苏。在对理事们的机密预测中，联邦储备高级职员预测第三季度经济恶化，随后在1979年圣诞假期前后出现衰退。Fed 职员认为，经济活动的收缩可能持续长达九个月。2
If correct, the forecast was a terrible political portent for the President—a shrinking economy leading right up to the 1980 election. Of all the many factors that influenced election returns, the most powerful was the ebb and flow of the national economy. A growing economy that increased real personal income and reduced unemployment rewarded the incumbent party and President; a declining economy threatened them with punishment from voters, regardless of other accomplishments or personal popularity.
如果预测正确，这对总统来说是一个糟糕的政治预兆——经济萎缩恰好赶在1980年大选之前。在影响选举结果的众多因素中，最有力的是国民经济的潮起潮落。一个增加实际个人收入并降低失业率的经济增长会奖励现任政党及其总统；一个衰退的经济则面临选民的惩罚，无论其他成就或个人声望如何。
As with other political issues, timing was everything. Naturally, no President would wish for recession. But, if recession was inevitable, the President might be better off if it occurred sooner rather than later —so the economy would be growing again in time for the campaign season, and the ill effects of the unemployment would be forgotten by voters. Earlier in 1979, at least one adviser, Treasury Under Secretary Anthony M. Solomon, had urged President Carter “to take a recession” now and get it over. Otherwise, Solomon warned, the President would be confronted with both rapid inflation and rising unemployment in the midst of his re-election campaign. Jimmy Carter, ever the optimist, ignored the advice.
与其他政治问题一样，时机决定一切。自然，没有总统会希望出现衰退。但如果衰退不可避免，总统最好让它来得更早而不是更晚——这样经济就能及时在竞选季节恢复增长，失业的不良影响也会被选民遗忘。早在1979年初，至少一位顾问——财政部副部长 Anthony M. Solomon——曾敦促卡特总统现在就"接受衰退"并熬过去。否则，Solomon 警告说，总统将在连任竞选期间同时面临高速通货膨胀和不断上升的失业。永远乐观的 Jimmy Carter 无视了这一建议。
By late summer, the President’s options looked much worse. Lyle E. Gramley, a member of the President’s Council of Economic Advisers and former research director at the Federal Reserve Board, described the bind confronting White House policy makers: “We face a cruel dilemma. If policy actions are taken to offset slow growth and a rising unemployment rate, we will lose some of the gains we expect on inflation. If we tighten budgetary policy to dampen inflation further, we will lower growth and raise unemployment.”3
到了夏末，总统的选择看起来糟糕得多。总统经济顾问委员会成员、前联邦储备委员会研究主任 Lyle E. Gramley 描述了白宫决策者面临的困境："我们面临一个残酷的两难困境。如果采取政策行动来抵消增长放缓和失业率上升，我们将失去在通胀方面预期取得的一些成果。如果我们收紧预算政策以进一步抑制通胀，我们将降低增长并提高失业率。"3
The same trade-off that faced the President’s budget policy confronted the Federal Reserve and its monetary policy. If the Fed tightened credit in order to brake the inflationary spiral, would it also tip the economy into recession? If the Fed failed to tighten, inflation would likely grow worse.
总统预算政策面临的同样取舍也困扰着联邦储备及其货币政策。如果 Fed 收紧信贷以阻止通胀螺旋，它是否也会使经济陷入衰退？如果 Fed 未能收紧，通胀可能会变得更加严重。
In public, Paul Volcker did not sound as gloomy as did the predictions from the Fed staff. Economic adjustments, Volcker suggested, “need not by themselves set in motion a deep or prolonged contraction in activity . . . some of the economic and financial dislocations and imbalances that usually have presaged severe cyclical declines have been avoided . . . [the position] that the economy should grow moderately in 1980 still seems reasonable.”
在公开场合，Paul Volcker 听起来并不像 Fed 职员预测的那样悲观。Volcker 表示，经济调整"本身不一定引发深刻或持久的经济活动收缩……一些通常预示着严重周期性衰退的经济和金融失调与失衡已经被避免……[认为]经济应在1980年温和增长的观点似乎仍然合理。"
In private, Volcker was less sanguine. He was beginning to consider policy options that would provide a sharp brake, not a gradual deceleration, and he shared his thoughts with a very small circle of insiders. Like a cautious politician preparing for a major campaign, Volcker was testing the political climate inside his own institution. If he was going to lead, the chairman first had to be sure that others would follow.
私下里，Volcker 则不那么乐观。他开始考虑能够提供急剧刹车而非逐步减速的政策选项，并与一个非常小的内部圈子分享了他的想法。像一位准备重大战役的谨慎政治家一样，Volcker 正在测试其机构内部的政治气候。如果他要领导，主席首先必须确保其他人会跟随。
Both the Board of Governors and the federal open market committee were divided and drifting. Miller’s weak leadership had produced months of contention and some confusion. Volcker was familiar with the arguments because, as president of the New York Reserve Bank, he had been a regular voting member of the FOMC for the past four years and participated in them. “This is a brand-new chairman,” Coldwell said of him. “Sure, Paul had been in the System a long time . . . but he was still a new chairman. Others who had been supporting Miller hadn’t been weaned away from that position.”
无论是理事会还是联邦公开市场委员会都处于分裂和摇摆之中。Miller 的弱领导导致了数月的争论和混乱。Volcker 对这些争论很熟悉，因为作为纽约储备银行行长，他在过去四年一直是 FOMC 的正式投票成员，并参与了这些争论。"这是一位全新的主席，"Coldwell 这样评价他，"当然，Paul 在体系内已经很久了……但他仍然是一位新主席。那些曾经支持 Miller 的人还没有脱离那个立场。"
Back in the spring, when the inflation rate was taking off, Volcker himself had challenged Miller’s stewardship. Normally, the New York president worked closely with the chairman and tried to support him on policy issues, but at FOMC meetings in March and April Volcker had joined Wallich and Coldwell in formal dissents. The three most senior and respected members were voting against the chairman, pushing him toward a tougher money policy. “That created quite a stir,” Coldwell said. “The three of us had never ganged up on the chairman before, and I think we bent things a little bit. Others became a little disenchanted with the chairman. It became a very fragmented board.”
早在春季，当通胀率开始飙升时，Volcker 本人就曾挑战过 Miller 的领导。通常，纽约行长与主席密切合作，并试图在政策问题上支持他，但在3月和4月的 FOMC 会议上，Volcker 加入了 Wallich 和 Coldwell 的正式异议。三位最资深且最受尊敬的成员投票反对主席，推动他采取更强硬的货币政策。"这在当时引起了不小的骚动，"Coldwell 说，"我们三个以前从未联合起来反对主席，我想我们稍微改变了一些局面。其他人对主席有些失望。董事会变得非常分裂。"
At a distance, the arguments seemed to be over quite small differences, even petty distinctions. The FOMC members often split over whether the Federal Funds rate, the key barometer for credit conditions, should be raised a quarter of 1 percent or not. Chairman Miller supported periodic increases, but they were always quite modest. Miller and his supporters feared that more dramatic action on interest rates would depress sectors like housing and autos, which depended heavily on lending for their sales, and that would lead to a general recession.
表面上看，这些争论似乎只涉及相当微小的差异，甚至是琐碎的区别。FOMC 成员经常为联邦基金利率——信贷状况的关键晴雨表——是否应提高0.25%而分裂。Miller 主席支持定期提高利率，但幅度始终相当温和。Miller 及其支持者担心，对利率采取更激烈的行动将压制住房和汽车等严重依赖贷款销售的行业，从而导致全面衰退。
“They were arguing that the Federal Reserve would be in the position of killing off the whole recovery,” Coldwell said with impatience. “We were only talking about a quarter percent increase—and within the year we would be talking about 2 or 3 percent.”
"他们争论说，联邦储备将会扼杀整个复苏，"Coldwell 不耐烦地说，"我们只是在讨论提升0.25%——而年内我们将要讨论的是2%或3%。"
The internal conflicts crystallized for Volcker on September 18, confronting him with a delicate dilemma. The twelve Reserve Bank presidents came to town for the regular meeting of the Federal Open Market Committee and joined the seven governors at the huge mahogany table in the boardroom. By meeting time, they had all digested the voluminous staff reports on the economy, including the most important one, the so-called “blue book,” which described the policy choices. Through the morning, each governor or president would have a turn to speak, though only five of the bank presidents would have a vote. Usually before a lunch break, the chairman offered a proposition and the twelve members of the FOMC announced their votes.
9月18日，内部冲突在 Volcker 面前 crystallized，使他面临一个微妙的困境。十二位储备银行行长来到镇上参加联邦公开市场委员会例会，与七位理事一起坐在董事会会议室巨大的红木桌旁。到开会时，他们已消化了关于经济的大量员工报告，包括最重要的一份——所谓的"蓝皮书"，其中描述了政策选择。整个上午，每位理事或行长都有机会发言，尽管只有五位银行行长拥有投票权。通常在午休前，主席会提出一项提案，FOMC 的十二名成员宣布他们的投票。
The “blue book” prepared by the senior staff for the September 18 session described the market effects of what the FOMC had authorized six weeks before, in early August: “In response to the System’s tightening actions, short-term interest rates have generally advanced about three quarters to 1.5 percentage points . . . and long-term bond yields have moved up 25 to 45 basis points. The relative sharp response of market interest rates appeared to reflect anticipation of further tightening by the System in the face of continued rapid inflation and monetary growth.” For the last three months, the basic money aggregate, M-l, had grown at an annual rate of 10.6 percent, far above what the Fed itself had set as its goal for the year. Only further tightening would bring down the money-supply growth to within the target range. The financial markets, therefore, expected more of the same—higher interest rates to dampen credit expansion.4
为9月18日会议准备的高级职员"蓝皮书"描述了 FOMC 六周前（8月初）授权行为的市场效应："为响应体系的紧缩行动，短期利率普遍上升了约0.75到1.5个百分点……长期债券收益率上升了25到45个基点。市场利率相对剧烈的反应似乎反映了市场预期体系将在持续高速通货膨胀和货币增长的背景下进一步紧缩。"在过去三个月里，基本货币总量 M-1 的年增长率为10.6%，远高于 Fed 自身设定的年度目标。只有进一步紧缩才能使货币供应增长降至目标区间内。因此，金融市场预期更多相同——更高的利率以抑制信贷扩张。4
The question was: how much higher? The senior staff did not make recommendations, but it offered the details of plausible alternatives— one for easing, one for standing pat and one for tightening. None of these policy alternatives suggested anything more than small changes in the Fed’s posture. According to the minutes, no FOMC member was in favor of easing credit, not when inflation was close to 14 percent and the money supply was far above the target.
问题是：还有多高？高级职员没有提出建议，但提供了可行替代方案的细节——一种是放松，一种是维持现状，一种是收紧。这些政策选择中没有一个暗示 Fed 的立场会有超过小幅度的变化。根据会议记录，没有任何 FOMC 成员赞成放松信贷——在通胀率接近14%、货币供应量远高于目标的情况下。
But some members argued for no change at all. Interest rates had risen substantially in recent months and sooner or later that ought to have an impact on credit expansion and the economy. The cautious members thought the Federal Open Market Committee “should avoid policy actions that might intensify the developing weakness in the economic activity.”
但一些成员主张完全不变。利率近几个月已大幅上升，迟早应对信贷扩张和经济产生影响。谨慎的成员认为，联邦公开市场委员会"应避免可能加剧经济活动正在出现的疲软的政策行动。"
But the majority opted for “slight additional firming,” as the FOMC minutes put it. That meant Alternative C from the “blue book,” which called for raising the Federal Funds rate only .5 percent, from 11.5 to 12 percent, with leeway to go as high as 12.5 percent. That was supposed to slow down the money growth in the coming weeks, but only gradually.
但多数人选择了"轻微进一步收紧"，正如 FOMC 会议记录所述。这意味着"蓝皮书"中的方案 C，该方案仅将联邦基金利率从11.5%提高到12%，并可上浮至12.5%。这预计将在未来几周内减缓货币增长，但只是逐步的。
To get a majority, Volcker had to soften even that small step. The final proposal, adopted as an operating directive for the Open Market Desk in New York, authorized the traders there to shoot for a Federal Funds rate of only 11.75—an increase of only one-quarter of a percent. Given the roaring pressures of inflation, this was hardly more than a nudge.
为获得多数支持，Volcker 不得不软化甚至这一小步。最终提案作为纽约公开市场操作台的运作指令被采纳，授权那里的交易员将联邦基金利率目标设定为仅11.75%——仅提高0.25%。考虑到通胀的汹涌压力，这几乎算不上什么推动。
The vote was 8 to 4, because several committee members thought the policy was too soft. Three entered dissents—Governors Wallich and Coldwell and John Balles, president of the San Francisco Reserve Bank—on the grounds that the Fed should tighten more aggressively. The Federal Funds rate should be pushed up to 12 percent, they argued, or the money supply would continue to expand excessively. One other governor, Emmett J. Rice, dissented in the other direction —insisting that further tightening, even one-quarter of a percent, might be too much for the economy.
投票结果是8比4，因为几位委员会成员认为政策过于软弱。三人提出异议——理事 Wallich 和 Coldwell 以及旧金山储备银行行长 John Balles——理由是 Fed 应更激进地收紧。他们认为，联邦基金利率应被推高至12%，否则货币供应将继续过度扩张。另一位理事 Emmett J. Rice 则提出相反的异议——坚持认为进一步收紧，即使是0.25%，对经济来说也可能太多。
After the FOMC finished its business, the seven governors reconvened in a meeting of the Board of Governors to consider the prerogative it did not share with the Reserve Bank presidents—the power to set the Discount rate, the rate the Fed charged for its short-term liquidity loans to banks. Proposals for increases or decreases were forwarded as recommendations from the boards of directors of the Reserve Banks, but the question was decided only by the seven governors in Washington.
FOMC 完成其事务后，七位理事再次召开理事会会议，审议其不与储备银行行长共享的特权——设定贴现率的权力，即 Fed 为其向银行的短期流动性贷款收取的利率。加息或降息的提案由储备银行董事会推荐提交，但问题仅由华盛顿的七位理事决定。
The Board of Governors was split too, 4 to 3. They voted for the increase—only this time the dissenters were pulling in the other direction. Governors Teeters, Partee and Rice argued together for no change at all. “More time was needed,” they asserted, “to assess current uncertainties in the economic outlook and the impact of increasing restraint . . . over the course of recent months.”5
理事会也同样分裂，4比3。他们投票支持加息——只是这一次异议者朝另一个方向拉动。理事 Teeters、Partee 和 Rice 共同主张不作任何改变。"需要更多时间，"他们断言，"来评估经济前景中当前的不确定性，以及近几个月来不断增加的约束的影响……"5
For the new chairman, the situation resembled a squeeze play— dissenters pulling in opposite directions. One faction insisted the Fed must get tougher with the economy and the other feared that the Fed might already be too tough.
对于新任主席来说，情况类似于 squeeze play——异议者向相反方向拉扯。一派坚称 Fed 必须对经济更强硬，另一派则担心 Fed 可能已经过于强硬。
Vice Chairman Schultz said:
副主席 Schultz 说：
You have to recognize that Nancy Teeters was the most liberal member of the board, a dyed-in-the-wool Keynesian. She had less concern about inflation than any of the others. She continued to believe that a gradualist approach would work. She thought we ought to wait and see after the FOMC action. Chuck [Partee] was kind of on the fence. The inflationary thing was bothering him a great deal, but he wanted to go slowly. Emmett [Rice] came around a little bit later.
"你必须认识到，Nancy Teeters 是董事会中最自由的成员，一个彻头彻尾的凯恩斯主义者。她对通货膨胀的关注比其他任何人都少。她仍然相信渐进主义的方法是可行的。她认为我们应该在 FOMC 行动之后等待观望。Chuck [Partee] 有点骑墙。通货膨胀问题让他非常困扰，但他想慢慢来。Emmett [Rice] 后来稍微转变了。"
Board members used the labels sparingly, but “liberal” and “conservative” implied distinct philosophical differences about the purposes of monetary policy. A liberal like Teeters would focus on the trade-off between inflation and unemployment, between the benefit that higher interest rates might produce in moderating prices and the damage they would do to the real economy, destroying jobs and businesses. Most of the senior economists in the Federal Reserve had been schooled in the same tradition—a sense that they must balance their choices and weigh the costs. Inflation must be resisted, but easier money policy also produced social benefits—full employment, rising incomes, fewer Americans who could not find work.
理事会成员很少使用这些标签，但"自由派"和"保守派"暗示了对货币政策目的的截然不同的哲学差异。像 Teeters 这样的自由派会关注通胀与失业之间的权衡——高利率在平抑价格方面可能带来的好处与它们对实体经济造成的损害（摧毁就业和企业）之间的权衡。联邦储备中的大多数高级经济学家都受过相同传统的教育——即他们必须平衡自己的选择并权衡成本。通胀必须被抵制，但更宽松的货币政也会带来社会效益——充分就业、收入增长、更少的美国人找不到工作。
“I am one of those who came out of graduate school convinced that a little inflation was a good thing,” Governor Partee reflected. “Inflation lubricated the economy and it was better to have low unemployment. People’s incomes would adjust.”
"我是那种从研究生院出来就相信一点通胀是好事的人之一，"Partee 理事回忆说，"通胀润滑了经济，失业率低一点更好。人们的收入会自行调整。"
A conservative like Henry Wallich would argue that there really wasn’t any choice. “I regard inflation as a form of fraud,” Wallich said, much worse than an ordinary swindle because it was the government itself defrauding citizens—wiping out the value of their assets by debasing the nation’s money. From Wallich’s perspective, the Federal Reserve had only one choice—to do its best to stabilize the value of money.
像 Henry Wallich 这样的保守派则会争辩说，实际上根本没有选择。"我认为通胀是一种欺诈形式，"Wallich 说，比普通诈骗要糟糕得多，因为是政府本身在欺骗公民——通过贬低国家的货币来抹去他们的资产价值。从 Wallich 的角度来看，联邦储备只有一个选择——尽最大努力稳定货币的价值。
These underlying tensions collided in the arguments of September 18, as they often did in the Federal Reserve’s deliberations. The Fed was divided between “hawks” and “doves,” code words that entered the language during the war in Vietnam to define the political debate over continuing the war. “Phil Coldwell and Henry Wallich were the hawks and I was getting there pretty fast,” Fred Schultz said. “Paul was for the Discount increase but the strong arguments were from me, Coldwell and Wallich.”
这些潜在的紧张在9月18日的争论中碰撞，正如在联邦储备的 deliberation 中经常发生的那样。Fed 在"鹰派"和"鸽派"之间分裂，这些代码词是在越南战争期间进入语言体系的，用以界定关于是否继续战争的政治辩论。"Phil Coldwell 和 Henry Wallich 是鹰派，而我很快也加入了那个行列，"Fred Schultz 说，"Paul 支持提高贴现率，但最强有力的论据来自我、Coldwell 和 Wallich。"
The cautious, gradual incrementalism of the September decisions was exactly what conservative critics had complained about for years. Politicians might pressure the Fed to proceed slowly, but some academic economists, market analysts and bankers argued that the Fed’s action was always too little and too late. The Federal Funds rate and the Discount rate would be raised, but never sharply enough to make much difference—“always behind the curve,” critics complained. The Fed, they argued, was too worried about keeping interest rates stable, for fear of upsetting the money markets. It should forget about interest rates and, instead, focus directly on the one lever through which it could really control the money supply—the creation of bank reserves.
9月决策的谨慎、渐进的增量主义正是保守派批评者多年来抱怨的。政客可能会施压 Fed 缓慢行事，但一些学术界经济学家、市场分析师和银行家认为，Fed 的行动总是太少、太迟。联邦基金利率和贴现率会被提高，但从未高到足以产生显著差异——"总是落后于曲线，"批评者抱怨说。他们认为，Fed 过于担心维持利率稳定，害怕扰乱货币市场。它应该忘记利率，转而直接关注其真正能够控制货币供应的唯一杠杆——银行储备的创造。
Federal Reserve officials read the newspapers. They followed quite closely the daily chatter from the financial markets, as reported in the “Credit Markets” column of The Wall Street Journal and elsewhere in the financial press. They also read the scholarly critiques published in economic journals, but they listened most diligently to the market talk.
联邦储备官员阅读报纸。他们密切关注金融市场每日的 chatter，如《华尔街日报》的"信贷市场"专栏和其他金融媒体所报道的那样。他们也阅读发表在经济学杂志上的学术评论，但他们最认真地倾听的是市场言论。
Paul Volcker was not especially upset by the closeness of the 4-3 vote on the Discount increase—but the financial markets were. The reactions from Wall Street the next day interpreted it as a defeat for him. Short-term interest rates fell sharply as investors concluded that the Fed under Volcker would be less aggressive than they had expected, that the dissenters would keep him from any future increases.
Paul Volcker 对贴现率提高的4比3投票的接近程度并不特别沮丧——但金融市场却如此。第二天的华尔街反应将其解读为他的一次失败。短期利率急剧下降，因为投资者得出结论，Volcker 领导下的 Fed 将比他们预期的更不激进，异议者将阻止他未来的任何加息。
The 4-3 split, The Wall Street Journal reported, “indicates that Mr. Volcker’s drive for a restrictive monetary policy may encounter increasing opposition.” Wall Street analysts made a practice of reading the dissenting votes at the Fed like a leading indicator: if the governors became evenly divided on their direction, that meant they would not go any further.6
据《华尔街日报》报道，4比3的分裂"表明 Volcker 先生推行限制性货币政策的努力可能遭遇日益增长的反对。"华尔街分析师习惯于将 Fed 的异议票解读为领先指标：如果理事们在方向上平分秋色，那就意味着他们不会再走得更远。6
Moreover, if Wall Street was skeptical about the new chairman’s ability to halt inflation, so was the rest of America.
此外，如果华尔街对新主席抑制通胀的能力持怀疑态度，美国其他地区也是如此。
Jack Brod, owner of Empire Diamond and Gold, a precious-metals exchange on the sixty-sixth floor of the Empire State Building in Manhattan, had to hire a security guard to handle the crowds. He roped off part of the hallway to make the lines more orderly. Brod could still not keep up.
Jack Brod 是帝国钻石与黄金公司的老板，这是一家在曼哈顿帝国大厦66层的贵金属交易所，他不得不雇用一个保安来维持秩序。他用绳索隔开部分走廊，使排队更加有序。Brod 仍然应付不过来。
“I’d get here in the morning and there would be people waiting outside the office in the lobby,” he said. “They took blankets and they slept outside until we opened the door. They brought food and ate their breakfast.”
"我早上到这里，就会有人在外面的办公室里等着，"他说，"他们带着毯子，在外面睡觉直到我们开门。他们带着食物吃早餐。"
The people brought old jewelry and silverware, candelabra, platters, rings, anything that contained gold or silver. A dentist brought a pile of gold inlays and silver fillings he had removed from teeth over five years, a $3,000 sale. Others showed up with fine heirlooms that had been in their families for generations.
人们带来了旧首饰和银器、枝形烛台、大盘子、戒指，任何含有金或银的东西。一位牙医带来了一堆他从牙齿上取下来的金嵌体和银填充物，积累了五年，价值3000美元。其他人则带着家族中传承了几代的精美传家宝出现。
“We were getting calls from all over the world, people wanting to sell silver bars,” Brod said. “I could have bought a hundred thousand ounces of silver in a day if I wanted, but I didn’t want to get stuck with it. The refiners got several months behind. We had to send it to Switzerland where they would process it faster.”7
"我们接到来自世界各地的电话，人们想出售银条，"Brod 说，"如果我愿意，一天之内可以买下十万盎司白银，但我不想被套住。精炼商已经落后了好几个月。我们不得不把它送到瑞士，那里加工更快。"7
The frenzy at Empire Diamond and Gold was repeated in jewelry stores, pawnshops and other exchanges across the country—ignited by the price of gold and silver. Silver had sold for $6 an ounce in January. By August, it rose to $10 and hit $17 in September—nearly tripled in value in nine months. Gold, which sold for $300 in July, was now at $450 and still climbing. In the months ahead, silver prices would triple again, surpassing $50 an ounce, and the price of gold would peak at $875.
帝国钻石与黄金公司的狂热在全国各地的珠宝店、当铺和其他交易所重复上演——由黄金和白银的价格点燃。1月份白银售价为每盎司6美元。到8月，它涨到了10美元，9月达到17美元——九个月内价值几乎翻了三倍。黄金在7月份售价为300美元，现在已涨到450美元，并且仍在攀升。在接下来的几个月里，白银价格将再次翻三倍，超过每盎司50美元，黄金价格将达到875美元的峰值。
Wallace Silversmiths, Inc., which manufactured silverware in Wallingford, Connecticut, would have to lay off more than half of its workers as the price of its basic commodity kept escalating. “We publish a price list to dealers,” said Arthur Bowker, vice president of the company, “but we couldn’t print the price list fast enough. They had to be printed, then mailed to the main store and they were always out of date.”8
位于康涅狄格州沃灵福德的 Wallace Silversmiths, Inc. 生产银器，随着其基本商品价格持续上涨，将不得不裁掉一半以上的工人。"我们向经销商发布价目表，"公司副总裁 Arthur Bowker 说，"但我们印刷价目表的速度跟不上。它们必须被印刷，然后邮寄到主要商店，而它们总是过时的。"8
This was what Paul Volcker called the “froth” of inflationary expectations. In the autumn of 1979, the United States experienced a kind of price frenzy—a flight from its own currency. Americans were in a rush of buying and selling which assumed that dollars would continue to lose more and more of their value. The only safe haven for their wealth—and the opportunity to increase it—lay in tangibles. Buy gold, buy art and antiques, buy real estate. The rush of new buyers drove prices even higher—reinforcing the impression that this was the place to put one’s money. The trick was to buy and sell fast while the price was rising and before it collapsed, as bubbles of speculations always did.
这就是 Paul Volcker 所谓的通胀预期的"泡沫"。1979年秋，美国经历了一种价格狂热——逃离本国货币。美国人陷入买卖狂潮，认为美元将继续失去越来越多的价值。他们财富的唯一避风港——以及增加财富的机会——在于有形资产。购买黄金，购买艺术品和古董，购买房地产。新买家的涌入推动了价格进一步上涨——强化了这是投资之地的印象。诀窍是在价格上涨时快速买卖，并在价格崩溃之前脱手——正如投机泡沫总是会破裂一样。
In the Middle West, the expectations of continuing inflation led otherwise cautious farmers to make serious long-term investments, to buy expensive new farm machinery or another hundred acres of farmland. When farmers borrowed, they used their land as collateral, and as land prices escalated, their collateral grew, expanding their ability to borrow.
在中西部，持续通胀的预期导致原本谨慎的农民进行重大的长期投资，购买昂贵的新农用机械或再买一百英亩农田。农民借贷时以土地为抵押，随着土地价格上涨，他们的抵押品增值，扩大了借贷能力。
“Lenders would come out to the farm,” Jim Clark of Mount Ayr, Iowa, explained, “and they would say, ‘That tractor looks a bit aging.’
"放贷人会来到农场，"来自爱荷华州 Mount Ayr 的 Jim Clark 解释说，"他们会说，'那台拖拉机看起来有点旧了。'""所以农民就会买一台新的。为什么不呢？土地在涨价，农机在涨价。"来自州立大学的农业经济学家给农民同样的建议——增加你的持有量、你的资本投资，以保持领先于通胀。
So a farmer would buy a new one. Why not? Land was going up, machinery was going up.” Farm economists from the state universities gave farmers the same advice—increase your holdings, your capital investment, to stay ahead of inflation.
"他们告诉我们：'把那块地买下来，它不会再像现在这么便宜了，'"Mount Ayr 的另一位居民 Sandy Shields 说。
“They were telling us: ‘Get that land bought, it won’t be as cheap as it is now,’” said Sandy Shields, another resident of Mount Ayr.
"我们没有把钱白给任何人，"当地银行家、Hawkeye Bank and Trust 总裁 Roger Kerndt 坚持说。来自爱荷华州立大学的一位经济学家，Kerndt 说，"告诉人们要加杠杆。经济学家们说，你最好有10比1的杠杆。而你可以做到。人们，包括银行家，都在做他们认为正确的事情。你根据你所看到的情况做决定。几乎没有人认为通胀会继续上升。"
“We didn’t throw money at anybody,” a local banker, Roger Kerndt, president of Hawkeye Bank and Trust, insisted. An economist from Iowa State University, Kerndt said, “was telling people to leverage. Economists were saying you’d better be leveraged 10 to 1. And you could be. People, including bankers, were doing what they thought was the right thing to do. You make your decisions on what you see. There wasn’t hardly a soul who didn’t think inflation would keep going up.”
对于农民来说，增加他们的债务杠杆很容易，只是银行账面上的一个简单问题，正如 Kerndt 所解释的那样。"当他旁边的八十英亩土地挂牌出售时，当然它看起来是一笔好交易，我们就同意了，"这位银行家说，"每个人的记忆都如此短暂。如果一个家伙的财务报表看起来很糟，而他想买地，你只需在他的抵押品上每英亩加一百美元。""9
For farmers, increasing their debt leverage was easy, a simple matter of bookkeeping at the bank, as Kerndt explained. “When that eighty acres went up for sale next to him, of course it looked like a good deal and we went along,” the banker said. “Everybody’s memory was so short. If a guy’s financial statement looked bad and he wanted to buy land, you just add a hundred dollars to the acre on his collateral.”9
在南加州，热点在于住房。在全国范围内，房产价值每月以年率17%的速度增长，但真正引人注目的繁荣是在洛杉矶以南太平洋沿岸的优美城镇和城市。圣地亚哥房地产经纪人协会的会员数量自1975年以来翻了一番。在像 Newport Beach 这样的富裕社区，住房价值在不到五年内增长了100%。10
In southern California, the action was in housing. Across the nation, the value of property was increasing monthly at an annual rate of 17 percent, but the truly spectacular boom was in the splendid towns and cities along the Pacific coast south of Los Angeles. The San Diego Board of Realtors had doubled its membership since 1975. In affluent communities like Newport Beach, housing values increased by 100 percent in less than five years.10
Herbert Young 是洛杉矶储蓄与贷款机构 Gibraltar Financial Corporation 的总裁，他一直以为随着住房抵押贷款利率的持续上升，市场会冷却下来。1985年初，加利福尼亚的抵押贷款利率是9.25%。一年后，它达到了10.75%。到了9月，它超过了12%。
Herbert Young, president of Gibraltar Financial Corporation, a Los Angeles savings and loan, kept thinking the market would cool off as interest rates on home mortgages continued to rise. In the beginning of 1985, the mortgage rate in California was 9.25 percent. A year later it hit 10.75 percent. By September, it was above 12 percent.
"我曾经发誓11.5%会把它钉在棺材里，"Young 说，"但这里的买家贪得无厌。"购房者不是在向后看，而是向前看。"人们通常会回看六个月或九个月前，他们看到较低的利率，并期望这些利率会回归，"Young 解释说，"但这次没有发生。这一次，他们在向前看，看到了更高的利率。"11
“I would have sworn that the 11.5 would put the nail in the coffin,” Young said. “But the buyers out here are voracious.” Instead of looking backward, home buyers were looking forward. “People usually look back to six or nine months ago and they see lower rates and they expect those rates to return,” Young explained. “But that didn’t happen. This time, they were looking ahead and saw higher rates.”11
人们不仅仅是为了居住而购买房屋，甚至也不是作为长期投资。他们购买房屋是为了迅速转卖。Irvine Ranch 是橙县的一个大型住宅开发项目，因一笔交易而闻名：一位买家以87,050美元购买了一套公寓，两周后——在抵押贷款结算完成之前——以117,500美元转手。12
People were not simply buying homes to live in or even as long-term investments. They were buying homes in order to sell them again quickly. The Irvine Ranch, a huge housing development in Orange County, was celebrated for a transaction in which a buyer bought a condominium for $87,050 and resold it two weeks later—before the mortgage closing was completed—for $117,500.12
David Parry 是一位研究旧金山湾区房地产繁荣的经济学家，他无意中听到 Contra Costa County 一个住宅小区里两位买家之间的对话。他们两人总共购买了六条不同街道上的六栋房子。该开发项目的经理承认，其60%的销售额来自于不打算居住在这些房子里的人。
David Parry, an economist studying the real-estate boom in the San Francisco Bay area, overheard a conversation between two buyers in a Contra Costa County subdivision. Between them, they were purchasing six houses on six different streets. The manager of the development conceded that 60 percent of his sales were to people who did not intend to live in the homes.
"你可以在广告中看到投机，"Parry 说，"尚在建设中的房子就被广告转售。在广告中，他们使用了房屋宣传册中的图片。"
“You could see the speculation in the advertisements,” Parry said. “Homes were advertised for resale that hadn’t been built yet. In the ads, they used pictures from the housing brochure.”
像黄金和白银一样，投机者涌入住房增加了对房屋的需求——人为地——并刺激了更多的建设。"建筑商往往相当不成熟，"Parry 说，"他们基于 me-too 心态建设。他们看到有一百栋房子在市场上被建造和购买。他们说，我也要这样做，很快就有了一千栋房子。投机者不代表真实需求，所以他们加剧了供应过剩。"13
Like gold and silver, the rush of speculators into housing increased the demand for homes—artificially—and stimulated more construction. “Builders tend to be rather unsophisticated,” Parry said. “They build on the me-too syndrome. They see a hundred homes being built on the market and bought. They say, me too, and pretty soon there are a thousand homes. The speculators don’t represent real demand, so they exacerbate the oversupply.”13
以不那么明显的方式，同样的扭曲正在蔓延至整个美国经济。不仅是房屋建筑商、白银交易商和农民以通胀预期行事。这些冲动也在驱动大型企业和制造商的决策，以及最大商业银行的贷款政策。尽管经济以温和速度增长，9月份银行总贷款额增长了近22%——几乎是8月份已经很高的信贷扩张率的两倍。建设贷款、住房抵押贷款和农业债务占比很小。大部分新增借贷由商业和工业贷款构成。在三个月的时间里，美国公司在其资产负债表上增加了400亿美元的新负债。14
In less obvious ways, the same distortions were spreading throughout the U.S. economy. It was not just home builders and silver dealers and farmers who acted on inflationary expectations. The impulses were driving the decisions of major businesses and manufacturers and the lending policies of the largest commercial banks. Even though the economy was growing at a modest rate, total bank lending increased by nearly 22 percent in September—nearly double the already high rate of credit expansion that had occurred in August. Construction loans, home mortgages and farm debt were minor shares. Most of the new borrowing was made up of commercial and industrial loans. In a three-month period, U.S. corporations added $40 billion in additional liabilities to their balance sheets.14
这既令人振奋，也有点令人恐惧。随着过度活跃经济的证据不断积累，联邦储备委员会的理事们和高级经济学家们开始重新考虑渐进主义。
It was exhilarating and also a bit frightening. As evidence of the hyperactive economy accumulated, the governors and senior economists of the Federal Reserve Board were having second thoughts about gradualism.
Partee 理事尤其感到震惊。在理事会以4比3的分裂投票提高贴现率的几天后，他去纽约在一个市场分析师会议上发表讲话。"我们的信誉极差，"Partee 后来回忆说，"我遇到了极其充满敌意的问题。我因为没有投票支持提高贴现率而受到谴责。难道我不知道贵金属市场正在疯狂吗？难道我不知道墨西哥比索现在比美元更受欢迎吗？"
Governor Partee was particularly taken aback. A few days after the Board of Governors had split, 4 to 3, on raising the Discount rate, he went to New York to address a meeting of market analysts. “We had extremely poor credibility,” Partee said later. “And I got extremely hostile questions. I was chastised for not voting for the Discount increase. Didn’t I realize the metals markets were going wild? Didn’t I realize the Mexican peso was now favored over the dollar?”
Partee 改变了主意。鹰派是对的。必须采取更多行动。
Partee changed his mind. The hawks were right. Something more had to be done.
Coldwell 理事和其他人一样，接到了来自全国各地未经请求的电话，来自那些自己对局势走向感到担忧的银行家同行们。
Governor Coldwell, among others, was getting unsolicited phone calls from around the country, from fellow bankers who were themselves worried about where things were headed.
"金融市场和银行业正在进行狂野的投机，"Coldwell 说，"一些银行家打电话给我，说，'听着，Phil，事情正在失控。你们必须做点什么。'我说，'我希望你能和其他理事谈谈。'"
“Wild speculation was under way in the financial markets and in banking,” Coldwell said. “Some bankers were calling me, saying, ‘Look, Phil, this thing is getting out of hand. You’ve got to do something.’ I said, ‘I wish you’d talk to some of the other governors.’”
新任副主席也感到震惊。"每天你都有一种感觉，世界正在分崩离析，"Fred Schultz 说，"你在每个市场都能看到。这是真正的逃离货币——人们投资于有形资产、艺术品、珠宝、邮票、黄金。一切有形资产都在增值。这简直就是教科书式的案例。"
The new vice chairman was alarmed too. “Every day you got this sense that the world was coming apart at the seams,” Fred Schultz said. “You saw it in every market. There was a genuine flight from the currency—people investing in tangibles, art, jewelry, stamps, gold. Everything that was tangible was increasing in value. It was just a textbook case.”
七位联邦储备理事中只有一位亲身经历过恶性通货膨胀——价格在一年甚至一个月内翻倍或翻三倍的情况。Henry Wallich 在1920年代初期还是德国的一个小男孩，当时德国货币因恶性通胀变得一文不值，价格几乎每天翻倍，公民们不得不用手推车运输现金。这场经历是一场政治创伤，加上第一次世界大战的失败，动摇了德国社会，为纳粹主义和 Adolf Hitler 的崛起铺平了道路。尽管其他人感到焦虑，Wallich 本人并不真正相信美国处于如此灾难的边缘。即使美国的通胀飙升到20%或25%，那也仍然无法与德国所经历的相提并论。
Only one of the Federal Reserve governors had had personal experience with hyperinflation, when prices double or triple in a single year or even in a single month. Henry Wallich had been a small boy in Germany during the early 1920s when the German currency was rendered worthless by hyperinflation, when prices doubled almost daily and citizens literally had to transport their cash in wheelbarrows. The experience was the political trauma that, coupled with the defeat in World War I, destabilized German society and paved the way for the rise of Nazism and Adolf Hitler. Despite the anxieties of others, Wallich did not himself really believe that America was on the brink of such a disaster. Even if U.S. inflation soared to 20 or 25 percent, that would still not be anything like what Germany had gone through.
"我曾经说过，我从未、从未想过这会在美国发生，"Wallich 俏皮地解释说，"但现在我只说一个'从未'。"
“I used to say that I never, never thought this could happen in the United States,” Wallich explained puckishly. “But now I only say one ‘never.’”
无论数字显示什么，对 Fed 来说，显然它必须采取更多行动。Coldwell 理事将 Fed 在金融体系中的角色比作繁忙十字路口的交警。
Whatever the numbers showed, it seemed obvious to the Fed that it had to do something more. Governor Coldwell compared the Fed’s role in the financial system to a traffic cop at a busy intersection.
"每隔一段时间，"Coldwell 说，"Fed 就会亮起黄灯，人们就像华盛顿的司机对黄灯的反应一样——他们加速冲过十字路口。然后 Fed 不得不亮起红灯，当红灯亮起时，人们会尖叫着停下来——在几个人试图闯红灯之后。"
“Every once in a while,” Coldwell said, “the Fed flicks on the yellow light and people react the way drivers in Washington react to a yellow light—they speed up and get through the intersection. Then the Fed has to flick on the red light, and when it hits red, people come to a screeching stop—after a few of them try to run the light.”
这个比喻描述了当下的时刻。几周前还谨慎而犹豫的理事们现在正在改变主意。Paul Volcker 正在逐个与他们交谈，引导他们走向新的共识。主席说，是时候亮起红灯了。
The metaphor described the moment. Governors who had been cautious and uncertain a few weeks earlier were now changing their minds. Paul Volcker was talking to them one by one, coaxing them toward a new consensus. It was time, the chairman said, to flick on the red light.
这个矮胖秃顶、有着精灵面孔和尖刻舌头的小个子教授最初看起来像一个堂吉诃德式的人物，他 cheerful 地从一个国会听证会到另一个，带着他令人难以置信的论点。没有人质疑 Milton Friedman 的学术才华，但他的想法似乎无可救药地过时了。媒体称他为"知识界的激进分子"。MIT 的 Paul Samuelson，统治经济学的凯恩斯主义正统中的权威，将他比作"一个用箔纸攻击战舰的人"。15
The bald little professor with the elfin face and the tart tongue seemed a quixotic figure at first, as he trudged cheerfully from one congressional hearing to another with his improbable arguments. No one questioned Milton Friedman’s scholarly brilliance, but his ideas seemed hopelessly out-of-date. The press called him an “intellectual radical.” Paul Samuelson of MIT, an eminence in the Keynesian orthodoxy that reigned over economics, likened him to “a man with a foil attacking a battleship.”15
1963年，这位芝加哥大学的经济学家与合作者 Anna J. Schwartz 出版了他职业生涯的代表作《美国货币史，1867-1960》。对专业人士而言，Friedman 的著作是一项令人敬畏的学术成就，一部厚重的巨著，细致入微地追溯了从 Ulysses S. Grant 到 John F. Kennedy 的整个货币、银行和美国货币政策进程。16 对华盛顿的大多数政客来说，Milton Friedman 似乎只是一个有着古怪执念的人物。他不断出现在学术和政治论坛上，宣讲着同一个深刻的保守派布道：主导经济学三十年的凯恩斯主义正统是错误的。不是稍微错了，Friedman 坚持说，而是完全地、根本性地、灾难性地错了。
In 1963, the University of Chicago economist had published with coauthor Anna J. Schwartz the magnum opus of his career, A Monetary History of the United States, 1867-1960. To professionals, Friedman’s work was an awesome piece of scholarship, a dense tome that traced in meticulous detail the entire course of money, banking and U.S. monetary policy from Ulysses S. Grant to John F. Kennedy.16 To most politicians in Washington, Milton Friedman seemed just a figure with an odd obsession. He kept reappearing in academic and political forums, preaching the same profoundly conservative sermon: the Keynesian orthodoxy that had dominated economics for thirty years was wrong. Not just a little wrong, Friedman insisted, but totally, fundamentally, disastrously wrong.
许多人被他好斗的坚持所逗乐；起初很少有人认真对待他的信息。毕竟，Friedman 开始他的传道是在1960年代初期，那是伟大英国经济学家 John Maynard Keynes 的自由派知识继承者在实践上取得胜利的时代。Kennedy 和 Johnson 的民主党政府正在积极运用 Keynes 在大萧条期间阐述的教训——即操纵联邦政府自己的预算以刺激私有经济增长。通过减税或增加联邦支出，或两者兼施，政府可以 augment 私有经济的总需求，即消费者和企业的总支出能力。本质上，政府通过减少税收、提供现金补贴或购买他们必须出售的任何东西——无论是劳动力、铺路水泥还是剩余小麦——来把更多的钱放进人们的口袋里。由华盛顿诱导的增加了的总需求将在私有经济中扩散并倍增。新雇佣的建筑工人买了一辆新车，汽车厂购买更多钢材来制造汽车，钢厂雇佣更多工人。收入和利润的增长刺激了买卖、生产和就业。
Many were bemused by his feisty persistence; few took his message seriously at first. Friedman had begun his proselytizing, after all, in the early 1960s, the era of practical triumph for the liberal intellectual inheritors of John Maynard Keynes, the great British economist. The Democratic Administrations of Kennedy and Johnson were aggressively applying the lessons elaborated by Keynes during the Great Depression—that is, manipulating the federal government’s own budget in order to stimulate growth in the private economy. Either by cutting taxes or increasing federal spending or doing both, the government could* augment the aggregate demand of the private economy, the total spending power of consumers and businesses. In essence, the government put more money in people’s pockets—either by taxing them less or giving them cash subsidies or buying more of whatever they had to sell, whether it was labor or paving cement or surplus wheat. The increased aggregate demand induced by Washington would ripple through the private economy and multiply. The newly hired construction worker bought a new car and the auto factory purchased more steel to make cars and the steel mill hired more workers. Rising incomes and profits stimulated buying and selling, production and employment.
由于政府放出的钱多于其通过税收收入的金额，这一策略自然会在联邦预算中产生暂时性赤字，这始终是保守派的一个警示信号。"新经济学"的自由派设计者令人信服地论证——至少对政治多数派来说有说服力——设计性的赤字具有治疗作用。由联邦财政政策刺激的经济活动增加将足以弥补预算缺口。对商品需求的增加引导财富所有者和企业投资于新工厂，这是资本形成过程，也是经济增长的核心。扩大生产意味着更多的就业和不断增长的个人收入，在社会中广泛分布。其结果是，联邦政府的收入基础——所有纳税的工人和企业——将被永久性地扩大。
Since the government was pumping out more money than it collected in tax revenue, the strategy naturally produced temporary deficits in the federal budget, a source of constant alarm to conservatives. The liberal architects of the “New Economics” argued persuasively— at least persuasively to the political majority—that engineered deficits were therapeutic. The increased economic activity stimulated by federal fiscal policy would more than compensate for the budget shortfall. The increased demand for goods led wealth owners and corporations to invest in new factories, the process of capital formation that was the heart of economic growth. Expanding production meant more jobs and rising personal incomes, broadly distributed in the society. As a consequence, the revenue base of the federal government—all the workers and businesses that pay taxes—would be permanently enlarged.
新经济学奏效了。事实上，它产生了惊人的回报。失业率在 Kennedy 上任时接近7%，随着税收和支出政策的实施开始急剧下降。美国经济进入了一个强劲扩张的长期周期，失业率降至5%以下，然后又降至4%以下，并维持了四年。最终，它降到了仅3.5%，这相当于美国的实际充分就业，每个想要工作的人都有工作。
The New Economics worked. In fact, it produced spectacular returns. The unemployment rate, which was nearly 7 percent when Kennedy took office, began to fall dramatically as the tax-and-spending policies took hold. The American economy entered a long cycle of robust expansion, and unemployment fell below 5 percent, then below 4 percent, where it remained for four years. Eventually, it dropped to only 3.5 percent, the practical equivalent of full employment in America, a job for everyone who wanted to work.
与此同时，Milton Friedman 则在担忧通货膨胀。他经常在国会作证，耐心地向重要的委员会主席们解释为什么他们也应该担心。在当时，这听起来像是一种奇怪的抱怨，甚至有些古怪。消费者价格指数每年以温和的幅度上涨，但直到1965年从未超过2%。富有的债券投资者在通胀率达到1.5%时可能会抱怨，但全国其他地方更 impressed 于普遍的繁荣。然而，Friedman 坚持他的悲观预测。
Milton Friedman, meanwhile, worried about inflation. He testified frequently before Congress and patiently instructed important committee chairmen on why they should worry too. At the time, it seemed an odd complaint, even cranky. The Consumer Price Index rose by modest increments each year, but never by as much as 2 percent through 1965. Wealthy bond investors might grumble when the inflation rate hit 1.5 percent, but the rest of the country was more impressed by the general prosperity. Friedman persisted, nevertheless, with his dire predictions.
作为一个自由意志主义的保守派，Friedman 基本上反对政府以任何形式干预私有市场的自然运作。但他对当时主导政治思维的凯恩斯主义观点有更尖锐的异议。Friedman 坚持认为，财政政策——凯恩斯主义者认为如此重要的预算选择——只能产生短暂的结果。政府对私有经济施加影响的根本来源是其对货币的控制——即由联邦储备制定的货币政策。Friedman 补充说，Fed 对货币供应量的操纵始终是破坏稳定和有害的。
As a libertarian conservative, Friedman objected fundamentally to nearly any form of government intrusion in the natural functionings of the private marketplace. But he had a more pointed quarrel with the Keynesian perspective that then dominated political thinking. Fiscal policy, the budget choices that Keynesians thought so important, produced only ephemeral results, Friedman insisted. The fundamental source of government influence over the private economy was its control of money—the monetary policy made by the Federal Reserve. The Fed’s manipulation of the money supply, Friedman added, was consistently destabilizing and damaging.
对于六十年代的主流经济学家，包括许多在联邦储备工作的人，Friedman 的文章和讲座起初听起来有些古怪。他们承认，他的基本思想实际上并不是新的。Friedman 本质上是在要求政治界放弃所有从 Franklin Roosevelt 新政中学到的东西。他大胆地试图复兴十九世纪的古典经济学说，即对自由市场的信仰和对政府干预的厌恶。这些思想本身曾是多少代经济学家、商人和政治领袖的正统智慧——直到它们被历史事件残酷地否定。
To mainstream economists in the sixties, including many who worked at the Federal Reserve, Friedman’s essays and lectures at first sounded quaint. His basic ideas were not actually new, as they recognized. Friedman was essentially asking the political community to renounce all that it had learned from Franklin Roosevelt’s New Deal. He was audaciously attempting to rehabilitate classical economic doctrines from the nineteenth century, the faith in free markets and an abhorrence of government intrusion. These ideas had themselves been the orthodox wisdom for generations of economists, businessmen and political leaders—until they were brutally discredited by events.
摧毁旧意识形态的重大事件是1929年的崩溃以及随后的大萧条。古典经济学教导说，自由市场总是会寻求并找到自然均衡，一种自我修正的能力，一旦价格和工资下降到足够低，就会复苏生产和就业。在大萧条中，美国经济没有复苏。世界其他地区也没有。年复一年，随着社会苦难加深，大规模失业持续了十多年，对自由市场的普遍信仰被粉碎了。新政或多或少随意地推进了一种新信条：一个活跃的全国性政府必须干预以克服私有企业的缺陷和弱点。这种新观念——政府有责任管理经济——被大萧条的国家创伤 legitimized，并在公众舆论和学术理论中都得到了 embrace。
The seminal events that destroyed the old ideology were the Crash of 1929 and the Great Depression that followed. Classical economics taught that free markets would always seek and find a natural equilibrium, a self-correcting capacity that revived production and employment, once prices and wages fell low enough. In the Great Depression, the American economy did not revive. Neither did the rest of the world’s. Year after year, as the social misery deepened and massive unemployment stretched on for more than a decade, the popular faith in free markets was shattered. Somewhat haphazardly, the New Deal advanced a new creed: an activist national government must intervene to overcome the shortcomings and weaknesses of private enterprise. This new idea—government’s obligation to manage the economy— was legitimized by the national trauma of Depression, embraced both in public opinion and in scholarly theory.
Friedman 对货币供应的过时关注与萧条年代传授的一个伴随教训相冲突。骄傲地称自己为凯恩斯主义者的新一代经济学家从三十年代的经历中得出结论，货币供应不是刺激经济增长的可靠工具。财政政策——税收和支出——要有效得多、直接得多。毕竟，在大萧条年代，国家充斥着过剩的流动性，储蓄过剩。简单地说，问题不是缺钱——问题是人们害怕花钱。如果消费者不买，资本所有者就不会投资新工厂或重新雇佣失业者。每个人都退缩，谨慎和收缩的循环自我强化。正如凯恩斯主义者的陈词滥调所说，试图通过扩大货币供应来振兴经济就像"推一根绳子"。
Friedman’s old-fashioned preoccupation with the money supply collided with a corollary lesson taught by the Depression years. The new generation of economists, who proudly called themselves Keynesians, concluded from the experience of the thirties that the money supply was not a reliable tool for stimulating growth in the economy. Fiscal policy—taxes and spending—was much more potent and direct. After all, the nation was awash with excess liquidity, an overabundance of savings, during the Depression years. The problem, crudely stated, was not a shortage of money—the problem was that people were afraid to spend it. If consumers would not buy, then capital owners would not invest in new factories or rehire the unemployed. Everyone held back, and the cycle of caution and contraction fed on itself. As the Keynesians’ cliche put it, trying to revive the economy by expanding the money supply was like “pushing on a string.”
因此，在这种思想交锋中，Friedman 的著作精彩地重新论证了大萧条的历史记录，并坚称传统的分析是错误的。Friedman 发现，1929年的最初崩溃和实体经济的长期收缩都可能是由联邦储备未能提供足够的货币供应所导致的，而且肯定被其 exacerbate。在经济学家的辩论中，Friedman 的立场被概括为一个新的陈词滥调："货币很重要"。
In this contest of ideas, therefore, Friedman’s book brilliantly reargued the historical record of the Depression and insisted that the conventional analysis was mistaken. Friedman found that both the original collapse of 1929 and the long contraction of the real economy were probably caused and certainly exacerbated by the Federal Reserve’s failure to provide an adequate money supply. In the debates among economists, the Friedman position was summarized with a new cliche: “money matters.”
如果六十年代的美好时光继续下去，所有这些智识交锋对普通美国人的生活不会有多大影响。Milton Friedman 可能仍然默默无闻和孤独，一个活在错误时代的 eccentric 思想家。然而，他在1976年获得了诺贝尔经济学奖。他即使不算名人，也至少被广泛誉为一位先知。聪明的年轻研究生们听着他们的凯恩斯主义教授，然后又听 Friedman 的，一些人开始称自己为"货币主义者"——这个标签被用于他的追随者。华尔街分析师更紧密地关注货币数据——M-1 的每周波动——并得出结论，正如 Friedman 所说的那样，M-1 比变化的联邦预算更可靠地预测经济波动。许多重要银行的高管和企业经济学家也转变了，金融媒体的记者和编辑也是如此。最终，皈依者包括了华盛顿的重要政治人物，甚至自由派政客。
None of this intellectual combat would have counted for much in the lives of ordinary Americans if the good times of the sixties had continued. Milton Friedman might have remained obscure and lonely, an eccentric thinker living in the wrong age. Instead, he won the Nobel Prize for economics in 1976. He became, if not a celebrity, at least widely celebrated as a prophet. Bright young graduate students listened to their Keynesian professors and then to Friedman, and some began calling themselves “monetarists,” the label applied to his disciples. Wall Street analysts followed the money numbers more closely, the weekly fluctuations of M-l, and concluded, just as Friedman had said, that M-l was a more reliable predictor of economic swings than the changing federal budget. Many executives of important banks and corporate economists were converted too, along with reporters and editors in the financial press. Eventually, the converts included important political figures in Washington, even liberal politicians.
从1960年代中期开始，在十多年的时间里，精英意见中发生了一场非凡的转变——一种反改革，崛起挑战凯恩斯主义自由派的霸权。Friedman 是这场运动的领袖，超过任何其他人。每一代人，似乎都从自己的经验中发现新的真理，并抛弃来自过去的竞争性真理。当事件的潮流转变时，集体的智慧也随之转变，有时重新发现前一代人已经遗忘的东西。回溯到1930年代，Keynes 以其一贯的滑稽口吻观察到，政治领袖们在不自觉中是"某个已故经济学家的奴隶"。现在，货币主义者声称，Keynes 本人就是那个已故的经济学家。
Over a decade, starting in the mid-1960s, a remarkable transformation took place in elite opinion—a kind of counter-reformation that rose to challenge the hegemony of the Keynesian liberals. More than anyone else, Friedman was its leader. Each generation, it seemed, discovered new truth from its own experience and discarded competing truths from the past. When the tide of events shifted, the collective wisdom shifted with it, sometimes rediscovering what the previous generation had forgotten. Back in the 1930s, with his usual drollery, Keynes had observed that political leaders were, unaware, “the slaves of some defunct economist.” Now, the monetarists claimed, Keynes himself was the defunct economist.
货币主义的挑战——货币作为经济政策核心因素的新地位——源于一个单一的扰动，即国家面临的新经济创伤——物价上涨的兴起。1966年，当消费者价格指数增长超过3%，为十五年来最陡峭的通胀时，国家感到震惊。到1969年，物价年增长率超过6%。在短暂的衰退之后，Richard Nixon 实施了工资和价格管制，通胀率一度回落，但仍保持在3%左右。到1973年，物价再次快速上涨，CPI 以战后新纪录8.8%的幅度上升。次年，1974年，OPEC 推高了石油价格，物价水平上涨了12.2%。17
The challenge of monetarism, the new respectability of money as a central factor in economic policy, flowed from a single disturbance, a new economic trauma confronting the nation—the rise of price inflation. In 1966, the nation was alarmed when the Consumer Price Index increased more than 3 percent, the steepest inflation in fifteen years. By 1969, the annual rate of price increases was above 6 percent. With a brief recession followed by the wage-and-price controls imposed by Richard Nixon, the inflation rate subsided for a time, but still remained about 3 percent. By 1973, prices were escalating rapidly again and the CPI rose by a new postwar record, 8.8 percent. The following year, 1974, OPEC pushed up oil and the price level rose 12.2 percent.17
预设的补救措施是一场漫长而深刻的衰退，一种由联邦储备的紧缩货币政策引起的经济收缩。随着衰退持续十五个月，失业率上升到9.1%的峰值；国家工业生产萎缩了近15%。18 然而，当经济复苏并恢复增长时，通胀也同样复苏。事实上，混乱变得更加严重：通胀率再次接近两位数，但经济从未恢复到任何接近充分就业的状态。在凯恩斯主义的分析中，这两种痛苦——通胀和高失业——本不应同时发生。
The presumed remedy was a long and deep recession, an economic contraction induced by tight monetary policy from the Federal Reserve. As recession continued for fifteen months, the unemployment rate rose to a peak of 9.1 percent; the nation’s industrial production shrank by nearly 15 percent.18 Yet, when the economy recovered and resumed its growth, so did inflation. Indeed, the disorders got worse: the rate of inflation rose toward double digits again but the economy never returned to anything resembling full employment. In the Keynesian analysis, these twin aggravations—inflation and high unemployment—were not supposed to occur simultaneously.
究竟是什么导致了这种现代通胀螺旋？人们提出了许多解释，事实上，经济学家们给出了令人眼花缭乱的各类不同答案。Lyndon Johnson 被指责在已达最大产能的情况下过度刺激经济——在为印度支那战争增加数百亿美元新支出的同时，联邦预算中还有蓬勃发展的教育、卫生和扶贫方面的伟大社会支出。阿拉伯酋长和 OPEC 被指责导致了1973年及以后的石油价格冲击。工人和工会被指责要求超过其生产力增长的工资增长；企业管理者被指责过于轻易地屈服于劳工的要求，并将其成本转嫁到产品价格上。所有这些分散的因素都与通胀相关，然而这种复杂性并不能令人满意。到底是谁的错？经济学家的标准解释似乎在责备每个人，但又没有专门责备任何人。
What exactly was causing this modern spiral of inflation? Many explanations were proffered, indeed, a bewildering variety of different answers came from the economists. Lyndon Johnson was blamed for overstimulating the economy when it was already at its maximum capacity—adding tens of billions in new spending for the war in Indochina to the federal budget alongside the burgeoning new Great Society spending for education, health and poverty. The Arab sheiks and OPEC were blamed for the oil price shocks of 1973 and later. Workers and labor unions were implicated for demanding wage increases that exceeded the increase in their productivity; corporate managers were accused of giving in too easily to labor’s demands and passing on the cost in the price of their products. All of these diffuse factors were relevant to the inflation, yet the very complexity was unsatisfying. Who exactly was to blame? The standard explanations from economists seemed to blame everyone, a bit, and no one singularly.
Milton Friedman 为这个问题提供了一个更简单的答案：通货膨胀是由联邦储备引起的。
Milton Friedman offered a much simpler answer to the question: inflation was caused by the Federal Reserve.
他经常被引用的学术格言实际上是一个指控："通货膨胀无处不在且在任何地方都是一种货币现象。"翻译过来就是说：如果联邦储备没有向经济提供过多的货币，价格通胀就不会发生。Friedman 说，相反，联邦储备不负责任地来回摇摆——提供过多的货币导致通胀，然后突然收紧货币供应，扼杀经济并导致衰退。
His scholarly dictum, often quoted, was actually an accusation: “Inflation is everywhere and anywhere a monetary phenomenon.” In translation, that meant: price inflation could not have occurred if the Federal Reserve had not provided excessive amounts of money to the economy. Instead, Friedman said, the Federal Reserve swerved back and forth irresponsibly—supplying too much money and causing inflation, then tightening the money supply abruptly, which choked off the economy and produced recession.
他的解决方案是激进的。国会应该剥夺 Fed 的特权独立性，并制定立法指令来指导美国的货币政策。Fed 必须被强制接受一条管理其运作的简单规则：周复一周、月复一月、年复一年，没有偏离，货币供应应以固定速率扩张，基本总量 M-1 大致每年增长3%左右。这样，货币增长将被控制在国民经济的 historical 平均增长率，并将避免剧烈波动。私营企业、金融市场和消费者然后可以依靠一个稳定的未来，而不是在通胀和衰退之间来回摇摆。Friedman 解释说，一条货币规则不会消除商业周期的自然高低，但会使其缓和——并保证政府的干预不会使情况更糟。
His solution was radical. Congress should strip the Fed of its privileged independence and enact legislative instructions for U.S. monetary policy. The Fed must be compelled to accept a simple rule governing its functions: from week to week, month to month, year to year, without deviation, the money supply should be expanded at a fixed rate, somewhere around 3 percent a year for the basic aggregate, M-l. Thus, money growth would be held to the historic average growth rate for the national economy and would avoid the sharp swings. Private business, financial markets and consumers could then count on a stable future, instead of lurching back and forth from inflation to recession. A monetary rule, Friedman explained, would not eliminate the natural highs and lows of the business cycle, but it would moderate them—and guarantee that government intrusion did not make things worse.
Friedman 学说的核心实际上是一种关于人类预期的深度保守的陈述。它用经济学的语言重新阐述了关于 Icarus 和 Daedalus 的古老神话，其中人类试图挣脱其尘世局限的束缚——飞得太靠近太阳——于是悲剧发生。货币主义基于同样的信念，即人类社会如果谦逊地接受自身的局限性，情况会更好。经济抱负不应试图超越过去所积累的历史经济增长平均值。生活所能期望的存在自然限制。如果民主的雄心试图将经济推超这些限制，混乱和无序将随之而来。
The core of Friedman’s doctrine was, in fact, a profoundly conservative statement on human expectations. It reformulated, in the language of economics, the ancient myth of Icarus and Daedalus, in which man tried to escape the bounds of his earthly limitations—flying too close to the sun—and tragedy resulted. Monetarism rested on the same belief, that human society would be better off if it modestly accepted limits for itself. Economic aspirations should not attempt to accomplish more than the historical average of economic growth compiled by the past. There were natural limits to what could be expected from life. If democratic ambitions tried to push the economy beyond those limits, chaos and disorder would follow.
货币主义的药方——一条固定的货币规则——是对通胀不稳定性的一种诱人解决方案，许多人匆忙 embrace 它，但它回避了一个重要问题：如果 Friedman 是正确的，为什么联邦储备自己不遵循这样的规则？Fed 将自己描绘为最致力于稳定价格目标的治理机构。如果固定的货币增长率是显而易见的答案，联邦储备会持续拒绝采用它，这合理吗？
The monetarist prescription, a fixed rule for money, was an alluring solution to the instabilities of inflation, and many rushed to embrace it, but it begged an important question: if Friedman was correct, why didn’t the Federal Reserve follow such a rule itself? The Fed portrayed itself as the governing institution most dedicated to the goal of stable prices. If a fixed rate of money growth was the obvious answer, was it plausible that the Federal Reserve would persistently refuse to adopt it?

The Federal Reserve System is a political institution, and like every political institution, it is seeking to retain its own power, its own decisions, its own prestige. To what end do politicians want to maintain their positions? To what end does a CEO of a business concern want to run that concern for profit? 一条严格的货币规则，如果被忠实遵守，将为它们做出决策。联邦公开市场委员会就不必每隔六到八周以高度保密的方式开会，审议新的经济发展以及应如何回应。
A strict money rule, if faithfully followed, would make their decisions for them. The Federal Open Market Committee would not have to meet every six or eight weeks in deep secrecy to deliberate on the new economic developments and how it should respond to them.

Let’s suppose that over the last thirty years the Federal Reserve System had followed the policies I’ve been recommending [Friedman mused]. There would have been milder business cycles. We would have still had business cycles, but they would have been mild. But all that the Fed would have been doing is making sure that the money supply rose by 3 percent a year.

Friedman 相信，只有公众的 indignation 才能改变这一切。Fed 的官僚 mentality 顽固地致力于保护自己的自由裁量权。"他们是重要人物，"他说，"他们不可能相信，自己因重要而造成了伤害——而且他们通过做职员会做更多的好事。"
In one dimension, Friedman’s conservative critique of the Fed resembled the liberal complaints of the Texas Populist congressman Wright Patman. The Federal Reserve, he contended, assiduously served the interests of one important constituency—the commercial banks—and the political power of the banks was used, in turn, to protect the independent status of the Fed. “If I said to you, ‘Here I’ve got a major sector of the economy in which no enterprise ever fails, no one ever goes broke,’ you would tell me, ‘My God, there must not be any competition there,’” Friedman said. “That’s correct. The banking industry has been a highly protected, sheltered industry. That’s because the banks have been the constituency of the Federal Reserve.”
在他的私人办公室里，一位联邦储备理事以异常激烈的语气说话。"我不明白为什么金融媒体如此倾向于货币主义，"这位理事痛苦地说，"货币主义者是现代经济学的 chiropractors。"
Only public indignation could alter any of this, Friedman believed. The bureaucratic mentality of the Fed was stubbornly devoted to protecting its own discretionary powers. “They are important people,” he said. “It’s impossible for them to believe that they are doing harm by being important—and that they would do more good by being clerks.”
在公开场合，Fed 对其货币主义批评者的蔑视通常被礼貌的技术性论据所掩盖。私下里，联邦储备的大多数高级官员都有同感，即这么多人被 Friedman 的论点"欺骗了"。在他们看来，货币主义者扫除了中央银行的所有复杂性，并轻率地假装它们无关紧要——关于货币本身的定义、公众对货币需求的季节性波动、通过银行体系和实体经济产生回响的外部力量等问题。联邦储备试图为所有这些以及其他对货币供应的影响进行调整。这就是联邦公开市场委员会每隔一个月辩论的本质；这就是为什么他们认为不可能每周、每月提供相同数量的储备而不根据变化进行调整。换句话说，人类社会并不是按照整齐的历史平均值运行的。正如 Henry Wallich 理事所述：
In the privacy of his office, a Federal Reserve governor spoke with uncharacteristic heat in his voice. “I don’t understand why the financial press has such a monetarist bias,” the governor said bitterly. “The monetarists are the chiropractors of modern economics.”
Milton Friedman 的意思是，实体经济中没有需要影响货币政策的波动——没有投资热潮、没有住房热潮、没有石油冲击。我们应该只是提供稳定的货币增长。
In public, the Fed’s contempt for its monetarist critics was usually concealed by polite technical arguments. In private, most of the senior officials of the Federal Reserve shared the sense of frustration that so many were “taken in” by the Friedman argument. In their view, the monetarists swept away all of the complexities of central banking and blithely pretended they did not matter—questions about the definition of money itself, about seasonal fluctuations in the public’s need for money, about the external forces that reverberated through the banking system and the real economy. The Federal Reserve tried to adjust for all these and other influences on the money supply. That was the essence of what the Federal Open Market Committee debated every other month; that was why they thought it was impossible to supply the same amount of reserves each week, each month, without adjusting for change. Human society, in other words, did not proceed by neat historical averages. As Governor Henry Wallich stated:
Fed 觉得它必须平滑储备、调整季节性影响。货币主义者说这些都非必要。货币主义者说："市场将学会适应——那些正确的人将繁荣，那些错误的人将受到惩罚。"我说，"如果你接受这一点，最终你将只剩下那些聪明和富裕的人，其他所有人都将崩溃。"
Milton Friedman implies that there are no fluctuations in the real economy that need affect monetary policy—no investment boom, no housing boom, no oil shocks. We should simply supply a steady growth of money.
然而，Fed 是极其脆弱的。Fed 的经济学家可以对 Friedman 提出的货币规则僵化性提出技术性论据。但过去十五年的现实是无可辩驳的：价格通胀已稳步升级，如果联邦储备没有允许它发生，这一切就不可能发生。
The Fed feels it has to smooth out reserves, adjust for seasonal influences. Monetarists say that none of this is necessary. Monetarists say, “The markets will learn to adjust to this—those who were right will prosper, those who were wrong will be punished.” I say, “If you accept that, eventually you will have only those left who are wise and prosperous and everyone else will be ruined.”
在公开场合，联邦储备官员充满防御性和回避性。私下里，在体系内部，甚至那些鄙视 Friedman 尖刻攻击的理事也不得不承认他的更大论点：从长远来看，无论如何，Fed 至少默许了通货膨胀。无论是通过诚实的错误还是缺乏意志，Fed 提供了比经济实际所需更多的货币。
The Fed was enormously vulnerable, however. Fed economists could make technical arguments against the rigidity of Friedman’s proposal for a monetary rule. In public, Federal Reserve officials were defensive and evasive. I think it was things like the way we financed the Vietnam War, the oil price shocks in the 1970s, the tremendous increase in government regulations on the environment and the workplace. But, you know, if over twenty years inflation accelerates a lot, you have to say that a good bit of the responsibility has to fall ultimately on the conduct of monetary policy.
经济学家们就这两种不同效应相对于 Fed 最初政策行动之间存在多长的滞后时间存在分歧。例如，一些货币主义者坚持认为这种关系相当可预测：货币增长在3到6个月内刺激实体经济更多的产出，但好处将在相对不长时间内被随后的通胀侵蚀，也许九个月或一年后。其他人则认为并非如此简单明了，滞后时间随季节变化，取决于许多其他因素而不仅仅是货币。无论如何，时间上的不匹配使 Fed 在其决策中面临持续的紧张关系：它总能产生短期的愉悦——但代价是以后导致混乱的风险。
The Fed’s diligent management of money was complicated by a fundamental political dilemma—the impact of its actions unfolded unevenly in the real economy, which encouraged hesitation and error. As most economists agreed, the first consequence of a substantial increase in the money supply was positive—a surge in economic activity. The negative impact of excessive money growth would not be felt until much later—increased inflation.
诱惑是显而易见的：联邦公开市场委员会可以提供短期的满足，并希望负面后果不会在长期内显现。正如 Philip Coldwell 所观察到的：
The economists disagreed among themselves over how long these two different effects lagged behind the original policy action by the Fed. Some monetarists, for instance, insisted that the relationship was quite predictable: money growth stimulated more output in the real economy within three to six months, but the benefits would be wiped out relatively soon by the subsequent inflation, perhaps nine months or a year later. Others argued that it was not so neat and simple, that the lag time varied from season to season, depending on many other factors besides money. In any case, the mismatch in timing confronted the Fed with a permanent tension in its decision making: it could always produce pleasure in the short run—but at the risk of causing disorder later on.
在我们的社会中，我们对 governors 没有那么宽容。你在汽车上装一个限速器来限制速度，但有人会找到绕开它的方法，因为他想开得更快。没有哪位总统会对3%的经济增长感到满意，即使你向他解释从长期来看，3%的增长对每个人都是有利的。他会说："我要追求5%的增长，让下一个家伙接受3%吧。"

由多年证据支持的总体指控是，联邦储备过于轻易且过于频繁地向短期愿望屈服。Lyle Gramley 认为，这种行为并非源于白宫或国会对 Fed 的政治压力，而是源于决策者的共同心态。"联邦储备与经济学界共享信念，与公众共享期望，"Gramley 说。

The general indictment, supported by many years of evidence, was that the Federal Reserve had yielded too easily and too often to the short-run aspirations. Lyle Gramley believed that this behavior did not stem from political pressures on the Fed from the White House or Congress, but from the common mind-set of the policy makers. “The Federal Reserve shared the beliefs of the economic profession and the aspirations of the public,” Gramley said.
但联邦储备也受到来自自由派的攻击。在一个失控的通货膨胀时代，保守派的抗议是自然的。
The sober reassessment of attitudes inside the System was not at all apparent to outsiders in the autumn of 1979. As a political institution, the Fed felt embattled, surrounded by critics. To them, the Fed seemed obstinate and arrogant, stubbornly refusing to change its methods despite the accumulated evidence oi failure. “Monetary control remains a marginal concern basically alien to the Federal Reserve bureaucracy’s tradition,” the Shadow Open Market Committee declared. The Shadow Committee was a rump forum for monetarist economists from banking and academia who met regularly to issue withering critiques of the Fed’s performance.19
他们是"硬通货"的传统倡导者，是资本和富裕公民的捍卫者——后者的金融资产正在被通胀侵蚀。然而，奇怪的是，国会中的许多自由派民主党人与货币主义的保守学说找到了共同目标。尽管他们从未声称这个标签，民主党人还是与共和党人一起向 Fed 官员施压，要求他们转向 Friedman 及其盟友所倡导的操作方法——对基本货币总量 M-1 实行严格而稳定的控制。像威斯康星州众议员 Henry Reuss 这样的劳工-自由派民主党人，他接替 Wright Patman 担任众议院银行委员会主席，也推动了货币主义方法。甚至 Patman 本人在其最后几年也被其吸引。
But the Federal Reserve was under attack from liberals too. In an era of runaway inflation, an outcry from conservatives was natural.
"我总是对那些接受货币主义路线的自由派民主党人感到惊讶，"Fed 退休研究主任 Daniel Brill 说，"因为这使他们站在了自己政治立场的错误一边。"
They were the traditional advocates of “hard money,” the defenders of capital and the wealthy citizens whose financial assets were being eroded by inflation. Oddly enough, however, many liberal Democrats in Congress found common cause with the conservative doctrine of monetarism. While they never claimed the label, Democrats joined Republicans in pressuring Fed officials to move toward the operating methods advocated by Friedman and his allies—strict and steady control over the basic money aggregate, M-l. A labor-liberal Democrat like Representative Henry Reuss of Wisconsin, who succeeded Wright Patman as chairman of the House Banking Committee, pushed a monetarist approach. Even Patman himself in his last years was drawn to it.
尽管存在矛盾，国会议员们有一个有吸引力的动机来 embrace 货币主义批评，特别是那些推动了联邦预算大幅扩张的自由派民主党人。如果联邦储备真的对通胀负有责任，那么国会就可以免责。如果稳定价格仅仅是联邦储备谨慎货币管理的问题，那就可以转移对行政和立法部门挥霍无度的财政政策——六十年代和七十年代常年联邦赤字以及不断积累的国家债务——的批评。对于国会中的许多人来说，他们是无辜而别人是罪魁祸首这一安慰性观念助长了他们对 Fed 的 righteous 愤慨。
“I was always amazed at the liberal Democrats who fell for the monetarist line,” said Daniel Brill, the Fed’s retired research director, “because it put them on the wrong side of their own political position.”
国会对货币主义的鼓动产生了一个讽刺性的标志，表明主流政治意见已经发生了多大转变。1978年的《充分就业与平衡增长法案》，更广为人知的是 Humphrey-Hawkins 法案，是凯恩斯主义说服力的最后立法喘息，是参议员 Hubert Humphrey 和其他自由派中坚分子将政府政策重新聚焦于失业工人苦难的一次注定失败的努力。到该法最终颁布时，它已成为一个空洞的象征；其中的自由派内容已被彻底掏空。
Despite the contradiction, members of Congress had an appealing motive for embracing the monetarist critique, especially liberal Democrats who had promoted the great expansion of federal budgets. If the Federal Reserve was actually to blame for inflation, then Congress was absolved. If stable prices were simply a question of careful monetary management by the Federal Reserve, that deflected criticism from the free-spending fiscal policies of the executive and legislative branches—the perennial federal deficits of the sixties and seventies and the accumulating national debt. For many in Congress, the comforting notion that they were innocent and someone else was the culprit fed their righteous indignation toward the Fed.
但 Humphrey-Hawkins 法案包含一项实质性的改革——一项货币主义条款，命令联邦储备公开其对三个货币总量——三个 M——增长的年度目标区间。联邦储备主席将被要求每年两次出现在众议院和参议院银行委员会面前，解释这些目标以及任何偏离情况。这旨在让国会更好地进行监督，确保 Fed 在控制货币供应方面信守承诺。自由派的共同提案人忽略了这种讽刺，但保守派内部人士感到好笑：他们日益崛起的货币主义学说正骑着衰落的 Keynes 正统观念的立法尸体走向胜利。20
The congressional agitation for monetarism produced an ironic token of how much the mainstream political opinion had shifted. The Full Employment and Balanced Growth Act of 1978, better known as the Humphrey-Hawkins Act, was the last legislative gasp of the Keynesian persuasion, a doomed effort by Senator Hubert Humphrey and other liberal stalwarts to refocus government policy on the suffering of jobless workers. By the time the law was finally enacted, it was an empty symbol; the liberal content had been thoroughly eviscerated.
除了 Fed 的外部批评者，敌人也在大门之内。多年来，圣路易斯联邦储备银行已将自己打造成联邦储备体系内货币主义的游击前哨。其研究主任 Homer Jones 是 Friedman 的老师，并招募了一批研究经济学家，热衷于批评华盛顿的"总部"并反对其操作策略。圣路易斯 Fed 的历任行长同样也是联邦公开市场委员会内部的常客——直接向教父们宣扬异端学说。
But the Humphrey-Hawkins Act contained one substantive reform —a monetarist provision ordering the Federal Reserve to make public its annual target ranges for growth in the three monetary aggregates, the three Ms. The Federal Reserve chairman would be required to appear twice a year before House and Senate banking committees to explain these goals and any deviations from the targets. This was intended to give Congress a better handle for oversight, a way to make sure the Fed kept its word on controlling the money supply. Liberal cosponsors ignored the irony, but conservative insiders were amused:
Lawrence K. Roos 是一位商业银行家，在1976年被任命为圣路易斯 Fed 行长之前，曾担任三届圣路易斯民选县行政长官。"当我接受这份工作时，"Roos 说，"我不知道货币主义者和凯恩斯主义者有什么区别，但我迅速接受了基础培训，觉得货币主义方法对我有意义。"

In addition to the Fed’s outside critics, the enemy was also within the gates. Over the years, the St. Louis Federal Reserve Bank had made itself into a kind of guerrilla outpost for monetarism within the Federal Reserve System. Its research director, Homer Jones, was a teacher of Friedman’s and had recruited a staff of research economists eager to critique the “home office” in Washington and oppose its operating strategy. A succession of presidents at the St. Louis Fed were, likewise, regular nettles inside the Federal Open Market Committee—preaching heretical doctrine directly to the church fathers.
相互冲突的哲学具体体现在相互竞争的计算机模型中。自1970年以来，Fed 运作着一个复杂的经济计量模型，试图预测货币政策和其他影响对美国经济的效果。计算机模型本质上是一系列数学方程，以特定方式连接，使它们能够处理众多变量并报告理论结果。如果货币供应每月增长4%，那将如何影响六个月或一年后的就业、价格和利率？该模型通过方程运算数字并打印出答案。华盛顿的联邦储备委员会的计算机模型不断增加其分析 sophistication，最终增长到超过160个方程，包括从石油价格到住房开工的所有内容。圣路易斯 Fed 的货币主义者设计了自己的经济模型——它只包含八个方程。货币比其他任何事情都更重要。在联邦公开市场委员会的每次会议上，中央职员会部分基于其复杂的经济计量模型提供预测。Roos 则从货币主义者的计算机中提出相反的结果。

Roos’s monetarist speeches became predictable features of the FOMC meetings, eliciting occasional snickers and some irritation from his colleagues. He complained that the Fed refused to set long-term goals and stick to them. He criticized the deliberate obfuscation, the bewildering variety of economic yardsticks that other FOMC members used to justify their decisions. “Even though it’s a central bank and central banks love to be secretive and mysterious, I think the Fed should tell the American public where it intends to be in two years, its long-range goals for prices, for output and other things. 对某些理事来说[Coldwell 说]，这真的是一个战斗性问题。每次圣路易斯的人离任时，都有一两位理事来找我说，"看在上帝的份上，我们这次不能再让另一个货币主义者进去了。"我会说，"那么，十二个银行中有一个是货币主义者的有什么问题呢？当然，他们令人讨厌，而且争论得有点太大声了，但那是应该被代表的一种观点。"圣路易斯从未给我造成大问题。我们总能投票击败他。
The conflicting philosophies were concretely reflected in competing computer models. Since 1970, the Fed had operated an elaborate econometric model that attempted to forecast the effects of monetary policy and other influences on the American economy. Computer models were essentially a series of mathematical equations linked together in a fashion that allowed them to work through the myriad of variables and report the theoretical consequences. If the money supply increased by 4 percent a month, how would that alter employment, prices, interest rates six months or a year from now? The model worked the numbers through the equations and printed out answers. The Federal Reserve Board’s computer model in Washington kept adding sophistication to its analysis, growing eventually to more than 160 equations, including everything from oil prices to housing starts. The monetarists at the St. Louis Fed designed their own model of the economy—it contained only eight equations. Money mattered more than anything else. At each meeting of the Federal Open Market Committee, the central staff would provide forecasts based, in part, on its complicated econometric model. Roos delivered contrary results from the monetarists’ computer.
随着时间的推移，这种情况越来越不符合事实。其他储备银行的行长也被吸引到货币主义阵营——Richmond 的 Robert Black、旧金山的 John Balles、亚特兰大的 Monroe Kimbrel，偶尔还有其他人。其他行长没有 Roos 那样教条、那样可预测，但他们也认为 Friedman 的论点令人信服。如果联邦储备不做得更多，它至少应该控制住货币总量。
Governor Philip Coldwell, who like Wallich was a “hawk” on tight money but disdainful of monetarism, was less rankled by Roos’s dissents than some other governors.
"你们这些小小的货币主义小集团，"另一位 FOMC 成员轻蔑地对他们说。然而，他们在场并投票，他们的一方正在变得越来越强大。无论喜不喜欢，新任联邦储备主席将不得不与货币主义者打交道。
For some governors [Coldwell said], this was really a fighting matter. Every time a St. Louis man left office, one or two governors would come to me and say, “By God, we’re not to let another monetarist in there this time.” I’d say, “Well, what’s the matter with having one bank out of twelve that is monetarist? Sure, they’re obnoxious and they argue a bit too loudly, but that is one view that ought to be represented.” St. Louis never created a big problem for me. We could always outvote him.
经济学家竞争理论的丛林对外人来说是最令人生畏的。其他聪明的公民——没有经济学研究生学位——被 intimidate 陷入沉默。他们被迫被动地倾听专家之间的激烈争论，几乎无法理解辩论的用语，更不用说就能做出理智判断哪一方是对的哪一方是错的了。这种无知障碍扭曲了每一个需要经济专业知识的政治问题，用科学抽象概念麻痹了正常的辩论。
Over time, this was less and less true. The presidents of other Reserve Banks were drawn toward the monetarist camp too—Robert Black of Richmond, John Balles of San Francisco, Monroe Kimbrel of Atlanta and occasionally others. The other presidents were less doctrinaire, less predictable than Roos, but they too found the Friedman case compelling. If the Federal Reserve did not do anything else, it at least ought to get control over the money aggregates.
但许多政治经济学问题可以用一种更简单的方式来处理，一种避开经济学家晦涩语言并寻求更广泛答案的替代方法。替代方法是通过历史：美国的过去关于通货膨胀揭示了什么，可能帮助公民理解自己的经历？
“Your little monetarist clique,” another FOMC member said of them contemptuously. Nevertheless, they were present and voting and their side was growing stronger. Like it or not, the new Federal Reserve chairman would have to deal with the monetarists.
在这个问题上，记忆可能具有误导性。每一代人都怀旧地回望过去，想象着一个更简单、更少困扰、当然也更便宜的过去。这种渴望表现在一个著名的俏皮话中："这个国家需要的是五分钱的好雪茄。"现代美国人可以深情地回忆起自己生命中的某个时代，电影票50美分，Hershey 巧克力棒卖一毛钱，一辆家庭用 Chevrolet 不到3000美元。
The thicket of competing theories from economists was most intimidating to outsiders. Otherwise intelligent citizens, who did not have a graduate degree in economics, were bullied into silence. They were compelled to listen passively to the heated arguments among experts, barely able to understand the language of the debate, much less to make a reasoned judgment about which side was right and which was wrong. This barrier of ignorance deformed every political question that required economic expertise, benumbing the normal debate with scientific abstractions.
对低价的怀旧掩盖了通货膨胀的实际历史。在1970年代末期，当物价以二十年来最快的速度上涨时，这并不是最糟糕的时期。1979年的通胀平均超过13%，这令人震惊，但不如1947年通胀率14.4%时那么糟糕，也不如1918年通胀率超过17%时，更不如1864年通胀率达到27%时。
But many questions of the political economy could be approached in a simpler fashion, an alternative approach that skirted the opaque language of the economists and sought broader answers. The alternative approach was through history: what did the American past reveal about inflation that might help citizens understand their own experience?
美国的过去并非平静和 golden。物价通常要么上涨要么下跌，并且在任何一种情况下都可能产生破坏性后果。在整个美国历史上，只有少数几个短暂的时期可以被称为"价格稳定"。每一次这样的 episode 对许多普通公民来说都是灾难性的——物价和收入都太低了。
On this subject, memory could be misleading. Every generation looked backward with nostalgia, imagining a past when life was simpler and less troubled and, certainly, cheaper. The longing was expressed in the famous quip: “What this country needs is a good five-cent cigar.” Modern Americans could remember fondly a time in their own lives when movies cost 50 cents and Hershey bars sold for a dime and a family-sized Chevrolet could be bought for less than $3,000.
如果一个历史学家审视跨越整个美国历史的价格数据，一个明显的模式是可见的。是什么导致了通货膨胀？通胀螺旋的反复出现强烈表明，这些创伤的根本来源不在于经济学而在于政治学——即政府做出的选择，或者更准确地说，是政府拒绝做出的选择。这并非政府深思熟虑地决定点燃价格暴涨的问题，然而周期性的极端通胀爆发显然源于政府的决策。
The nostalgia for lower prices obscured the actual history of inflation. In the late 1970s, when prices were rising at the fastest pace in twenty years, this was not the worst of times. Inflation in 1979 averaged more than 13 percent, which was alarming, but not so bad as 1947 when the inflation rate was 14.4 percent or 1918 when it was more than 17 percent or 1864 when inflation hit 27 percent.
通过美国历史的几十年，价格指数起起落落，通常幅度温和，但有六个 distinct 的时期物价急剧上涨——在美国通胀历史图表上看起来像尖锐的向上 spikes（见附录 A，美国物价从1800年到1986年）。历史学家会问：在这些时期发生了什么导致这些通胀尖峰？
The American past was not placid and golden. Prices were usually either rising or falling and could have damaging consequences either way. In the entire history of the United States, there were only a few brief eras of what might be called “price stability.” Each of those episodes was disastrous for many ordinary citizens—prices and incomes were too low.
第一个是共和国诞生之后，革命战争后的通胀狂热。这发生在收集可靠价格数据之前，但这种创伤仍然以一种口语表达被记住——"不值一个大陆币"。第二次通胀尖峰发生在1813-1814年，当时年轻的共和国与英国进行了另一场战争并赢得了胜利，即1812年战争。第三次也是最剧烈的通胀爆发是在1860年代。Lincoln 担任总统，国家深陷其血腥、代价高昂的内战。
If a historian examined the data on prices across the entire sweep of American history, an obvious pattern was visible. What caused inflation? The recurring experience of inflationary spirals strongly suggested that the underlying source of these traumas lay not in economics but in politics—the choices made by government or, more precisely, choices that the government refused to make. This was not a matter of the government deciding in a deliberate fashion to ignite an explosion of price increases, yet the periodic bursts of extreme inflation clearly flowed from the government’s decisions.
下一次戏剧性的通胀爆发紧接着第一次世界大战。第五次尖峰发生在第二次世界大战结束时。最后一次——当代通胀的开始——与越南战争有关。在除最后两次之外的所有事件中，每一次快速通胀之后都伴随着漫长而痛苦的通货紧缩时期，即价格急剧下跌的时代。现代美国人对通缩并不熟悉，除了那些记得1930年代大萧条经济事实的老年人，但价格下跌产生了更严重版本的痛苦和混乱、银行和企业倒闭、农场止赎、大规模失业和贫困化。
Through the decades of U.S. history, the price index rose and fell, usually by moderate degree, but there were six distinct episodes when prices escalated abruptly—periods that look like sharp upward spikes on a historical chart of U.S. inflation (see Appendix A, U.S. prices from 1800 to 1986). A historian would ask: what happened in those periods to cause these inflationary spikes?
这些简单的历史事实为这个问题提供了一个直接的答案：当国家卷入战争时，恶性通胀就会发生。当然，这并没有解释为什么会这样，并且每次的细节都不同。但所有的通胀 episode 都遵循相同的政府行为模式——它通过花费远超其税收收入的资金来动员国家和经济。在每次事件中，从革命战争到第二次世界大战，从 Lincoln 到 Lyndon Johnson，国家政府都认为自己面临着对国家生存的威胁。在这些情况下，政府做了在其他时期不敢做的事情——它的支出远超其财力，无论它认为赢得战争需要什么，都将如何支付战争费用的问题推到一边。它将在之后处理经济后果。
The first one followed the birth of the Republic, the inflationary frenzy that came after the Revolutionary War. This was before reliable data on prices were collected, but the trauma was still remembered in a colloquial expression—“not worth a Continental.” The second spike of inflation occurred in 1813-1814 after the young nation had fought and won another war with the British, the War of 1812. The third and sharpest explosion of inflation came in the 1860’s. Lincoln was President and the nation was embroiled in its bloody, costly Civil War.
战时政治选择总是没有吸引力的。理论上，政府可以简单地将税收提高到足够支付战争的水平，但这将削弱公众对战争的支持。Lincoln、Wilson、Roosevelt 和 Johnson 都提高了战争税，但他们都没有选择主要通过税收来资助战争。正如 Lyndon Johnson 害怕增税会使美国公众转而反对越南战争冲突一样，Abraham Lincoln 担心额外税收会加深内战中的痛苦和牺牲，进一步激化反对。将 Lincoln 维护联邦的斗争与 Lyndon Johnson 在印度支那注定失败的冒险相提并论似乎很 bizarre，但相关的点是财政后果是相似的。
The next dramatic burst of inflation followed immediately after World War I. A fifth spike occurred at the end of World War II. The final one—the beginning of the contemporary inflation—was associated with the war in Vietnam. In all but the last two instances, each rapid run-up of inflation was followed by long and painful periods of deflation, eras when prices fell drastically. Deflation was unknown to modern Americans, except for those elderly citizens who remembered the economic facts of the Great Depression in the thirties, but falling prices produced a much more severe version of suffering and dislocation, failed banks and businesses, farm foreclosures, widespread unemployment and impoverishment.
对于每一次国家安全危机，联邦政府都通过借款来支付战争费用。它在联邦预算中积累巨额战时赤字，并出售国债来弥补新债务。在 Lincoln 的案例中，解决方案更为直接：国家政府直接印刷新货币，近5亿美元的一种称为"绿背纸币"的新货币，然后花掉它们。
These simple facts of history suggested a straightforward answer to the question: virulent inflation resulted when the country went to war. That did not explain, of course, why this was so, and the details were different in each instance. But all the inflationary episodes followed the same pattern of behavior by the government—it mobilized the country and the economy by spending vastly more money than it collected in taxes. In each case, from the Revolution to World War II, from Lincoln to Lyndon Johnson, the national government saw itself confronted with a threat to national survival. In those circumstances, government did what it would not have dared to do in other times—it spent far beyond its means, whatever it thought was needed to win the war, pushing aside the problem of how to pay for the war. It would deal with the economic consequences afterward.
每次的即时结果都是戏剧性的经济增长和随后的通胀爆炸性爆发。容易看到历史联系，但不容易解释政府的财政行动是如何传递到价格中的。经济学家们就这种联系如何运作存在分歧。一些人指出战后对实体经济的压力。当公民回归和平生活时，他们对国内商品的被压抑需求超过了经济的生产能力。突然的短缺导致了更高的价格。其他人强调货币供应的作用——人为膨胀以适应政府的战时支出，并使资助战争的大规模借贷成为可能。在战后环境中，货币供应相对于经济来说过于充裕，结果就是通货膨胀。像经济学中的许多争议一样，非经济学家没有必要在两种解释中选择一种。两者都可能成立——政府的财政政策和货币政策与过度紧张的经济相互作用，产生了快速上涨的价格尖峰。
The political choices in wartime were always unattractive. In theory, the government could simply raise taxes high enough to pay for the war, but that would undermine popular support for the struggle. Lincoln, Wilson, Roosevelt and Johnson all raised war taxes, but all of them declined to finance the war primarily from tax revenue. Just as Lyndon Johnson feared that a tax increase would turn the American public against the military conflict in Vietnam, Abraham Lincoln worried that additional taxes would deepen the suffering and sacrifice in the Civil War, further aggravating the opposition. It seemed bizarre to equate Lincoln’s struggle to preserve the Union with Lyndon Johnson’s doomed venture in Indochina, but the relevant point was that the financial consequences were similar.
现代通胀螺旋在一个方面是独特的——其前所未有的持续时间——因此更令人费解。当代的价格上涨从1966年的越南战争开始明显可见。然而，与其他事件不同的是，它在几年后并没有停止并消退。通胀持续上升了十多年——即使在和平时期，即使经济疲软时也是如此。1960年代和1970年代的通胀激增不如过去其他时期那样陡峭，但它持续更久，并且比美国过去发生过的任何事情都更为 dramatically 不断加剧。
For every great crisis of national security, the federal government borrowed the money to pay for the wars. It would accumulate huge wartime deficits in the federal budget and sell Treasury bonds to cover the new debt. In Lincoln’s case, the solution was much more direct: the national government simply printed new money, nearly $500 million in a new currency called “greenbacks,” and spent it.
消费者价格指数以1967年的价格为100计算。到1970年，价格指数达到116。到1975年，是161。四年后，是217。在内战期间，价格在短短几年内翻了一番，但 Lincoln 在打仗。这一次，价格水平在不到二十年内几乎翻了三倍。而国家是和平的。
The immediate result in every instance was dramatic economic growth and an explosive burst of inflation afterward. It was easy to see the historical connection, but not so easy to explain how the government’s fiscal actions were transmitted into prices. Economists argued among themselves on how the linkage worked. Some pointed to the postwar pressures on the real economy. When citizens returned to peaceful pursuits, their pent-up demand for domestic goods exceeded the economy’s ability to produce them. The sudden shortages led to higher prices. Others emphasized the role of the money supply— expanded artificially to accommodate the government’s wartime spending and to make possible the massive borrowing that financed it. In the postwar environment, the money supply was simply too ample for the economy and the result was inflation. Like many disputes in economics, it was not necessary for noneconomists to choose one explanation over the other. It was entirely plausible that both were true, that the government’s fiscal policy and its monetary policy interacted with an overexerted economy to produce the spikes of rapid rising prices.
历史问题必须被反过来：现代战后时代有什么不同，产生了这种前所未有的趋势？可能的答案本质上是政治性的。正如经济历史学家 Walt W. Rostow 所解释的那样，世界殖民统治的终结不可避免地给新独立的国家——其中大多数贫穷且不发达——在与包括美国在内的西方工业国家的贸易条件方面带来了稍多一些的杠杆。这意味着较穷的国家可以对其出售给富裕国家的原材料——从铜到咖啡——要求略高一点的价格。这种全球力量新现实的最引人注目的情况当然是原油。进口原材料成本上涨自然推动国内价格向上（尽管大多数欠发达国家仍然处于弱势谈判地位，并且仍然抱怨工业资本主义的剥削）。
The modern spiral of inflation was unique in one respect—its unprecedented duration—and, therefore, more puzzling. The contemporary run-up of prices started visibly with the Vietnam War in 1966. Yet, unlike the other episodes, it did not halt after a few years and subside. Inflation continued upward for more than a decade—even in peacetime, even when the economy was slack. The inflationary surge of the 1960s and 1970s was not as steep as others in the past, but it persisted and compounded more dramatically than anything that had happened before in the American past.
另一个新的政治条件是大萧条仍然鲜活的记忆。第二次世界大战后，两个政党都不愿冒险重复1930年代的苦难，这种集体恐惧将政策制定者推向了相反的方向——追求经济扩张并接受通胀风险。事实上，政府经济管理的一个现代成就是，二战后美国人不再需要忍受另一场毁灭性的通货紧缩——那种总是伴随此前战争的通货紧缩。
The Consumer Price Index was calculated as 100 with 1967 prices. By 1970, the price index was at 116. By 1975, it was 161. Four years later, it was 217. During the Civil War, prices doubled in only a few years, but Lincoln was fighting a war. This time, the price level nearly tripled in less than twenty years. And the nation was at peace.
还有一个根本性的变化：与苏联的冷战。第二次世界大战后，美国经济实际上从未真正回到和平时期的基础。它短暂复员，但迅速重新武装以进行全球斗争。在接下来的三十年里，受与苏联冲突恐惧的驱动，美国为战争做准备。这与美国历史上任何先前的和平时期都不同。武装部队被永久保持在与发动战争相称的兵力水平，并从国防工业部门购买不断增长的现代武器库。政府的冷战国防支出吸收了经济产出的永久份额，增加了就业和收入，但也与其他需求竞争。
The historical question had to be turned around: what was different about the modern postwar era that produced this unprecedented trend? The plausible answers were essentially political. As economic historian Walt W. Rostow has explained, the end of colonial domination in the world inevitably gave the newly independent nations, most of them poor and undeveloped, somewhat more leverage over the terms of trade with the industrial nations of the West, including the United States. This meant the poorer nations could demand somewhat higher prices for the raw commodities, from copper to coffee, that they sold to the wealthy nations. The most spectacular case of this new reality of global power was, of course, crude oil. Higher costs for imported raw materials naturally pushed domestic prices upward (though most less-developed countries remained in weak bargaining positions and still complained of exploitation by industrial capitalism).
在美国保持动员状态的同时，国家也恢复了和平国家的完全国内繁荣——以前所未有的数量生产和消费消费品。典型美国家庭的生活水平达到了一种舒适甚至奢侈的程度，这是前几代人无法梦想的——郊区的 new 住宅、令人眼花缭乱的厨房电器、车库里的 new 汽车，或者也许两辆汽车。一度，现代美国人拥有了两个世界最好的东西：和平的愉悦和战争的经济能量。
Another new political condition was the still-fresh memory of the Great Depression. After World War II, neither political party wanted to risk repeating the misery of the 1930s, and that collective fear pushed policy makers in the opposite direction—pursuing economic expansion and accepting the risk of inflation. In fact, a modern triumph of the government’s economic management was that after World War II Americans did not have to endure another episode of ruinous deflation, the kind that had always followed previous wars.
尽管通胀和其他烦恼，这个时期将正确地被颂扬为美国时代——在社会和经济方面都是一个繁荣进步的非凡时期。战后年代的经济扩张在美国生活中产生了一种全新的富足和公平标准。贫困没有被完全消除，但大大减少，在繁荣时期，社会感到足够自信去解决一些长期存在的不公正，特别是种族歧视。联邦政府追求的雄心勃勃的经济目标当然得到了整个国家的完全认同。大型跨国公司正在将其触角延伸至全球，这是美国新霸权的象征。膨胀的军费开支似乎是同一冲动的自然延伸。美国是世界的新领袖，建造着有史以来最大规模的军火库，在数十个外国领土上预置军队。多年来，美国似乎确实可以同时承担战争与和平。
There was one other fundamental change: the Cold War with the Soviet Union. After World War II, the U.S. economy never actually returned to a peacetime footing. It demobilized briefly but quickly rearmed for global struggle. For the next three decades, driven by fears of conflict with the Soviets, the United States prepared for war. This was unlike any prior era of peacetime in American history. The armed forces were kept permanently at a strength level consistent with war making, and a burgeoning arsenal of modern weapons was purchased from the defense industrial sector. The government’s defense spending for the Cold War absorbed a permanent share of the economy’s output, boosting employment and incomes but also competing with other desires.
但是，随着时间的推移，在两者之间不作选择终于赶上了经济和政府的资产负债表。这不仅仅是联邦赤字变大了；问题比这更复杂。多年来，尽管出现了新的赤字，但国债总量相对于国家经济规模实际上在 shrinking——这是健康的标志。每年预算都在未偿债务总额上增加适度的新赤字，但经济增长速度远超政府借款速度。1945年，在第二次世界大战的大量赤字支出之后，国债占年度国民生产总值的119%。但随着战后经济强劲扩张，负担急剧减轻。到1960年，债务仅占 GNP 的58%。到1969年，已降至40%。1974年，它降到了国家经济产出的35%。
While the U.S remained mobilized, the country also resumed the full domestic prosperity of a nation at peace—producing and consuming consumer goods in unprecedented volume. The standard of living for typical American families reached a level of comfort and even luxury undreamed of by earlier generations, new homes in the suburbs, dazzling appliances in the kitchen, a new car in the garage or perhaps two cars. For a time, modern Americans had the best of both worlds, the pleasures of peace and the economic energy of war.
然后历史趋势被逆转了。1975年，自二战以来第一次，国家债务相对于经济没有下降。新趋势最初是温和的，远不如战时产生的债务那样 dramatic，但一个奇怪的事件转折会加剧它。一个保守派政府将在1980年代掌权，它没有像保守派政府被期望的那样缓和财政失衡，反而加速了这种趋势。政府积累了比以前大得多的赤字，国债增长速度快于经济。到1984年，债务比率将从35%的低点上升到 GNP 的约45%——回到1969年以上的水平。21 换句话说，政府的资产负债表开始类似于通常只在战争时期才被容忍的财政状况。
Notwithstanding inflation and other aggravations, this period would rightly be celebrated as the American era—an extraordinary time of prosperous progress in both social and economic terms. The economic expansion during the postwar years generated an entirely new standard of abundance and equity in American life. Deprivation was not eliminated entirely but greatly reduced and, in prosperous times, the society felt confident enough to address some of its long-standing injustices, racial discrimination in particular. The ambitious economic goals pursued by the federal government were, of course, fully shared by the nation at large. Great multinational corporations were extending their reach around the globe, symbols of America’s new hegemony. The swollen military spending seemed a natural extension of the same impulse. America was the new leader of the world, building the largest arsenal ever imagined, prepositioning American troops in dozens of foreign lands. For many years, it seemed, the United States could indeed afford both war and peace at the same time.
政治辩论没有聚焦于这些数字，但它们一次又一次地回到同一个基本问题——关于国家优先事项的争论。基本选择是在战争与和平之间，这是一个政治价值观的问题，而不是经济理论。据说冷战威胁着美国的生存，因此国防开支必须优先。或者，相反，有人争辩说苏联的威胁被夸大了，军费开支过度，政府的资源应导向国内发展。在这个问题的两边形成了广泛的派别，并为控制权而斗争。但在实际层面，它们之间形成了一种政治共识，一种持续了三十多年的默契理解，横跨保守派和自由派政权，在民主党和共和党领导下皆是如此。政府拒绝在战争动员与和平时期支出之间做出选择。它两者都要。
But, in time, the failure to choose between the two caught up with the economy and the government’s balance sheet. It was not simply that federal deficits got larger; the question was more complicated than that. For years, after all, despite new deficits, the total national debt was actually shrinking in real terms compared to the size of the nation’s economy—a sign of health. Each annual budget added modest new deficits to the total outstanding debt, but the economy was growing much faster than the government’s borrowing. In 1945, following the massive deficit spending of World War II, the national debt was 119 percent of the annual Gross National Product. But the burden was shrinking dramatically as the postwar economy expanded robustly. By 1960, the debt was only 58 percent of the GNP. By 1969, it had fallen to 40 percent. In 1974, it was down to 35 percent of the nation’s economic output.
"经过多年的通货膨胀，"Paul Volcker 在1979年秋季对一群听众说，"长远终于追上了我们。"22
Then the historic trend was reversed. In 1975, for the first time since World War II, the national debt did not decline in relation to the economy. The new trend was mild at first, not nearly as dramatic as the debt generated in wartime, but an odd turn of events would aggravate it. A conservative government would assume power in the 1980s, and instead of moderating the fiscal imbalance as conservative governments were expected to do, it accelerated the trend. The government accumulated deficits much larger than before, and the national debt grew much faster than the economy. By 1984, the debt ratio would rise from a low of 35 percent to about 45 percent of the GNP—back above the level achieved in 1969.21 The government’s balance sheet, in other words, began to resemble the financial conditions normally tolerated only during a war.
这个信息对联邦储备委员会的每位成员来说都很清晰，甚至对那些最近投票反对哪怕适度提高贴现率的三个勉强理事也是如此。在9月下半月，他们对即将到来的衰退的担忧被商务部传来的关于经济产出的新数据所否定。尽管有各种预测，经济并没有陷入收缩；它正在再次加速。
The political debates did not focus on these numbers, but they returned again and again to the same fundamental question—an argument over national priorities. The basic choice was between war and peace, a question of political values, not economic theory. The Cold War, it was said, threatened American survival and, therefore, the defense spending must take precedence. Or, conversely, it was argued that the Soviet threat was exaggerated, that military spending was excessive and the government’s resources should be directed to domestic development. Broad factions formed on both sides of the question and struggled for control. But, in practical terms, a political consensus was fashioned between them, an implicit understanding that endured for more than three decades, through conservative and liberal regimes, under Democrats and Republicans. The government would refuse to choose between war mobilization and peacetime spending. It would do both.
而且，尽管 Fed 通过有节制的加息逐步努力减缓局势，银行体系实际上正在加速放贷。银行信贷以年率超过20%的速度扩张，而且，正如 Fed 官员从担忧的银行家那里听到的那样，大量新增信贷正在流入投机性事业——企业和个人借贷以在价格上涨时购买物品，从黄金、白银到房地产的投机性投资。他们在赌通胀将把价格推得更高。聪明的投机者随后将出售商品或其他有形资产，偿还贷款，并获得巧妙而快速的利润。
“After years of inflation,” Paul Volcker told an audience in the autumn of 1979, “the long run has caught up with us.”22
投机看起来不像是冒险的赌注：总体通胀率接近13%，石油价格以每月超过6%的惊人速度上涨——年通胀率接近80%。黄金在一个月内价格跃升了28%，达到每盎司411美元的新纪录。同期，白银价格上涨了惊人的53%，达到每盎司16.89美元。
The message was clear to every member of the Federal Reserve Board, even to those three reluctant governors who had recently voted against even a modest increase in the Discount rate. In the last half of September, their worries about imminent recession were contradicted by the new data on economic output coming in from the Commerce Department. Despite all the forecasts, the economy wasn’t tipping into a contraction; it was accelerating again.
"1929年的幽灵被我和其他人提起，"Coldwell 理事说，"听着，我们正处于美国走向恶性通货膨胀的边缘。"
And, despite the Fed’s gradual efforts to slow things down with measured increases in interest rates, the banking system was actually accelerating its lending. Bank credit was expanding at an annual rate of more than 20 percent, and, as Fed officials heard from worried bankers, a lot of that new credit was going into speculative ventures —businesses and individuals borrowing in order to buy things on the rising prices, speculative investments from gold and silver to real estate. They were betting that inflation would drive prices much higher. The smart speculator would then sell the commodities or other tangibles, repay the loans and reap a smart, quick profit.
虽然这听起来过于世界末日，但借贷和购买狂热确实类似于经典投机泡沫的可能性——一种在经济史上周期性发生的狂热之一。市场失去与真实价值的联系，陷入一场收购狂欢。无论是股票还是债券、大城市的拐角地块还是佛罗里达未开发的沼泽地，投机泡沫都源于一个信念：买家确信在几天、几周或几个月内，他们将变成卖家，并以利润出售其购买。泡沫最终总会破裂；狂热消退，价格急剧下跌。然后投机者被迫亏损卖出。他们失败，而借钱给他们赌博的银行也会失败。这大致就是1929年华尔街发生的事情，当时金融投机泡沫破裂，股票市场崩溃。
Speculation did not look like a risky bet: the overall inflation rate was near 13 percent and the price of oil was increasing at an alarming rate of more than 6 percent a month—an annual inflation rate of nearly 80 percent. Gold had jumped 28 percent in value in a single month, reaching a new record of $411 an ounce. The price of silver, in the same period, had increased by a staggering 53 percent, up to $16.89 an ounce.
正是在这种焦虑的氛围中，联邦储备主席开始逐个拜访其他理事，尝试他的新想法。在他的任期初期，Volcker 已指示高级职员开始进行改变 Fed 基本操作方法的技术研究，在9月18日理事会4比3投票的尴尬之后，Volcker 更激进地推进了这个想法。他的一些同事感到惊讶。新任主席不仅仅是在倡导改变联邦公开市场委员会调节货币供应的基本方式。他是在提出一种货币主义的解决方案——即 Fed 最尖锐的批评者所倡导的方法。
“The specter of 1929 was raised by me and others,” Governor Cold-well said. “Look, we’re on the verge of going into a hyperinflation in the United States.”
当 Volcker 顺道拜访 Henry Wallich 的办公室时，Wallich 以他礼貌但直率的方式告诉他，他反对主席的提议。Wallich 的办公室，沿着大理石走廊，在 Volcker 办公室的拐角处，与其他办公室相比显得阴暗和凌乱，散乱地堆放着员工论文和书籍，散发着淡淡的雪茄烟味。Wallich 在写字台上一盏低矮的灯下阅读，不断把玩着一根长雪茄。他有时戴绿色眼罩以保护他微弱的视力，那是记账员曾经使用的那种。这位最年长的理事看起来像是一位和蔼可亲的古怪教授，享受着他巢穴的 solitude，一个摆满书籍的关闭书斋。
While that sounded much too apocalyptic, the frenzy of borrowing and buying did resemble the potential for a classic speculative bubble, one of those fevers that has occurred periodically in economic history. The marketplace loses touch with real value and plunges forward in an orgy of acquisition. Whether it is stocks or bonds, corner lots in big cities or undeveloped swampland in Florida, speculative bubbles all derive from one conviction: the buyers are convinced that in a few days or weeks or months they will become sellers and unload their purchase at a profit. Bubbles always collapsed eventually; the fever broke and prices fell drastically. Then speculators were forced to sell at a loss. They failed and so would banks that lent them money to take their gambles. That is approximately what happened to Wall Street in 1929, when the bubble of financial speculation burst and the stock market collapsed.
其他理事的办公室比 Wallich 的更明亮、不那么令人望而生畏，但它们所有——包括 Volcker 的——似乎更适合学术生活而非强大的政府决策者。每个办公室都有一面书架和一个漂亮的黑色大理石壁炉，通常周围摆放着沙发和椅子。通常，董事会主席不会在私下办公室里游说其他理事；联邦储备委员会的 collegial 伦理在其成员之间预设一种礼貌的距离，每个理事应自行决定政策问题，不受其他理事的粗暴压力。Volcker 的一对一访问表明了要点：主席认为这是一个极其重要的问题。
It was in this anxious setting that the Federal Reserve chairman began to call on the other governors, one by one, to try out his new idea. Early in his tenure, Volcker had directed the senior staff to begin technical studies on changing the Fed’s basic operating method, and after the embarrassment of the board’s 4-3 vote on September 18, Volcker pushed the idea more aggressively. Some of his colleagues were taken aback. The new chairman was not just advocating a fundamental change in the way the Federal Open Market Committee regulated the money supply. He was proposing a monetarist solution —the approach advocated by the Fed’s angriest critics.
"这是与魔鬼的契约，"Henry Wallich 警告 Volcker 说。
When Volcker dropped by Henry Wallich’s office, Wallich told him in his polite but blunt manner that he opposed the chairman’s proposition. Wallich’s office, down the marble corridor and around the corner from Volcker’s, was dark and jumbled compared to the others, cluttered with random stacks of staff papers and books, smelling faintly of old cigar smoke. Wallich read by a low lamp on his desk, constantly fondling a long cigar. He sometimes wore a green eyeshade, the kind that bookkeepers once used, to protect his weak vision. The eldest governor resembled a genially eccentric professor, enjoying the solitude of his lair, a shuttered study lined with books.
Wallich 解释了他的反对意见。Wallich 说，联邦公开市场委员会应该大幅收紧货币和信贷，但不能通过采用一种有风险的新操作系统，该系统将制造自己的混乱。货币主义者长期以来敦促 Fed 放弃其对利率水平的 preoccupation，而是仅仅专注于货币供应本身以及 Fed 为银行体系提供的储备水平。然后利率将被允许自动地 response 上升或下降。Wallich 认为这将导致利率的极端波动，从而 destabilize 金融市场和实体经济。
The other governors’ offices were brighter and less forbidding than Wallich’s, but all of them, including Volcker’s, seemed more suited to the academic life than to powerful decision makers of government. Each had a wall of bookshelves and a fireplace handsomely faced in black marble, usually with a sofa and chairs grouped around it. Normally, the board chairman did not lobby the other governors in the privacy of their offices; the collegial ethic of the Federal Reserve Board presumed a polite distance among its members, in which each governor was to decide policy questions on his own, free of heavy-handed pressure from others. Volcker’s one-on-one visits made hi& point: the chairman considered this a question of extraordinary importance.
"如果你让利率自动化，"Wallich 预测说，"你对它们的位置就没有影响力了。只要经济强劲，你就抑制货币供应，利率上升。但是，一旦经济疲弱，你就必须让利率下降。我们首先会得到非常高的利率，然后是非常低的利率，然后又高。是的，你控制了货币供应，但利率变得非常 volatile。"
“It’s a pact with the devil,” Henry Wallich warned Volcker.
"有时候，"Volcker 简洁地回答说，"你必须与魔鬼打交道。"
Wallich explained his objections. The Federal Open Market Committee ought to tighten money and credit dramatically, Wallich said, but not by adopting a risky new operating system that would create its own disorders. The monetarists had long urged the Fed to abandon its preoccupation with the level of interest rates and, instead, concentrate solely on the money supply itself and the level of reserves the Fed provided for the banking system. Interest rates would then be allowed to rise or fall automatically in reaction. Wallich thought this would lead to extreme swings in interest rates that would destabilize the financial markets and the real economy.
当 Volcker 拜访 Philip Coldwell——董事会中另一位最坚定的"鹰派"——时，Coldwell 和 Wallich 一样持否定态度。他几乎不能相信，这位他知道并非真正信徒的新主席竟在认真倡导一种货币主义方案。
“If you make interest rates automatic,” Wallich predicted, “you have no influence over where they are. So long as the economy is strong, you restrain money supply and interest rates go up. But, as soon as the economy weakens, you have to let interest rates go down. We first get very high interest rates, then very low interest rates, then high again. Yes, you control the money supply, but interest rates become very volatile.”
"Paul，我不理解，"Coldwell 说，"你想转向货币主义控制？"Volcker 说是的。"好吧，我对此没有任何同情，"Coldwell 说，"它在某种程度上束缚了我们的手脚。它阻止我们做出政策判断。"Volcker 坚持说他想尝试一下。
“Sometimes,” Volcker replied laconically, “you have to deal with the devil.”
主席本人实际上对于不做任何根本性改变、只是推进 Fed 的正统方法——通过大幅提高利率来收紧信贷——也会感到满意。但主席面临一个内部政治困境：像 Coldwell 和 Wallich 这样的成员愿意甚至渴望推高利率以阻止通胀螺旋。但像 Partee、Teeters 和 Rice 这样的其他人则更不愿意直接投票支持如此激烈的行动。Volcker 能否组建一个可持续的多数派，投票将利率提高两个百分点或更高？并保持在那里？如果需要，FOMC 成员是否有勇气将利率提高三四个百分点？过去，Fed 的政策制定者从未执行过如此 dramatic 的措施。答案似乎显而易见。
When Volcker called on Philip Coldwell, the other most stalwart “hawk” on the board, Coldwell was as negative as Wallich. He could hardly believe that the new chairman, who he knew was not a true believer, was seriously advocating a monetarist scheme.
"我们本可以只是收紧，"Volcker 承认，"但我可能难以让政策收紧到需要的程度。我本可以接受更正统的收紧，但我看到改变我们做事方式参数的价值。"
“Paul, I don’t understand,” Coldwell said. “You want to shift to a monetarist control?” Volcker said that he did. “Well, I don’t have any sympathy for that at all,” Coldwell said. “It kind of binds our hands. It keeps us from making policy judgments.” Volcker insisted he wanted to try it.
货币主义的替代方案为 Volcker 的内部政治困境提供了一个巧妙的解决方案：它将作为一个 veil 掩盖艰难的决定。如果联邦公开市场委员会公开宣布它不再将其政策锚定在利率上，而是锚定在 M-1 的水平上，这将掩饰其操作，并可能转移当利率急剧上升时的公众攻击。Fed 成员可以不诚实地解释说，利率上升归因于"市场压力"。在狭义上，这是正确的，但在更广泛的意义上，是一种回避。推高利率的"市场压力"将直接源于联邦储备自身收紧储备供应的行动。
The chairman would actually have been content himself not to change anything fundamental, but simply to push forward with the Fed’s orthodox approach—tightening credit by pushing interest rates up sharply. But the chairman faced an internal political dilemma: members like Coldwell and Wallich were willing, even eager, to drive up interest rates and halt the inflationary spiral. But others like Partee and Teeters and Rice were much more hesitant to vote directly for such drastic action. Could Volcker assemble a sustainable majority that would vote to raise interest rates two points or much more? And keep them there? Would the FOMC members have the nerve, if it were needed, to push up interest rates by as much as three or four points? Nothing that dramatic had ever been executed by Fed policy makers in the past. The answer seemed obvious.
正如一位储备银行行长所解释的那样：
“We could have just tightened,” Volcker acknowledged, “but I probably would have had trouble getting policy as much tighter as it needed to be. I could have lived with a more orthodox tightening, but I saw some value in just changing the parameters of the way we did things.”
每个人都可以说："看，没有动手。"通过一个自我强加的、半自动化的规则来做这件事，比委员会定期决策要容易。这不仅是对外部压力的感知，也是内部压力。内部来看，没有人真正知道需要多少紧缩才能打破通胀，或者利率实际上必须走多远。
The monetarist alternative offered a clever solution to Volcker’s internal political dilemma: it would serve as a veil that cloaked the tough decisions. If the Federal Open Market Committee declared publicly that it was no longer pegging its policy on interest rates, but on the level of M-l, that would obscure its hand and might deflect the public attacks when interest rates rose sharply. Fed members could explain, disingenuously, that the rising interest rates were attributable to “market pressures.” In a narrow sense, that would be correct, but, in a larger sense, an evasion. The “market pressures” that drove up interest rates would derive directly from the Federal Reserve’s own actions to tighten the supply of reserves.
多年来，为了控制储备，联邦储备间接而非直接地处理三个 M。它没有将政策锚定在储备数量上，而是锚定在联邦基金利率上，即一家银行将其超额储备借给另一家短缺银行时收取的价格。在会议上，联邦公开市场委员会主要聚焦于将这个短期利率保持在什么位置，然后指示纽约的公开市场操作台实现该目标。每天，随着货币市场压力的波动，纽约操作台的 Fed 管理人员会购买或出售证券，注入或撤回储备，以保持联邦基金利率在选定水平。
As a Reserve Bank president explained:
当联邦基金利率被提高时，它向所有参与者发出信贷可用性收紧的信号，因此被预期会阻止进一步的信贷扩张。当银行看到联邦基金利率上升时，它们应该缓和自己的放贷——否则就要在货币市场上付出高昂代价来筹集所需储备。如果银行没有相应反应，联邦公开市场委员会将提高其联邦基金利率目标，并希望银行体系能理解这个信息。
Everyone could say: “Look, no hands.” It was easier to do this with a self-imposed, semiautomatic rule than it would be with periodic decisions by the committee. It wasn’t just the perception of outside pressures, but also inside pressures. Internally, nobody really knew how much tightening would be needed to break inflation or how far interest rates would really have to go.
对货币主义者来说，这是 backwards。他们抱怨说，Fed 采用了一种放任的、间接的方法，这使它不断地在事后对事件做出反应。一种简单、直接的方法会有效得多——直接瞄准其打算提供的储备数量，然后坚持这个目标。
For years, in order to control reserves, the Federal Reserve had dealt with the three Ms obliquely rather than directly. Instead of pegging its policy to the quantity of reserves, it pegged it to the Federal Funds rate, the price that one bank charged when it lent its excess reserves to another bank that was short. In its meetings, the Federal Open Market Committee focused primarily on where to hold this short-term interest rate and then instructed the Open Market Desk in New York to achieve that goal. Each day, as pressures in the money market fluctuated, the Fed managers at the New York desk would buy or sell securities, injecting or withdrawing reserves, in order to keep the Fed Funds rate at the chosen level.
Milton Friedman，这位大师级教师，提供了一个清晰的例子，说明为什么货币主义者认为 Fed 是错误的：
When the Fed Funds rate was raised, it signaled tighter credit availability to all the players and, therefore, was expected to discourage further credit expansion. When banks saw the Fed Funds rate going up, they were supposed to moderate their own lending—or else pay dearly in the money market to raise the required reserves. If the banks did not respond accordingly, the Federal Open Market Committee would move its target for the Fed Funds rate higher and hope that the banking system got the message.
假设我想控制美国生产的汽车数量。有两种方法可以做到。我可以说："我将试图找出人们会在什么价格购买这么多汽车。然后我将把价格钉在那个水平，我会出售所有我想要的汽车。如果我发他们买的比我想象的多，我将提高价格。"
To monetarists, this was backwards. The Fed, they complained, employed a permissive, indirect approach that constantly left it reacting to events after the fact. A simple, straightforward method would be much more effective—directly targeting the quantity of reserves it intended to provide, then sticking to that target.
还有另一种方法。我可以说："嗯，没有钢铁你就造不了汽车。我将限制可用于制造汽车的总钢铁量，然后让汽车价格变成它所能变成的样子。"这两种方法都可以最终限制生产的汽车数量。
Milton Friedman, the master teacher, provided a lucid illustration of why the monetarists thought the Fed was wrong:
货币数量也是如此。限制货币数量的一种方法是说："让我试着弄清楚人们在什么利率水平上愿意持有等于我想要提供的货币数量。"然后我将尝试将利率设定在那个水平。或者我可以说："贷款的原材料是储备——高能货币。需要多少高能货币来支持我想要在市场上看到的货币数量？"我将提供那个数量的高能货币。这就像生产那个数量的粗钢。
Suppose I want to control the number of automobiles produced in the United States. There are two ways in which I can do it. I can say, “I’m going to try to figure out at what price people would buy so many automobiles. Then I’m going to peg the price at that level and I’ll sell all the automobiles I want. If I find they are buying more than I want, I’ll raise the price.”
原则上，如果我知道得足够多，这两种方法将是相同的。我会选择相同的答案。但实践中，我知道得不够多。对你来说很明显，通过限制钢铁量来控制汽车数量比试图猜测价格到底应该是多少要容易得多。我认为在货币市场上也是如此。
Then there’s another way. I can say, “Well, you can’t make automobiles without steel. I will limit the total amount of steel that’s available to build automobiles and then let the price of autos turn out to be whatever it turns out to be.” Those are both ways in which you can ultimately limit the number of automobiles produced.
Friedman 的比喻有一个问题——银行不是汽车公司，货币不是钢铁。货币和信贷的日常扩张或收缩既不像炼钢和卖车那样容易看见和定义，其后果也没有如此 neatly 有限。如果汽车价格突然波动，那会影响汽车销售。如果银行储备的价格突然变化，那会通过经济中的每一笔信贷交易产生震颤，从六个月 CD 到汽车贷款再到新工厂的资本投资。Friedman 的系统可能对银行储备产生更平滑的控制，但它也会在利率中产生可怕的锯齿形波动，因为市场会竞相将货币价格上下 bidding。
Exactly the same is true with respect to the quantity of money. One way to limit the quantity of money is to say, “Let me try to figure at what interest rate people want to hold a quantity of money equal to the amount I want to make available.” Then I will try to set the interest rate at that level. Or I can say, “The raw material of making loans is reserves—high-powered money. How much high-powered money is required to support the quantity of money I want to see out there?” I will provide that quantity of high-powered money. That’s like producing that amount of raw steel.
Fed 像所有中央银行一样，非常关心稳定性。传统上，Fed 官员将其法定使命解读为包括维持"有序市场"的责任。换一种说法，他们认为有义务避免可能 destabilize 庞大而复杂的金融机制的行动。实践中，当 Fed 收紧或放松时，它试图设计出利率的平滑变化，以" nudging "金融市场朝正确方向前进，而不至于使其震惊到歇斯底里的过度反应。货币主义者声称，这种关切反映了 Fed 对一种特殊利益的狭隘依附——华尔街的货币市场交易员。货币主义者抱怨说，Fed 对利率的谨慎管理使市场交易员的生活容易得多，但并不一定服务于更广泛的公共利益。然而，Fed 管理者传统上认为公共利益在于维持稳定的利率，避免可能通过整个经济传递错误信息的剧烈波动。
In principle, if I knew enough, the two approaches would be identical. I’d pick the same answer. But, in practice, I don’t know enough. It would be clear to you that it’s a lot easier to control the number of automobiles by limiting the amount of steel than it is by trying to guess what the hell the price should be. I think the same thing is true in the money market.
但那是过去。Paul Volcker，尽管他在 Fed 官僚机构中作为公开市场交易员接受教育并分享了其对"有序市场"的关切，现在却想实现相反的目标：Volcker 想冲击银行和金融市场，甚至在它们心中注入一点恐惧。如果华尔街已经对 Fed 控制通胀的意志失去信心，他如何说服他们？如果货币市场交易员和愤世嫉俗的投机者相信联邦储备缺乏打破通胀螺旋的决心，什么信息能改变他们的想法？操作方法的突然改变——一种将颠覆金融市场对利率稳定的舒适假设的改变——可以作为一种治疗性的雷霆——一种创造性不确定性的练习。
There was one thing wrong with Friedman’s metaphor—banks aren’t automobile companies, money is not steel. The daily expansion or contraction of money and credit was neither so easily visible and definable as steelmaking and auto selling nor was it so neatly limited in consequence. If the price of autos fluctuated suddenly, that affected auto sales. If the price of bank reserves changed suddenly, that sent tremors through every credit transaction in the economy, from six-month CDs to auto loans to capital investment in new factories. Friedman’s system might produce smoother control over bank reserves, but it would also create horrendous zigzags in interest rates, as markets bid the price of money up and down.
这种方法还有另一个好处[Volcker 解释说]。它向体系注入了不确定性，在那个时候，我认为这是可取的。普遍感觉是联邦储备将保持利率平稳。Fed 可能会提高利率，但市场不会波动。银行可以继续放贷，并且它们总是能够以仅稍高的利率为其贷款融资，而联邦储备不会干预。现在，在这种方法下，存在不确定性。银行必须承担利率对他们不利的风险。
The Fed, like all central banks, cared a lot about stability. Traditionally, Fed officials had interpreted their legal mandate to include a responsibility for maintaining “orderly markets.” Put another way, they felt obligated to avoid actions that might destabilize the vast and complicated machinery of finance. In practice, when the Fed tightened or eased, it tried to engineer smooth changes in interest rates that would “nudge” the financial markets in the right direction without shocking them into hysterical overreactions. Monetarists claimed this concern reflected the Fed’s narrow attachment to one special interest, the money-market traders of Wall Street. The Fed’s cautious management of interest rates made life a lot easier for market traders, the monetarists complained, but did not necessarily serve the larger public interest. Nevertheless, the Fed managers traditionally saw the public interest in maintaining stable interest rates and avoiding wild swings that might send wrong messages through the entire economy.
为了使信息更加尖锐，Volcker 提出了一个辅助性变更，旨在惩戒最激进的银行——对银行为融资其贷款扩张而借入的资金加收8%的额外准备金要求。传统上，典型的银行按照标准的审慎规则，依赖于其免息存款作为发放贷款的资金池；银行是金融中介，使用储户存放的资金来借给需要资金的其他人。因此，银行的贷款扩张受到其吸引额外储户速度的制约。耐心的老传统正在崩溃。
But that was the past. Paul Volcker, though he was schooled as an open-market trader in the Fed bureaucracy and shared its solicitude for “orderly markets,” now wanted to accomplish the opposite: Volcker wanted to shock the banks and financial markets, even put a little fear in their hearts. If Wall Street had lost faith in the Fed’s will to control inflation, how could he convince them? If money-market traders and cynical speculators were persuaded that the Federal Reserve lacked the resolve to break the inflationary spiral, what message would change their minds? An abrupt change in the operating method, one that would disrupt the financial markets’ comfortable assumptions about interest-rate stability, might serve as a therapeutic thunderbolt —an exercise in creative uncertainty.
在过去的二十年里，一种新的 go-go 精神在美国银行家中占据主导，无论是在华尔街还是在雄心勃勃的地区银行中，一种推动增长的创业热情。这种新精神反映在他们的建筑中。银行传统上被设计成类似于希腊神庙，带有大理石柱的阴森 sanctuary，信任的象征。1960年代和1970年代的新银行建筑表达了不同的陈述：它们是高耸的玻璃和闪亮钢铁塔楼，开放而充满动感的结构，传达出大胆和乐观的精神。现代银行建筑反映了内部占据主导的新管理精神。
This approach had another benefit [Volcker explained]. It injected uncertainty into the system, which, at that point, I thought was desirable. There was a general feeling that the Federal Reserve was going to keep interest rates smooth. The Federal Reserve might raise rates, but the market is not going to be volatile. Banks can continue lending and they would always be able to fund their loans at a rate only slightly higher and the Federal Reserve would not intervene. Now, under this approach, there was uncertainty. Banks had to take the risk that interest rates would go against them.
美国银行业发现了一种更激进的经营管理方法，规避了存款增长的传统制约，一种实现更快增长的方法，特别吸引那些最大和最有雄心的银行。银行不再等待新存款，而是继续发放新贷款，然后自己借入巨额资金，实际上在货币市场上"购买"其存款基础来为信贷扩张融资。这种做法被称为"管理负债"，因为常规存款和银行的借款都构成其资产负债表上的负债——它欠别人的钱。相反，银行未偿还的贷款是其资产——它将从别人那里收回的钱（与家庭或商人描述其资产负债表的方式完全相反）。管理负债依赖于银行发行大额金融工具，如100,000美元及更大金额的存单和欧洲美元借款。只要银行能够以低于其放贷利率的利率借入新资金，利差就保证了它们的利润。23
To sharpen the message, Volcker proposed an auxiliary change designed to chasten the most aggressive banks—an added reserve requirement of 8 percent on the funds that banks borrowed to finance their loan expansion. Traditionally, a typical bank, following the standard rules of prudence, depended on its interest-free deposits as the pool of money for making loans; the bank was the financial intermediary that used the money parked by depositors in order to lend to others who needed it. A bank’s expansion of lending was, thus, inhibited by how fast it could attract additional depositors. The old tradition of patience was crumbling.
Volcker 提议的转变将破坏银行对其利率计算的信心。如果它们以13%发放贷款，而短期利率突然大幅上升怎么办？如果它们自己不得不以更高利率借款，就会失去利差。8%的边际准备金要求也会使管理负债的做法对银行更加昂贵。在所有除了最小的银行中，Fed 对支票账户活期存款的准备金要求是16.25%，但对通过大额 CD 或在联邦基金市场或欧洲美元借款筹集的资金，准备金要求低得多或为零。根据 Volcker 的方案，银行将不得不将其借入资金的另外8%作为准备金——存放在其当地联邦储备银行的 sterile 资金，不产生利息。对于银行在货币市场筹集的每100万美元，它必须在 Fed 的一个闲置账户中存8万美元。这不会禁止它们激进放贷，但银行家比其他生物更厌恶闲置资金——不产生利息收入的资金。最重要的是，Volcker 希望新的准备金要求可能会稍微吓到一些更激进的银行，让它们怀疑 Fed 是否准备以后采取更多行动。
Over the last two decades, a new go-go spirit had taken hold among American bankers, both in Wall Street and among ambitious regional banks, a driving entrepreneurial zeal for growth. The new spirit was reflected in their architecture. Banks were traditionally designed to look like Greek temples, dour sanctuaries with marble columns, symbols of trust. The new bank buildings of the 1960s and 1970s made a different statement: they were soaring towers of glass and gleaming steel, open and dynamic structures that conveyed a spirit of boldness and optimism. The modern banking buildings reflected the new managerial spirit that had taken hold inside.
当 Volcker 拜访其他理事时，所有这些要素都被纳入他的更大论点中：如果联邦储备采取足够 dramatic 的行动，那可能会产生重要的心理胜利。如果金融市场相信 Fed 最终准备在控制货币方面变得强硬并保持强硬，那些为了自我保护免受未来通胀影响而在利率中要求"通胀溢价"的投资者可能会改变他们的想法。因此，Volcker 辩称，激烈行动肯定会推高整个贷款谱系的利率，包括短期信贷和长期投资如公司和政府债券。但是，如果市场对遏制通胀的长期前景变得更加乐观，那么长期利率应该回落到更正常的水平。
American banking had discovered a more aggressive approach to management that evaded the traditional inhibitions of deposit growth, a method for achieving faster growth that appealed especially to the largest and most ambitious banks. Instead of waiting for new deposits, the banks would go ahead and make new loans, then turn around and borrow huge sums themselves, in effect “buying” their deposit base in the money market to finance the expansion of credit. The practice was known as “managed liabilities,” since both the regular deposits and the borrowing done by a bank constitute the liabilities on its balance sheet—money it owes to someone else. Conversely, a bank’s outstanding loans were its assets—the money it will collect from someone else (exactly the reverse of how families or businessmen would describe their balance sheets). Managed liabilities depended on banks issuing such large financial instruments as certificates of deposit and Eurodollar borrowings of $100,000 and in much greater sums. As long as the banks could count on borrowing new funds at a lower interest rate than they charged for lending them out, the interest-rate spread assured their profits.23
"我所希望的是，"Volcker 说，"市场会有强烈反应。利率起初会急剧上升，但那将对通胀预期产生有利影响，从而使长期利率开始下降。心理成功的标志是长期利率能否稳定并开始下降。"
Volcker’s proposed shift would disrupt the banks’ confidence in their interest-rate calculations. What if they made loans at 13 percent and short-term interest rates were suddenly going up sharply? They would lose the spread if they themselves had to borrow at higher rates. The marginal reserve requirement of 8 percent would also make the practice of managed liabilities more expensive for the banks. At all but the smallest banks, the Fed’s reserve requirement on the demand deposits in checking accounts was 16.25 percent, but it was much lower or zero on funds raised through large CDs or in the Federal Funds market or the Eurodollar borrowings. Under Volcker’s scheme, banks would have to set aside another 8 percent of their borrowed funds as reserves—sterile money held at their local Federal Reserve Bank where it collected no interest. For every $1 million a bank raised in the money market, it would have to set aside $80,000 in an idle account at the Fed. This would not prohibit them from aggressive lending, but bankers, more than other creatures, detested idle money, funds that were not producing interest income. Above all, Volcker hoped the new reserve requirement might scare the more aggressive banks a bit, make them wonder if the Fed was prepared to do even more later.
随着 Volcker 与其他同事逐一交谈，他的政治困境得到了解决：三位对利率激烈行动最犹豫的理事对他的提议接受得多。Wallich 和 Coldwell 不喜欢它，但 Teeters、Rice 和 Partee 愿意做出转变，深知这意味着利率急剧飙升——这是他们之前不愿意直接采取的行动。
When Volcker called on other governors, all these elements were made part of his larger argument: if the Federal Reserve took sufficiently dramatic action, that might produce an important psychological victory. If the financial markets were convinced that at last the Fed was prepared to get tough and stay tough on controlling money, the investors who were demanding an “inflation premium” in interest rates to protect themselves against future inflation might change their thinking. Thus, Volcker argued, the drastic action would certainly push up interest rates across the spectrum of lending, both for short-term credit and long-term investments like corporate and government bonds. But, if the markets became more optimistic about the long-range prospects for curbing inflation, then long-term interest rates should drop back down to more normal levels.
"在新制度下，"Nancy Teeters 观察到，"我们可以说我们正在专注于货币总量。对我来说很明显，如果你把货币增长设定得太低，那将推高利率。这一点从未有疑问。以联邦基金利率为目标的问题是你必须设定它。这确实让我们稍微后退了一步。"
“What I hoped,” Volcker said, “was that there would be a strong reaction in the markets. Interest rates would go up sharply at first, but that would have a favorable impact on inflationary expectations so that long-term rates would start coming down. The sign of psychological success was whether long-term rates would stabilize and start coming down.”
Emmett Rice 四个月前加入董事会，他自己也曾质疑利率目标，确信直接控制储备更为合理。"我准备改变它，"Rice 说，"我们大多数人都相当高兴 Paul 愿意咬紧牙关。"
As Volcker made the rounds with his other colleagues, his political dilemma was resolved: the three governors who had been most hesitant about drastic action on interest rates were much more receptive to his proposal. Wallich and Coldwell didn’t like it, but Teeters, Rice and Partee were willing to make the shift, well aware that it meant a sharp spike in interest rates—the action they had been reluctant to take directly.
Rice 也认识到新操作系统为理事们提供的政治掩护：
“Under the new system,” Nancy Teeters observed, “we could say what we were doing was concentrating on the monetary aggregates. It was perfectly obvious to me that if you set the money growth too low, that would send interest rates up. That was never in doubt. The problem with targeting the Fed Funds rate is that you had to set it. This did let us step back a bit.”
这意味着你对利率发生的情况不负直接责任。这是优势之一。如果利率不得不达到20%——我必须说，没有人认为它们会达到那么高——那么这将是通过程序做到的。我不会称之为掩护，但我不认为委员会中的任何人愿意投票将利率推高到20%。这是一种达成结果的方式，一种更有效的达到目标的方式。
Emmett Rice, who had joined the board four months earlier, had questioned interest-rate targeting himself, convinced that it would make more sense to control reserves directly. “I was ready to change it,” Rice said. “Most of us were rather pleased that Paul was willing to bite the bullet.”
Chuck Partee，另一位勉强的"鸽派"，被一个不同的论点吸引到操作转变上。Partee 本人不是货币主义者，但他认为货币主义方法可能克服 Fed 制度性反射中的一个缺陷——过于顽固地坚持一个强硬立场太久，对经济造成比预期更大的损害。Partee 在1974-1975年漫长而深刻的衰退期间担任研究主任，当时 Fed 被指控确实做了那样的事——长期保持货币和信贷紧缩，使经济承受了比必要更多的困境、更多的破产和更多的失业。
Rice also recognized the political cover that the new operating system offered the governors:
正如他所说：
This meant you were not directly responsible for what happened to interest rates. This was one of the advantages. If interest rates had to go to 20 percent—and I have to say that nobody thought they would go that high—then this would be the procedure doing it. I wouldn’t call it a cover, but I don’t think anyone on the committee would have been willing to vote to push interest rates as high as 20 percent. This was a way to achieve a result, a more effective way to get there.
这可能听起来奇怪，但我更喜欢货币主义者的公平方法。我非常担心一种心态会导致我们直接陷入衰退——收紧并保持收紧——并导致我们陷入像1930年代那样的衰退。我发现自己对通过以货币供应为目标的理念——即我们可能有一个更公平的方法——的敌意远小于对应该一次性提高利率并持续加息的想法的敌意。问题在于，一旦利率被提高，也有一种降低利率的犹豫。我的担忧源于我对早先几次衰退的反思，特别是1974-1975年。我担心的是，我们对疲弱反应缓慢，允许货币和信贷发生大幅收缩。这种可能性很大，即我们可能只是 locked 进一个相当长时期内保持紧缩的立场。
Chuck Partee, the other reluctant “dove,” was attracted to the operating shift by a different argument. Partee was not a monetarist himself, but he thought that the monetarist approach might overcome a flaw in the Fed’s institutional reflexes—sticking stubbornly with a strong position too long and causing more damage to the economy than it had intended. Partee had been research director during the long and deep recession of 1974-1975 when the Fed was accused of doing exactly that—holding money and credit tight for so long that it drove the economy into more distress than was necessary, more bankruptcies and more unemployment.
货币主义方法的自动性承诺避免这种极端。据推测，如果经济疲弱，Fed 将继续提供同样数量的储备，随着需求下降，货币价格也会下降。因此，以储备供应为目标将在经济活动上升时迫使利率走高，但也允许利率在下降时回落。
As he said:
"我认为这是一个巨大的赌注，"Partee 补充说，"在正常情况下，你可能永远不会下这么大的赌注。"
It may sound odd, but I would prefer the evenhanded approach of the monetarists. I became very concerned about a mind-set that would lead us right into a recession—get tight and stay tight—and lead us into a recession like the 1930s. I found myself far less hostile to the notion that we might have a fairer approach by targeting the money supply than I was to the idea that we should raise interest rates one time and keep raising them. The problem is, there is also a hesitancy to reduce interest rates once they have been raised. My concern grew out of my reflection on several earlier recessions, particularly 1974-1975. My concern was that we would be slow to respond to weakness and permit a substantial contraction in money and credit to occur. There would be a great chance of that, that we might just get locked into a position of holding tight for a rather extended period.
现在 Volcker 有了变革的共识。Wallich 和 Coldwell 虽然不喜欢这个想法，但同意如果这是 Volcker 让其他人支持更强硬政策的唯一途径，他们就不坚持反对。Fred Schultz，新任副主席，在这个问题上听从 Volcker 的判断，同意必须采取 dramatic 的行动。Volcker 相信，这可能是实现他和"鹰派"希望看到的更紧缩货币的唯一途径。
The automatic quality of the monetarist method promised to avoid that extreme. Presumably, if the economy weakened, the Fed would continue to provide the same supply of reserves, and as the demand fell, the price of money would fall too. Targeting the supply of reserves would, thus, force interest rates higher on an upswing in economic activity, but it would also allow the rates to fall on the downside.
Wallich 没有多争辩。"我不确定人们会按我的方式做，"他说，"使用一种产生紧缩共识的方法可能是明智的。"
“I considered it a big gamble,” Partee added. “You probably would never take such a big gamble under normal circumstances.”
9月的最后一个星期五，即28日，七位理事在主席的办公室集合——在那里他们比在董事会会议室更不引人注意——以敲定最终细节。只有少数高级职员被包括在内。如果在主席准备行动之前有丝毫风声泄露，将在金融市场引起疯狂谣言。理事们都同意支持转向新的操作方法，但关于如何运作还有许多问题需要解决。主席现在确信，他可以在下次会议时将这个想法卖给联邦公开市场委员会。他已通过电话与十二位储备银行行长中的几位讨论了这一提议，但他们中的大多数都不知道正在酝酿什么。Volcker 不担心获得他们的支持。鉴于他们对货币主义的热情，储备银行行长们会很高兴得知总部终于转向了他们的思维方式。
Now Volcker had his consensus for change. Wallich and Coldwell, though they didn’t like the idea, agreed not to press their objections if this was the only way Volcker could get the others to support a tougher policy. Fred Schultz, the new vice chairman, deferred to Volcker’s judgment on the issue, agreeing that something dramatic had to be done. Volcker believed that this was probably the only way to get the tighter money that he and the “hawks” wanted to see.
在白宫，总统一无所知。Jimmy Carter 的经济政策顾问们也毫不知情，联邦储备正准备货币政策的一次重大 departure，旨在向国家金融体系传递一次心理冲击。恰好，总统和他的工作人员正忙于制定自己的反通胀计划。最近几周，白宫工作人员与主要公司的高管和 AFL-CIO 的劳工领袖举行了无数次会议，谈判一项关于工资和价格的新"协议"，希望缓和商业-劳工对更高工资、更高价格的要求。白宫工作人员耐心地调解关于自愿协议措辞的争论性讨论，劝说劳工官员和企业领袖签署。他们当然不知道，总统的反通胀努力将被三个街区外 Paul Volcker 刚刚在联邦储备大楼的私密环境中谈判达成的新协议所抹杀。
Wallich did not argue much. “I was not sure people would do it my way,” he said. “It was probably wise to use a method that produced a consensus for tightening.”
尽管表面上超然，联邦储备与围绕它的许多政治利益保持着密切联系。Paul Volcker 定期与广泛利益集团及其代表会面，甚至包括那些对 Fed 持敌对态度并反对其政策的人。在一个典型的工作日，主席可能与独立银行家协会的一位说客共进早餐，然后与全国房屋建筑商协会的一位经济学家共进午餐。晚上，他可能顺道参加一个国会招待会，在那里他从华盛顿的政治八卦中获取 casual 情报。
On the last Friday of the month, September 28, the seven governors assembled in the chairman’s office, where they would attract less attention than in the boardroom, to thrash out the final details. Only a few of the senior staff members were included. If even a hint leaked out before the chairman was ready to move, it would send wild rumors through the financial markets. The governors all agreed to support the shift to a new operating method, but there were many questions to settle about how it would operate. The chairman was now confident that he could sell the idea to the Federal Open Market Committee when it next met. He had discussed the proposal with several of the twelve Reserve Bank presidents by telephone, but most of them had no idea of what was in the works. Volcker did not worry about getting their support. Given their enthusiasm for monetarism, the Reserve Bank presidents would be delighted to learn that the home office was finally coming around to their way of thinking.
主席的性格不太适合华盛顿午餐中常见的 exaggerated 亲昵风格，但他也不像他的公众形象那样 austere 和遥远。近距离接触时，Volcker 似乎比那个偶尔在电视新闻中出现、叼着大雪茄、发表不祥声明的高大男人更友好、更随意。他问很多问题，他不修边幅的举止中的某种东西让他人觉得他在认真倾听他们的回答。这自然对那些能接触到他的人是一种恭维；与 Paul Volcker 的私密交谈是一个人自身重要性的证据。
At the White House, the President did not know anything either. Nor did any of Jimmy Carter’s advisers on economic policy have any idea that the Federal Reserve was preparing a major departure in monetary policy, intended to deliver a psychological shock to the nation’s financial system. As it happened, the President and his staff were busy working up their own anti-inflation initiative. In recent weeks, the White House staff had held uncounted meetings with business executives from major corporations and labor leaders from the AFL-CIO, negotiating a new “accord” on wages and prices that they hoped would moderate the business-labor demands for higher wages, higher prices. White House staffers patiently mediated tendentious discussions over how the voluntary agreement was to be worded, coaxing the labor officials and business leaders to sign. They did not know, of course, that the President’s anti-inflation effort would be rendered irrelevant by the new agreement Paul Volcker had just negotiated three blocks away in the privacy of the Federal Reserve building.
律师、说客和政治家，甚至一些经常遇到他的新闻记者，都倾向于在谈话中随意提及他们最近与联邦储备主席的聊天。"正如我在告诉 Paul 的时候……"很少有人听到这些人说："正如 Paul 在告诉我的时候……"Volcker 倾听很多，但对自己的想法透露不多。
Despite its appearance of aloofness, the Federal Reserve kept in close touch with the many political interests that surrounded it. Paul Volcker met regularly with a wide circle of interest groups and their representatives, even some that were hostile to the Fed and opposed to its policies. On a typical day, the chairman might breakfast with a lobbyist from the Independent Bankers Association, then lunch with an economist from the National Home Builders Association. In the evening, he might drop by a congressional reception where he would pick up casual intelligence from the stream of Washington political gossip.
但有一个特殊利益群体，高于所有其他群体，拥有接触联邦储备主席和其他理事的优先权——即联邦储备体系的6000家成员商业银行。每年四次，由每个联邦储备区的成员银行选举产生的十二位银行家委员会私下与 Volcker 和其他理事会面，告诉他们自己对 Fed 政策的看法以及应该如何改变。这是联邦政府中一个独特的安排——被监管行业与监管者举行保密会议，抱怨、批评和推荐新政策。如果这是政府监管的另一个领域——航空安全、水污染或工业卫生规则——监管者与被监管企业之间的私下对话将可疑并需要及时披露。在 Fed，银行家建议的保密性由法律 consecrated。
The chairman’s personality was not well suited to the style of exaggerated chumminess common to Washington luncheons, but neither was he as austere and remote as his public image. Up close, Volcker seemed friendlier and more casual than the imposingly tall man with the big cigar who appeared periodically on the TV news with ominous pronouncements. He asked many questions and something in his rumpled manner allowed others to feel he was listening earnestly to their answers. This was naturally flattering to those who had his ear; an intimate conversation with Paul Volcker was evidence of one’s own importance.
联邦咨询委员会从一开始就引发了怀疑。它作为一项妥协被添加到1913年最初的联邦储备立法中，以安抚失望的商业银行家，他们原以为自己应单独控制新的联邦储备体系。作为一种安抚，银行家们被赋予作为正式顾问的优先地位。由于它的会议保密，联邦咨询委员会在许多针对 Fed 的阴谋论中占据突出地位。人们想，这一定是银行家告诉 Fed 该做什么的地方。
The lawyers, lobbyists and politicians, even some news reporters who encountered him regularly, were likely to sprinkle their conversations with casual references to their recent chat with the Federal Reserve chairman. “As I was telling Paul ...” Seldom did one hear any of these people say: “As Paul was telling me ...” Volcker listened a lot, but did not reveal much about his own thoughts.
联邦储备官员以无奈的态度 shrugs off 这些指控。他们说，与银行家委员会的会议是关于银行问题的有益讨论，但 hardly sinister。毕竟，Fed 官员一直与私人银行家交谈，也与消费者和储蓄与贷款高管的咨询小组会面。
But one special-interest group, above all others, had preferred access to the Federal Reserve chairman and the other governors—the six thousand commercial banks that were members of the Federal Reserve System. Four times a year, a committee of twelve bankers, elected by the member banks in each Federal Reserve district, met privately with Volcker and other governors to tell them what they thought of Fed policy and how it should be changed. It was a unique arrangement in the federal government—a regulated industry holding confidential meetings with the regulators to complain and criticize and recommend new policies. If this were another area of regulatory government, airline safety or water pollution or industrial health rules, private conversations between the regulators and the regulated business would be suspect and subject to prompt disclosure. At the Fed, the secrecy of the bankers’ advice was consecrated in the law.
联邦咨询委员会的官方会议记录揭示了一个更复杂的图景——也许不是 sinister，但也不完全无辜。银行家们定期利用这些私下会议进行自己的特殊 pleading，反复敦促 Fed 对他们更宽松，并对其竞争对手实施更严格的监管。
The Federal Advisory Council had inspired suspicions from the start. It was added to the original Federal Reserve legislation of 1913 as a compromise to assuage the disappointed commercial bankers who thought they alone should have control over the new Federal Reserve System. As a sop, the bankers were given a preferred status as official advisers. Since its meetings were kept secret, the Federal Advisory Council figured prominently in many of the conspiracy theories focused on the Fed. This must be the place, people figured, where the bankers tell the Fed what to do.
在全国数千家小型独立银行家中，许多人憎恨咨询委员会，因为在他们看来，这像是大银行的私人游说团体。例如，1979年，十二位咨询委员会成员包括 Walter B. Wriston，花旗银行董事会主席，全美第二大银行，也是新型激进增长管理风格中最成功的例子。Wriston 本人是一位直言不讳、大胆的银行家，蔑视政府对银行业的干预，也是货币主义的重要皈依者。十二位委员会成员包括八位来自美国最大一百家银行的首席执行官，其中有芝加哥的 Continental Illinois、达拉斯的 Republic Bank、匹兹堡国民银行和费城的 Girard Bank。他们对银行业问题的看法自然反映了他们的规模。咨询委员会中一位足够警觉的银行家可能会从理事们的问题中推断出他们正在考虑未来的什么。
Federal Reserve officials shrugged off the accusations with an air of resignation. The meetings with the bankers’ council, they said, were useful discussions of banking issues but hardly sinister. After all, Fed officials talked to private bankers all the time and also met with advisory groups of consumers and savings and loan executives.
咨询委员会的会议记录并未证实对阴谋的 lurid 指控，但银行家的报告确实提供了一个粗略的 gauge，反映了联邦储备对自身美国经济的看法与主要商业银行狭隘自身利益之间的接近程度。1979年秋季，这种对齐被证明相当接近。当联邦咨询委员会于9月7日开会时，其成员祝贺理事们最近提高了短期利率。但银行家们敦促他们继续增加类似的措施。
The official minutes of the Federal Advisory Council revealed a more complicated picture—not sinister perhaps, but not entirely innocent either. The bankers regularly used these private sessions for their own special pleading, repeatedly urging the Fed to be more lenient on them and to adopt more stringent regulations for their competitors.
"主要的担忧是这些利率是否足够高，以抑制货币总量中出现的膨胀，"银行家们说，"近几个月来，货币供应的过度增长表明信贷创造增速超过了期望……在这方面，放缓货币总量快速增长的需求应优先于对国内利率进一步上行的担忧。"这正是理事们自己将在当月晚些时候同意的政策视角。
Among the thousands of small, independent bankers around the nation, many resented the advisory council because it looked to them like a private lobby for the largest banks. In 1979, for instance, the twelve advisory council members included Walter B. Wriston, board chairman of Citibank, second-largest bank in the nation and the most successful example of the new style of aggressive growth management. Wriston was himself an outspoken and bold banker, contemptuous of government interference in banking and also an important convert to monetarism. The twelve council members included eight chief executives from banks that ranked in the largest hundred in the nation, among them Continental Illinois of Chicago, Republic Bank of Dallas, Pittsburgh National Bank and the Girard Bank of Philadelphia. Their perspective on banking issues naturally reflected their size. A reasonably alert banker on the advisory council might infer from the board’s questions what the governors were contemplating for the future.
银行家们还要求联邦储备考虑改变其操作方法，这正是 Volcker 当时与其他理事机密讨论的同一提议。会议记录没有反映在私下讨论中是谁提出了这种可能性，但银行家们在其总结报告中提到了这一点：
The advisory council’s minutes did not corroborate the lurid accusations of conspiracy, but the bankers’ reports did offer a rough gauge of how close the Federal Reserve’s own perspective on the American economy was to the narrow self-interests of the major commercial banks. In the fall of 1979, the alignment proved to be quite close. When the Federal Advisory Council met on September 7, its members congratulated the governors on their recent increases in short-term interest rates. But the bankers urged them to do more of the same.
"委员会的一些成员对联邦储备的操作技术表达了关切，即在确定适当政策时，它应关注利率目标还是货币总量。这是一个难以回答的问题，但建议对操作技术进行重新审视。"
“The main concern is whether these rates are high enough to stem the bulge which has occurred in the monetary aggregates,” the bankers said. “In recent months, the excessive growth in the money supply suggests that credit creation is rising at a faster pace than desired. ... In this respect, the need to slow the rapid growth in monetary aggregates should take priority over concerns about further upward moves in domestic interest rates.” This was the same policy perspective the governors themselves would agree on later in the month.
这个建议引人注目，因为它发生在理事们达成关于转向货币主义方法的私下协议的三周前——远在 Jimmy Carter 被告知联邦储备意图之前。24
The bankers also asked the Federal Reserve to consider changing its operating methods, the same proposal that Volcker was then discussing confidentially with the other governors. The minutes did not reflect who brought up this possibility in the private discussions, but the bankers addressed it in their summary report:
9月29日星期六，在理事们解决了关于重新组织 Fed 货币监管的分歧后的第二天，联邦储备主席登上了飞往欧洲的飞机，前往南斯拉夫贝尔格莱德参加国际货币基金组织的年度会议。起初，Volcker 想跳过 IMF 会议，因为他忙于操作转变的细节，迫切希望尽快实施。其他 Fed 官员说服他去。IMF 会议汇集了世界的财政部长和中央银行家，138个国家的代表，无论贫富，如果美国中央银行的新主席不参加，那看起来会很奇怪。
Some members of the council expressed concern over the operational techniques of the Federal Reserve in respect to whether it should focus on interest-rate targets or the monetary aggregates in determining an appropriate policy. This is a difficult question to answer, but it is suggested that a re-examination of operational techniques be made.
飞机是财政部长的专机，其他乘客包括 Volcker 的前任 Fed 主席 G. William Miller，他现在在财政部，以及 Charles L. Schultze，卡特总统经济顾问委员会主席的自由派经济学家。飞越大西洋时，Volcker 借此机会首次告知卡特政府的这些官员，他打算如何在联邦储备进行变革。
This recommendation was striking because it occurred three weeks before the governors would reach their own private agreement on shifting to the monetarist approach—and long before Jimmy Carter was told about the Federal Reserve’s intentions.24
两人立刻对这个想法充满敌意。他们一致认为联邦储备必须采取更多措施来收紧货币和信贷供应，但他们都反对 Volcker 正在准备的操作方法变化。Miller 抱怨说，如果 Fed 开始直接 targeting 储备，将导致利率更大的 volatility、信贷成本的剧烈波动，从而 disrupt 经济，并可能实际加剧通胀。
On Saturday, September 29, the day after the governors resolved their differences over reordering the Fed’s regulation of money, the Federal Reserve chairman boarded a plane for Europe, bound for the annual conference of the International Monetary Fund, in Belgrade, Yugoslavia. At first, Volcker had wanted to skip the IMF meeting, because he was so busy with the details of the operating shift and so eager to move quickly to implement it. Other Fed officials persuaded him to go. The IMF conference drew the finance ministers and central bankers of the world, the representatives of 138 nations, rich and poor, and it would look odd if the new chairman of America’s central bank did not attend.
Schultze 有更根本的反对意见：新的操作系统将锁定 Fed，使其走上一条无法回头的道路。而那条道路迟早将导致衰退。
The airplane was the Secretary of the Treasury’s jet, and the other passengers included Volcker’s predecessor as Fed chairman, G. William Miller, who was now at Treasury, and Charles L. Schultze, the liberal economist who was chairman of President Carter’s Council of Economic Advisers. Crossing the Atlantic, Volcker took the occasion to inform these officials of the Carter Administration, for the first time, how he intended to change things at the Federal Reserve.
交谈中没有人需要指出明年是1980年，Jimmy Carter 将竞选连任。
Both of them were immediately hostile to the idea. They agreed that the Federal Reserve must do more to tighten the supply of money and credit, but both argued against the change in operating methods that Volcker was preparing. Miller complained that if the Fed began targeting reserves directly, it would produce more volatility in interest rates, sharp fluctuations in credit costs that would disrupt the economy and might actually exacerbate inflation.
Volcker 听取了这些论点，并告诉他们："我会考虑的。"他承诺，当他们回到华盛顿时，在 Fed 采取行动之前，政府将有机会对计划做出更详细的反应。然而，Volcker 认为，改变操作方法是货币政策的技术问题，最终只有 Fed 自己才能决定。
Schultze had more fundamental objections: the new operating system would lock in the Fed, put it on a course from which it could not turn back. And that course would lead, sooner or later, to recession.
根据法律，联邦储备主席没有正式义务告知白宫任何事情，或考虑其反对意见。然而，作为实际政治问题，Volcker 需要知道卡特白宫可能如何反应。如果总统在利率飙升时攻击他，并伴随着愤怒的众议员和参议员合唱，他的倡议可能会陷入党派争议。
No one in the conversation needed to point out that next year was 1980 and Jimmy Carter would be running for re-election.
"他们有权知道我们在做什么，只要他们遵守游戏规则，不通过泄露来背叛你，"Volcker 说，"你有直接兴趣了解他们的感受。你想知道是否会存在反对。这可能正影响行动的信誉和行动的可持续性。你担心可能被启动从而破坏你将要采取的行动的反制力量。"
Volcker listened to the arguments and told them: “I’ll think it over.” When they returned to Washington, he promised, the Administration would have a full opportunity to react to his plan in more detail before the Fed took action. Volcker believed, however, that changing the operating method was a technical issue of monetary policy, a question that ultimately only the Fed itself could decide.
Volcker 与卡特政府的非正式沟通说明了联系联邦储备与联邦政府其他部分——经选举产生的总统和国会——的模糊条款。这种关系在任何地方都没有以具体规则和义务表达。相反，它的边界模糊而主观，由政治判断决定。在每个重要实例中，Fed 主席必须在行动前评估局势，很像任何当选政治家在承诺重大议题或新竞选前衡量其潜在反对力量。联邦储备可以独立行动，但它应走多远？在什么点上它会引发如此强大的政治反应，以至于最终可能被剥夺其珍视的独立性？
Under the law, the Federal Reserve chairman had no formal obligation to tell the White House anything or to take its objections into account. As a practical matter of politics, however, Volcker needed to know how the Carter White House might react. If the President was going to attack him when interest rates shot up, joined by a chorus of angry representatives and senators, his initiative might become mired in partisan controversy.
正如 Volcker 所解释的：
They’re entitled to know what we are doing, if they play the game and don’t double-cross you by leaking it [Volcker said]. You’ve got a direct interest in finding out how they feel. You want to know if there is going to be opposition or not. It might just affect the credibility of the action and the sustainability of the action. You are concerned about the counterforces that might be set in motion that could undermine what you are going to do..
联邦储备作为一个机构必须在人们能够理解和支持的大致参数内运作。你无法完全180度地背离其他人所理解的一切。他们必须知道你的目的，并理解其中涉及一些痛苦，即使他们不同意每个细节。如果你离开了那种模糊的共识，你就有麻烦了。
Volcker’s informal communication with the Carter Administration illustrated the ambiguous terms that linked the Federal Reserve with the rest of the federal government, the elected President and Congress. The relationship was nowhere expressed in concrete rules and obligations. Instead, its boundaries were vague and subjective, determined by political judgment. In every important instance, the Fed chairman had to evaluate the situation before acting, much the way any elected politician measured his potential opposition before committing himself to a major issue or a new campaign. The Federal Reserve could act independently, but how far should it go? At what point would it provoke such a powerful political reaction that, ultimately, it could be stripped of its cherished independence?
卡特政府的决策者对他的计划不满意，但至少他们同意必须采取更 dramatic 的行动来打破通胀 momentum。在去贝尔格莱德的途中，飞机首先在德国汉堡停留，在那里 Volcker 的紧迫感得到了一次有影响力的背书。美国官员们听取了西德总理 Helmut Schmidt 关于需要更强有力货币措施的严厉讲座。
As Volcker explained:
当代表团到达 IMF 会议时，Volcker 和其他人与西欧的中央银行家们和财政部长们交谈，听到了同样的信息，而且是最强烈的措辞。美元在国际外汇市场上的价值仍在下滑，美国必须采取有力行动扭转下跌。前 Fed 主席 Arthur Burns 在贝尔格莱德以一场题为"中央银行的痛苦"的讲座为阴郁氛围做出了贡献。Burns 惋惜道，期望中央银行在民众政治力量向另一个方向拉时扑灭通胀，这是虚幻的。
The Federal Reserve as an institution has to operate within some broad parameters of what people can understand and support. You just can’t cut 180 degrees across everything that other people understand. They have to know your purpose and understand if some pain is involved, even if they don’t agree in every detail. If you are outside that vague consensus, you’re in trouble.
Volcker 提前离开了。他已用含蓄的措辞与其他中央银行家中的几位好友讨论了他的重大政策转变计划，但认为没有必要停留更久。星期二，会议仍在进行中，Volcker 启程返回华盛顿，这一发展在欧洲和美国的金融市场触发了疯狂谣言和价格波动。
The Carter policy makers were unhappy with his plans, but at least they agreed that something more dramatic had to be done to break the inflationary momentum. En route to Belgrade, the plane stopped first in Hamburg, Germany, where Volcker’s sense of urgency received an influential endorsement. The American officials sat through a stern lecture from Helmut Schmidt, chancellor of West Germany, on the need for stronger monetary measures.
在华尔街内部人士中，贝尔格莱德的事件顺序创造了一个关于 Volcker 动机的持久神话——即 Fed 主席被欧洲金融家的抱怨所震动，以至于匆忙回家采取 drastic 行动。这种版本在细节上是错误的——Volcker 在参加 IMF 会议之前就决定采取行动——但在更广泛意义上并非错误。美国政府已被国际金融界的领先声音——包括美国银行家和欧洲人——连续数月打击，要求制止美元在国际汇率中的下跌。联邦储备对这个支持群体的反应比当选政治家更为敏感。
When the delegation arrived at the IMF conference, Volcker and the others heard the same message, in the strongest terms, in conversations with the central bankers and finance ministers of Western Europe. The dollar was still sliding in value on international exchange markets and the United States must act forcefully to reverse the decline. Arthur Burns, the former Fed chairman, contributed to the gloomy atmosphere in Belgrade with a lecture entitled “The Anguish of Central Banking.” It is illusory, Burns lamented, to expect central banks to extinguish inflation when popular political forces pull the other way.
弱化的美元在一个意义上实际上对美国实体经济有益。它给美国出口商在与外国生产商的贸易竞争中带来了巨大优势，在国际市场上从农业到重工业的人为价格优势。这意味着更大的美国产出和更多就业。外国政府自然抱怨，并指责美国故意贬值货币以获取更大全球市场份额。
Volcker left early. He had discussed his plans for a major policy shift in elliptical terms with several of his best friends among the other central bankers, but saw no point in staying longer. On Tuesday, with the conference still under way, Volcker departed for Washington, a development that touched off wild rumors and price gyrations in the financial markets of Europe and the United States.
但国际金融中最有影响力的机构遭受了相应的损失——特别是跨国银行家，包括美国和外国，他们在全球范围内进行美元计价交易。美元贬值意味着他们的美元资产 steadily 失去价值。例如，美国最大的银行向拉丁美洲发展中国家贷出了数百亿美元，所有这些贷款都以美元计价。当美元在国际汇率中下降时，国际贷款的实际价值自动下降——减轻了外国借款者的债务负担，就像国内通胀为美国购房者减轻了负担一样。跨国贷方因与国内债权人同样的原因而损失。
Among Wall Street insiders, the sequence of events in Belgrade created an enduring myth about Volcker’s motivation—that the Fed chairman was so jarred by the complaints of European financiers that he rushed home to do something drastic. That version was mistaken in the particulars—Volcker had decided to act even before attending the IMF conference—but it was not wrong in the larger sense. The American government had been pounded for many months by the leading voices of international finance, both American bankers and European, demanding a halt to the dollar’s decline in international exchange. The Federal Reserve responded more sensitively to this constituency than did elected politicians.
出于明显的政治目的，联邦储备官员试图淡化国际金融在迫使他们采取行动中的重要性。对国内价格通胀的正面攻击可能受到美国公众的广泛欢迎，即使它需要一些痛苦。但为了拯救货币中心银行和其他全球金融家的资产而大幅提高利率，听起来不像一项受欢迎的事业。无论动机如何，两者无论如何都是同一货币政策的表现。无论动机如何，Fed 在不影响美元国际价值的情况下无法对抗国内通胀。
The weakening dollar was, in one sense, actually beneficial for the real economy in the United States. It gave American exporters an enormous edge in trading competition with foreign producers, an artificial price advantage in international markets from agriculture to heavy manufacturing. That meant greater U.S. output and more jobs. Foreign governments naturally complained and accused the U.S. of debasing its currency deliberately in order to grab a larger share of global markets.
当财政部长 Miller 和 CEA 主席 Schultze 于周四返回华盛顿时，他们加大努力说服 Volcker 放弃推进他的计划。他们没有多少时间——Volcker 计划在10月6日星期六举行联邦公开市场委员会特别会议。Schultze 开始了一系列匆忙的会议，试图说服 Volcker 让步，并让白宫抵制。Schultze 将问题提交给总统国内政策首席顾问 Stuart Eizenstat，并请他帮助说服总统干预。
But the most influential institutions of international finance suffered proportionately—particularly the multinational bankers, both American and foreign, who dealt worldwide in dollar-denominated transactions. The depreciating dollar meant their dollar assets were steadily losing value. The largest U.S. banks, for instance, had lent tens of billions to the developing nations of Latin America, and these loans were all pegged in dollars. When the dollar declined in international exchange, the real value of the international loans automatically declined—reducing the debt burden for foreign borrowers just as domestic inflation eased it for home buyers in the United States. Multinational lenders lost for the same reason creditors at home were losing.
在接下来的两天里，在会议和电话中，Schultze 再次敦促 Volcker 重新考虑。他恳求道，如果必须，就提高利率，但不要改变操作系统。"Volcker 将行动得很快，因为一旦泄露，情况会非常糟糕，"Schultze 说，"承认必须做点什么，我的反对意见是，一旦你做了这个，就无法后退了。一旦你告诉世界这是货币目标，无论利率发生什么我们都会遵循，你就必须坚持它，你没有灵活性。"
For obvious political purposes, Federal Reserve officials tried to downplay the importance of international finance in forcing their hand. A frontal attack against domestic price inflation might be widely welcomed by the American public, even if it required some pain. But raising interest rates sharply in order to save the assets of the money-center banks and other global financiers did not sound like a popular cause. The two, in any case, were expressions of the same monetary policy. Regardless of its motivation, the Fed could not confront domestic inflation without also affecting the international value of the dollar.
这不仅仅是经济学家之间的技术性争论：Schultze 预见到了即将到来的选举季节的 bleak 后果。"我能非常清楚地看到，尽管不知道确切时间，"他说，"鉴于我们承受的通胀压力，如果 Fed 采纳刚性货币政策并向其客户承诺坚持到底，我们很可能被推入衰退，而我们无法从中退出。"
When Treasury Secretary Miller and CEA Chairman Schultze returned to Washington on Thursday, they stepped up their efforts to talk Volcker out of going forward with his scheme. They did not have much time—Volcker was planning a special meeting of the Federal Open Market Committee on Saturday, October 6. Schultze began a series of hurried meetings, trying to persuade Volcker to relent and the White House that it should resist. Schultze took the issue to Stuart Eizenstat, the President’s chief adviser for domestic policy, and asked him to help convince the President to intervene.
Schultze 也认识到吸引 Fed 理事们接受该计划的政治优势。
Over the next two days, in meetings and phone calls, Schultze again urged Volcker to reconsider. Raise interest rates, if you must, he pleaded, but don’t change the operating system. “Volcker was going to move very quickly, because if it ever leaked, it would be very bad,” Schultze said. “Granting that something had to be done, my objection was that once you do this, you can’t back out. Once you tell the world this is the money target and we are going to follow it no matter what happens to interest rates, you have to stick with it and you have no flexibility.”
"在 Fed 的思维中，整个举措在最广泛的意义上是一种政治举措，而非经济举措。理论上，Fed 本可以继续大幅度提高利率，但它在政治上做不到。这项新政策的美妙之处在于，随着利率持续上升，Fed 可以说，'嘿，这里只有我们这些 chicken。我们没有提高利率，我们只是在 targeting 货币供应。'这样他们就可以提高利率，而没有人能责备他们。"
This was more than a technical argument between economists: Schultze foresaw bleak consequences for the election season ahead. “What I could see very clearly, without knowing the exact timing,” he said, “was that given the inflationary pressures we were under, if the Fed adopted a rigid monetary policy and promised its clients to stick to it, very likely we would be driven into a recession and we wouldn’t be able to back out of it.”
货币供应与货币价格（即利率）之间的基本关系对经济学家来说是显而易见的——如果供应紧缩，利率上升。对许多非经济学家来说，包括美国总统，这并不那么明显。事实上，Volcker 在这个问题上和行政当局的经济学家开玩笑。Charles Schultze 回忆说："Paul 对我们说，'实际上是和 Jimmy Carter 的一次会面给了我灵感。Carter 对我说："为什么你不能在不提高利率的情况下控制信贷数量？" ' Volcker 告诉我们，正是这个给了他以货币供应为目标而不是利率的想法。"当然，Volcker 的言论是开玩笑的，是专业人士之间关于外行人对基本原理无知的一个内部笑话。
Schultze also recognized the political advantages that attracted the Fed governors to the scheme.
虽然 Volcker 没有屈服于 Schultze 的反对意见，但他对潜在麻烦的关注足以让他试探另一位行政当局官员，一位对 Fed 处境更为同情的官员。Volcker 咨询了负责货币事务的财政部副部长 Anthony Solomon，他六个月后将接替 Volcker 的旧职，担任纽约联邦储备银行行长。Solomon 在1960年代曾在国务院与 Volcker 密切合作，当时 Volcker 在财政部；他们在这些问题上想法一致。现在 Solomon 认同 Fed 主席的紧迫感，并希望帮助他避免与白宫的公开冲突。Solomon 建议 Volcker 和行政当局官员直接向总统陈述他们的论点，让他听取双方意见。
In the mind of the Fed, this whole move was, in the broadest sense, a political move, not an economic move. In theory, the Fed could have kept on raising the bejesus out of the interest rates, but that’s what it couldn’t do politically. The beautiful thing about this new policy was that as interest rates kept going up, the Fed could say, “Hey, ain’t nobody here but us chickens. We’re not raising interest rates, we’re only targeting the money supply.” This way they could raise the rates and nobody could blame them.
当 Volcker 告诉 Schultze 和 Miller 他愿意见总统解释联邦储备的意图时，他们告诉他不必麻烦。相反，他们两人在 Eizenstat 的陪同下亲自去找 Jimmy Carter——并敦促总统向联邦储备主席表达他个人的反对。"你们对 Volcker 不公平，"Solomon 后来对他们抱怨说，"Volcker 应该在那次会议上解释他的立场。"
The fundamental relationship between the supply of money and the price of money, better known as interest rates, was obvious to economists—if supply shrinks, interest rates go up. It was not so obvious to many noneconomists, including the President of the United States. In fact, Volcker kidded the Administration economists on this point. Charles Schultze recalled: “Paul said to us, ‘It was really a meeting with Jimmy Carter that gave me the idea. Carter said to me, “Why can’t you control the quantity of credit without raising interest rates?” ’ Volcker told us that’s what gave him the idea to target money supply instead of interest rates.” Volcker’s remark was facetious, of course, an inside joke among professionals about a layman’s ignorance of the fundamentals.
Jimmy Carter 与他所有的现代前任一样，对货币政策问题没有很强的把握。美国政治领导人的经验中没有什么是准备他们处理这一主题的；Fed 独立的传统鼓励了一种观念，即货币 regulation 是留给专家的事情，一个政客不应侵入的禁区。Richard Nixon，和任何现代总统一样为行政首脑的工作做好了充分准备，在多年后的一次采访中告诉一位 interviewer，他最大的遗憾之一是他从未掌握对联邦储备所发生事情的理解。25 大多数在白宫以及国会任职的政客都可能做出类似 confession。正如 Tony Solomon 所观察到的：
While Volcker did not yield to Schultze’s objections, he was concerned enough about the potential for trouble that he sounded out another Administration official, one who would be more sympathetic to the Fed’s situation. Volcker consulted Anthony Solomon, the Treasury Under Secretary for Monetary Affairs, who six months later would assume Volcker’s old position, president of the New York Federal Reserve Bank. Solomon had worked closely with Volcker on international economic issues during the 1960s when he was at the State Department and Volcker was at Treasury; they thought alike on these questions. Now Solomon shared the Fed chairman’s sense of urgency and wanted to help him avoid a public conflict with the White House. Solomon suggested that Volcker and the Administration officials take their argument directly to the President and let him hear both sides.
"没有总统真正理解这些事情，但 Carter 令人不安的是，他试图使用经济术语就像他理解一样。他试图让自己看起来像是理解了技术性论据，而显然他没有。像 Lyndon Johnson 这样的总统从不假装理解所有这些事情——他只对底线感兴趣。他会问：政治影响是什么？利率会发生什么？他需要做什么来处理它？Carter 会参与他只会部分理解的技术性讨论。"
When Volcker told Schultze and Miller that he was willing to see the President to explain the Federal Reserve’s intentions, they told him not to bother. Instead, the two of them, accompanied by Eizen-stat, went to Jimmy Carter themselves—and urged the President to express his personal opposition to the Federal Reserve chairman. “You didn’t play fair with Volcker,” Solomon complained to them later. “Volcker should have been at that meeting to explain his position.”
最终，Jimmy Carter 拒绝做任何事情。Carter 告诉他的顾问们，鉴于他自己削弱的政治地位，抵抗是不可能了。白宫别无选择，只能默许。总统不能挑战由联邦储备委员会主席独立决定的新经济政策。此外，正如 Eizenstat 所说，"总统基本上是一个相当诚实的人，他相信政治体系的 integrity。他让那个人在那个位置上，看在上帝的份上，他会支持他。"
Jimmy Carter, like all his modern predecessors, did not have a strong grasp of the issues of monetary policy. Nothing in the experience of American political leaders prepared them to deal with the subject; the tradition of Fed independence encouraged the notion that money regulation was something left to the experts, a forbidden area where politicians were not supposed to intrude. Richard Nixon, who was as well trained for the Chief Executive’s job as any modern President, told an interviewer years later that one of his greatest regrets was that he never mastered an understanding of what went on at the Federal Reserve.25 Most of the politicians who served in the White House, as well as in Congress, could make a similar confession. As Tony Solomon observed:
Schultze 的解释是
No President really understands these things, but the disturbing thing about Carter was that he tried to use the economic jargon as though he did. He tried to make it seem that he understood the technical arguments when he clearly didn’t. A President like Lyndon Johnson never pretended to understand all these things—he was only interested in the bottom line. He would ask: What were the political effects? What would happen to interest rates? What did he have to do in order to deal with it? Carter would enter into technical discussions which he only partially understood.
"Fed 手握所有底牌。失业率低，通胀在上升。政治权力的总体结构使得所有商业和金融界都坚定支持 Fed。美元不稳定。白宫必须非常小心对待 Fed。除非它真的想采取一种 populist 运动，使美元进一步下跌，否则我们必须非常小心。"
In the event, Jimmy Carter declined to do anything. Resistance was not possible, Carter told his advisers, given his own weakened political status. The White House had no choice but to acquiesce. The President could not challenge the new economic policy determined independently by the chairman of the Federal Reserve Board. Besides, as Eizenstat said, “The President is basically a very honest guy who believes in the integrity of the political system. He had put that guy in there and, by God, he was going to stand by him.”
两个月前，Carter 几乎偶然地选择了 Paul Volcker。现在总统在含蓄地将控制权拱手让给他。联邦储备现在将引导国家政府的经济政策。在接下来的几年里，Fed 在美国体系中的真实角色将越来越为普通公民所见，因为 Volcker 在经济生活中主张其 leverage。他将个人受到诋毁和赞扬，在民意调查中被认为是这片土地上第二有权势的人。事后看来，人们会理解1979年秋季是国家政治进程中的一个历史性节点。但当时未被理解的是，这一关键交易是获得了一个衰弱的总统的默许，而非他的批准。
Schultze’s explanation was that
这一事件也需要联邦储备官员的深入政治判断。除了战术性论据之外，Volcker 在含蓄地做出自己的国家政治 assessment——假设国家已准备好接受他打算做的事情，即对通胀的持续攻击，包括随之而来的经济痛苦。当然，这正是银行家和金融市场想要的。
the Fed held all the cards. Unemployment was down and inflation was rising. The general structure of political power was such that all of the business and financial community was solidly behind the Fed. The dollar was shaky. The White House had to be very careful about taking on the Fed. Unless it literally wanted to take on a populist campaign that would drive the dollar down even further, we had to be very careful.
如果公众会支持这一倡议，甚至可能希望它发生，那么对 Jimmy Carter 政治未来的潜在影响可能无关紧要。1979年秋季，Jimmy Carter 已经显得严重受伤。他的公众声望仍然很低；他自己的政党关于他连任的前景存在深刻分歧。可以合理地假设，总统的命运可能已经由事件、他自己的错误和软弱以及坏运气所决定。无论如何，如果联邦储备的行动为总统的困境增加了一次大选年的衰退，这些行动不一定会引发针对 Fed 的政治攻击。衰退可能只是不重新选举 Jimmy Carter 的又一个原因。
Two months earlier, Carter had chosen Paul Volcker almost by accident. Now the President was implicitly surrendering control to him. The Federal Reserve would steer the national government’s economic policy now. Over the coming years, the Fed’s true role in the American system would become more and more visible to ordinary citizens, as Volcker asserted its leverage over economic life. He would be personally reviled and praised, recognized in public-opinion polls as the second most powerful person in the land. In hindsight, it would be understood that the autumn of 1979 was a historic juncture in the political course of the nation. What was not understood was that this crucial transaction occurred with an enfeebled President’s acquiescence, but not with his approval.
大理石楼梯顶部的董事会会议室是一个洞穴般的房间；沐浴在柔和的金色光芒中。墙壁覆盖着浅金色锦缎，一盏巨大枝形吊灯悬挂在金色链条上，远远高于地板，装饰着守护巨大金币的金色雄鹰图案。七位理事和十二位储备银行行长，加上几位专业职员，围坐在壁炉前巨大的桌子旁，在他们私密空间的令人敬畏的比例中显得渺小。
The episode also required deep political judgments by officials of the Federal Reserve. Beyond the tactical arguments, Volcker was implicitly making his own political assessment of the nation: an assumption that the nation was ready for what he intended to do, a sustained assault on inflation, including the economic pain that would come with it. Certainly that is what the bankers and financial markets wanted.
"我们被召集到华盛顿，而我们一无所知，"圣路易斯 Fed 行长 Larry Roos 说，"当我发现我所有曾经鄙视货币主义的同事们现在都支持货币主义方法时，我几乎从椅子上摔下来。"
If the general public would support the initiative, perhaps even wanted it to happen, then the potential impact on Jimmy Carter’s political future might be irrelevant. In the fall of 1979, Jimmy Carter already looked gravely wounded. His popular standing remained low; his own party was deeply divided about his prospects for a second term. It was plausible to assume that the President’s fate had perhaps already been settled by events, by his own errors and weakness and bad luck. In any case, if the Federal Reserve's actions added an election-year recession to the President’s troubles, such actions would not necessarily provoke political attacks aimed at the Fed. A recession might simply be one more reason not to re-elect Jimmy Carter.
联邦公开市场委员会这次非凡的星期六会议持续了一整天，Volcker 和其他人一遍又一遍地审查这项 watershed 决定的细节。主席本人扮演了勉强的倡导者角色，提出疑虑和问题，让其他成员消除它们。"Paul 很巧妙，"Fred Schultz 说，"我完全知道他在做什么。其他人最终与他争论，说服他去做。到了那天结束，他已经让他们完全承诺了。"
The boardroom at the top of the marble stairs was a cavernous chamber; subtly bathed in gold. The walls were covered in a pale-gold damask, and a monumental chandelier hung far above the floor on strands of golden chains, decorated with a motif of golden eagles guarding huge gold coins. The seven governors and twelve Reserve Bank presidents, joined by a few staff professionals, sat around the massive table before the fireplace, dwarfed by the awesome proportions of their private place.
在白宫，Charles Schultze 还没有完全放弃。他再次打电话给 Volcker，做了最后一次恳求——不要这样做。如果必须，就提高利率，但不要 locked 进一个保证衰退的刚性系统。然而，总统的顾问没有虚张声势。他私下告诉 Volcker，如果他一意孤行，白宫不打算公开攻击 Fed 主席。
“We were summoned to Washington and none of us knew a damned thing,” said Larry Roos, president of the St. Louis Fed. “I nearly fell out of my chair when I observed that all of my colleagues, many of whom had been disdainful, were now all supporting a monetarist approach.”
他们决定的含义对 FOMC 成员来说是清楚的。他们实质上是在投票使国民经济陷入收缩。这是预期的结果，Volcker 本人也向同事透露了他自己的悲观。
The extraordinary Saturday meeting of the Federal Open Market Committee lasted all day, as Volcker and others went over and over the details of the watershed decision. The chairman himself played the reluctant advocate, raising doubts and questions and allowing other members to dispel them. “Paul was masterful,” Fred Schultz said. “I knew exactly what he was doing. The others ended up arguing with him, talking him into doing it. By the end of the day, he had them fully committed.”
"其他人会对我说，'嗯，你知道这很可能导致衰退，'"Coldwell 理事说，"我告诉他们，'是的，我知道。但这就是在通胀方面走得太远你必须付出的代价。'董事会知道衰退会随之而来，这一点毫无疑问。"
At the White House, Charles Schultze had not quite given up. He phoned Volcker again and made one last plea not to do it. Raise interest rates, if you must, but don’t get locked into a rigid system that guarantees recession. The President’s adviser, however, did not attempt to bluster. The White House, he confided to Volcker, did not intend to attack the Fed chairman publicly if he proceeded.
当新闻界在下午晚些时候被召集时，联邦储备并未告诉美国公众这些。公告以金融交易员可能理解的技术性语言表述，但这些词语对普通公民甚至大多数政客毫无意义。关于 Fed 对美国经济的意图，什么也没有说。事实上，当一位记者问 Volcker 可能的结果是否是增长放缓和失业率上升时，Volcker 回避了问题，并隐藏了他的真实期望。
The import of their decision was clear to the FOMC members. They were voting, in effect, to put the national economy into a contraction. That was the expected outcome, and Volcker himself confided his own pessimism to colleagues.
"嗯，关于这个你会有不同的意见，"联邦储备主席回答说，"我不认为它会在那方面产生重要影响。我对这些行动的结果感到乐观……我认为我现在拥有的最佳迹象——在一个不确定的世界中——是这件事可以相当平稳地完成。"
“Others would say to me, ‘Well, you know this will likely cause a recession,’” Governor Coldwell said. “I told them, ‘Yes, I know that. But that’s the penalty you have to pay for going out too far on the inflation side.’ There wasn’t any question that the board knew that recession would follow.”
When the press was summoned in late afternoon, that is not what the Federal Reserve told the American public. The announcement was couched in technical language that financial traders might understand, but the words had no meaning for average citizens or even most politicians. There was nothing said about what the Fed intended for the U.S. economy. In fact, when a reporter asked Volcker if the likely result would be slower growth and higher unemployment, Volcker evaded the point and concealed his real expectations.
“Well, you get varying opinions about that,” the Federal Reserve chairman replied. “I don’t think it will have important effects in that connection. I would be optimistic about the results of these actions. ... I think the best indications that I have now, in an uncertain world, is that this can be accomplished reasonably smoothly.”
