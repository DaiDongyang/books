CHAPTER 17MORNING AGAIN IN AMERICA

PART FOUR: THE RESTORATION OF CAPITAL
When Paul Volcker appeared before the Senate Banking Committee in early February 1984, Senator William Proxmire reminded him, in a friendly way, of the Fed’s political history.
1984年2月初，当Paul Volcker出现在参议院银行委员会面前时，参议员William Proxmire以友好的方式提醒他美联储的政治历史。
“Chairman Volcker,” the senator said, “some have argued that the Federal Reserve has historically tried to help the incumbent in the White House during a presidential election year by easing monetary policy. There’s one conspicuous exception to that. In October 1979, the new chairman of the Federal Reserve Board instituted a policy of slowing down the rate of increase in the supply of money and I don’t think anybody could argue that Paul Volcker was a big help to Jimmy Carter in 1980. . . .
"Volcker主席，"参议员说，"有些人认为，美联储历来试图在大选年通过放松货币政策来帮助白宫的在任者。但有一个明显的例外。1979年10月，美联储理事会的新任主席实施了一项减缓货币供应量增长率的政策，我想没有人会说Paul Volcker在1980年对Jimmy Carter有多大帮助……"
“Do you have an understanding with the Administration,” Proxmire asked, “explicit or tacit, that you will follow an accommodative policy in 1984?”
"您是否与政府达成过谅解，"Proxmire问道，"无论是明确的还是默许的，即您将在1984年采取宽松政策？"
“No, sir,” the chairman answered.
"没有，先生，"主席回答。
“Will you agree to report to this committee and to the House Banking Committee any instances of the Administration bringing pressure on the Federal Reserve to relax its policies?”
"您是否同意向本委员会和众议院银行委员会报告政府向美联储施压要求其放松政策的任何情况？"
Volcker balked. He was in regular communication with Administration officials and properly so, the Fed chairman explained, but he did not interpret those conversations as political pressure.
Volcker犹豫了。他解释说，他与政府官员保持定期沟通是正当的，但他并不认为这些对话是政治压力。
“Well, Chairman Volcker, you obviously weren’t born yesterday,” the senator said. “I think you know political pressure when you see
"好吧，Volcker主席，您显然不是昨天才出生的，"参议员说。"我想您看到政治压力时是能认得出来的。"
“That’s right,” Volcker said. “That’s why I make a distinction. ...”
"没错，"Volcker说。"所以我才加以区分……"
Proxmire, the ranking Democrat, was simply inviting the Federal Reserve chairman to come to the Democratic Party for assistance if the Republicans in the White House began to play rough. Most of all, Democrats wanted Volcker to do no more for Ronald Reagan’s political fortunes than he had done for Jimmy Carter’s.
Proxmire，这位资深民主党人，只是在邀请美联储主席在共和党人开始粗暴行事时向民主党寻求帮助。最重要的是，民主党人希望Volcker为Ronald Reagan的政治命运所做的贡献不要超过他为Jimmy Carter所做的。
“Now I know you don’t think in political terms and you shouldn’t,” Proxmire told Volcker at another hearing. “But the fact is that this is an election year and I hope you will treat President Ronald Reagan with the same meticulous disregard for political consequences [with which] you treated President Carter. When Vince Lombardi was coach of the Green Bay Packers, a reporter asked one of the players whether Lombardi showed any unfairness in the treatment of players. The player told the reporter, ‘Listen, Coach Lombardi treats us all the same—like dogs.’ ’M
"我知道您不从政治角度思考问题，您也不应该这样做，"Proxmire在另一次听证会上告诉Volcker。"但事实是，今年是大选年，我希望您以对待President Carter同样那种对政治后果缜密漠视的态度来对待President Ronald Reagan。当Vince Lombardi执教绿湾包装工队时，一位记者问一名球员Lombardi是否在对待球员上表现出任何不公。该球员告诉记者：'听着，Lombardi教练对我们一视同仁——都像对狗一样。'"
Proxmire’s allusion to football was revealing in itself. The Federal Reserve chairman was cast as the coach. Elected politicians were merely players on the team. In fact, the situation sometimes resembled that relationship. The Federal Reserve chairman could not impose his will, but Paul Volcker regularly instructed Congress and the President on how he thought they should alter their economic policies. When politicians tried to do the same to the Federal Reserve, they were scolded in the press for trespassing on the Fed’s authority. Players were not supposed to criticize the coach, particularly this coach.
Proxmire对橄榄球的比喻本身就很有揭示性。美联储主席被塑造成教练的角色。当选的政治家只是球队中的球员。事实上，这种情况有时确实类似于那种关系。美联储主席不能强加他的意志，但Paul Volcker经常教导国会和总统应该如何改变他们的经济政策。当政治家试图对美联储做同样的事情时，他们就会因侵犯美联储的权力而受到媒体的谴责。球员不应该批评教练，尤其是这位教练。
“The political stock of the Federal Reserve right now is very high,” Frank Morris of the Boston Fed observed. “The country is breathing a great sigh of relief that we’ve got inflation behind us and the economy is going again. I don’t think this is the proper political season to be knocking the Federal Reserve.”
"美联储现在的政治资本非常高，"波士顿联储的Frank Morris观察到。"国家松了一口气，因为我们摆脱了通货膨胀，经济又开始运转了。我认为现在不是抨击美联储的合适政治季节。"
Most Democrats appeared to agree. Except for a few dedicated critics, Democrats were notably quiet about the Federal Reserve’s extraordinary strategy for restraining the recovery and preventing full employment. Perhaps, in their innermost thoughts, some wished the Fed to succeed. A sterner monetary policy, after all, would hurt the Republicans in ’84, just as it hurt the Democrats back in ’80.
大多数民主党人似乎同意这一点。除了少数坚定的批评者外，民主党人对美联储为抑制经济复苏和防止充分就业而采取的非常规战略保持了显著的沉默。也许，在他们内心深处，有些人希望美联储成功。毕竟，更严厉的货币政策会在84年伤害共和党人，就像80年伤害民主党人一样。
Many Republicans, on the other hand, thought that Volcker was already centrally engaged in the politics of 1984—playing his own rough game to achieve his own purpose. By keeping interest rates high, they suspected, the Fed intended to force the President and Congress to come to terms on major deficit reductions. That would require both spending cuts and tax increases in an election year, neither of which would be helpful to the incumbent President. Senator John Heinz of Pennsylvania confronted Volcker with the suspicions.
另一方面，许多共和党人认为Volcker已经深度参与了1984年的政治——为了达到他自己的目的而采取他自己的粗暴策略。他们怀疑，通过保持高利率，美联储打算迫使总统和国会在大幅削减赤字问题上达成一致。这将需要在选举年同时削减支出和增税，两者都对在任总统不利。宾夕法尼亚州参议员John Heinz带着这些疑虑质问Volcker。
“You’ve been in Washington long enough to know that when it comes to getting Congress to bite the tough bullet—in this case, spending—and when it comes to getting the President to bite a tough issue for him—taxes—there’s going to have to be a critical reason for the two aforementioned players to do it,” Heinz told Volcker. “And you’re the only person by following a restrictive, noninflationary monetary policy who can create that crisis.”
"您在华盛顿待得够久了，知道要让国会啃硬骨头——在这种情况下是削减支出——以及让总统啃对他而言的难题——增税——前述两个参与者需要一个关键的理由才能这样做，"Heinz告诉Volcker。"而您是唯一一个通过实行紧缩的、非通胀的货币政策来制造这场危机的人。"
“It is not our job to artificially provoke a crisis,” Volcker protested. “We are not going to go out there and conduct a tight-money policy for the sake of trying to bring leverage on the Congress or on the Administration.” 2
"人为地挑起危机不是我们的工作，"Volcker抗议道。"我们不会为了试图对国会或政府施加杠杆而实施紧缩货币政策。"
Volcker’s repeated complaints about deficits succeeded, however, in diverting the normal political debate, shifting the locus of blame away from monetary policy and toward fiscal policy. “You are playing a kind of game of Russian roulette,” Volcker warned the senators, vaguely allowing that the game might end in another recession. When the stock market heard his words, it dropped 24 points.
然而，Volcker对赤字的反复抱怨成功地转移了正常的政治辩论，将指责的重心从货币政策转向了财政政策。"你们在玩一种俄罗斯轮盘赌的游戏，"Volcker警告参议员们，含糊地暗示这场游戏可能以另一场经济衰退告终。当股市听到他的话时，下跌了24点。
“A masterful job of redefinition has been performed by the Fed,” E. F. Hutton told its clients. “It has essentially removed monetary policy from congressional debate. . . . Quite remarkably, high interest rates—which are correctly seen as a threat to this expansion’s long-term viability—have come to be associated with deficits, not with a tightfisted Fed.”3
"美联储已经完成了一次出色的重新定义工作，"E. F. Hutton告诉其客户。"它基本上已将货币政策排除在国会辩论之外……非常值得注意的是，高利率——这被正确地视为对本轮扩张长期活力的威胁——已经与赤字联系在一起，而不是与吝啬的美联储联系在一起。"
A guerrilla band of supply-side Republicans tried to reverse the focus by mounting a frontal attack on the Fed. Congressman Jack Kemp and like-minded conservative reformers accused Volcker of preparing to clamp down again on credit, just as the recovery appeared to be moderating, and thus risking yet another recession induced by monetary policy.4
一群供应学派共和党人试图通过发动对美联储的正面攻击来逆转这一焦点。众议员Jack Kemp和志同道合的保守派改革者指责Volcker准备再次收紧信贷，就在经济复苏似乎正在放缓之际，从而冒着再次由货币政策引发衰退的风险。
Volcker mostly ignored Kemp’s frequent complaints, but privately the chairman worried about the potential for larger political problems. The Fed was always more vulnerable when the political attacks came from the right. The conservative party was the natural reservoir of support for the central bank and “sound money.” Many traditional Republican conservatives kept their distance from the aggressive young supply siders like Kemp, but if the New York congressman gathered enough Republican supporters for reforming the Fed, they could make common cause with liberal Democrats and perhaps actually do something.
Volcker大多无视Kemp频繁的抱怨，但私下里，主席担心可能出现的更大政治问题。当政治攻击来自右翼时，美联储总是更容易受到攻击。保守党是中央银行和"稳健货币"的天然支持者堡垒。许多传统的共和党保守派与像Kemp这样的激进年轻供应学派保持距离，但如果这位纽约众议员聚集了足够多的共和党支持者来改革美联储，他们可能与自由派民主党人结成共同的事业，也许真的能做成一些事情。
Volcker suspected, moreover, that Jack Kemp was playing point man for the political managers in the White House. The congressman could say things about the Fed that the White House did not wish to say directly itself. The chairman worried aloud to associates that the Fed was being set up—in case anything went wrong with the economy and the President’s campaign.
此外，Volcker怀疑Jack Kemp是在为白宫的政治管理者们担任先锋。这位众议员可以说白宫自己不想直接说的话。主席向同事们公开担心，美联储正在被设局——以防经济出现任何问题以及总统的竞选出现问题时成为替罪羊。
The White House did, indeed, share Kemp’s nervousness about the recovery and the Federal Reserve, but it was not sure what to do about it. In early January, a few weeks before the congressman’s attack, Treasury Secretary Donald Regan wrote an unusual memorandum for the President, summarizing anxieties expressed by the Cabinet Council on Economic Affairs: “All members of the Cabinet Council agree that a sustained period of near zero growth in the money supply poses the strong threat of a recession sometime in 1984 and is clearly undesirable. However, the ambiguity of Federal Reserve policy intentions and the accuracy of the policy indicators create some uncertainty regarding the actual course of monetary policy.” Regan promised to keep the President posted, as the situation became clearer.5
白宫确实与Kemp一样对经济复苏和美联储感到紧张，但它不确定该如何应对。1月初，在众议员发起攻击的几周前，财政部长Donald Regan为总统撰写了一份不寻常的备忘录，总结了内阁经济事务委员会表达的焦虑："内阁经济事务委员会的所有成员都同意，货币供应量持续接近零增长对1984年某个时候出现经济衰退构成了严重威胁，这显然是不可取的。然而，美联储政策意图的模糊性以及政策指标的准确性造成了一些不确定性，难以判断货币政策的实际走向。"Regan承诺，随着情况变得更加明朗，他会随时向总统通报。
Representative Kemp, meanwhile, continued what the Democrats had abandoned—a legislative campaign to reform the Fed and reduce its political independence. “We need not sit by helplessly, awaiting a third economic recession engineered by the Fed in four years,” Kemp complained. “Congress is constitutionally authorized to coin money and regulate its value. The American people can act through Congress to bring monetary policy into line with the Administration’s growth-oriented fiscal policies by passing reform legislation.”6
与此同时，众议员Kemp继续着民主党已经放弃的事业——一场旨在改革美联储并削弱其政治独立性的立法运动。"我们不必坐以待毙，等待着美联储在四年内策划第三场经济衰退，"Kemp抱怨道。"宪法授权国会铸造货币并规范其价值。美国人民可以通过国会采取行动，通过改革立法，使货币政策与政府以增长为导向的财政政策保持一致。"
Dozens of Republicans, mostly younger members who admired Kemp’s style and ideas, signed up as cosponsors for his measure to change the Federal Reserve. Among other things, the Kemp bill would make the Secretary of the Treasury a member of the Federal Open Market Committee—restoring the original arrangement of 1913 that gave the Chief Executive direct access to the central bank’s deliberations and some influence over its decisions. The Republican reform legislation, though it never advanced, would be brandished from time to time like a threatening sword throughout the campaign season.
数十名共和党人，大多是钦佩Kemp风格和想法的年轻议员，签署成为他改革美联储法案的共同提案人。除其他事项外，Kemp法案将使财政部长成为联邦公开市场委员会的成员——恢复了1913年的原始安排，即赋予行政长官直接参与中央银行审议并对其决策施加一定影响的权力。这项共和党改革立法虽然从未取得进展，但会像一把威胁之剑在整个竞选季节被不时挥舞。
The Democratic Party, meanwhile, came to the Federal Reserve’s defense. When White House officials planted nasty gibes at Volcker in the press, leading members of the opposition party answered them. Senator Lloyd Bentsen of Texas, the chairman of the Senate Democratic Campaign Committee for the 1984 elections, warned: “I cannot help but reflect on the possibility that the Administration is trying to set up the Fed chairman as a scapegoat in case the economic recovery goes sour between now and the first Tuesday in November.” The Fed was doing a good job, the senator declared, and ought to be left alone.
与此同时，民主党人前来为美联储辩护。当白宫官员在媒体上对Volcker进行恶毒的挖苦时，反对党的主要成员给予了回击。德克萨斯州参议员Lloyd Bentsen，1984年选举参议院民主党竞选委员会的主席，警告说："我不禁想到，政府正试图把美联储主席当作替罪羊，以防经济复苏从现在到11月第一个星期二之间出现问题。"参议员宣称，美联储做得很好，应该让它独善其身。
Any problems that developed in the economy would be blamed on the President and the huge federal deficits that flowed from Reagan tax cuts in 1981 (tax cuts that, incidentally, Bentsen and most Democratic senators had voted for).7
经济中出现的任何问题都将归咎于总统和1981年Reagan减税所产生的大规模联邦赤字（顺便说一句，Bentsen和大多数民主党参议员都投票支持了那些减税）。
For the 1984 campaign, the historic positions of the two dominant political parties were reversed. The Democratic Party felt trapped by its own past, still tarnished by the inflationary anxieties experienced under Jimmy Carter, still tagged as the reckless “big spenders.” Democratic leaders decided they must convince the public of their newly developed sense of responsibility and so they campaigned for fiscal discipline. Invoking the party’s old liberal agenda—full employment, low interest rates, rapid economic growth, aggressive federal management of prosperity—might simply remind voters of the party’s old self.8
对于1984年的竞选，两个主要政党的历史立场发生了逆转。民主党感到被自己的过去所困，仍然被Jimmy Carter时期经历的通货膨胀焦虑所玷污，仍然被贴上鲁莽的"大支出者"标签。民主党领导人决定，他们必须让公众相信他们新培养出的责任感，因此他们为财政纪律而竞选。援引该党旧的自由主义议程——充分就业、低利率、快速经济增长、积极的联邦繁荣管理——可能只会让选民想起该党过去的自己。
The President’s party, meanwhile, had effectively abandoned the old Republican orthodoxy that the Democrats were now attempting to mimic. Republicans, it was true, remained steadfast in their rhetorical loyalty to the idea of a balanced federal budget and fiscal order. In actual performance, however, the conservative party had embraced the opposite—an economy driven by increased federal spending and increased debt, the most stimulative fiscal policy ever attempted in peacetime. The old Republican complaint—that deficit spending ultimately led to ruinous inflation—was conveniently discarded in the presence of Republican deficits.
与此同时，总统所在的政党已经有效地放弃了民主党现在正试图模仿的旧共和党正统观念。诚然，共和党人在言辞上仍然坚定地忠于平衡联邦预算和财政秩序的理念。然而，在实际表现中，这个保守党已经拥抱了相反的东西——一种由增加的联邦支出和增加的债务驱动的经济，这是和平时期尝试过的最具刺激性的财政政策。共和党人过去对赤字支出最终导致毁灭性通货膨胀的抱怨，在共和党赤字面前被方便地丢弃了。
The role reversal in the two political parties’ traditional philosophies was deeper than election-year posturing. Aside from the obvious hypocrisies, it at least suggested that both parties were breaking free of their traditional moorings and, for better or worse, casting about for new sets of beliefs. In terms of managing the economy, what did the Republican Party now stand for? Or the Democrats? It was no longer so easy to summarize either one.
两个政党传统哲学的角色互换比选举年的姿态更为深刻。除了明显的虚伪之外，这至少表明两党都在挣脱其传统的束缚，无论好坏，都在寻找新的信仰体系。就管理经济而言，共和党现在代表什么？或者民主党？要概括其中任何一个都不再那么容易了。
At the very least, new boundaries were being defined for the central debate of American politics: the government’s management of the economy through taxes and spending and federal deficits. If a conservative regime could accept $200 billion deficits with equanimity, what would be the new standard for imprudent management in the future —deficits of $300 or $400 billion? Certainly, neither party was soon going to seriously pursue the old Republican objective of balanced budgets. The twisted political alignments of 1984 rested on this paradox: the old “liberal” approach to political management of the economy, denounced as reckless and inflationary, supposedly discredited by two decades of failed promises, seemed to be working wonderfully well for the Republican Party.
至少，正在为美国政治的核心辩论——政府通过税收、支出和联邦赤字对经济的管理——划定新的界限。如果一个保守主义政权能够平静地接受2000亿美元的赤字，那么未来轻率管理的标准会是什么——3000亿或4000亿美元的赤字？当然，短期内两党都不会认真追求共和党人过去平衡预算的目标。1984年扭曲的政治联盟建立在这个悖论之上：旧有的"自由派"经济政治管理方式，被谴责为鲁莽和通货膨胀，据称因二十年失败的承诺而信誉扫地，似乎对共和党运作得非常出色。
Republicans, in fact, now held the symbolic high ground that the Democratic Party had once owned in national politics—the party of growth and prosperity. Through the campaign, the President would be the confident apostle of optimism and limitless opportunity, while the Democratic opposition assumed the role of public scold. The Democratic Party invoked “fairness” as its theme, but it muted the old liberal standards. This shift in emphasis was not entirely new. The Democrats had been shifting rightward in their economic perspective for some years, exemplified by the financial deregulation legislation of 1980. By 1984, instead of attacking the high interest rates, Democrats came to the defense of the Federal Reserve. Partisan stalemate was once again turned to the Fed’s advantage.
事实上，共和党现在占据了民主党曾经在全国政治中拥有的象征性高地——增长与繁荣的政党。在整个竞选过程中，总统将是乐观和无限机会的自信使者，而民主党反对派则扮演公共斥责者的角色。民主党以"公平"为主题，但淡化了旧的自由主义标准。这种侧重点的转变并非全新。民主党多年来一直在经济观点上向右转，1980年的金融放松管制立法就是例证。到1984年，民主党人不是攻击高利率，而是来为美联储辩护。党派的僵局再次变成了美联储的优势。
That fall voters asked a reasonable question—what was so bad about the huge deficits if the economy was flourishing?—and the Democrats never provided a convincing answer. To do so, they would have had to explain the Federal Reserve’s “game of chicken” and the consequences of high interest rates. Instead, their presidential nominee, the former Vice President, Walter Mondale, would bravely propose a painful remedy—a major tax increase and substantial budget reductions to restore fiscal order to Washington. For his part, President Reagan bravely proposed an endless vista of “good times” all across America.
那年秋天，选民们提出了一个合理的问题——如果经济繁荣，巨额赤字有什么不好？——而民主党从未给出令人信服的答案。要做到这一点，他们就必须解释美联储的"胆小鬼博弈"和高利率的后果。相反，他们的总统候选人，前副总统Walter Mondale，勇敢地提出了一种痛苦的补救措施——大幅增税和大幅削减预算以恢复华盛顿的财政秩序。而President Reagan则勇敢地提出了一幅全美各地"好时光"的无尽前景。
Preston Martin, the vice chairman of the Federal Reserve Board and the only Reagan appointee, did not act like a central banker. He was a gregarious western businessman, hearty and direct in manner, a man who sprinkled his conversation with expressions like “hell’s bells” and “my goodness.” Central bankers, Martin once joked, “are expected to look and act as if they are moving from one funeral to the next.”9 Preston Martin acted as though he was on his way to a family wedding.
Preston Martin，美联储理事会副主席，也是唯一由Reagan任命的人，表现得不像是位央行官员。他是一个善于交际的西部商人，态度热情直率，言谈中夹杂着"见鬼"和"我的天啊"之类的表达。Martin曾开玩笑说，央行官员"应该表现得像是从一个葬礼赶往另一个葬礼"。Preston Martin表现得像是正在赶往一场家庭婚礼。
Given his personal style, it was easy for Preston Martin’s colleagues at the Fed to underestimate him, though the vice chairman had ample practical experience in finance. He had been a housing developer and financier himself. He had chaired the Federal Home Loan Bank Board, which regulated the savings and loan industry. In Los Angeles, Martin had developed his own successful mortgage insurance company, which he had sold to Sears, Roebuck. As chairman of Sears’s Seraco Enterprises, Martin was instrumental in making Sears a major player in financial services, through purchase of the Dean Witter Reynolds brokerage.
鉴于他的个人风格，Martin在美联储的同事们很容易低估他，尽管这位副主席拥有丰富的金融实践经验。他本人曾是一名房地产开发商和金融家。他曾担任联邦住房贷款银行委员会主席，该委员会负责监管储蓄和贷款行业。在洛杉矶，Martin创办了自己成功的抵押贷款保险公司，并将其出售给了Sears, Roebuck。作为Sears旗下Seraco Enterprises的董事长，Martin通过收购Dean Witter Reynolds经纪公司，在使Sears成为金融服务领域主要参与者方面发挥了关键作用。
Still, as he was acutely aware, he did not speak the language of the System. “I became quite a bore,” Martin acknowledged. “I reminded them of the thrifts, of small business, of housing. I could see the ears turn off around the table when I made my little speech. But I made it anyway.”
然而，正如他敏锐地意识到的，他不会说美联储体系的"语言"。"我变得相当无趣，"Martin承认。"我向他们提醒储贷机构、小企业和住房问题。当我发表我的小演讲时，我能看到桌子周围的人耳朵都关上了。但我还是讲了。"
It wasn’t merely style. Martin saw things differently, and in the opening months of 1984, his perspective was strikingly at odds with the economic analysis of Volcker and other senior governors. They saw the economy roaring forward too fast. Martin saw it slowing down. They thought the Fed should assert some pressure with higher interest rates, lest things get out of control. Martin thought the economic recovery would soon need help and the Fed ought to be thinking about lowering interest rates, not raising them.
这不仅仅是风格问题。Martin看待事物的方式不同，在1984年最初几个月，他的观点与Volcker和其他资深理事的经济分析惊人地不一致。他们认为经济向前冲得太快了。Martin认为经济正在放缓。他们认为美联储应该通过提高利率施加一些压力，以免事态失控。Martin认为经济复苏很快就需要帮助，美联储应该考虑降低利率，而不是提高利率。
A staff officer of the Federal Open Market Committee observed:
联邦公开市场委员会的一名工作人员观察到：
Martin doesn’t have much influence. He always has to say something. His remarks will always be reflecting what is generally understood to be the Administration’s position. Or he will give an update on the thrifts and the housing industry, but it’s not particularly sophisticated. His mind operates on the level of platitudes, and he seems not to understand that a good analysis and argument can change minds.
Martin没有太大影响力。他总是得说点什么。他的言论总是反映通常被认为是政府立场的东西。或者他会介绍储贷机构和住房行业的最新情况，但并不是特别深入。他的思维停留在陈词滥调的层面，他似乎不明白好的分析和论证可以改变人们的想法。
“He speaks his piece,” said Charles Partee. “He doesn’t seem to me to be trying to bring a group of people with him. He just doesn’t structure his argument in a way that you could capture votes by what he’s saying. He’s usually isolated.”
"他说他的道理，"Charles Partee说。"在我看来，他并不是试图带动一群人跟他走。他只是没有以一种能够通过他的言论来赢得选票的方式构建他的论点。他通常是孤立的。"
The informal knowledge that Preston Martin wished to succeed Paul Volcker as Federal Reserve chairman did not enhance his influence around the boardroom table. When the vice chairman argued for an easier monetary policy, others inferred that he was simply ingratiating himself with the Reagan White House at Volcker’s expense. “It’s hard to figure what he’s doing and why,” an aide to Volcker said. “I can see some consistency in his arguing for an easier policy, but it’s not in his own interest to take issue with this particular chairman. Why do you want to pick a fight with Godzilla?”
关于Preston Martin希望接替Paul Volcker出任美联储主席的非正式共识，并没有提升他在理事会会议室周围的影响力。当副主席主张放松货币政策时，其他人推断他只是在以牺牲Volcker为代价讨好Reagan白宫。"很难弄清楚他在做什么以及为什么这样做，"Volcker的一名助手说。"我能看到他在主张放松政策方面有一定的一致性，但与这位特定的主席唱反调对他自己没好处。你为什么要找哥斯拉打架呢？"
Alone and discounted by his colleagues, Preston Martin persisted nonetheless in making the case for relaxing monetary policy. The economy, it was true, had accelerated in the first quarter of 1984, surprising everyone with the rapid expansion. Real GNP appeared to be growing at 6 to 7 percent, perhaps even faster. But Martin’s business soundings convinced him that sales and production would actually be tapering off in the weeks ahead. Another increase in interest rates at this point could be harmful.
尽管被孤立并被同事们看轻，Preston Martin仍然坚持主张放松货币政策。诚然，经济在1984年第一季度加速增长，其快速扩张令所有人惊讶。实际GNP似乎以6%至7%的速度增长，甚至可能更快。但Martin的商业触觉使他相信，未来几周内销售和生产实际上将逐渐减少。此时再次提高利率可能是有害的。
The moment of truth arrived at the March 26 meeting of the Federal Open Market Committee. On its face, it sounded like another dreary argument among economists over competing economic forecasts, but the stakes this time were much more fateful. In direct terms, the Federal Reserve governors were arguing over how much of the booming prosperity they were going to permit Americans to enjoy. The President’s generous campaign promises notwithstanding, the central bank would decide the question for itself. Was it time, in William McChesney Martin’s celebrated phrase, to take away the punch bowl, now that the party was going good? It was. In a deeper sense, the Fed’s governors were implicitly deciding a question of political power too. Did the Federal Reserve claim the authority to cut against all the obvious public aspirations for the unfolding prosperity? It did. And would it have the nerve? It would.
关键时刻在3月26日的联邦公开市场委员会会议上到来。表面上，这听起来像是经济学家之间关于相互竞争的经济预测的又一场沉闷的争论，但这一次的利害关系要重大得多。用直接的话来说，美联储的理事们正在争论他们将允许美国人享受多少繁荣昌盛的成果。尽管总统做出了慷慨的竞选承诺，但中央银行将自行决定这个问题。用William McChesney Martin那句著名的话来说，是不是到了该撤走潘趣酒杯的时候了，既然派对正在进行？是的。从更深层次的意义上说，美联储的理事们也在含蓄地决定一个政治权力的问题。美联储是否有权逆着公众对正在展开的繁荣的明显愿望而行？它有权。它有没有这个胆量？它有。
Preston Martin tried to make the case for keeping the robust expansion as vigorous as possible. His own reading of business conditions told him that the boom was about to subside anyway on its own. And there was virtually no sign anywhere that price inflation was about to resume. Why should the Fed step in and spoil things?
Preston Martin试图论证尽可能保持强劲扩张的理由。他对商业状况的判断告诉他，无论如何，繁荣很快就会自行消退。而且几乎没有任何迹象表明价格通胀即将重新抬头。美联储为什么要插手破坏好事？
I relied on the business data from the Federal Reserve Bank directors a bit more than some of my colleagues [Martin explained]. These directors are people from business who are not looking back; they’re looking forward. Their bonuses depend on whether those retail sales goals are realized. The forward indicators looked like this huge surge was calming down and it was going to be a more normal recovery. Inventories had come into line with sales. Wages were not threatening—this was the year of the labor give backs. The boom was coming down. Things really were under control. So I concluded that inflation’s not around the corner and we could afford not to tighten.
我比一些同事更多地依赖美联储银行董事提供的商业数据[Martin解释道]。这些董事来自商界，他们不是向后看；他们是向前看。他们的奖金取决于那些零售销售目标是否实现。前瞻性指标显示，这股巨大的激增正在平息，这将是一个更正常的复苏。库存已经与销售保持一致。工资没有构成威胁——这是劳工让步的一年。繁荣正在消退。一切确实在掌控之中。所以我得出结论，通胀不会马上到来，我们可以承受不收紧的代价。
Within the Board of Governors, Martin’s plea was almost bound to fail. Arguing the opposite case in the strongest terms was the second most influential member of the board, Lyle Gramley. “The economy is just growing too fast,” Gramley grumbled to associates, “and we let it happen.” The present growth rate would lead to trouble down the road. The Fed must ignore the risks of political attack and assert its restraint now. Gramley’s argument was aimed not at Preston Martin but at Paul Volcker.
在理事会内部，Martin的呼吁注定要失败。以最强有力的措辞论证相反观点的是理事会中第二有影响力的成员Lyle Gramley。"经济增长太快了，"Gramley向同事们抱怨道，"而我们却任其发生。"当前的增长速度会在未来导致麻烦。美联储必须无视政治攻击的风险，现在就实施紧缩。Gramley的论点不是针对Preston Martin，而是针对Paul Volcker的。
“Lyle Gramley is kind of the conscience of the Fed right now,” David Jones, the Fed watcher at Aubrey G. Lanston, explained. “Volcker is behind on where he should be in tightening. Gramley feels the same way. He’s very objective and he’s usually right.”10
"Lyle Gramley现在有点像美联储的良心，"Aubrey G. Lanston的美联储观察家David Jones解释道。"Volcker在紧缩方面落后于应有的位置。Gramley也有同样的感觉。他非常客观，而且他通常是对的。"
Gramley himself explained why he took a stern position: “If people say I’m a hawk, there is one reason for it—it’s an acute awareness that all of these gains can be lost. All of these costs could be for naught, if we don’t keep control of inflation.” Gramley was a “System man” and he remembered the 1970s when the Federal Reserve endured its most scornful criticism.
Gramley本人解释了他为什么采取严厉立场："如果说我是鹰派，那只有一个原因——那就是我敏锐地意识到所有这些成果都可能付诸东流。如果我们不能控制通货膨胀，所有这些代价都可能白费。"Gramley是一位"美联储体系的人"，他记得1970年代美联储曾遭受最轻蔑的批评。
For Gramley and like-minded governors, the choice could no longer be avoided. The FOMC had declined to alter credit conditions at its January meeting, and all the economic news since then told them they had grossly underestimated the force of the expansion. In fact, the economic news was spectacular.
对于Gramley和持相同观点的理事们来说，选择已经无法回避了。FOMC在1月份的会议上拒绝改变信贷条件，自那以来的所有经济消息都告诉他们，他们严重低估了扩张的力量。事实上，经济消息是惊人的。
Housing starts jumped in February to an annual rate of 2.2 million units, the strongest construction boom since 1978. Early in the month, auto sales were up 33 percent over the year before. The Commerce Department’s survey of businesses reported spending on new plants and equipment would increase in 1984 by a bullish 9.4 percent—then the estimate was revised upward to 13.6 percent. Unemployment was declining steadily, from 10 percent the summer before to 7.8 percent in March.
2月份住房开工率跃升至年化220万套，这是自1978年以来最强劲的建筑热潮。当月早些时候，汽车销量同比增长33%。商务部对企业的调查显示，1984年新厂房和设备的支出将增长9.4%，势头强劲——随后该估计被上调至13.6%。失业率稳步下降，从去年夏天的10%降至3月份的7.8%。
Paul Volcker worried publicly about the “very strong” economy and whether “bottlenecks” might develop in production and labor that could drive prices upward. “I don’t think it is in our interest to see it expand at a maximum rate for two or three quarters,” Volcker said, “if it is going to last only two or three quarters.”11
Paul Volcker公开担心"非常强劲"的经济，以及生产和劳动力领域是否可能出现"瓶颈"从而推高价格。"我认为，看到它以最高速度扩张两三个季度，然后只能持续两三个季度，这不符合我们的利益，"Volcker说。
The bond market was also distressed by the startlingly good news from the buoyant economy. Beginning in early February, long-term interest rates had begun to creep upward and the Fed had allowed market pressures to edge short-term rates higher too. Perhaps, the policy makers thought, this was the beginning of the long-predicted “crowding out”—a collision in credit markets between the federal government’s borrowing and the demand for loans from business and consumers.
债券市场也因经济繁荣带来的惊人好消息而备受困扰。从2月初开始，长期利率开始攀升，美联储允许市场压力也推高了短期利率。政策制定者认为，这可能是长期预测的"挤出效应"的开始——联邦政府借款与企业及消费者贷款需求在信贷市场上的碰撞。
Stable prices had been secured for more than a year and a half, yet the bond investors were still skeptical. Would the Fed accommodate or would it stand fast? The long-term investors still claimed to be worried that resurgent price inflation was imminent. By late March, the rate on twenty-year Treasury bonds had risen 80 basis points, to above 12 percent.
物价稳定已经保持了超过一年半，但债券投资者仍然持怀疑态度。美联储是会顺应形势还是坚守阵地？长期投资者仍然声称担心重新爆发的价格通胀即将来临。到3月下旬，二十年期国债收益率已上升80个基点，达到12%以上。
Wall Street cynics thought this was the point where the Fed might cave in—retreat before the normal political pressures of an election year and yield to faster economic growth. But others took Volcker at his word. David Jones predicted: “The Fed will be tougher than anyone thinks.” E. F. Hutton assured its clients: “The Fed will play brakeman for this expansion.”12
华尔街的愤世嫉俗者认为，这是美联储可能屈服的时刻——在大选年正常的政治压力面前退缩，屈从于更快的经济增长。但也有人相信Volcker的话。David Jones预测："美联储将比任何人想象的都要强硬。"E. F. Hutton向其客户保证："美联储将为这次扩张扮演刹车员角色。"
The Federal Reserve chairman had already received opposite instructions from the President. On February 15, Volcker was summoned to the Oval Office for another session intended to put him on notice. Like his earlier meetings with Ronald Reagan, this one was mostly generalities about money and economic growth and inflation. At one point, Volcker delivered one of his rambling meditations on the economy that was so contorted that afterward the President asked Donald Regan and James Baker what the man had said. Neither of them could say.
美联储主席此前已从总统那里收到了相反的指示。2月15日，Volcker被召到椭圆形办公室接受又一次旨在让他知悉的会谈。与他之前与Ronald Reagan的会面一样，这次会谈大多是关于货币、经济增长和通货膨胀的泛泛之谈。有一次，Volcker发表了他关于经济的长篇大论，其内容如此曲折费解，以至于事后总统问Donald Regan和James Baker那人说了什么。两个人都说不出个所以然。
The White House really delivered its intended message, afterward, in public, by pointedly alerting the press. The President, reporters were told, had asked Volcker to insure that monetary policy would not interrupt healthy economic growth in 1984. “All I am asking ... in monetary policy is that we have an increase that is commensurate with that growth that can continue the recovery without returning us to inflation,” the President told reporters.
事后，白宫通过公开场合向媒体明确示意的方式传达了其真正想要传达的信息。据报道，总统曾要求Volcker确保货币政策不会中断1984年健康的经济增长。"我要求的……只是货币政策方面的增长与那个增长相称，以便能够继续复苏，而不会让我们回到通胀，"总统告诉记者。
Anonymous White House aides put it somewhat more sharply: the Fed must not tighten. Volcker was told, in effect, “We don’t care if you err on the top side of the targets, but we do care if you err on the low side,” one senior official explained to The Wall Street Journal. Columnists Evans and Novak described the White House warning in the most melodramatic terms: “Reagan won’t send the Marines down Constitution Avenue to the Federal Reserve, but he seems determined to prevent the central bank from triggering another recession to calm inflationary nightmares of the creditor class.”
匿名的白宫助手们说得更尖锐一些：美联储不能收紧。"实际上，我们被告知，'我们不在乎你是在目标的上方还是下方犯错，但我们在乎你是否在低端犯错，'"一位高级官员向《华尔街日报》解释。专栏作家Evans和Novak以最耸人听闻的措辞描述了白宫的警告："Reagan不会派海军陆战队沿着宪法大道前往美联储，但他似乎决心阻止中央银行为了安抚债权人阶级的通胀噩梦而引发另一场衰退。"
A week later at a press conference, the President stated his objective more coherently: “I think the Federal Reserve is now on a path of a money-supply increase that is consistent with a sound recovery without inflation. To go one way in excess, they could cause more inflation and I don’t think they are planning that. They could go the other way, tighten the strings too much and interfere with the recovery, and I don’t think they’re going to do that.”
一周后在新闻发布会上，总统更连贯地阐述了他的目标："我认为美联储现在正处于一条货币供应量增长的道路上，这条道路与没有通胀的稳健复苏相一致。如果过分偏向一边，他们可能会导致更多通货膨胀，我不认为他们计划那样做。他们可能偏向另一边，收得太紧，干扰复苏，我不认为他们会那样做。"
Even these mild efforts to influence the Fed were scolded by the press. “The White House is stalking the Federal Board again,” a Washington Post editorial complained, “in the kind of maneuver that usually turns out to be clever but not wise.”13
即使是这些试图影响美联储的温和努力也受到了媒体的批评。"白宫又在追猎美联储了，"《华盛顿邮报》一篇社论抱怨道，"这种策略通常被证明是聪明而非明智的。"
Thus, when the FOMC met in late March, it was another of those excruciating moments when the Federal Reserve had to choose between its audiences. Which power center would the Fed respond to— the White House or the bond market? One side or the other was sure to be disappointed by the FOMC’s decision and possibly quite angry. Volcker’s old friend and mentor Robert V. Roosa of the investment-banking house of Brown Brothers Harriman described the dilemma: “They are really in a trap with anything they try to do in this situation.”14
因此，当FOMC在3月底开会时，这又是美联储不得不在其受众之间做出选择的痛苦时刻之一。美联储会回应哪个权力中心——白宫还是债券市场？无论哪一方，肯定会对FOMC的决定感到失望，甚至可能非常愤怒。Volcker的老朋友和导师，投资银行Brown Brothers Harriman的Robert V. Roosa描述了这一困境："在这种形势下，无论他们试图做什么，都真的陷入了一个陷阱。"
Inside the FOMC, Volcker was also in the middle. Lyle Gramley was arguing for sterner action than Volcker had in mind, given all the competing considerations in the “real world.” On the other side, Preston Martin, the only businessman on the board, was warning that tightening credit now could choke off the recovery prematurely. Martin’s warning sounded like a preview of what the governors expected to hear from Republicans if Martin was right.
在FOMC内部，Volcker也身处中间位置。考虑到"现实世界"中的各种竞争性因素，Lyle Gramley主张采取比Volcker所设想的更强硬的行动。另一方面，Preston Martin，理事会上唯一的商人，警告说现在收紧信贷可能会过早扼杀复苏。Martin的警告听起来像是理事们预料如果Martin是对的，他们会从共和党人那里听到的话的预演。
As usual, the debate was conducted not in the broad terms of national goals or social equity but in the esoteric language of monetary analysis. The governors’ arguments focused on the obscure measurement known as “capacity utilization,” a statistic meant to convey how much of America’s productive capacity was now active and how much remained idle. Gramley, joined by Wallich and Partee, argued that capacity utilization, though still quite low, was rising at a threateningly rapid rate. Unemployment was still high, they acknowledged, and prices were flat, but if the Fed did not move swiftly to restrain now, it might soon be too late. The economy would quickly reach its full capacity and another round of inflation could be touched off by the bidding for scarce labor, scarce goods.
像往常一样，辩论不是以国家目标或社会公平的广泛措辞进行的，而是以货币分析的精深语言进行的。理事们的论点集中在被称为"产能利用率"的晦涩指标上，这个统计数据旨在传达美国的生产能力中有多少正在运转，还有多少处于闲置状态。Gramley，得到Wallich和Partee的支持，认为产能利用率虽然仍然相当低，但正在以危险的速度上升。他们承认，失业率仍然很高，物价持平，但如果美联储不迅速采取行动加以抑制，可能很快就太晚了。经济将很快达到其全部产能，另一轮通货膨胀可能因对稀缺劳动力和稀缺商品的竞标而触发。
“Capacity utilization is a useless standard,” Preston Martin countered. The measure, as everyone knew, was based on notoriously unreliable data. More important, as Martin argued, the capacity level of domestic manufacturing was irrelevant in the global economy because U.S. companies and labor had to compete worldwide on prices. It made no sense, he pleaded, to imagine that American corporations and labor unions might somehow bid up wages and prices at the very time they were being hammered down by foreign competitors. Thanks to the rising dollar, American prices were being held down by the cheap imports—and the world had unused productive capacity that was vastly in excess. Martin’s analysis did not persuade.15
"产能利用率是一个无用的标准，"Preston Martin反驳道。众所周知，该指标基于出了名不可靠的数据。更重要的，正如Martin所论证的那样，在国内制造业的产能水平在全球经济中是无关紧要的，因为美国公司和劳工必须在全球范围内在价格上竞争。他恳求说，想象美国公司和工会会在恰恰被外国竞争对手压低的时刻竞相推高工资和价格，这是毫无意义的。由于美元升值，廉价进口商品压低了美国物价——而世界拥有远远过剩的闲置产能。Martin的分析未能说服人。
“The two groups had very different views of what was forthcoming in the economy,” Martin said. “It was pretty fundamental.” If the Fed tightened, Martin warned, the young economic recovery would be suppressed prematurely, robbed of its forward thrust. The “System men” were worried about the opposite—that there was too much momentum.
"两派对于经济即将发生什么有着截然不同的看法，"Martin说。"这是相当根本的分歧。"如果美联储收紧，Martin警告说，年幼的经济复苏将被过早压制，失去其向前的动力。"美联储体系的人"担心的是相反的情况——势头太强了。
“I could understand the strong feelings of Wallich and Gramley,” Martin said. “They had been in that building in the 1970s and they had seen inflation eat up values and they didn’t want to see that happen again.” Indeed, the memory of the seventies was invoked by Gramley and others in their arguments. The Fed must stand up now or risk the same ridicule later. To Preston Martin, they sounded like generals refighting the last war instead of the present one. “I haven’t seen signs of an overheated economy,” Martin declared. “I have seen overheated economists.”16
"我能理解Wallich和Gramley的强烈感受，"Martin说。"他们1970年代就在那座大楼里，他们看到通胀吞噬了价值，他们不想看到这种情况再次发生。"事实上，Gramley和其他人在他们的论证中援引了七十年代的记忆。美联储现在必须挺身而出，否则以后又要冒同样的风险。对Preston Martin来说，他们听起来像是在重打上一场战争而不是当前战争的将军。"我没有看到经济过热的迹象，"Martin宣称。"我看到了过热的的经济学家。"
The chairman agreed with Gramley and Wallich and the others. The Fed must brake. But Volcker did not wish to brake quite as hard as they proposed. Volcker chose the demands of the bond market over the political community’s, but, typically, he attempted to fashion a compromise between the two extremes. His tightening would likely upset the White House Republicans, but it would not upset them nearly as much as what Gramley and Wallich wanted. The move proposed by the chairman was intended to be more in the nature of what Frank Morris had called a “mid-course correction.” The FOMC directive would order a measured tightening of credit—pushing short-term interest rates up gradually, no more than 100 to 150 basis points.
主席同意Gramley、Wallich和其他人的意见。美联储必须刹车。但Volcker不想像他们提议的那样猛烈刹车。Volcker选择了债券市场的要求而不是政治界的要求，但一如既往，他试图在两个极端之间达成一种妥协。他的收紧措施可能会让白宫的共和党人不安，但远不及Gramley和Wallich想要的那样让他们不安。主席提出的举措更像是Frank Morris所称的"中期修正"。FOMC的指令将要求适度收紧信贷——逐步推高短期利率，不超过100至150个基点。
Since November 1982, the Fed had been keeping the Federal Funds rate remarkably stable. The prescribed target range was between 6 and 10 percent, and the interest rate for overnight reserve borrowing among banks had been averaging a little above 9.5 percent. Now Volcker proposed that the target range for the Fed Funds rate be raised to 11.5 percent, enough leeway to permit an increase of no more than 150 basis points in short-term interest rates if credit pressures required it. In addition, the FOMC had restored M-l as an operating target, now that money velocity had returned to a normal trend line, and the chairman’s directive proposed “greater restraint” if M-l grew faster than 6.5 percent.
自1982年11月以来，美联储一直保持着联邦基金利率的显著稳定。设定的目标区间在6%到10%之间，银行间隔夜储备借贷的利率平均略高于9.5%。现在Volcker提议将联邦基金利率的目标区间提高到11.5%，如果信贷压力需要，这足以允许短期利率最多上升150个基点。此外，FOMC恢复了M1作为操作目标，因为现在货币流通速度已回到正常趋势线，主席的指令提出，如果M1增长超过6.5%，将实行"更大程度的限制"。
That was not enough for Lyle Gramley. He thought the Fed should restrain more now or else face a worse choice later on, closer to the election. Henry Wallich agreed and they both dissented. It was Gram-ley’s first dissent in his four years on the Board of Governors. “I don’t dissent,” he said, “unless it’s really a fundamental disagreement, a feeling that the course of policy is really a mistake.”
这对Lyle Gramley来说还不够。他认为美联储现在应该进一步收紧，否则以后临近选举时将面临更糟糕的选择。Henry Wallich同意他的看法，两人都投了反对票。这是Gramley在理事会任职四年来第一次投反对票。"我不会反对，"他说，"除非真的是根本性的分歧，感觉政策的方向确实是个错误。"
Preston Martin dissented too, but for opposite reasons.
Preston Martin也投了反对票，但原因相反。
Every time I dissented, people would say I was doing it because I was a Reagan appointee and was worried about the election [he said]. I was very careful not to have contact, either by telephone or in person, with my California friends, with the White House or Treasury during that period because I knew people would think it was political. But that’s what they said, understandably. I would probably have said the same thing. It goes with the territory.
每次我投反对票，人们都会说我是因为我是Reagan任命的人，而且担心选举[他说]。那段时间我非常小心，避免与我的加州朋友、白宫或财政部有任何联系，无论是电话还是当面，因为我知道人们会认为那是政治性的。但那就是他们说的，可以理解。我可能也会说同样的话。这是地盘的一部分。
The effects of the Fed’s tightening were visible almost immediately in the financial markets. The Federal Funds rate moved up about 50 basis points a week after the meeting. The key interest rate also bounced up and down more erratically, a signal to Fed watchers that the Open Market Desk had now returned to targeting the supply of money reflected by M-l and the other aggregates. That meant interest rates would be allowed to fluctuate more freely, pushed up or down by the demand pressures in the money market.
美联储紧缩的影响几乎立即在金融市场上显现出来。会议一周后，联邦基金利率上升了约50个基点。关键利率也更不规则地上下波动，这向美联储观察家发出信号，表明公开市场操作台现在已恢复以M1和其他总量指标所反映的货币供应量为目标。这意味着利率将被允许更自由地波动，由货币市场的需求压力推动上下浮动。
The consequences were not moderate, however. The move was more like applying a choke-hold to interest-sensitive economic sectors, just as they were charging forward. The mortgage interest rate moved up sharply from around 13 percent a month later. By June, it was at 15 percent and the housing boom retreated swiftly from its February peak. Rates on auto loans went up, too, and auto sales declined accordingly. The economic expansion was confronted now with tighter credit, a rising price for borrowed money. Even though the unemployment rate was still close to 8 percent, the Federal Reserve was applying the brakes.
然而，后果并不温和。这一举措更像是给对利率敏感的经济部门施加了扼喉之锁，而它们才刚刚开始加速前进。一个月后，抵押贷款利率从大约13%急剧上升。到6月，达到了15%，住房繁荣从2月份的峰值迅速回落。汽车贷款利率也上升了，汽车销量相应下降。经济扩张现在面临着更紧的信贷和不断上升的借贷成本。尽管失业率仍接近8%，美联储却在踩刹车。
The bond market was pleased. “The deceleration of the economy is welcome news to the bond market, which has been battered for several weeks,” Shearson American Express informed its investor clients. “ . . . Despite all the talk of a tighter policy at the Federal Reserve, the monetary authorities have been slow to take decisive action. . . .”17
债券市场感到满意。"经济减速对债券市场来说是个好消息，该市场已经遭受了几周的打击，"Shearson American Express通知其投资者客户。"……尽管关于美联储采取更紧缩政策的所有讨论，货币当局在采取果断行动方面一直很迟缓……"
But the Federal Reserve’s decision to play brakeman proved to be much more than a “mid-course correction.” It effectively marked the end of Ronald Reagan’s boom. It drew a line across the future’s potential and defined the limits for the unfolding prosperity. The vigorous recovery that had begun eighteen months before would lose its strength rapidly—just as Preston Martin had warned—and that strength would never be regained.
但美联储扮演刹车员的决定被证明远不止是一次"中期修正"。它实际上标志着Ronald Reagan繁荣的终结。它为未来的潜力划出了一条线，并界定了正在展开的繁荣的极限。十八个月前开始的强劲复苏将迅速失去其动力——正如Preston Martin所警告的那样——而这种动力将永远无法恢复。
Such a momentous decision by the government—a decision to halt the surging economy—might have been expected to provoke great controversy, especially since the decision was made by an obscure group of unelected technocrats in defiance of the President’s wishes and in the midst of an election campaign. Yet there was no public outcry. The “brakeman” was allowed to throw the switch in virtual privacy. Of all the institutions of Washington, none was more deferential toward the Federal Reserve than the press. The news media did not report the significance of what the Fed was doing to the economy, not then and not even later when the effects of the Fed’s tightening were fully visible.
政府做出的如此重大的决定——一个停止经济迅猛增长的决定——本应引起巨大争议，尤其是因为这个决定是由一群默默无闻的非选举技术官僚在违背总统意愿的情况下、在竞选期间做出的。然而，没有公众的抗议。"刹车员"被允许在几乎是秘密的状态下扳动开关。在华盛顿的所有机构中，没有比媒体对美联储更为恭敬的了。新闻媒体没有报道美联储正在对经济做什么的重要性，当时没有，甚至在后来美联储紧缩的效果完全显现时也没有。
Within two months, unemployment would reach its low point— stuck at 7.1 percent—and then begin to creep back upward. Manufacturing’s capacity utilization, which had so worried the senior governors, peaked shortly thereafter—far below its potential—and began declining. The dollar’s international value was driven still higher by the increase in U.S. interest rates, and the competitive damage was deepened for all the trade-sensitive sectors of the American economy, from farming to autos to machine tools. Within a matter of months, the Federal Reserve would find itself struggling with the opposite problem—trying to re-stimulate the economy to avert a recession.
两个月内，失业率达到了最低点——停留在7.1%——然后开始缓慢回升。制造业的产能利用率（曾让资深理事们如此担忧）此后不久达到顶峰——远低于其潜在水平——并开始下降。美元的国际价值因美国利率的上升而被推得更高，对美国经济中所有贸易敏感部门的竞争力损害加深了，从农业到汽车到机床。不出几个月，美联储将发现自己正与相反的问题作斗争——试图重新刺激经济以避免衰退。
Preston Martin, the businessman disparaged for his platitudes and sloppy thinking, had been right about the future. The economy decelerated more abruptly than any of the others expected. The Fed’s decision to raise interest rates was, as Martin had warned, braking an engine that was already slowing down.
Preston Martin，那个因其陈词滥调和思维混乱而被轻视的商人，对未来的判断是对的。经济减速比任何其他人预期的都要突然。正如Martin所警告的那样，美联储提高利率的决定，是在给一台已经正在减速的引擎踩刹车。
The technocrats who ran the central bank were not only fallible— “not infrequently wrong,” as Lyle Gramley once admitted—but they also seemed incapable of listening seriously to competing perspectives. Inside the Fed, Martin’s opinions were dismissed as politically inspired—unscientific—much as Nancy Teeters’ dissents had been discounted during the long recession because she was regarded as a “knee-jerk liberal.” Yet Martin’s analysis of what lay ahead for the American economy proved to be more accurate than the expert forecasts from Paul Volcker, Lyle Gramley, Henry Wallich and the Fed’s other respected voices.
管理中央银行的这些技术官僚不仅会犯错误——"经常出错"，正如Lyle Gramley曾经承认的那样——而且他们似乎也无法认真倾听相反的观点。在美联储内部，Martin的意见被认为是出于政治动机——不科学——被否定了，就像Nancy Teeters的不同意见在漫长的经济衰退期间被轻视一样，因为她被视为一个"条件反射式的自由派"。然而，Martin对美国经济前景的分析被证明比Paul Volcker、Lyle Gramley、Henry Wallich和美联储其他受人尊敬的声音的专业预测更为准确。
“I was the only one dissenting on that side,” Martin reflected, “and I was right. My God, we just couldn’t afford to have an eighteen-month expansion, but it was already slowing down.”
"我是唯一在那个方面投反对票的人，"Martin回忆道，"而我是对的。天哪，我们不能只满足于十八个月的扩张，但当时它已经在放缓了。"
If anything, Preston Martin became even less popular among his colleagues at the Federal Reserve Board. They did not concede that he had been correct in March. Soon Martin was making even more dire forecasts about where the economy was headed.
如果说有什么变化的话，Preston Martin在美联储理事会的同事中变得更加不受欢迎。他们不承认他在三月份是正确的。很快，Martin开始对经济的走向做出更加可怕的预测。
That’s when I began to talk about a “growth recession” [Martin said]. My colleagues didn’t like that phrase. It doesn’t have a lot of study behind it; it’s kind of a journalistic phrase. But that is what we were headed for —a growth recession. Unemployment began creeping up again and we didn’t have much real growth. We don’t go into a recession, but we also have growth of 2 percent or less.
就是从那时起我开始谈论"增长型衰退"[Martin说]。我的同事们不喜欢这个词。它背后没有大量的研究支撑；它有点像新闻用语。但这就是我们正在走向的——一场增长型衰退。失业率开始再次攀升，而我们的实际增长并不多。我们没有进入衰退，但我们的增长也只有2%或更少。
As the recovery deteriorated, Preston Martin continued to dissent against Paul Volcker’s management of money, usually alone. Each time, he was characterized in the press (and in the hallway gossip at the Fed) as politically ambitious, a tool of the White House. But if one searched through the policy record of the Federal Reserve during the Reagan recovery, Preston Martin’s dissents were like a crude but reliable leading indicator for turns in the economy. His worries usually came true. Certainly, he was consistently closer to the mark than the other assembled experts at the Federal Reserve Board.
随着复苏的恶化，Preston Martin继续对Paul Volcker的货币管理投反对票，通常只有他一个人。每一次，他在媒体上（以及在美联储走廊里的闲谈中）都被描述为有政治野心，是白宫的工具。但如果仔细梳理Reagan复苏期间美联储的政策记录，Preston Martin的反对意见就像是一个粗糙但可靠的领先指标，预示着经济的转折。他的担忧通常都成真了。当然，他一贯比美联储理事会其他聚集的专家更接近真相。
The vice chairman seemed good-humored about his lonely position and the occasional slurs. If he resented the fact that Fed colleagues ignored his opinions, he kept the resentment to himself. He did not try to adapt to their methods of analysis or accept their perspective. Meeting after meeting, the vice chairman continued to deliver the same rambling speeches, knowing that others would pay little attention.
这位副主席似乎对他孤独的立场和偶尔的诋毁保持乐观。如果他因为美联储同事忽视他的意见而心怀怨恨，他也把怨恨藏在心里。他没有试图适应他们的分析方法或接受他们的观点。一次又一次的会议，副主席继续发表同样的长篇大论，明知其他人很少会注意。
The central bank had its own unwritten protocol and Preston Martin, with a bitter edge in his voice, observed it. “You don’t go back to your colleagues and say: T told you so. I told you so in March,’” Martin explained. “Oh, no, you never, never do that.”
中央银行有自己不成文的规矩，Preston Martin带着一丝苦涩的语气遵守着它。"你不能回到同事面前说：'我告诉过你们。我在三月份就告诉过你们，'"Martin解释道。"哦，不，你永远、永远不能那样做。"
Governor Charles Partee sounded defensive, but did not apologize for the Federal Reserve’s decision. “You can look back in retrospect and say now, of course, knowing the economy grew much more slowly from that point on, you can say, ‘Sure, we could have let it grow a little longer,’” Partee conceded. “But, as a principle of monetary management, I think it was correct to slow things down.”
理事Charles Partee听起来有些防卫性，但没有为美联储的决定道歉。"你可以事后回顾，现在当然知道经济从那以后增长得更慢，你可以说'当然，我们可以让它多增长一会儿，'"Partee承认。"但作为货币管理的一个原则，我认为放缓事态是正确的。"
Paul Volcker was unwilling to concede even that much.
Paul Volcker甚至不愿意承认那么多。
In their own narrow terms, perhaps Volcker and Partee were right. It wasn’t a mistake if one accepted the paternal values that guided the Federal Reserve. This was its role in life, to decide what was good for others, even when it knew that the others might disagree. To the Fed, therefore, it did not seem wrong to break the booming economy, just as Americans were enjoying the new glow of prosperity. It was the Federal Reserve’s responsibility to maintain order and so it did.
用他们自己狭隘的标准来看，也许Volcker和Partee是对的。如果接受指导美联储的家长式价值观，那不是一个错误。这就是它在生活中的角色——为他人决定什么是最好的，即使它知道他人可能不同意。因此，对美联储来说，打破蓬勃发展的经济似乎没有错，尽管美国人正在享受繁荣的新光芒。维护秩序是美联储的责任，所以它这样做了。
The governors, it was true, had been mistaken in the economic analysis that led them to the fateful decision, but it was not simply their mistakes. Every agent of government will make mistakes at one time or another, just as every human errs. The heart of the matter was the autocratic mind-set of the institution itself, the peculiar perspective that nearly always led the Federal Reserve in the same direction, toward the same sort of choice.
诚然，理事们在导致这一决定性决策的经济分析中犯了错误，但这不仅仅是他们个人的错误。每一个政府机构都会时不时地犯错，就像每个人都会犯错一样。问题的核心是该机构本身的专制心态，这种特殊的视角几乎总是引导美联储朝着相同的方向，做出相同类型的选择。
The policy makers were voting, after all, to avoid the one kind of economic risk that mattered most to their institution—the risk to money—and to authorize other kinds of economic loss—lost jobs and incomes, lost production and opportunity, lost equity in society. Even though their fears of inflation proved utterly unfounded, even though the real economy suffered grievously as a result, this was the posture of caution that the Federal Reserve would always find most comfortable. It was the choice its own constituencies in finance and banking would always applaud. In their own minds, central bankers could never be wrong in principle so long as they came down on the side of money.
毕竟，政策制定者们投票是为了避免对其机构最重要的一种经济风险——对货币的风险——并授权其他种类的经济损失——失去的工作和收入、失去的生产和机会、社会中失去的公平。尽管他们对通胀的恐惧被证明完全没有根据，尽管实体经济因此遭受了严重损失，但这正是美联储始终感到最舒适的谨慎姿态。这是其在金融和银行业的选民们总会为之喝彩的选择。在他们自己看来，只要央行官员站在货币一边，他们在原则上就永远不会错。
If any single moment could convey the larger political meaning of the Federal Reserve, perhaps this was such a moment. Baldly stated, the Fed’s decision meant that the demands of capital triumphed and democracy lost. Behind the economics, money management was usually a choice over whom to please and whom to disappoint, a political question of who would be made to take the loss, wealth holders or wage earners. In this pivotal instance, as it had done so often in its history, the central bank was deciding to defend wealth and let workers take the loss. The financial side of capitalism would be protected from risk, even excessively enhanced. Enterprise in the productive economy would be restrained, even injured. Opportunities would be canceled, ambitions checked, so that the owners of money would feel more secure about their wealth. The Federal Reserve’s ideological values always were inclined toward that choice.
如果有一个单一时刻能够传达美联储更大的政治含义，也许这就是这样的时刻。直白地说，美联储的决定意味着资本的要求获胜了，而民主失败了。在经济学的背后，货币管理通常是一种取悦谁和让谁失望的选择，一个谁将被要求承受损失的政治问题——是财富持有者还是工薪阶层。在这个关键的例子中，正如在其历史上经常发生的那样，中央银行决定捍卫财富而让工人承受损失。资本主义的金融方面将受到保护，免于风险，甚至被过度增强。生产性经济中的企业将受到限制，甚至受到损害。机会将被取消，雄心将被遏止，以便货币所有者对他们的财富感到更安全。美联储的意识形态价值观总是倾向于这种选择。
The orderly world described in economics textbooks was, thus, inverted. Economics taught that finance existed to facilitate business and commerce, that the real economy was the core of capitalism and that finance merely served it like a veil. When money's value was given paramount priority, the order was turned upside down. The diverse energies of the real economy, the vast aspirations of Americans generally, would now be suppressed in order to protect the interests of money.
因此，经济学教科书中描述的有序世界被颠倒了。经济学教导说，金融的存在是为了促进商业和贸易，实体经济是资本主义的核心，而金融只是像一层纱一样为其服务。当货币的价值被赋予最高优先权时，秩序就被颠倒了。实体经济多样的能量，美国人广泛的抱负，现在将为了维护货币的利益而被压制。
The civic mythology of representative democracy was also exquisitely mocked by these events. In the middle of a presidential election season, the President was impotent. It was not his decision and he could not stop the Federal Reserve from making it. The elected representatives in Congress were not even consulted. Neither branch could object in candid terms without accentuating its own weakness. The American public could hardly protest either, for the public did not know that the unelected government in Washington had decided to end the boom.
代议制民主的公民神话也被这些事件巧妙地嘲弄了。在总统大选季节的中期，总统是无能为力的。这不是他的决定，他也不能阻止美联储做出这个决定。国会中的民选代表甚至没有被征求意见。两个分支都不能坦率地反对，而不凸显其自身的软弱。美国公众也几乎无法抗议，因为公众不知道华盛顿的非选举政府已经决定结束繁荣。
Jim Baker was furious and said so in the bluntest terms. The President’s chief of staff was attending a private dinner party, surrounded by friends and White House associates. His dinner partners, including Walter Wriston of Citibank, were aghast as Baker vented his anger at Paul Volcker.
Jim Baker怒不可遏，并以最直白的方式表达了这一点。总统的幕僚长正在参加一个私人晚宴，周围都是朋友和白宫同事。当Baker发泄他对Paul Volcker的愤怒时，他的晚餐伙伴，包括Citibank的Walter Wriston，都惊呆了。
“We made a deal,” Baker said, according to one who was at his table. “The deal was that if we got the budget under control, the Fed would ease up. That’s our part of the bargain. We did our part and now the Fed is double-crossing us. The Fed is tightening.”
"我们达成了协议，"Baker说，据在场的一位人士说。"协议是，如果我们控制住预算，美联储就会放松。那是我们那一方的协议。我们履行了我们的部分，现在美联储在欺骗我们。美联储在收紧。"
The White House had not actually gotten the budget under control, but Baker had worked hard at it and made some progress. After much negotiating and arranging, Baker had persuaded the reluctant President to endorse a $150 billion three-year deficit reduction package, including what Ronald Reagan most resisted, tax increases totaling $48 billion. “A down payment” on the deficits, the President called it. The Administration’s deficit proposal was a long way from congressional approval (and most of it would be abandoned in the pressures of election-year politics), but Baker still felt “double-crossed” by Paul Volcker.
白宫实际上并没有控制住预算，但Baker为此付出了努力并取得了一些进展。经过大量的谈判和安排，Baker说服了不情愿的总统支持一项1500亿美元的三年赤字削减计划，其中包括Ronald Reagan最抵制的——总计480亿美元的增税。总统称之为赤字的"首付"。政府的赤字提案距离国会批准还有很长的路要走（其中大部分将在选举年政治压力下被放弃），但Baker仍然感到被Paul Volcker"欺骗"了。
James Baker had assumed control over most of the major issues in the Reagan White House, including surveillance of the Fed’s monetary policy. Theoretical arguments among the Administration’s competing economists continued to swirl around him, but Baker decided what the White House would say or do. Without title, he was also functioning as campaign manager for the President’s re-election and the Federal Reserve was one of his principal anxieties.
James Baker已经控制了Reagan白宫中大多数主要事务，包括对美联储货币政策的监督。政府内部相互竞争的经济学家之间的理论争论继续在他周围进行，但由Baker决定白宫会说什么或做什么。虽然没有头衔，但他也在担任总统连任的竞选经理，而美联储是他主要的焦虑之一。
“Baker had the general worries of a full-time political manager,” David Stockman said. “He thought he had a commitment that nobody was going to screw up the economy in the election year and now everybody was telling him that the numbers were bad. He didn’t want anything that would screw up the economic environment, that’s all.”
"Baker有一个全职政治经理的一般性担忧，"David Stockman说。"他认为自己得到了承诺，没有人会在选举年搞砸经济，但现在所有人都告诉他数字很糟糕。他不希望有任何会破坏经济环境的事情，仅此而已。"
On April 6, the Federal Reserve Board raised its Discount rate to 9 percent, the first change since December 1982. The Discount increase followed in step with the tightening that the Federal Open Market Committee had already authorized. The White House had a sense of what the Fed was doing, but it could not easily react to vague suspicions. On Tuesday, May 8, a tangible event presented itself. Commercial banks raised the prime rate for the second time in six weeks. The White House jumped on the Fed.
4月6日，美联储理事会将贴现率提高到9%，这是自1982年12月以来的首次变化。贴现率的提高紧随联邦公开市场委员会已经授权的紧缩步伐。白宫对美联储正在做的事情有所感觉，但它无法轻易对模糊的怀疑做出反应。5月8日星期二，一个具体的事件出现了。商业银行在六周内第二次提高了优惠利率。白宫对美联储发难了。
“We are disappointed that the prime interest rate has increased to 12.5 percent,” Larry Speakes, the White House press spokesman, declared. “Although the economy has been growing at a healthy pace and inflation remains at a low level, it appears the money supply is not accommodating real economic growth.”
"我们对优惠利率已升至12.5%感到失望，"白宫新闻发言人Larry Speakes声明。"尽管经济一直以健康的速度增长，通胀仍处于较低水平，但似乎货币供应量并未适应实际经济增长。"
Baker and Richard Darman, his deputy, sharpened the criticism of the Fed, speaking as the anonymous “senior officials” who were quoted in various press accounts. Other Administration officials were instructed to join the chorus.
Baker和他的副手Richard Darman强化了对美联储的批评，以匿名"高级官员"的身份出现在各种新闻报道中。其他政府官员被指示加入合唱。
For Treasury Secretary Donald Regan, this required a sudden turnabout. On Sunday, he had appeared on a television talk show and declared that the Fed was doing a good job. On Wednesday, Regan attacked the Fed. “You bet your life this has us worried,” the Treasury Secretary told reporters. Commerce Secretary Malcolm Baldrige added his voice. “I hope the Fed is looking far enough ahead,” Bald-rige said.
对于财政部长Donald Regan来说，这需要一个突然的转变。周日，他出现在一个电视谈话节目中，宣称美联储做得很好。周三，Regan攻击了美联储。"你当然可以打赌这让我们担心，"财政部长告诉记者。商务部长Malcolm Baldrige也加入了。"我希望美联储看得足够远，"Baldrige说。
By Friday, Donald Regan was warning that the Fed might produce another recession if it did not swiftly change its monetary policy. “This is not a campaign against the Fed,” Regan said. “It’s a campaign against high interest rates.”
到了周五，Donald Regan警告说，如果美联储不迅速改变其货币政策，它可能会引发另一场衰退。"这不是一场针对美联储的运动，"Regan说。"这是一场反对高利率的运动。"
The Administration’s frantic flurry of “Fed bashing” was orchestrated by Baker and Darman, who figured that a public campaign to intimidate Volcker might as well be tried, since private persuasion had clearly failed. At the very least, the White House officials hoped the public criticism would establish a “political predicate” for finger pointing later if the economy did fall apart before the election. “There’s a pure political purpose, in terms of laying off blame,” an Administration official explained.18
政府这场疯狂的"抨击美联储"浪潮是由Baker和Darman策划的，他们认为既然私下说服显然已经失败，不妨试试公开运动来恐吓Volcker。至少，白宫官员希望公开批评能为选举前经济真的崩溃时事后指责任意"政治铺垫"。"这有一个纯粹的政治目的，就是为了转嫁责任，"一位政府官员解释道。
Aside from the political objective, Baker and Darman also thought they were right about the economics. For months, they had been listening more attentively to the supply-side advocates, Jack Kemp and Jude Wanniski, argue that Volcker’s tight-money strategy could stall out the recovery right in the middle of the campaign. Now events seemed to be confirming the warnings. Within the Reagan Administration, the influence of the competing ideological camps had shifted again. Monetarist advisers, who had been so influential back in 1981 urging a stern monetary policy, were now eclipsed. The supply siders, whose earlier complaints about the Fed had been ignored, were ascendant. At least Baker and Darman took their warnings seriously.
除了政治目的外，Baker和Darman也认为他们在经济上是对的。几个月来，他们一直在更密切地倾听供应学派倡导者Jack Kemp和Jude Wanniski的观点，他们认为Volcker的紧缩货币策略可能会在竞选正酣时使复苏停滞。现在事件似乎证实了这些警告。在Reagan政府内部，相互竞争的意识形态阵营的影响力再次发生了转变。曾在1981年提倡严厉货币政策时如此有影响力的货币主义顾问，现在被边缘化了。供应学派，他们之前对美联储的抱怨被忽视了，现在占据上风。至少Baker和Darman认真对待了他们的警告。
“I said, let’s forget about those monetary aggregates, let’s keep sufficient growth to keep this wonderful recovery going,” an Administration official explained. “There isn’t any sign that inflation is coming back and there’s a serious risk that the Fed is too tight. What I meant was that there was room for additional growth and no sign of inflation. I think I was right about that. Volcker wasn’t letting the recovery go far enough.”
"我说，让我们忘记那些货币总量吧，让我们保持足够的增长以维持这个美妙的复苏继续下去，"一位政府官员解释道。"没有任何迹象表明通胀会回来，而美联储过紧存在严重风险。我的意思是，还有进一步增长的空间，也没有通胀的迹象。我想我在这点上是对的。Volcker没有让复苏走得足够远。"
The economic argument made by the White House political advisers was perfectly plausible, a legitimate criticism of the Fed’s policy that turned out to be correct. “If you looked at all the economic factors,” Richard Darman said, “any reasonable person could conclude that there was no inflationary risk in loosening and substantial danger on the other side. We had the merits on our side. It was politically desirable, but it was also reasonable economics.”
白宫政治顾问们的经济论点完全合理，是对美联储政策的合法批评，而且结果证明是正确的。"如果你看所有的经济因素，"Richard Darman说，"任何理性的人都能得出结论，放松没有通胀风险，而另一面存在重大危险。我们的理由站得住脚。这在政治上可取，但在经济上也是合理的。"
Yet Volcker brushed the White House comments aside as political complaints. The Fed was used to attack in election seasons. Politicians were expected to defer to the higher judgment of economics. That standard was enforced by the news media, which, once again, criticized the White House for meddling in the Fed’s decisions.
然而，Volcker把这些白宫的评论当作政治抱怨置之不理。美联储习惯了大选季节受到攻击。政客们应该听从经济学的更高判断。这个标准由新闻媒体执行，它们再次批评白宫干预美联储的决策。
The bankers on the Federal Advisory Council, when they met with the Fed in early May, congratulated the governors on their recent tightening. The bankers took note of “the highly charged political atmosphere” and warned that this turbulence “increases the contribution that monetary policy must make to maintaining confidence. . . .”19
联邦咨询委员会的银行家们，在5月初与美联储会面时，祝贺了理事们最近的紧缩行动。银行家们注意到了"高度紧张的政治氛围"，并警告说这种动荡"增加了货币政策在维持信心方面必须做出的贡献……"
Volcker, in any case, had his own allies in the White House, and they swiftly mobilized to defend the Federal Reserve. Like other episodes in the Reagan Presidency, when the senior advisers battled for the President’s mind, they often did their fighting in public. Martin Feldstein, the CEA chairman, dissented in public. The Fed’s tightening was “not inappropriate,” he said, contradicting the White House “senior officials.”
无论如何，Volcker在白宫有自己的盟友，他们迅速动员起来为美联储辩护。就像Reagan总统任期的其他事件一样，当高级顾问们为总统的思想而战时，他们经常在公开场合战斗。CEA主席Martin Feldstein公开表示了异议。他说美联储的紧缩"并非不当"，这与白宫的"高级官员"相矛盾。
“I think it would be a terrible mistake to try to push interest rates down by expansionary monetary policy,” Feldstein told reporters. He said he was “frankly surprised” to hear the Treasury Secretary advocate the opposite.
"我认为试图通过扩张性货币政策来压低利率将是一个可怕的错误，"Feldstein告诉记者。他说他听到财政部长主张相反的观点"坦率地说感到惊讶"。
The budget director, David Stockman, likewise began lobbying against the anti-Fed broadsides emanating from his White House colleagues. “We called the wrath of God down on Baker after he put that out,” Stockman said. “We got lots of Wall Street types to call him to say, ‘My God, the markets will go crazy.’ So many bricks came down on his head, Baker must have wondered, ‘What the hell did I get into?’”
预算主任David Stockman也开始游说反对他的白宫同事发出的反美联储攻击。"当Baker放出那些话后，我们召唤了上帝的愤怒到他头上，"Stockman说。"我们让很多华尔街类型的人打电话给他说，'天哪，市场会发疯的。'那么多砖头砸到他头上，Baker一定在想，'我到底卷进了什么鬼东西？'"
The biggest brick from Wall Street hit on Monday, May 14, when the financial markets opened. The bond market choked on a new issue of thirty-year Treasury bonds and dealers found it difficult to find customers for the $4.75 billion in new government debt. Stock prices declined but bond prices were hit hardest, off by nearly one and a third points. The markets may have simply been reacting to the rising interest rates, but Wall Street analysts blamed the collapse in bonds on the “Fed bashing” in Washington.
来自华尔街的最大一块砖头在5月14日星期一金融市场开盘时砸了下来。债券市场在新的三十年期国债发行中窒息，交易商发现很难为47.5亿美元的新政府债务找到客户。股票价格下跌，但债券价格受到最严重的打击，下跌了近1又3分之1个百分点。市场可能只是对利率上升做出反应，但华尔街分析师将债券的崩溃归咎于华盛顿的"抨击美联储"。
The contending factions at the White House quickly negotiated a truce. They agreed that the President must hold an impromptu press conference to calm the financial markets and still the controversy. Federal Reserve officials “are doing the best they can,” Reagan declared to reporters, nullifying all previous comments to the contrary. The Administration’s public campaign to pressure the Fed ended as abruptly as it had begun.
白宫内相互竞争的派系迅速谈判达成了休战。他们同意总统必须举行一次即兴新闻发布会以安抚金融市场并平息争议。美联储官员"正在尽其所能"，Reagan向记者宣布，从而否定了之前所有相反的说法。政府向美联储施压的公开行动像它开始时一样突然结束了。
At the Fed, some governors were more amused than intimidated. “This Administration has been weird,” Nancy Teeters said. “On Sunday, the Treasury Secretary is on TV saying we are doing a good job. Then interest rates bump up on Monday and on Wednesday he’s attacking us. Then the President has a press conference and says we’re doing fine. Within ten days, we went from being white hats to black hats and back again.”
在美联储，一些理事感到更多的是有趣而非恐吓。"这届政府太诡异了，"Nancy Teeters说。"周日，财政部长在电视上说我们做得很好。然后周一利率稍微上升，周三他就攻击我们。然后总统开了个新闻发布会，说我们做得很好。十天之内，我们从白帽子变成了黑帽子，又变了回来。"
Inside the White House, the leading “Fed bashers” proclaimed the exercise a success. “Darman said we’ve accomplished our purpose,” Stockman said. “We’ve given a warning to the Fed. It’s a shot across the bow and now the Fed knows what we expect between now and November.”
在白宫内部，主要的"抨击美联储者"宣称这次行动是成功的。"Darman说我们已经达到了目的，"Stockman说。"我们已经向美联储发出了警告。这是一次警告性的射击，现在美联储知道我们在从现在到十一月之间期望什么。"
Both Baker and Darman told associates that their public complaints probably prevented the Fed from adopting an even tougher policy. Volcker was in the middle among his own colleagues at the Fed and the frontal attacks from the White House would make it easier for the Fed chairman to resist the stronger action urged by Gramley and other governors. “They might have been even tighter if we hadn’t done that,” an Administration official said.
Baker和Darman都告诉同事们，他们的公开抱怨可能阻止了美联储采取更严厉的政策。Volcker在美联储自己的同事中处于中间位置，来自白宫的正面攻击会使美联储主席更容易抵制Gramley和其他理事所敦促的更强烈行动。"如果我们没有这样做，他们可能会更紧，"一位政府官员说。
The Federal Reserve, it was true, did hold off from a further decision to tighten money and credit. But the policy makers at the central bank were inhibited by a much larger event than the White House sniping. The Fed was confronted, simultaneously, with another major shock to the American financial system—the collapse of the Continental Illinois National Bank.
诚然，美联储确实没有进一步决定收紧货币和信贷。但中央银行的决策者受到了比白宫狙击更大事件的制约。美联储同时面临着美国金融体系的另一次重大冲击——Continental伊利诺伊国民银行的崩溃。
The “run” began in Tokyo on Wednesday, May 9, the same day Donald Regan was attacking the Federal Reserve back in Washington. The Commodity News Service had carried a brief wire story reporting “rumors” that a Japanese bank might acquire Continental Illinois in Chicago. When the item was picked up by a Japanese news service, the translator turned “rumors” into “disclosure” and Far Eastern investors holding Continental’s certificates of deposit panicked at the implications. That day, as much as $1 billion in Asian money fled from the Continental Illinois bank.
"挤兑"始于5月9日星期三的东京，同一天Donald Regan在华盛顿攻击美联储。商品新闻社发布了一条简短的通讯社报道，称"传言"一家日本银行可能收购芝加哥的Continental伊利诺伊。当这条消息被一家日本新闻社转载时，译者将"传言"译成了"披露"，持有Continental存单的远东投资者恐慌了起来。当天，高达10亿美元的亚洲资金从Continental伊利诺伊银行逃离。
The “run” spread around the globe, with the speed of electronic communications. When financial markets opened in Europe on Thursday, withdrawals from Continental reached the same awesome scale, driven by the same fears. Something was obviously wrong with the Chicago bank. Prudent investors would simply get out of its paper, at least until they could learn more.
"挤兑"以电子通讯的速度蔓延全球。当欧洲金融市场周四开盘时，出于同样的恐惧，从Continental提取的资金达到了同样惊人的规模。这家芝加哥银行显然出了问题。谨慎的投资者只会撤出它的票据，至少在他们了解更多情况之前。
In Washington, the Comptroller of the Currency, Todd Conover, issued a vaguely reassuring statement: “The Comptroller’s Office is not aware of any significant changes in the bank’s operations, as reflected in its published financial statements, that would serve as a basis for these rumors.”
在华盛顿，货币监理官Todd Conover发表了一份含糊的安抚性声明："货币监理署未发现该行运营有任何重大变化，如其已公布的财务报表所反映的那样，这些变化可以作为这些传言的依据。"
Financial markets, foreign and domestic, were not taken in by his artful phrasing. Major depositors worldwide, from brokerages to money-market funds to other commercial banks, continued to pull their money out of Continental CDs and the “run” worsened. Before the close of business on Friday, Continental had to borrow $3.6 billion at the Discount window of the Chicago Federal Reserve Bank in order to make up for its lost deposits. And, in Washington, the heads of the three bank regulatory agencies—the Comptroller, the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation and the Federal Reserve—gathered in Paul Volcker’s office to deal with the crisis.
海内外金融市场没有被他的巧妙措辞所迷惑。全球各地的主要存款人，从经纪公司到货币市场基金再到其他商业银行，继续将资金从Continental的存单中撤出，"挤兑"加剧了。在周五收市前，Continental不得不从芝加哥联邦储备银行的贴现窗口借款36亿美元以弥补其流失的存款。而在华盛顿，三家银行监管机构的负责人——货币监理官、联邦存款保险公司和美联储——聚集在Paul Volcker的办公室里处理这场危机。
As swiftly as that, the nation’s seventh-largest commercial bank was brought to ruin. But, as Volcker and the other regulators well understood, Continental’s collapse had not begun with an erroneous news report forty-eight hours earlier. The real origins went back to the bank’s aggressive lending strategy during the late 1970s and early 1980s when the bank was hailed for its brilliant management. The federal regulators had not intervened then to restrain Continental’s high-growth strategy, nor had they taken firm action after July 1982, when the failure of the Penn Square Bank in Oklahoma City revealed how reckless Continental’s loan officers had been. The Chicago bank was burdened with the $1 billion in bad loans it had purchased from Penn Square and at least another billion from other ventures. But nothing of consequence had been done by Washington regulators to force the bank to clean up its weaknesses. At the time, Roger Anderson, Continental’s ambitious chairman, boasted to bank employees: “We have no intention of pulling in our horns.”
就这样，美国第七大商业银行迅速走向了毁灭。但正如Volcker和其他监管机构清楚了解的那样，Continental的崩溃并非始于48小时前的一则错误新闻报道。真正的根源可以追溯到该行在1970年代末和1980年代初激进的放贷策略，当时该行因其出色的管理而受到赞誉。联邦监管机构当时没有干预以抑制Continental的高速增长策略，也没有在1982年7月之后采取坚定行动——当时俄克拉荷马城Penn Square银行的倒闭揭示了Continental的贷款官员有多么鲁莽。这家芝加哥银行背负着从Penn Square购买的10亿美元不良贷款以及来自其他项目的至少另外10亿美元。但华盛顿的监管机构没有采取任何有意义的行动来迫使该行清理其弱点。当时，Continental雄心勃勃的董事长Roger Anderson曾向银行员工吹嘘说："我们无意退缩。"
Indeed, through 1982 and 1983, Continental continued to distribute its regular $2-per-share dividends to stockholders as though nothing had happened. Late in 1983, a new examination by the Comptroller revealed that the bank’s condition had deteriorated steadily since the summer of 1982 and was now in even worse shape. Its earnings were declining and the management was maintaining appearances by selling off subsidiaries in order to keep up the dividend payments.
事实上，在整个1982年和1983年，Continental继续像什么都没发生过一样向股东发放每股2美元的常规股息。1983年底，货币监理官的一次新检查显示，该行的状况自1982年夏季以来持续恶化，现在甚至更糟。其收益在下降，管理层通过出售子公司以维持股息支付来维持表面文章。
William Isaac, chairman of the FDIC, said he asked the other federal regulators why nothing had been done to discipline the bank. “Why aren’t we cutting the dividend? We should have done it back in ’82,” Isaac said. “The response from the Fed and the Comptroller was: ‘You’re right, we probably should have done it in ’82. But now, if we do it, the markets will panic.’ I didn’t disagree with that.”20
FDIC主席William Isaac说，他曾问其他联邦监管机构为什么没有对该行采取任何惩戒措施。"我们为什么不削减股息？我们在82年就应该这样做，"Isaac说。"美联储和货币监理官的回应是：'你说得对，我们可能在82年就应该这样做。但现在，如果我们这样做，市场会恐慌。'我对此没有异议。"
Investors were already catching on anyway, at least in American financial markets. Continental continued to offer a much higher rate on its CDs than other money-center banks, a sure sign it was having trouble raising the $8 billion it needed to borrow every day to fund its operations. Even more telling, Continental sold off its profitable credit-card operation to another major bank early in 1984 in a desperate effort to raise funds. “They were dismembering the carcass,” Isaac said.
无论如何，投资者已经开始察觉了，至少在美国金融市场是这样。Continental继续为其存单提供比其它货币中心银行高得多的利率，这明确表明它在筹集每天运营所需的80亿美元资金方面遇到了困难。更有说服力的是，1984年初，Continental在绝望的筹资努力中将其盈利的信用卡业务出售给了另一家大银行。"他们在肢解这具尸体，"Isaac说。
Roger Anderson abruptly retired in February. The bank’s stock, which had traded as high as $40 three years before, was declining sharply, down to $12 by early May. Continental Illinois was, in fact, making discreet inquiries among other multinational banks about a merger or take-over, though not apparently in Japan. As American money became more wary, the bank had to rely more and more heavily on investors in Europe and Asia to lend it the billions needed for daily operating funds. When the foreign money suddenly caught on to the dangers, the game was over. The global “run” began.21
Roger Anderson在2月份突然退休。该行股票三年前曾交易到40美元的高位，现在急剧下跌，到5月初已跌至12美元。事实上，Continental伊利诺伊一直在其他跨国银行中低调地寻求合并或收购，但显然不是在日本。随着美国资金变得更加谨慎，该行不得不越来越依赖欧洲和亚洲的投资者来借给它日常运营所需的数十亿美元。当外国资金突然意识到危险时，游戏结束了。全球性的"挤兑"开始了。
Back in the summer of 1982, Paul Volcker had privately urged the Chicago bank directors to take drastic remedial action, but he had not ordered them to do so. After the collapse, though he did not reveal what he had attempted, Volcker obliquely conceded some regret that he had not been more forceful. The chairman told a Senate hearing: “. . . one could argue—and the argument becomes even better with hindsight—that it would have been better to look at the dividends right then. I think, with hindsight, that’s an unassailable argument.”22
早在1982年夏天，Paul Volcker曾私下敦促芝加哥银行的董事采取严厉的补救措施，但他没有命令他们这样做。在崩溃之后，尽管他没有透露他曾经尝试过什么，Volcker含蓄地承认对于没有更加强硬感到有些遗憾。主席在一次参议院听证会上说："……有人可能会争辩——而且事后看来这个论点更加有力——当时就应该直接处理股息问题。我认为，事后看来，这是一个无可辩驳的论点。"
At the Fed, bank regulators felt, with some merit, that a large share of the blame belonged to William Isaac and the FDIC. By insisting that the Penn Square Bank be liquidated in 1982, with huge losses for “upstream” banks like Continental, Isaac set in motion the unraveling of confidence. When Isaac insisted that only insured depositors up to $100,000 would be reimbursed in the Penn Square failure, that sent a scary message to all large-scale depositors. Isaac had wanted to teach “free market” discipline to the financial markets; instead, he simply frightened them. The complaint against Isaac, however, was also a way to evade the Fed’s own culpability—its failure to act when disciplinary action might have made the difference.
在美联储，银行监管人员有些道理地认为，很大一部分责任应该归咎于William Isaac和FDIC。由于坚持在1982年对Penn Square银行进行清算，给像Continental这样的"上游"银行带来巨大损失，Isaac引发了信心的瓦解。当Isaac坚持在Penn Square倒闭中只有不超过10万美元的受保存款人将得到赔偿时，这向所有大规模存款人发出了一个可怕的信息。Isaac本想教给金融市场"自由市场"纪律；相反，他只是吓坏了他们。然而，对Isaac的抱怨也是一种逃避美联储自身罪责的方式——它在惩戒行动可能发挥作用的时机没有采取行动。
In any case, the basic failures of regulation were already history. Now Volcker and the other government agencies faced an immediate crisis of unprecedented dimensions—how to stop what was the largest bank “run” in history, how to rescue Continental Illinois if the “run” couldn’t be stopped. They agreed that the FDIC should prepare to make a huge infusion of capital, as much as $2 billion. If the bank’s condition did not stabilize, they would try to arrange a take-over by another major bank.
无论如何，监管的基本失败已成为历史。现在Volcker和其他政府机构面临着一场规模和紧迫性空前的危机——如何制止历史上最大规模的银行"挤兑"，如果无法制止挤兑，如何拯救Continental伊利诺伊。他们同意FDIC应准备注入高达20亿美元的巨额资金。如果该行的状况没有稳定下来，他们将尝试由另一家大银行进行收购。
None of the regulators, including Isaac, entertained, even briefly, the option they had followed in Penn Square—paying off only the insured depositors, letting Continental fail and close it doors. If this bank failed, the Comptroller projected, that might lead to another hundred bank failures, mostly small banks that had parked their own money at Continental Illinois. About twenty-three hundred banks were among Continental’s large depositors and these assets would be frozen, at least temporarily, if the bank was liquidated. The potential losses to smaller banks were not the real danger, however.
包括Isaac在内的所有监管机构，甚至没有短暂考虑过他们在Penn Square所遵循的选择——只赔付受保存款人，让Continental倒闭关门。如果这家银行倒闭，货币监理官预测，那可能导致另外一百家银行倒闭，主要是那些将自己的资金存放在Continental伊利诺伊的小银行。大约2300家银行是Continental的大额存款人，如果该行被清算，这些资产将至少被暂时冻结。然而，对小银行的潜在损失并不是真正的危险。
How many of them would fail [Isaac asked aloud]? I don’t know. Could we handle it? Yes. But more important was the situation in the largest banks. First Chicago was going to announce big quarterly losses of its own. First Chicago would not have withstood that announcement if we hadn’t saved Continental. There would have been a run on Manny Hanny [Manufacturers Hanover]. There were all sorts of rumors about Manny Hanny rippling through the market. Bank of America, Interfirst of Dallas had problems too. Financial Corporation of America [the nation’s largest savings and loan] could not have lasted a minute if we had done a deposit payoff at Continental. In my judgment, we would have had worldwide chaos.
其中有多少会倒闭[Isaac大声问道]？我不知道。我们能应付吗？是的。但更重要的是那些大银行的状况。第一芝加哥银行即将宣布其自身的巨大季度亏损。如果我们没有拯救Continental，第一芝加哥将无法承受那个宣布。Manny Hanny（制造商汉诺威）会发生挤兑。关于Manny Hanny的各种谣言正在市场蔓延。美洲银行、达拉斯的第一国际银行也有问题。美洲金融公司（全国最大的储蓄贷款机构）如果我们在Continental进行存款赔付，连一分钟都撑不下去。依我判断，我们将会面临全球性的混乱。
This time, whatever the cost, the federal government had to reassure the money managers. If Continental failed, as Governor Charles Partee explained, the corporate treasurers, money managers and local governments that got “burned” there would have a powerful incentive to protect themselves at other banks.
这一次，无论代价如何，联邦政府必须安抚资金管理者。正如理事Charles Partee所解释的那样，如果Continental倒闭，在那里被"烧伤"的公司财务主管、资金管理者和地方政府将有强烈的动机在其他银行保护自己。
Bank of America was exposed on real estate and other loans, Manny Hanny had a very large exposure on LDC debt [Partee said]. If you were a money manager and there was even the slightest question that you might not be able to get your money out of one of these banks, there was no percentage in maintaining your deposit there. If you had $1 million in Bank of America and you had seen its reports coming out quarter after quarter with these loan losses and then you couldn’t get the money, there’s no question you’d be fired. You might earn a few extra basis points on interest by keeping your funds there, but if you are wrong, you get fired. It’s a very uneven choice.
美洲银行在房地产和其他贷款上存在风险敞口，Manny Hanny在发展中国家的债务上有非常大的风险敞口[Partee说]。如果你是一个资金管理者，只要有一丁点你不确定能否从这些银行之一取出资金的可能性，把你的存款留在那里就没有任何好处。如果你在美洲银行有100万美元，而你看到它一个季度接一个季度地报告这些贷款损失，然后你取不出钱，毫无疑问你会被解雇。把资金留在那里，你可能在利息上多赚几个基点，但如果你错了，你就被解雇了。这是一个非常不平衡的选择。
Over the weekend, Continental’s management tried to work out its own rescue by lining up support from the nation’s other largest banks. A hastily assembled consortium of sixteen banks, led by Morgan Guaranty, agreed to put up a $4.5 billion loan, a private “safety net” for their troubled competitor. The announcement failed to persuade. When the money markets opened on Monday, May 14, Continental’s hemorrhaging resumed.
在周末，Continental的管理层试图通过争取全国其他最大银行的支持来安排自己的救援。一个由Morgan Guaranty牵头的十六家银行匆忙组成的财团同意提供45亿美元的贷款，为其陷入困境的竞争对手建立一个私人"安全网"。这一声明未能说服市场。当货币市场在5月14日星期一开盘时，Continental的失血仍在继续。
“The banks tried to get together without government and it didn’t work,” a Treasury official said. “Then they fell on their knees with hands outspread and said, ‘Save us!’”
"银行试图在没有政府的情况下联合起来，但没有成功，"一位财政部官员说。"然后他们跪倒在地，伸出双手说，'救救我们！'"
While Continental borrowed more from the Fed and from its “safety net” of private banks, Volcker, Isaac and Conover worked out the terms for the federal rescue. The three flew to New York on Wednesday to meet with the leading bankers to enlist their support and assistance. The FDIC would guarantee an infusion of at least $1.5 billion and the banks were asked to put up $500 million as an expression of confidence. Present were executives from seven banks—Morgan, Chase, Citibank, Chemical, Manufacturers Hanover, Bankers Trust and Bank of America—who were to sell the package to twenty-five or thirty other major banks. In order not to alarm, the bankers assembled at Morgan Guaranty’s uptown branch, rather than at the New York Fed in Wall Street.
在Continental从美联储及其私人银行"安全网"借入更多资金的同时，Volcker、Isaac和Conover敲定了联邦救援的条款。三人周三飞往纽约与主要银行家会面，争取他们的支持和协助。FDIC将保证至少注入15亿美元，银行被要求拿出5亿美元作为信任的表达。出席的有七家银行的高管——Morgan、Chase、Citibank、Chemical、Manufacturers Hanover、Bankers Trust和Bank of America——他们将向其他二十五到三十家大银行推销这个方案。为了不引起警惕，银行家们在Morgan Guaranty的上城分行集会，而不是在华尔街的纽约联邦储备银行。
The meeting was faintly reminiscent of Wall Street’s glorious past, the era when J. P. Morgan and partners could meet among themselves and assemble a package of emergency loans to save the banking system. Several bank executives made patriotic speeches during the meeting, alluding to the Morgan tradition and the spirit of noblesse oblige inherited by Wall Street’s most important banks.
这次会议略微软弱地让人想起华尔街辉煌的过去，那个J. P. Morgan和合伙人们可以自行开会并汇集紧急贷款方案来拯救银行系统的时代。几位银行高管在会议上发表了爱国演讲，提及Morgan传统和华尔街最重要银行继承的贵族责任精神。
The reality was utterly different, however. It was the federal government that would save the banks from possible calamity, including the seven banks assembled at the table. All of them, in varying degrees, were vulnerable to the same sort of panic that had brought down Continental. They all depended heavily on the daily money-market borrowing required by “managed liabilities” banking. They were all overexposed on foreign lending. Even so, Citibank, with characteristic independence, balked at the terms. It demanded ironclad language in the Fed-FDIC agreement to protect itself from loss. The lawyers argued through the night and finally Citibank relented.
然而，现实完全不同。是联邦政府将拯救银行免于可能的灾难，包括聚集在会议桌旁的这七家银行。它们在不同程度都容易受到同样的恐慌——那种曾击垮Continental的恐慌——的伤害。它们都严重依赖"管理负债"银行业务所需的每日货币市场借款。它们在外贷方面都过度暴露。尽管如此，Citibank以其特有的独立性在条款上犹豫不决。它要求在美联储-FDIC协议中加入措辞强硬的条款以保护自己免受损失。律师们争论了一整夜，最终Citibank做出了让步。
On Thursday morning, eight days after the “run” began, the federal regulators announced the largest bailout in the history of banking. The FDIC would pump $4.5 billion in immediate new capital into the bank, assuming liability for the bulk of Continental’s bad loans while it searched for a new owner to take over the institution. The Federal Reserve promised that, in the meantime, it would lend whatever was necessary in short-term funds to keep Continental afloat. In the course of fulfilling that promise, the Fed made emergency loans to Continental Illinois that rose to a breathtaking $8 billion.23
周四上午，在"挤兑"开始八天后，联邦监管机构宣布了银行业历史上最大规模的救助。FDIC将向该行注入45亿美元的即时新资本，承担Continental大部分坏账的责任，同时它为该机构寻找新的所有者接管。美联储承诺，在此期间将提供维持Continental运营所需的任何短期资金。在履行这一承诺的过程中，美联储向Continental伊利诺伊提供的紧急贷款达到了惊人的80亿美元。

但救援方案中最关键也最具争议的元素是保护所有人——无论大小——免受任何损失的担保。大额存款人可以放心了。他们在Continental的钱是安全的，由美联储和FDIC的联合声明实际上提供了保险。任何负债将由联邦政府承担，也就是由普通公众承担。
But the essential and most controversial element of the rescue package was the guarantee to protect everyone, large and small, from any losses. The large depositors could relax. Their money at Continental was safe, effectively insured by the joint announcement from the Federal Reserve and the FDIC. Any liabilities would be assumed by the federal government, that is, by the general public.25
一些愤怒的声音被提了出来。罗德岛州的众议员Fernand St. Germain，他接替众议员Reuss担任众议院银行委员会主席，轻蔑地称之为"为有权有势者的救助"。政府救援Continental的规模，St. Germain说，"使得联邦政府在Lockheed、Chrysler和纽约市救助中提供的担保和支出的总和相形见绌。"然而，他说，这是由非选举产生的官僚安排的，没有经过任何公开辩论。
A few angry voices were raised. Representative Fernand St. Germain of Rhode Island, who had succeeded Representative Reuss as chairman of House Banking, scornfully called it “a bailout for the powerful.” The size of the government’s rescue of Continental, St. Germain observed, “dwarfs the combined guarantees and outlays of the federal government in the Lockheed, Chrysler and New York City bailouts.” Yet it was arranged, he said, by unelected bureaucrats, with no public debate.
在联邦监管机构向Continental伊利诺伊提供数十亿美元的同一天，他们关闭了田纳西州Pikeville的Bledsoe County Bank和路易斯安那州Opelousas的Planters Trust & Savings Bank。小型独立银行抱怨Continental交易中隐含的"双重标准"。1984年上半年，四十三家银行倒闭，其中大多数是小型农业银行，因为太多农民和小企业拖欠贷款而被迫关闭。在几乎所有案例中，FDIC只赔付了受保存款人，并安排由邻近银行接手。Continental的大额存款人比小银行的大额存款人得到了更慷慨的对待。尽管如此，Continental的管理层被不光彩地解雇了，其股东确实损失了大部分股权价值。很少有小型银行家愿意承受同样的命运。
The day after the federal regulators were providing billions to save Continental Illinois, they closed down the Bledsoe County Bank of Pikeville, Tennessee, and the Planters Trust & Savings Bank of Opelousas, Louisiana. Small independent banks complained about the “double standard” implicit in the Continental deal. During the first half of 1984, forty-three banks failed, most of them small agricultural banks forced to close because so many farmers and small businesses were defaulting on their loans.26 In nearly all cases, the FDIC paid off only the insured depositors and arranged a take-over by a neighboring bank. Large depositors at Continental were treated more generously than large depositors at small banks. Still, Continental’s managers were fired in disgrace and its stockholders did lose most of the value of their equity. Very few small bankers would wish to endure the same fate.
然而，Continental救助树立了一个联邦监管机构无法辩解的不舒服先例。如果之前还有任何疑问，Continental清楚地确立了最大银行将永远不允许倒闭，无论政府付出什么代价。在众议院听证会上被追问这个问题时，货币监理官承认，是的，最大的十一家跨国银行将不允许倒闭。作为现实情况，受保护的银行数量无疑远不止这些。很可能最大的五十家甚至一百家银行将永远是安全的，因为它们的倒闭所产生的涟漪被认为过于动荡而无法容忍。
The Continental bailout, nevertheless, posed an awkward precedent that federal regulators could not explain away. If there was any doubt before, Continental starkly established that the largest banks would never be allowed to fail, whatever the cost to the government. Badgered on the question in House hearings, the Comptroller conceded that, yes, the eleven largest multinational banks would not be allowed to fail. As a practical reality, the number of protected banks was undoubtedly much larger than that. Probably the largest fifty or even one hundred banks would always be safe because the ripples from their failures would be regarded as too unsettling to tolerate.
这种区别对货币市场以及投资者将大额存款存放在何处有着重要的后果。随着时间的推移，较小的银行将遭受竞争压力，而最大的银行将受益。为什么一位资金管理者要将其100万美元存款放在一家小银行，在那里他可能会在10万美元的联邦保险担保之上损失掉，而他知道只要他把钱放在一家最大的银行，无论其管理者多么鲁莽，他所有的钱都是安全的？联邦政府对银行安全的担保不经意间在大银行和小银行之间造成了不平等的纪律，成为银行系统集中的另一个激励因素。随着小银行和储蓄贷款机构被允许倒闭，它们的空间被大银行占据。Citibank、Chase、Chemical和其他银行在全国各地收购了数十家倒闭的机构，建立起了广泛的公司滩头阵地，同时等待国会允许它们成为全国性银行的那一天。
The distinction had important consequences for the money market and where investors would place large deposits. Over time, smaller banks would suffer competitively and the largest banks would benefit. Why should a money manager put a $1 million deposit in a smaller bank, where he might lose it above the $100,000 federal insurance guarantee, when he knew all his money would be safe in one of the largest banks, no matter how reckless the managers might be? The federal guarantees of bank safety had inadvertently created unequal discipline between large and small, another incentive for the consolidation of the banking system. As the smaller banks and savings and loans were allowed to fail, their space was taken by the big ones. Citibank, Chase, Chemical and others acquired dozens of failed institutions around the country, establishing far-flung corporate beachheads while they awaited the day when Congress would allow them to become nationwide banks.
然而，Continental救援中最引人注目的政治事实是缺乏争议。除了小型银行家之外，几乎没有任何来自公众的反应，来自政治家的批评也非常少。危机的规模如此巨大和复杂，以至于普通民众可能觉得他们无法质疑这些决定。另一种可能性是，大多数美国人不认为政府干预有什么特别之处。当最大、最有影响力的企业处于危险之中时，政府会来拯救它们。这就是人们期望美国体制运作的方式。
The most striking political fact of the Continental rescue, however, was the absence of controversy. Aside from small bankers, there was almost no reaction from the general public and very little criticism from politicians. The dimensions of the crisis were so awesome and complex that perhaps ordinary people felt they could not question the decisions. Another possibility was that most Americans regarded the government intervention as unexceptional. When the largest, most influential enterprises were imperiled, the government would come to their rescue. That was how people expected the American system to work.
由于联邦政府的支持承诺，Continental稳定了一段时间，但持怀疑态度的资金管理者仍然保持警惕，银行的融资进一步恶化。Continental伊利诺伊在萎缩。到仲夏，它已是第十三大银行，而非第七大。与此同时，联邦监管机构找不到愿意的买家。Chemical、Citibank和First Chicago都查看了Continental的账目，并与政府机构分享了它们的悲观评估：该行有40亿美元的坏账，而不是联邦监管机构所认为的27亿美元。没有人对为什么私人银行家发现了政府监管机构没有看到的问题给出好的解释。无论如何，没有人想买这具尸体。
Continental stabilized for a time, thanks to the federal promises of support, but skeptical money managers continued to be wary and the bank’s funding eroded further. Continental Illinois was shrinking. By midsummer, it was the thirteenth largest bank, not seventh. Meanwhile, the federal regulators could find no willing buyers. Chemical, Citibank and First Chicago all took a look at Continental’s books and shared their pessimistic assessments with the government agencies: the bank had $4 billion in bad loans, not $2.7 billion, as the federal regulators had believed. No one offered a good explanation as to why the private bankers found problems that the government regulators did not see. In any case, no one wanted to buy the carcass.
因此，在7月下旬，Continental伊利诺伊银行被国有化了。当然没有人使用这个词，但这就是华盛顿公告的含义。联邦政府买下了这家银行。由于找不到私人买家，美国政府自己成为所有者，并且在另行通知之前，成为全国第十三大银行的唯一所有者。
Therefore, in late July, the Continental Illinois bank was nationalized. That term was not used, of course, but that was the meaning of the announcement in Washington. The federal government bought the bank. Unable to find a private buyer, the U.S. government itself became the owner and, until further notice, sole proprietor of the nation’s thirteenth largest bank.
国有化产业的概念当然冒犯了仍然主导美国政治的自由市场伦理，但这是在没有争议的情况下完成的——而且是在Ronald Reagan，几十年来最保守的总统的治下。保守派评论员在政府执行其对自由市场的大规模干预时保持沉默。也许保守派感到安心，因为他们明白华盛顿无意将Continental伊利诺伊作为一家公共银行来运营，其贷款政策由更大的公共目的指导。Continental将继续像一家普通商业银行一样运营，严格致力于盈利。
The idea of nationalized industries was, of course, offensive to the free-market ethic that still dominated American politics, yet this was done without controversy—and under Ronald Reagan, the most conservative President to govern in many decades. Conservative commentators were silent as the government executed its massive intrusion in the free marketplace. Possibly conservatives were reassured by the understanding that Washington did not intend to operate Continental Illinois as a public bank, its lending policies guided by larger public purposes. Continental would continue to be run like an ordinary commercial bank, strictly devoted to profit making.
Continental的国有化实际上是现代自由主义的典型行为——国家为了私人利益和广泛的公共目的而进行干预。在这个本应是保守主义的时代，联邦当局正在搁置市场竞争的严酷裁决（并大规模扩大自身对私营经济的参与），以保护私人公民免受损失——从而拯救这个系统。华盛顿正在进入大规模银行业。
The nationalization of Continental was, in fact, a quintessential act of modern liberalism—the state intervening in behalf of private interests and a broad public purpose. In this supposedly conservative era, federal authorities were setting aside the harsh verdict of market competition (and grossly expanding their own involvement in the private economy) in order to protect private citizens from loss—and thereby save the system. Washington was going into big-time banking.
对Continental的救援也相当于对债权人阶级的大规模救助。也许这就是为什么保守派评论员没有抱怨。政府拯救投资者免于损失的范围，比它曾为处于危险中的债务人所做的任何事情的倍数还要大，比如Chrysler公司或Lockheed。在过去，保守派学者和权威人士曾对任何联邦政府干预私营经济的行为大声反对，特别是对陷入困境的公司的紧急援助。现在，他们几乎没注意到。也许如果这件事是由保守派斗士Ronald Reagan以外的人做的，他们会更加直言不讳。
The rescue of Continental also amounted to a vast bailout for the creditor classes. Perhaps that was why the conservative commentators did not complain. The government would save investors from their losses on a scale many times greater than anything it had ever done for endangered debtors, such as the Chrysler Corporation or Lockheed. In the past, conservative scholars and pundits had objected loudly at any federal intervention in the private economy, particularly emergency assistance for failing companies. Now, they hardly seemed to notice. Perhaps they would have been more vocal if the deed had been done by someone other than the conservative champion, Ronald Reagan.
在这种情况下，政府没有好的替代方案。FDIC收购了Continental的1.6亿股股票，解雇了管理层并任命了新高管。购买价格达55亿美元（不包括来自美联储的持续援助）。其假设是政府将拥有Continental，直到它恢复元气并重新盈利，然后将该行卖回给私人投资者。到1986年初，FDIC报告称，在其从Continental承担的坏账上，至今损失了12.4亿美元，但要过很多年才能知道对公众的完整成本。联邦政府可能也需要很多年才能退出银行业务。
Under the circumstances, the government did not have good alternatives. The FDIC acquired 160 million shares of Continental’s stock, fired the management and installed new executives. The purchase price amounted to $5.5 billion (not counting the continuing aid from the Federal Reserve). The assumption was that the government would own Continental until it was back on its feet and operating profitably again, then it would sell the bank back to private investors. By early 1986, the FDIC reported a loss to date of $1.24 billion on the bad loans it has assumed from Continental, but it would be many years before anyone knew the full cost to the public. It might also be many years before the federal government could get out of the banking business.
然而，银行业危机并未随着Continental伊利诺伊的救援而结束。恐惧和紧张继续困扰着全国一些最大的银行。金融市场充斥着关于另一场迫在眉睫的危机的谣言，可能是同样规模的。在夏天结束之前，美国银行业的震动将对美联储的货币政策产生直接、扭曲的影响——从而对美国经济造成破坏性后果。
The banking crisis did not end with the rescue of Continental Illinois, however. Fear and nervousness continued to stalk some of the largest banks in the country. The financial markets buzzed with rumors of another impending crisis, perhaps on the same scale. Before the summer was over, the tremors in American banking would have a direct, distorting impact on the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy—and, therefore, damaging consequences for the American economy.
美国农田的价值在三年内下降了1490亿美元，有些地区高达50%，只要农产品价格持平或下跌，抵押品就会继续缩水。南部和中西部又有数千名债务人将被逼入绝境，涉及能源和其他大宗商品以及农业。实物资产的大规模清算仍在继续。最终，它拖垮了那些为这些实物资产的生产提供融资的银行。
The value of American farmland had fallen $149 billion in three years, by as much as 50 percent in some areas, and the collateral would keep shrinking as long as agricultural prices remained flat or falling. Thousands more debtors across the South and the Midwest would be driven to the wall, in energy and other commodities as well as farming. The rolling liquidation of real assets was continuing. Ultimately, it brought down banks that had financed production of those real assets.27
虽然小乡镇银行的消亡给当地社区带来了创伤，并为电视网络新闻提供了常规专题报道，但真正的银行业危机在系统的另一端——在最大的机构之中。当选民们被关于众多其他主题的竞选演讲分散注意力时，联邦银行监管机构度过了一个焦虑的夏天，不断压制关于谁会下一个倒闭的反复出现的流言蜚语。
While the demise of small country banks traumatized local communities and offered a staple feature story for the network television news, the real crisis in banking was at the opposite end of the system —among the largest institutions. While the electorate was distracted by campaign speeches on a host of other themes, federal bank regulators sweated through an anxious summer, tamping out the recurring gossip about who would fail next.
毕竟，全国最大的"农业银行"是旧金山的美洲银行，也是全国最大的商业银行。美洲银行的贷款资助了加州农业综合企业的大规模企业。但该行还资助了达拉斯一栋基本空置的豪华公寓大楼，现已丧失抵押品赎回权。它为休斯顿Global Marine拥有的闲置海上钻井平台提供了资金，这些平台现在也遇到了麻烦。它为其他现在正在违约的项目又提供了数十亿美元。房地产、石油、农业、航运——美洲银行将在1980年代上半年被迫吞下41亿美元的不良贷款，而其账面上仍有70亿美元的风险敞口给予其他陷入困境的债务人——墨西哥、委内瑞拉和巴西。
The largest ‘‘farm bank” in the nation, after all, was Bank of America in San Francisco, also the nation’s largest commercial bank. B of A loans financed the vast enterprises of California agribusiness. But the bank had also financed a largely vacant luxury condo tower in Dallas, now foreclosed. It put up the money for the idle offshore drilling rigs owned by Global Marine in Houston, now in trouble too. It provided billions more for other ventures that were now defaulting. Real estate, oil. agriculture, shipping—Bank of America would be forced to swallow $4.1 billion in failed loans during the first half of the 1980s and yet still would have $7 billion on its books in exposure to other troubled debtors, Mexico, Venezuela and Brazil.
金融结构存在薄弱环节[Partee理事说]，但银行并不薄弱，因为它们鲁莽行事。银行一直在做它们一直做的事情，向企业和农民提供贷款。他们才是薄弱的一方。有相当多的公司陷入财务困难，很多房地产项目，很多农民，包括数百万美元的农业综合企业农场。薄弱的是客户，而不是银行。
There is weakness in the financial structure [Governor Partee said}, but the banks are not weak because they have been foolhardy. Banks have been doing what they've always done, making loans to business and farmers. They are the ones who are weak. There are quite a few companies in financial difficulty, lots of real-estate projects, lots of farmers, including multimillion-dollar agribusiness farms. It's the customers, not the banks.

William Isaac, the FDIC chairman, agreed:
在Continental伊利诺伊之后情况非常不稳定。如果Continentals没有被处理，我们就陷入困境了。旧金山的Crocker很薄弱，但它已经薄弱了很长时间。First Chicago。美洲银行。Manny Hanny。达拉斯的Interfirst。休斯顿的First National。美洲金融公司。密歇根国民银行。匹兹堡的Equibank。那些都是会陷入困境的。
It was very shaky after Continental Illinois. If Continental had not been handled, we would have been in the soup. Crocker of San Francisco was weak, but it had been weak for a long time. First Chicago. Bank of America, Manny Hanny. Interfirst of Dallas. First National of Houston. Financial Corporation of America. Michigan National. Equibank of Pittsburgh. Those are ones that would have been in the soup.
困境的最显着证据是相当可见的，尽管很少被注意到。一些最大和最自豪的金融机构正在出售其迷人的总部大楼，以筹集资金来抵消贷款损失。美洲银行、First Chicago、Crocker、Security Pacific、波士顿银行都出售了它们的现代办公大楼，然后向新业主租回——这是正在进行的被迫撤退的恰当象征。
The most striking evidence of the distress was quite visible, though barely noted. Some of the largest and proudest financial institutions were selling off their glamorous headquarters buildings in order to raise capital to offset loan losses. Bank of America, First Chicago, Crocker, Security Pacific, Bank of Boston all sold their modern office towers, then rented them back from the new owners—an appropriate symbol of the forced retreat that was under way.28
普通公众可能对危险浑然不觉，但金融市场不是。在5月份Continental伊利诺伊救援一周后，关于融资困难的传言转向了纽约的制造商汉诺威信托，全国第四大银行。投资者抛售Manny Hanny的股票，其价值一天下跌了11%，尽管该行予以否认。其他主要银行的股票也受到打击，甚至包括最负盛名、管理最好的如Morgan和Citibank。T. Rowe Price货币市场基金总裁Edward A. Taber解释说："市场在问：'谁是下一个？'"
The general public may have been oblivious to the peril, but financial markets were not. One week after the Continental Illinois rescue in May, the rumors of funding difficulties turned on Manufacturers Hanover Trust in New York, the nation’s fourth largest bank. Investors dumped Manny Hanny stock, and its value fell 11 percent in one day, despite the bank’s denials. Other major bank shares were driven down too, including even the most prestigious and best-managed ones like Morgan and Citibank. Edward A. Taber, president of the T. Rowe Price money-market fund, explained: “The market is saying, ‘Who’s next?’”29
纽约联储的公开市场操作台更冷静地报告了这些压力："Continental的融资问题在春末和夏季对金融市场投下了长长的阴影。"纽约操作台观察到，投资者普遍执行了"安全优先"操作，将资金从任何被认为是陷入困境的银行转移出去。
The New York Fed’s Open Market Desk reported the strains more soberly: “The funding problems of Continental cast a long shadow over the financial markets during the late spring and summer.” Investors, the New York desk observed, generally executed a “flight to quality,” moving money away from any banks perceived as troubled.30
整个夏天，大银行不得不提高其自身存单所提供的利率以维持其融资。三个月期银行存单与国库券之间的利差扩大到35到160个基点。为自身每日借款支付更多的利息进一步挤压了银行的利润，加剧了贷款损失已经造成的损害。
Through the summer, the larger banks had to bid up the interest rates offered on their own CDs in order to maintain their own funding. The interest-rate spread between three-month bank CDs and Treasury bills ballooned, from 35 to 160 basis points. Paying more for their own daily borrowing squeezed the banks’ profits further, adding to the damage already caused by loan losses.
带着因果报应的一点讽刺，商业银行现在正承受着正是它们敦促美联储追求的那些政策的严重余震——货币的急剧收紧和价格通胀的快速减速，这些已经摧毁或削弱了许多其他企业。商业银行在1980年和1981年当美联储将利率推至前所未有高峰时曾享受创纪录的利润，但美联储行动的严厉性现在也追上了它们。美联储没有给债务人时间适应通货紧缩的新现实，而且由于商业、农民、拉丁美洲国家的局面如此突然地转变，银行现在被困在大量不良贷款组合中。
With an element of poetic justice, commercial banks were now suffering a severe aftershock from the very policies they had urged the Federal Reserve to pursue—the stringent tightening of money and rapid deceleration of price inflation that had destroyed or undermined so many other enterprises. Commercial banks had enjoyed record profits back in 1980 and 1981 when the Fed pushed interest rates to unprecedented peaks, but the severity of the Fed’s action had now caught up with them too. The Fed had not given debtors time to adjust to the new reality of disinflation, and because the tables were turned so abruptly on businesses, on farmers, on Latin-American nations, the banks were now stuck with huge portfolios of troubled loans.
很少有人将两者联系起来，但从某种意义上说，这是为Volcker迅速而确定的通胀攻击仍在付出的部分代价。1982年漫长而严重的紧缩变成了1984年银行体系的脆弱和失败。一种更有耐心的方式会更耗时，但它对结构的破坏也会小一些，使更少的受害者在此后数年仍在挣扎求生。从独立石油钻探者到主权国家，这些受创的债务人现在正威胁着将贷款人一起拖垮。
Few people made the connection, but, in a sense, this was part of the price still being paid for Volcker’s swift and sure assault on inflation. The long and severe contraction of 1982 became the fragility and failure in the banking system in 1984. A more patient approach would have taken longer, but it also would have done less damage to the structure, with fewer victims still struggling to stay afloat years afterward. The stricken debtors, from independent oil drillers to sovereign nations, were now threatening to drag the lenders down with them.
1984年，商业银行核销了125亿美元的贷款损失，是1981年贷款损失准备金的两倍多。它们的利润率降至二十年来的最低水平。最大银行的股本回报率从1980年的13.95%下降到9.78%。在联邦监管机构的督促下，它们被迫筹集新资本以防范未来更多的贷款失败。
Commercial banks charged off $12.5 billion in loan losses in 1984, more than double the loan-loss provisions of 1981. Their profit rate fell to the lowest level in twenty years. The return on equity for the largest banks had declined from 13.95 percent in 1980 to 9.78 percent. Prodded by federal regulators, they were compelled to raise new capital to protect against more loan failures in the future.
"通常在经济复苏的这个阶段，"美联储报告说，"人们会预期贷款组合的质量有所改善，但1984年银行控股公司的贷款损失继续增加。"纽约联储的Anthony Solomon评论说："纽约主要货币中心银行的非 performing 贷款与总资产的比率在3%至4%之间。正常情况下在这个复苏阶段，考虑到我们已有两年的繁荣，这个数字可能低至1%至1.5%。这令人沮丧，显示了脆弱性。"
“Usually at this stage in an economic recovery,” the Federal Reserve reported, “the quality of loan portfolios would be expected to improve, but loan losses at bank holding companies in 1984 continued to mount.” Anthony Solomon of the New York Fed observed: “New York’s major money-center banks are running a ratio of 3 to 4 percent of nonperforming loans to total assets. Normally at this stage of a recovery, given that we’ve had two years of prosperity, it might be as low as 1 to 1.5 percent. That is distressing and shows the fragility.”31
公众对银行业问题的道德感相当混乱。那些对被迫关门的小镇银行家感到一丝同情的公民，可能在私下里对巨型货币中心银行的困境感到幸灾乐祸。然而，无论哪种情况，银行家都被同样的力量、同样的错误所困。大小银行之间没有实质性的区别，如果说有的话，最大的银行在为未经检验的未来融资时通常更加慷慨和富有想象力。
The public’s moral sensibilities about banking’s problems were terribly confused. Citizens who felt a tug of sympathy for a small-town banker forced to close his doors might secretly relish the distress of behemoth money-center banks. Yet, in either case, the banker was caught by the same forces, the same mistakes. There was no substantial distinction between large and small except, if anything, the largest banks were usually more generous and imaginative in financing the untested future.
事实上，陷入困境的银行家们犯了与借款人基本相同的错误——他们基于对未来的假设为新的风险项目放贷，而这些假设并未实现。银行家们也乘着通货膨胀曲线向上——而现在因为美联储在反通胀战役中转变了局势而陷入困境。尽管拥有金融专业知识的优势地位，大大小小的银行家们被证明并不比农民、房地产开发商、石油生产商或南美各国政府更有先见之明。而且，银行家们同样难辞其咎。
In fact, the bankers in trouble had made essentially the same mistakes that the borrowers had made—they had lent money for new ventures based on assumptions about the future that did not materialize. Bankers had likewise ridden the inflationary curve upward— and were now in trouble because the Federal Reserve turned the tables in its anti-inflation campaign. Despite their preferred position of financial expertise, the bankers, large and small, proved to be no more prescient than farmers, real-estate developers, oil producers or South American governments had been. And, the bankers were no less culpable.
在这一点上，联邦政府似乎特别混乱。Reagan政府的各种成员时常将债务问题归因于过去通货膨胀所产生的过度野心——购买了太多土地和设备的农民，鲁莽借贷的石油生产商。CEA主席Murray Weidenbaum曾称他们都是"投机者"，赌价格会永远上涨。然而，对于每一个过度扩张并随后被债务压垮的农民或投机石油商，都有一个同样过于乐观、下了同样赌注的银行家。谴责债务交易的一方鲁莽而同情另一方作为不幸的受害者，这根本不理性。但这基本上是政府的姿态——它急于帮助被烧伤的贷款人，同时让借款人听天由命。
The federal government seemed especially confused by this point. From time to time, various members of the Reagan Administration attributed the debt problems to excessive ambitions generated by the inflation of the past—farmers who had bought too much land and equipment, oil producers who had borrowed recklessly. The CEA chairman, Murray Weidenbaum, had called them all “speculators,” gambling that prices would continue to rise forever. Yet, for every farmer or freebooting oilman who had overreached and was subsequently crushed by debt, there was a willing banker who also had been too optimistic, who had made the same bet. It was simply not rational to condemn one side of the debt transaction as reckless and sympathize with the other side as an unfortunate victim. Yet that was essentially the government’s posture—it rushed to assist the lenders who had been burned and, simultaneously, left the borrowers to their fate.
债务人和贷款人之间的这种道德混乱在美联储尤为明显。美联储竭尽全力地担心帮助银行克服其错误，同时却将失败的借款人的困境视为遗憾但不可避免，甚至是通货紧缩过程中必要的。例如，Paul Volcker对破产的农民表达了有限的同情。农民受到了伤害，是的，但他们把自己置于了一个不可持续的位置。农民们建立的生产能力远远超过了世界愿意购买的数量。
This moral confusion between debtors and lenders was especially evident at the Federal Reserve. The Fed worried intensely about helping the banks to overcome their mistakes, yet it dismissed the plight of the failed borrowers as regrettable but unavoidable, even necessary to the process of disinflation. Paul Volcker, for instance, expressed limited sympathy for the bankrupt farmers. Farmers were hurt, yes, but they had put themselves in an unsustainable position. The farmers built up a lot more productive capability than the world is going to buy.
事实上，中央银行成功地逃避了对美国农民和其他债务人正在发生的事情的任何真正公共责任，就像它回避了在1920年代被美联储通货紧缩摧毁的中西部农民的抱怨一样。关于失败农民困境的详尽报纸和电视报道几乎从未提及与货币政策或美联储的任何联系。来自中密歇根大学的金融学教授Martha Seger，在1984年中期被President Reagan任命接替Nancy Teeters，成为理事会上唯一的女性。作为新成员，Seger理事对美联储如此轻易地与其农民的困境保持距离感到震惊。据Jude Wanniski报道的一次谈话中，Seger评论道："我很惊讶这里有这么多人坚持说我们与农业问题无关。这里除了我们这些'鸡'以外没别人！"
The central bank, in fact, successfully evaded any real public responsibility for what was happening to American farmers and other debtors, much as it had turned aside the complaints from Midwestern farmers destroyed in the Fed’s deflation back in the 1920s. The exhaustive press and television coverage on the plight of the failing farmers almost never mentioned any connection with monetary policy or the Federal Reserve. Martha Seger, a finance professor from Central Michigan University, was appointed by President Reagan to succeed Nancy Teeters in mid-1984 as the only woman on the Board of Governors. Governor Seger, as a new member, was struck by how easily the Fed distanced itself from the farm distress. In a conversation reported by Jude Wanniski, Seger remarked: “I’m amazed at how many people there are around here [at the Fed] who insist that we have nothing to do with the farm problem. Nobody here but us chickens!”32
然而，对于贷款人，即同样陷入困境的金融中介机构，美联储表现出了极大的关怀。事实上，Volcker和其他理事们不遗余力地确保在1983年和1984年银行监管规则不会被过于严苛地执行。美联储（以及财政部）指示联邦银行审查员在应用银行偿付能力会计准则时，要对陷入困境的银行家所处的特殊情况给予应有的同情。
For the lenders, however, the financial intermediaries who were also in trouble, the Federal Reserve demonstrated great solicitude. Indeed, Volcker and the other governors went to extraordinary lengths to make sure that the rules of bank regulation were not enforced too zealously in 1983 and 1984. The Federal Reserve (and the Treasury as well) instructed federal bank examiners to apply the accounting standards for bank solvency with due compassion for the special circumstances that troubled bankers found themselves in.

Others of us say, “No, it’s better if we try to work it out.”
但政治气候已经改变，现在国会希望美联储和财政部对银行采取强硬态度并施加新的监管控制。Volcker本人也愿意。他一直以来对金融放松管制持怀疑态度。尽管如此，在脆弱的环境下，美联储会谨慎行事。
The Fed’s solicitous response to the banks’ difficulties was, of course, consistent with Volcker’s preoccupation with the refinancing of LDC debt. Through 1983 and 1984, Volcker presided like a matchmaker over the new debt packages between the banks and the nations that needed the money, Brazil, Argentina, Mexico and the others, nudging each side toward settlement. If one deal had failed, if one country had defaulted, the whole universe of debt-burdened countries might have given up any attempt to service its loans. At one point, bankers on the Federal Advisory Council complained directly to the governors that Fed bank examiners were being overly zealous toward the banks’ shaky LDC loans. “Concern was expressed,” the bankers’ council reported, “about comments by bank examiners who did not appear to be conversant with the view of the Federal Reserve Board. ...”
逐渐地，一步一步，美联储和货币监理官都开始收紧程序。他们敦促资本不足的银行改善其资本基础并满足更高的资本要求。最大的银行——在1981年实施的新资本比率中被豁免——现在被要求筹集更多资本并达到6%的最低标准。这实际上将提高它们对未来如果主要发展中国家债务人之一违约时的保险。
Volcker himself confessed: “We’re ah being induced to close our eyes to loose banking practices.”33
货币监理署也开始施加公开纪律的刺痛，这是监管机构在Continental伊利诺伊崩溃前犹豫不决而不敢援引的法律武器。在秋季的一项非常举措中，First Chicago和美洲银行都受到公开谴责，并被强制接受一项监管命令，指示它们收紧贷款政策、增加资本准备金并采取措施保护自己免受那种曾击垮Continental的短期流动性危机的影响。
But the political climate had changed, and now Congress wanted the Fed and the Treasury to get tough with the banks and impose new regulatory controls. Volcker himself was willing. He had always been skeptical about the deregulation of finance. Still, in the fragile circumstances, the Fed would proceed cautiously.
银行家们对联邦监管机构新的强硬手段发牢骚，但金融市场几乎没有因这个消息而恐慌。投资者已经相当熟悉这些银行面临的重大问题，而且如果有什么不同的话，投资者可能感到放心，看到联邦监管机构终于迫使陷入困境的银行采取补救行动。
Gradually, a step at a time, both the Fed and the Comptroller did begin to tighten procedures. They pressed the undercapitalized banks to improve their capital base and meet the higher capital requirements. The largest banks, which had been exempted from the new capital ratios imposed in 1981, were now enjoined to raise more capital and meet the 6 percent minimum. This would, in effect, improve their insurance against future failure if one of the major LDC debtors defaulted.
但夏季银行业无声的危机即将对美国经济产生更为深远的影响。主要银行紧张不安的行为正在扰乱美联储自己对货币供应量的计算，而混乱导致中央银行陷入了另一个严重错误。美联储3月份紧缩的刹车效应将被无意中加重——美国经济被推到了衰退的边缘。
The Comptroller’s office also began applying the sting of public discipline, the legal weapon that regulators had hesitated to invoke against Continental Illinois before its collapse. In an extraordinary move in the autumn, First Chicago and Bank of America were both publicly chastised and compelled to accept a regulatory order that directed them to tighten their lending policies, build up their capital reserves and make provisions to protect themselves against the kind of short-term liquidity crisis that had brought down Continental.
谁是下一个？全国主要银行的管理者被黑暗的谣言和紧张的投资者所包围，他们随时准备在出现任何困难迹象时撤走数十亿美元。当美国金融公司，全国最大的储蓄贷款机构，也陷入融资危机时，第二次震动动摇了他们的神经。这家洛杉矶的储蓄机构迅速流失了80亿美元的存款，联邦监管机构不得不以巨额紧急贷款来支撑它。
Bankers grumbled at the new heavy-handedness from federal regulators, but the financial markets were hardly panicked by the news. Investors were already quite familiar with the big problems facing these banks, and, if anything, investors may have been reassured to see the federal regulators at last forcing troubled banks to take remedial action.
在这种情况下，任何被视为大量且频繁从美联储借款的银行可能成为市场流言中的下一个失败候选者。一夜之间，它可能像Continental一样被恐慌的投资者席卷而去。匹兹堡的一位银行经济学家直截了当地说："由于Continental伊利诺伊，我们不想从贴现窗口借款。如果人们看到我们大量借款，他们会认为我们处于同样的处境。"
But the summer’s silent crisis in banking was about to have a far more profound impact on the American economy. The jittery behavior of the major banks was disrupting the Federal Reserve’s own calculations about the money supply, and the confusion led the central bank into another serious error. The braking effects of the Fed’s March tightening would be inadvertently compounded—and the American economy driven to the edge of recession.
全国各地的紧张银行管理者采取了同样的策略——他们低调地囤积了更大规模的超额储备，以防万一，并大幅减少了从美联储的常规借款。连续数周，商业银行的贴现借款水平保持在神秘的低位——是美联储预期的一半或更少。
Who’s next? The managers of the nation’s leading banks were surrounded by dark rumors and nervous investors, poised to withdraw billions at the least hint of difficulty. A second tremor jarred their nerves when Financial Corporation of America, the largest savings and loan in the nation, was caught in a funding crisis too. The Los Angeles thrift swiftly lost $8 billion in deposits and federal regulators had to prop it up with huge emergency loans.34
货币管理的正常液压机制被此打乱，其效果是反常地具有破坏性的。"银行没有像我们预期的那样借款，"Volcker解释说。"因此，市场比我们预期的更紧。"Charles Partee更直白地说。"当银行停止借款时，"Partee说，"我们最终比我们打算的还要紧。"
Under these circumstances, any bank that was seen borrowing heavily and often from the Fed might become the next candidate for failure in the market gossip. Overnight, it might be swept away by panicky investors, just as Continental had been. A Pittsburgh bank economist put it plainly: “We didn’t want to borrow at the Discount window because of Continental Illinois. If people saw us borrowing a lot, they would think we were in the same place.”
然而，这些对理事们或美联储的操作者来说并不是立即清晰的。通常，美联储的技术人员依赖每日贴现借款水平作为信贷状况的主要指标。贴现贷款通常在资金紧张时上升；当货币供应充足和宽松时下降。到6月中旬，正常的信号变得完全混乱。银行的贴现借款在下降，这是供应过剩的信号。但市场利率在上升，这是条件正在收紧的迹象。到底是哪一种——宽松还是紧缩？美联储应该提取准备金还是增加准备金？它无法确定。
Across the country, nervous bank managers adopted the same strategy—they discreetly hoarded larger pools of excess reserves, just in case, and they grossly curtailed their routine borrowings from the Fed. For weeks, the level of Discount borrowing by commercial banks remained mysteriously low—half or less of what the Federal Reserve anticipated.
混乱误导了美联储自己的管理层，因为通常公开市场操作台假设它提供给银行系统的一部分新准备金会通过贴现窗口流出，其余部分通过公开市场交易流出。这两个阀门通常是相互协调的。但是，如果一个阀门卡住了，另一个阀门就必须开得更大——否则经济就会缺乏货币。
The normal hydraulics of money management were thrown off by this and the effect was perversely damaging. “The banks were not borrowing as we expected them to do,” Volcker explained. “Therefore, the market was tighter than we anticipated.” Charles Partee put it more bluntly. “When the banks stopped borrowing,” Partee said, “we ended up being tighter than we intended.”
这正是仲夏发生的情况。美联储迟迟未能认识到，是紧张的银行管理者，而不是变化的经济状况，解释了这一异常现象。美联储没有通过公开市场交易提供更多的资金来弥补贴现窗口的缺口，而是允许货币供应变得越来越紧。
None of this was instantly clear, however, to the governors or the Fed’s operators. Normally, Federal Reserve technicians relied on the daily level of Discount borrowing as a major indicator of credit conditions. Discount lending normally went up when money was tight; it subsided when the money supply was ample and easy. By mid-June, the normal signals became utterly confused. The Discount borrowing by banks was subsiding, a signal of excess supply. But the market interest rates were rising, a sign that conditions were tightening. Which was it—easy or tight? Should the Fed be withdrawing reserves or adding more? It couldn’t tell for sure.
联邦基金利率——美联储自己在3月份推高的——在6月和7月又上升了100到150个基点。其他短期利率相应上升。对实体经济的最终影响——尽管是无意的——是更用力地踩了刹车。
The confusion misled the Fed’s own management because normally the Open Market Desk assumed that a portion of the new reserves it provided to the banking system would flow through the Discount window, the rest through open-market transactions. The two valves were routinely coordinated with each other. But, if one valve got stuck, the other valve would have to be opened wider—or else the economy would be starved for money.
当联邦公开市场委员会在7月开会时，多数人拒绝了技术人员对所发生事件的解释，而是得出结论认为，利率上升反映了真实的经济势头。他们同意，经济正在从去年冬天的繁荣步伐放缓，但仍然以"相当大规模的扩张速度"在运行。
That is what happened by midsummer. The Federal Reserve was slow to recognize that it was the nervous bank managers, not changing economic conditions, that explained the anomaly. Rather than provide more money through open-market transactions to make up for the shortfall at the Discount window, the Federal Reserve allowed the money supply to get tighter and tighter.
Preston Martin再次成为少数的一人，他认为金融领域的脆弱状况太严重了，不能再让利率进一步上升。他再次引用了他老本行储蓄贷款行业的不稳定状态。"我们刚刚把他们扔给了狼群，"Martin抱怨道。"我论证了局势太脆弱了。由于经济的关键部门和金融市场对高利率的脆弱性，我们必须采取宽松政策。"
The Federal Funds rate, which the Fed itself had pushed up in March, rose again in June and July by another 100 to 150 basis points. Other short-term interest rates moved up accordingly. The ultimate effect on the real economy—though unintended—was to step still harder on the brake.
而Martin再次未能说服。结果是经济的进一步恶化。经济并没有像FOMC预测的那样继续稳健扩张；它急剧放缓，几乎停滞。美联储的管理错误反映在货币供应量所发生的情况上。6月份，M1增长了近11%。7月份，M1收缩了——减少了1%。
When the Federal Open Market Committee met in July, the majority rejected the technicians’ interpretation of what was happening and, instead, concluded that the rising interest rates reflected real economic momentum. The economy, they agreed, was slowing down from the booming pace of last winter, but it was still moving along at a “relatively sizable rate of expansion.”
对于Reagan繁荣来说，意外的仲夏紧缩就像最后一击——把它最后一口气也打掉了。实际利率是衡量美联储为了压制强劲复苏所做所为的最清晰尺度。在1984年春季和夏季，长期国债的实际利率上升到惊人的9.6%——这是自1979年Paul Volcker担任主席以来，按实际价值计算的最高货币成本。实际利率几乎是美联储在1982年管理经济收缩时所施加的惩罚性条件的双倍。它们大约是扩张周期正常货币成本的四倍。更糟糕的是，实际利率的上升不可避免地在外汇市场上将美元推得更高，加深了对美国生产商的损害。
Once again, Preston Martin was a minority of one, arguing that the fragile conditions in finance were too grave to let interest rates rise further. Once again, he cited the precarious state of his old industry, the savings and loans. “We just threw them to the wolves,” Martin complained. “I argued the situation is too vulnerable. We have to be accommodative because of the vulnerability of key sectors of the economy and of financial markets to the high interest rates.”
到了秋季季度，经济疲软，以乏善可陈的2.1%的速度增长，而且还在进一步减弱。8月份，失业率回升到7.5%。Ronald Reagan的繁荣结束了。
And, once again, Martin did not persuade. The outcome was further deterioration for the economy. It did not continue to expand smartly, as the FOMC predicted; it slowed down abruptly, almost to a halt. The Fed’s management error was reflected in what happened to the money supply. In June, M-l had grown by nearly 11 percent. In July, M-l contracted—shrinking by 1 percent.
"我们在放松方面有点慢，"Paul Volcker承认。"我当时认为我们有点晚了。"
For the Reagan boom, the inadvertent midsummer tightening was like a final thump—knocking the last wind out of it. Real interest rates were the clearest measure of what the Fed had done to squelch the roaring recovery. In the spring and ^summer of 1984, the real interest rate on long-term government bonds rose to an extraordinary 9.6 percent—the highest cost of money, in real terms, since Paul Volcker had become chairman in 1979. Real rates were nearly double the punishing conditions the Fed had imposed back in 1982 when it was managing the economic contraction. They were roughly four times higher than the normal cost of money during an expansionary cycle. Worse still, rising real interest rates, inevitably, drove the dollar still higher in foreign exchange, compounding the damage to American producers.
Volcker得出结论说美联储必须逆转方向——而且要快——但美联储主席无法说服他的同事们。当他们8月21日开会时，这一次他在联邦公开市场委员会内未能如愿。在他的整个任期内，只有少数几次Volcker对货币政策的看法在政策制定委员会中没有占上风，至少以妥协的形式。8月份的FOMC会议就是那些场合之一，也许是最明显的一次。
By the fall quarter, the economy was limp, growing at a lackluster rate of 2.1 percent and still weakening. In August, the unemployment rate crept back up to 7.5 percent. Ronald Reagan’s boom was over.
Volcker对前景相当确定，但委员会的大多数成员坚持认为美联储不能放松。"经济会强劲反弹的，"他们告诉他。
“We were a little bit slow to ease,” Paul Volcker conceded. “I thought at the time we were a bit late.”
在辩论中与主席对立的是一些资深的美联储系统成员——Gramley、Wallich、Partee——他们提出了一年多来一直提出的同样论点。美联储在早期周期中总是在这个点上失去控制。如果现在不坚守，价格通胀将在六九个月后升级，中央银行将不得不再次进行痛苦的追赶游戏。美联储的主要目标是维持物价稳定，它应该专注于这唯一的目标，而不是刺激经济的竞争性目标。
Volcker concluded that the Fed must reverse course—and quickly —but the Federal Reserve chairman could not convince his colleagues. When they met on August 21, for once, he did not get his way within the Federal Open Market Committee. Throughout his tenure, there were only a few occasions when Volcker’s perspective on monetary policy did not prevail inside the policy-making committee, at least in a compromised form. The August meeting of the FOMC was one of those occasions, perhaps the starkest one.
"Gramley赢得了这场争论，"一位FOMC官员说。"Gramley总是担心，如果他们现在不保持紧缩，按照趋势的发展，下一次就太晚了。"
Volcker was fairly certain about the outlook, but most of the committee members were insisting that the Fed couldn’t ease. “The economy’s going to come roaring back,” they told him.
Volcker援引技术原因来解释为什么货币太紧了，比他们打算的更紧。紧张的银行家们远离贴现窗口，打乱了正常的计算。大多数人没有买账。
Arrayed against the chairman in the debate were the senior System men—Gramley, Wallich, Partee—making much the same argument they had made through the year. The Fed had always lost control at this point in earlier cycles. If it did not hold firm now, price inflation would escalate six or nine months hence and the central bank would, once again, have to play a painful game of catch-up. The Fed’s primary objective was to maintain stable prices, and it should concentrate on that single goal, not on the competing objective of stimulating the economy.
"委员会不愿意接受正在发生异常情况的说法，"Partee理事说。"我们担心过于宽松。"
“Gramley won the argument,” an FOMC officer said. “Gramley always expressed concern that if they didn’t stay tight now, the next time would be too late with the way the trends are breaking.”
联邦公开市场委员会内部的尖锐分歧并未反映在最终投票或FOMC会议纪要中。主席本人通常不投异议票，即使他没有如愿。那会暗示中央银行内部的动荡，更别提对其领导人的个人尴尬。Volcker屈服于其他人，正如FOMC会议纪要晦涩地报告的那样："除了一个人之外，所有成员都表示他们接受一项指令，即此时不改变储备头寸的压力程度。"持异议者是Henry Wallich，他像往常一样希望美联储进一步收紧。
Volcker invoked the technical reasons to explain why money was too tight, tighter than they intended. Nervous bankers were staying away from the Discount window and throwing off the normal calculations. The majority did not buy it.
Paul Volcker输了。然而他仍然如愿以偿。尽管联邦公开市场委员会指示保持立场，美联储主席还是自己继续行动，放松了政策。Volcker在FOMC内只有一票，但他管理着美联储。特别是，他监督着纽约公开市场操作台的日常运营。8月会议后，Volcker对FOMC的指令做出了自己宽泛的解释，并告诉公开市场操作台放松。
“The committee was reluctant to accept the notion that something exceptional was going on,” Governor Partee said. “We were concerned about being too easy.”
"我知道他给纽约操作台什么指示，"纽约联储主席Anthony Solomon说，"我认为他没有忠实地遵守FOMC的指示。他让操作台采取了远远超出指令框架的重大行动。他个人过于任意了。"
The sharp division within the Federal Open Market Committee was not revealed by the final vote or by the FOMC’s minutes. The chairman himself did not normally dissent, even when he did not get his way. That would suggest internal turmoil at the central bank, not to mention personal embarrassment for its leader. Volcker yielded to the others, and as the FOMC minutes opaquely reported, “All but one member indicated their acceptance of a directive specifying no change at this time in the degree of pressure on reserve positions.” The dissenter was Henry Wallich, who as usual wanted the Fed to tighten still more.
Volcker对来自其他委员会成员的"抱怨"不以为意。他无视FOMC的决定而迅速行动还有一个次要动机——即将到来的秋季选举。越接近选举日，美联储就越难在不被一方或另一方指责为政治化的情况下执行重大的政策转向。
Paul Volcker lost. Yet he still got his way. The Federal Reserve chairman went ahead on his own and eased anyway, despite the Federal Open Market Committee’s instructions to hold firm. Volcker had only one vote within the FOMC, but he ran the Federal Reserve. In particular, he supervised the daily operations of the Open Market Desk in New York. After the August meeting, Volcker made his own liberal interpretation of the FOMC’s directive and told the Open Market Desk to ease.
"Volcker认为出于经济原因必须放松，"一位FOMC官员解释道，"但如果他等到选举之后，可能就太晚了。实际上，机会之窗正在关闭。从劳动节到选举日，机会之窗是关闭的。他想放松，而那大约是他能做到而不使其显得过于政治化的最后时机。"
“I knew what instructions he was giving the New York desk,” Anthony Solomon, president of the New York Fed, said, “and I thought he was not faithfully observing the instructions of the FOMC. He got the desk to take major action that went way beyond the framework of the directive. He was taking too much leeway personally.”
一些FOMC成员并不觉得好笑。当他们看到利率下降时，他们打电话给纽约联储的Tony Solomon寻求解释。"他们打电话给我，他们打电话给操作台，问：'这是怎么回事？'"Solomon说。"操作台在远远超出指令地放松。操作台放松是因为Volcker告诉它这么做。我告诉他：'你做的事情的实质没问题，但你之前应该与委员会召开电话会议。FOMC的士气会动摇。'"
Volcker shrugged off the “bitching” from other committee members. He had a secondary motive for ignoring the FOMC decision and moving quickly—the approaching fall election. The closer it came to election day, the harder it would be for the Federal Reserve to execute a major policy turn without being accused of politics by one side or the other.
尽管同事们有牢骚，Volcker在经济问题上是对的。它需要美联储的帮助——大量的帮助。主席单方面放松货币政策的决定拉低了短期利率，联邦基金利率逐步下降了约100个基点。然而，这还不够。美联储必须比那放松得更多才能保持复苏的活力。
“Volcker thought it was necessary to ease for economic reasons,” an FOMC official explained, “but if he waited until after the election, that might be too late. In effect, the window was closing. From Labor Day till election day, the window is closed. He wanted to ease and that was about the last point he could do it without making it look too political.”
相当突然地，美联储被迫将注意力从刹车转移到油门。几个月来，中央银行一直担心放慢它认为过于看涨的经济复苏。现在美联储全神贯注于重新启动一个突然看起来危险疲软的经济。
Some of the FOMC members were not amused. When they saw interest rates declining, they called Tony Solomon at the New York Fed for an explanation. “They called me, they called the desk, asking, ‘What’s going on?’” Solomon said. “The desk was easing way beyond the directive. The desk was easing because Volcker was telling it to. I told him: ‘The substance of what you did is all right, but you should have called a conference call with the committee before you did it. The morale of the FOMC gets shaky.’”
Ronald Reagan连任竞选所唤起的持久形象是美国柔焦的画面。他最具说服力的电视广告传达了对日常生活的浪漫化愿景，普通人物们起身忙碌、上班、升旗的场面。"美国又是早晨了，"播音员用睿智温和的声音宣布。镜头巡视着一个舒适的社区，到处瞥见了繁荣和好感，一场家庭婚礼，一个带有白色尖桩篱笆的新家。"镇上几乎所有人都在想着同一件事——既然我们的国家正在好转，我们为什么还要回头呢？"
Despite the grumbling from his colleagues, Volcker was right about the economy. It needed help from the Fed—lots of help. The chairman’s unilateral decision to relax money policy pulled short-term interest rates down as the Federal Funds rate gradually declined by about 100 basis points. That was not enough, however. The Federal Reserve would have to ease much more than that to keep the recovery alive.
在竞选集会之前，总统宣布了一个新时代的曙光。"美国回来了，"他在俄亥俄州说，"一个巨人重新出现在舞台上。我们国家在其重振的精神中强大，在其经济中强大，在世界经济中强大，并且在其保卫自身和维护和平的能力中强大。"
Rather abruptly, the Federal Reserve was compelled to shift its attention from the brake to the accelerator. For many months, the central bank had been worried about slowing down an economic recovery it considered too bullish. Now the Fed was preoccupied with restarting an economy that suddenly seemed dangerously weak.
对于加州观众，总统为他的第二个任期提供了一个新口号："你们还什么都没看到呢。"
The enduring image evoked by Ronald Reagan’s campaign for reelection was of America in soft focus. His most compelling television commercial conveyed a romanticized vision of everyday life, scenes of ordinary people up and doing, heading for work, raising the flag. “It’s morning again in America,” the moderator announced in a wise, gentle voice. The camera toured a comfortable community, glimpsing prosperity and good feeling everywhere it turned, a family wedding, a new home with a white picket fence. “Just about everyone in town is thinking the same thing—now that our country is turning around, why would we ever turn back again?”
他每到一处，人群挥舞着旗帜，自发地高呼起来，"美利坚——美利坚——美利坚。"国家获胜了。总统援引了奥运火炬的精神，这把火炬被人手传手传递穿越全国到达洛杉矶奥运会，在那里美国运动员在爱国庆典中赢得了许多金牌。"美利坚——美利坚——美利坚，"人群欢欣鼓舞地自我庆祝着。
Before campaign rallies, the President announced the dawning of a new era. “America is back,” he said in Ohio, “a giant re-emergent on the scene. Our country is powerful in its renewed spirits, powerful in its economy, powerful in the world economy, and powerful in its ability to defend itself and secure the peace.”35
于是，通过言语和图像，总统将民族主义和资本主义融合在一起，这是美国文化和政治的两个锚定观念——爱国主义和繁荣结合在一场庆典中。Ronald Reagan，凭借他的表演技巧和阳光个性，以现代政治中无与伦比的戏剧性热情唤起了这些熟悉的象征。然而，这种融合对美国政治来说并不新鲜。民族性的能量一直被用来支持资本主义企业的特权。
For a California audience, the President offered a new slogan for his second term: “You ain’t seen nothing yet.”
六十年前，Thorstein Veblen写道："……民族主义的幻觉使底层民众相信，共同利益与那些国家的体制逐渐被拖入为其服务的偿付能力巨头们的商业利益紧密相连……"还有Veblen写道："对国家自负的不加批判的奉献是一种值得称赞的习惯，也是一种有用的伪装物，是那些可能否则就会被质疑的营利性企业和交易的庇护所，是一种避免不利注意并获得有利可图的善意行径的手段。"
Everywhere he traveled, the crowds waved flags and broke into a spontaneous chant, “U.S.A.—U.S.A.—U.S.A.” The nation triumphant. The President invoked the spirit of the Olympic torch that had been carried hand to hand across the country to the Olympic games in Los Angeles, where American athletes won many gold medals amid the patriotic pageantry. “U.S.A.—U.S.A.—U.S.A.,” the crowds chanted in joyous self-congratulation.
1984年秋季的爱国庆祝活动，作为让Ronald Reagan连任的手段极为有效，同时也充当了一件"伪装物"。国旗就像一层掩盖巨大讽刺和伟大幻觉的面纱。就在Reagan竞选活动宣称"美国又是早晨"的那一刻，一片昏暗正在经济活动上降临。那不是黎明，而是Ronald Reagan繁荣经济接近黄昏的时刻。
Thus, with words and pictures, the President fused nationalism and capitalism, the two anchoring ideas of American culture and politics —patriotism and prosperity joined in a single celebration. Ronald Reagan, given his performing skills and sunny personality, evoked the familiar symbols with a melodramatic enthusiasm unmatched in modern politics. The fusion was not, however, new to American politics. The energy of nationhood had always been employed to support the prerogatives of capitalist enterprise.
当总统承诺"你们还什么都没看到呢"时，这句口号有一种滑稽的双关含义。公众确实没有看到。选民们从他们的经历出发有理由相信，国家正在向前向上发展。他们没有意识到迅猛的扩张阶段已经结束，经济正陷入一种时断时续的增长和停滞的不稳定常规，一个低于平均增长和高于平均失业的时代。
Sixty years earlier, Thorstein Veblen had written: “. . . the illusions of nationalism allowed the underlying population to believe that the common good was bound up with the business advantage of these captains of solvency into whose service the national establishment was gradually drawn. ...” And Veblen wrote: “Uncritical devotion to the national pretensions being a meritorious habit, it is also a useful article of camouflage, a shelter for gainful enterprises and transactions which might otherwise be open to doubt, a means of avoiding unfavorable notice and of procuring a profitable line of goodwill.”36
新闻媒体放大了这些幻觉。当商业版尽职地报道经济活动的放缓时，头版上的政治报道则发出更响亮的信息，支持总统的说法。美国人不知道——新闻媒体也没有告诉他们——繁荣的浪潮更像是虚假的短暂激增。
The patriotic celebration in the fall of 1984, wonderfully effective as a device to re-elect Ronald Reagan, also served as an “article of camouflage.” The flag was like a veil that concealed large ironies, great illusions. At the very moment when the Reagan campaign was proclaiming “morning again in America,” a dusky pallor was settling over business activity. It was not dawn, but closer to twilight for Ronald Reagan’s booming economy.
席卷全国的民族主义精神和爱国热情的温暖光芒尤其具有讽刺意味。复苏的美国经济确实正引领全球经济从全球衰退中恢复。但美国政府的经济政策一点都不民族主义。国内产业——制造业、农业、石油和其他矿产——正因华盛顿财政和货币政策的综合影响而遭受重创。Ronald Reagan美国复兴的象征——强势美元——实际上正在帮助美国的外国竞争对手，将越来越大份额的全球贸易让给其他国家。
When the President promised, “You ain’t seen nothing yet,” the slogan had a droll double meaning. The public, it was true, did not see. Voters believed, reasonably enough from what they had experienced, that the country was headed onward and upward. They did not realize that the roaring phase of expansion had already ended, that the economy was settling into a bumpy routine of fitful starts and stagnation, an era of below-average growth and above-average unemployment.
然而，总统的竞选主题如果不是看起来与美国选民的真实人类经验一致，就不可能有效。大多数普通公民从自己生活中所了解的似乎与总统告诉他们的真相相符。价格通胀的焦虑消失了。大多数人回到了工作岗位。最重要的是，在直接的政治意义上，人均实际可支配收入在整个竞选年份以非凡的速度增长——上升了5%。1980年，当Jimmy Carter寻求连任时，价格通胀超过13%，实际可支配收入由于短暂的衰退而略微下降。当President Reagan问"你比四年前过得更好吗？"时，答案对大多数美国人来说似乎不言而喻。
The news media amplified the illusions. While the business section dutifully reported the slowdown in economic activity, the political coverage on the front page blared a louder message, supporting the President’s claims. Americans did not know—and the news media did not tell them—that the rising tide of prosperity was more in the nature of a false, brief surge.37
改善的经济状况被总结在"痛苦指数"中，这是经济学家Arthur Okun的一个玩笑式的发明，两党的政客都借用在总统竞选活动中。痛苦指数简单地将失业率与通货膨胀率相加，作为经济不适的粗略衡量。1980年，它超过19%。到1984年，痛苦指数下降到11%。
The spirit of nationalism, the warming glow of patriotic fervor that swept the country, was especially ironic. The resurgent American economy was, indeed, leading the global economy back from the worldwide recession. But the U.S. government’s economic policies were anything but nationalistic. Domestic industries—manufacturing, agriculture, oil and other minerals—were being decimated by the combined effects of Washington’s fiscal and monetary policy. Ronald Reagan’s symbol of American revival, the strong dollar, was in reality assisting America’s foreign competitors, ceding a larger and larger share of world trade to other nations.
然而，这种比较中缺少了一个揭示经济困境的重要维度——历史高位的实际利率。如果民主党人想关注这个问题，他们可能会援引费城联储前行长David P. Eastburn设计的"困难指数"。为了将信贷成本作为经济痛苦的一个重要元素纳入，困难指数简单地将住房抵押贷款的实际利率与通胀率和失业率相加。这种组合提供了普通公民面临的经济现实的一个更好的快照。在Eastburn的困难指数上，1980年和1984年看起来相同。在这两年，该指数都停留在21的不舒服高位。
The President’s campaign themes could not have been effective, however, if they had not seemed consistent with the authentic human experience of American voters. What most ordinary citizens knew from their own lives appeared to match what the President told them was true. The anxiety of price inflation was gone. Most people were back at work. Most important of all, in immediate political terms, real disposable income per capita was rising throughout the campaign year at an extraordinary pace—up 5 percent. In 1980, when Jimmy Carter was up for re-election, price inflation was above 13 percent and real disposable income was shrinking slightly, thanks to the brief recession. When President Reagan asked, “Are you better off than you were four years ago?,” the answer seemed self-evident for the majority of Americans.
高利率带来的递减财富分配不容易转化为政治议题。诚然，人口被鲜明地分为金融净债务人和债权人——55%是借款人，45%是债权人。高货币成本的好处流向少数人，特别是拥有大部分金融净财富的富裕的10%。由于这在政治中从未被讨论过，没有多少选民理解这一点。
The improved economic conditions were summarized in the “misery index,” a playful invention of economist Arthur Okun, which politicians of both parties had borrowed for presidential campaigns. The misery index simply added the unemployment rate with the inflation rate as a rough measure of economic discomfort. In 1980, it was more than 19 percent. By 1984, the misery index was down to 11 percent.
然而，即使存在普遍意识，也不确定政治影响会是什么。鉴于选举民主的衰败状态，仅有略过半数的美国成年人在总统选举中投票。实际投票的选民中受教育程度更高、更富裕的美国人比例偏高——这些人整体上是从政府的高利率中受益的。可以想象，投票的美国人的多数实际上喜欢高利率。债务人虽然是公民的多数，但在选举时的影响较小。这有助于解释为什么政客，特别是那些自诩为不幸者说话的民主党人，几乎从不涉及债务和利率的阶级含义。为债务人说话时——其中许多人从不投票——候选人冒着冒犯确实投票的债权人的风险。
Still, a telling dimension of economic distress was missing from that comparison—the historically high level of real interest rates. Democrats, if they had wished to focus on the issue, might have invoked the “hardship index,” devised by David P. Eastburn, former president of the Philadelphia Fed. To incorporate the cost of credit as an important element of economic pain, the hardship index simply added the real interest rate on home mortgages to the inflation and unemployment rates. That combination provided a better snapshot of the economic reality facing average citizens. On Eastburn’s hardship index, 1980 and 1984 looked identical. In both years, the index was stuck at the uncomfortably high level of 21.38
无论如何，大多数公民和大多数政客并不以这些术语来评估经济福祉的状况。他们着眼于现在和不久前的过去，而不是对遥远未来的影响。1984年，收入上升的直接证据是远比以惩罚性利率借钱的长期后果更令人信服的现实。Walter Mondale的总统竞选无法成功反驳大多数公民相信为真实的东西。美国回来了，就像总统说的那样。
The regressive wealth distribution that flowed from the high interest rates did not easily translate into a political issue. The population, it was true, was starkly divided into net financial debtors and creditors —the 55 percent who were borrowers and the 45 percent who were creditors. The benefits of the high cost of money were flowing to the minority and especially to the wealthy 10 percent who owned most of the net financial wealth. Since this was never talked about in politics, not many voters understood it.
"对Reagan政府来说，"预算主任David Stockman沉思道，"纯属运气，复苏在选举时完美达到了顶峰。然后它放缓了，此后一直低于趋势运行。"
Yet, even if there had been general awareness, it was not certain what the political impact would be. Given the decayed condition of electoral democracy, barely more than half of adult Americans voted in presidential elections. The electorate that actually voted was top-heavy with better-educated, more prosperous Americans—the same people who, on the whole, benefited from the government’s high interest rates. Conceivably, a majority of the voting Americans actually liked the high rates. The debtors, though a majority of citizens, counted for less at election time. This helped explain why politicians, particularly Democrats who presumed to speak for the less fortunate, almost never addressed the class implications of debt and interest rates. In speaking for the debtors, many of whom never voted, a candidate risked offending the creditors, who did.

In the fall of 1984, the Fed was easing interest rates.

The recovery had been driven into the ground. Conceivably, as some might claim, the Federal Reserve was not to blame. An economic recovery always slowed down after an initial boom of eighteen to twenty-four months and, therefore, the abrupt subsidence in 1984 might be attributed to “natural forces” of the business cycle. No one, of course, could prove definitively that this was wrong. After the second quarter of 1984, the path of expansion remained below the historic trend line for economic growth, even below the growth rate during the decade of the 1970s, which was universally considered disappointing. The economy did not go into recession; it sputtered and started and disappointed normal expectations.

Paul Volcker was utterly unsuccessful in his efforts to persuade Congress to back off and reduce the federal deficits. Nevertheless, under extreme circumstances, the Fed had not given in. It successfully resisted all the pressures for an easier money policy, including threats and complaints from the White House. Jobs, production, home ownership, industrial development, an equitable distribution of incomes and wealth—all of society’s competing economic objectives would suffer the consequences of placing money first, as indeed they did. 对于农业来说，出口市场继续萎缩。两年内，这一趋势产生了一个惊人的标志，标志着美国作为食物和纤维生产国角色的逆转。到1986年，很明显美国，世界上最富饶的农业国，将成为农产品的净进口国。
Over four years, the cumulative appreciation of the dollar was more than 50 percent—a rough measure of the price disadvantage that the U.S. government had imposed on all the trade-sensitive sectors in the American economy. It was no mystery why American companies were closing domestic factories and moving production abroad or why farmers in Kansas could no longer sell their surplus wheat overseas.

Volcker, like Solomon, claimed the Fed was helpless. “Somewhere else,” in the Fed’s perspective, was fiscal policy. The central bank justified its own performance—passively tolerating the dollar’s gross swing in value—by insisting it could do nothing else in the face of the federal deficits. Paul Volcker, it seemed, was as adept as Ronald Reagan at deflecting blame. The Federal Reserve’s self-defense was, to put it charitably, strained. It suggested that the Fed had no discretion in the matter, that there was no other course for monetary policy except the one the Fed had chosen. But that left aside the controversial choices—and mistakes—that the Federal Reserve had made for itself, the extraordinary strategy of high interest rates it followed during recovery and, in particular, the decision to brake the boom in 1984.
对大多数美国家庭来说，人生中有两个主要购买——汽车和房子——没有其他交易对他们的幸福感如此重要。然而，在Reagan复苏中发生着奇怪的事情：美国人买房子和汽车的数量不像以前那么多。在对复苏繁荣的普遍兴高采烈中，这就像一个保守得最好的秘密。新闻杂志和其他媒体反而专注于记述雅皮士的出现，这是一群高收入、品味奢华的新城市专业人士。作为一个代表性群体，雅皮士不可能距离美国消费模式的真实情况更远了。
Neither of those choices was inevitable, and the excessive nature of what the Fed did was suggested by three different measures. First, real interest rates actually rose to their highest levels as the economy was losing steam—clear evidence that the central bank was overdoing its restraint. Second, economic growth did not simply moderate in 1984, it nearly came to a halt. Third, the general price average did not remain at the moderate level established by the great contraction of 1981-1982, but began falling further—tightening the squeeze on debtors and producers. If it had chosen, the Federal Reserve could have stopped well short of these extremes without yielding to runaway inflation. If the Fed had chosen to be more moderate on interest rates, the devastation from the rising dollar would have been less drastic too.

Congress and the President left the management of money to the central bank. The central bank pointed the finger back at Congress and the President. The ambiguity posed the sort of fine-grain question that policy analysts in Washington enjoyed studying. What was the economic cause of the dollar’s drastic appreciation? What or who was to blame for the unfortunate devastation—monetary policy or fiscal policy, the Federal Reserve or the executive branch and Congress? Their conclusions were invariably carefully hedged assessments of cause and effect. For the millions of citizens and business enterprises that suffered the consequences, a simpler answer was sufficient: the U.S. government was to blame, all of it.

The retreat from home buying was more striking and also more fundamental to the American standard of living. The housing industry surged and ebbed month to month during the recovery, usually following changes in interest rates, but home builders never came close to reaching the potential market of new home buyers in the 1980s. The lost customers in housing naturally extended to durable goods like refrigerators and washing machines that new homeowners would purchase.
四十年来第一次，拥有自己住房的美国人百分比在Reagan总统任期内实际上下降了。虽然在政治竞选中很少有人谈论它，但这对美国生活模式来说是最重大的转折点。自1940年以来，住房拥有率的上升一直是一个既定事实。在各种联邦补贴的刺激下，包括政府设定的利率上限，美国人的住房拥有率从1940年的44%稳步上升到1980年的66%。从1981年开始，住房拥有率自二战以来首次下降。到1984年，下降到64.5%。到1986年，将下降到63.9%。

If homeownership was the essential element of the “American dream,” the dream was becoming more selective.

For the first time in forty years, the percentage of American families that owned their own homes actually decreased during the Reagan Presidency. Though no one talked about it much in the political campaigns, this was a most significant turning point for the pattern of American life. Rising levels of homeownership had been a given since 1940. Stimulated by federal subsidies of various sorts, including the government-imposed ceilings on interest rates, the rate of homeownership among Americans had increased steadily from 44 percent in 1940 to 66 percent by 1980. Starting in 1981, homeownership began to decline for the first time since World War II. By 1984, it was down to 64.5 percent. By 1986, it would fall to 63.9 percent.

“Although the annual declines have been small, they cumulatively offset all of the gains in homeownership achieved during the boom years of the mid-to-late 1970s,” the MIT-Harvard Joint Center for Housing Studies reported. In other words, if homeownership was a shared measure of national progress, then the nation had regressed to the pre-1973 level during the era of Reagan and Volcker.46
然而，在整个复苏过程中，潜在损失的趋势被消费的普遍充足所掩盖。毕竟，经济学家定义的消费者需求相当强劲。个人收入平均强劲增长。Reagan复苏主要由个人支出推动（尽管可以肯定，美国人购买的更大份额是从国外进口的）。两者怎么可能同时正确——一个有利于消费的复苏同时失去数百万购买汽车、房屋和其他商品的潜在客户？
Virtually all of the lost ground was suffered by young people, families under thirty-five years old. Except perhaps for Yuppies, the young families that could buy got smaller homes for their money and were compelled to pay much larger shares of their incomes to keep them.
较高的利率是答案的一部分。另一个解释是收入的分配不公。总体上和平均而言，消费者可用于购买东西的货币收入看起来绰绰有余。当你仔细看时，很明显增加的收入中有巨大份额流向了高收入家庭（包括备受瞩目的雅皮士）。国家收入的递减份额流向了经济阶梯下半部分的所有家庭——那些本也会购买汽车、房屋和其他好东西的人，如果他们负担得起的话。
At age thirty, the average household must now devote 44 percent of its monthly earnings to owning a new home—double what young homeowners had had to pay ten years before.47
根据人口普查局的统计，最底层的40%的实际收入中位数从1980年到1984年下降了约每个家庭477美元，下降超过3%。而最顶层的10%的收入中位数增加了5085美元，增长超过7%。1980年代关键的政治决策，从Reagan经济计划到Volcker的货币政策，都促成了收入的递减性转变。依赖工资收入的人所失去的，被有利息收入的人所获得。
Given this elemental setback in their living standards, it was particularly ironic that younger people voted so enthusiastically for the President, but then the Democratic Party was also largely silent on the subject. As a practical matter, both political parties had retreated from the goal of universal homeownership, first the Democrats, when they abolished interest-rate controls in 1980, then the Republicans, by adopting an economic strategy that assigned a lower priority to providing homes for people. The decline of homeownership, if the government did nothing to reverse it, represented a major redefinition of the American idea of prosperity.
毫不奇怪，实际收入正在萎缩的人不是很好的消费者。经济是由那些有充足钱可花的人——上半部分人——推动的。其他人，特别是年轻人，只好满足于旧梦想的缩小版。总的来说，他们是购买汽车和房屋比以前少的美国人。
Throughout the recovery, however, the trend of lost potential was masked by the general abundance of consumption. After all, consumer demand, as defined by economists, was quite strong. Personal income rose vigorously on average. The Reagan recovery was driven largely by personal spending (though, to be sure, a larger share of what Americans bought was imported from abroad). How could both be true—a pro-consumption recovery that was simultaneously losing millions of the potential customers for cars, houses and other goods?
收入不足的美国人还有另一个选择——他们可以借钱。通过更深地陷入债务，他们可以继续消费并希望他们的前景会改善。数百万家庭可以理解地选择了这个选项。个人债务迅速积累。供应经济学本应带来的储蓄增加不仅没有发生，储蓄率反而降到了新低。家庭倾向于借钱而不是放弃购买，因为很难接受他们降级的新现实——当来自政治和新闻媒体的喧嚣坚称美国人正踏上繁荣的新时代时尤其困难。
Higher interest rates were part of the answer. The other explanation was the maldistribution of incomes. In the aggregate and on average, the money income available to consumers for buying things looked more than ample. When one looked closer, it was clear that a huge share of the increased money income was going to upper-income families (including the celebrated Yuppies). A decreasing share of national incomes was going to all the families on the bottom half of the economic ladder, people who would also buy cars and houses and other nice things, if they could afford them.
大众市场正在分裂，正如《财富》杂志所说，但它也在变小。正在出现的模式对美国经济的未来表现有深远的影响，更不用说家庭生活标准了，但它并没有成为经济学家们专注的问题。当经济学家计算"消费者需求"时，这不是人们生活中需要的总和，即Thorstein Veblen为高效经济提出的标准。消费者需求仅仅包括有购买能力的人们可用的总货币量。
According to the Bureau of the Census, the median real income of the bottom 40 percent had fallen from 1980 to 1984, about $477 per family, a loss of more than 3 percent. The top 10 percent, meanwhile, enjoyed an increase of $5,085 in their median income, an increase of more than 7 percent. The pivotal political decisions of the 1980s, from the Reagan economic program to Volcker’s monetary policy, all contributed to the regressive shift in incomes. What was lost by those who depended on wage incomes was gained by those with interest incomes.48
对于现代经济学家来说，哪些人有货币或他们会花在什么上面似乎无关紧要。一些家庭购买用于度假的第二套住房或第三辆车而其他家庭买不起他们的第一套住房或汽车，这被认为是一个社会问题，而不是经济问题。由于大多数现代政治家依赖于经济学家的定义，他们可能没有察觉到已经发生了什么变化。
Not surprisingly, people whose real incomes were shrinking did not make very good consumers. The economy was carried forward by those who had plenty of money to spend, the upper half. The others, especially young people, simply had to settle for a scaled-down version of the old dream. In general, they were the Americans who were buying fewer cars and houses than they used to buy.
然而，从长远来看，如果趋势不逆转，萎缩的大众市场将成为一个政治问题。这是普通美国人对他们经济的期望吗？或者政治领导人对他们的承诺？用Lyle Gramley的话说，政治含义是炸药，等待爆炸。
People with inadequate real incomes had one other option—they could borrow the money. By going deeper into debt, they could keep spending and hope that their prospects improved. Millions of families understandably chose that option. Personal debt accumulated rapidly. The increased savings that supply-side economics was supposed to deliver not only did not occur but the savings rate fell to new lows. Families were inclined to borrow rather than forgo purchases, because it was difficult to accept the new reality of their reduced status— especially difficult when the clamor from politics and the news media insisted that Americans were embarked on a new era of prosperity.
Paul Volcker战胜通货膨胀的最终讽刺是，从更广泛的意义上说，美联储实际上未能实现其历史目的。中央银行的首要责任是控制经济中信贷的总体扩张，确保社会的雄心不会超越现实从而用债务使未来负担过重。这一次，由于部分超出其控制的原因，美联储无法履行其最基本的职能。
The mass market was splitting apart, as Fortune magazine had said, but it was also getting smaller. The emerging pattern had profound implications for the future performance of the American economy, not to mention family living standards, yet it did not become an absorbing question for economists. When economists calculated “consumer demand,” it was not the sum of what people needed in their lives, the standard that Thorstein Veblen had proposed for an efficient economy. Consumer demand simply consisted of the total money available to the people who could afford to buy things.
相反，在这次经济复苏期间，美国人以前所未有和危险的速度借贷，无论个人还是机构，私营企业还是政府。尽管美联储愿意施加高利率，尽管它采取了抑制经济增长的行动，美联储发现自己在限制Paul Volcker所称的"全国性借贷狂欢"方面基本无能为力。
For modern economists, the question of which people had the money or what they would spend it on seemed irrelevant. The fact that some families were buying second homes for vacations or third cars while others could not afford their first home or car was considered a social question, not a problem for the economy. Since most modern politicians relied on the economists’ definitions, they perhaps did not perceive what had changed.
没有人比美联储理事们自己对债务的爆炸更为担忧。他们知道这就像一把指向未来的枪——同时威胁着公司资产负债表和家庭偿付能力，招致未来的广泛倒闭，或者相反，产生通过回归通胀解救债务人的不可抗拒的压力。
In the long term, however, if the trends were not reversed, the shrinking mass market would become a political question. Was this what average Americans expected from their economy? Or what political leaders had promised them? To use Lyle Gramley’s word, the political implications were dynamite, waiting to explode.
利率是您用来配给信贷需求使其不过度的手段[Partee理事说]。然而，如果你现在看看对信贷的需求，它是特别大——是巨大的。政府债务在上升，家庭债务，公司债务。你怎么能看着这个并得出结论说利率太高了？我说利率还不够高。然而我必须承认经济运行不是很好。这是一个离奇的谜题。
The ultimate irony in Paul Volcker’s triumph over inflation was that, in the larger sense, the Federal Reserve actually failed to accomplish its historic purpose. The overriding responsibility of the central bank was to control the overall expansion of credit in the economy, to insure that the society’s ambitions did not get ahead of reality and thereby overburden the future with debt. This time, for reasons partly beyond its control, the Federal Reserve was unable to fulfill its most basic function.
美联储主席也为这个困境而担忧。他抑制经济上升期的策略需要一种按过去标准高得离谱的信贷价格，实际利率在中央银行自身历史上无与伦比。然而各种借款人都没有却步。对于1984年，联邦公开市场委员会设定了国内总债务增长8%到11%的目标，但经济大幅超过了美联储的目标。全国未偿债务增长14.1%——有记录以来新增借贷最快的一年——创造至少比美联储认为审慎的多了3000亿美元的新债务。
Americans, on the contrary, were borrowing during this economic recovery at an unprecedented and dangerous pace, both individuals and institutions, both private business and government. Despite its willingness to impose high interest rates, despite its actions to restrain economic growth, the Fed found itself largely impotent in curbing what Paul Volcker called “a national binge of borrowing.”
这种巨大的债务创造让我担心[Volcker说]。为什么人们都在做这些坏账？人们可以说利率太高了，按照历史标准和经济状况我当然可能同意。但如果利率太高，为什么债务扩张得这么快？为什么债务相对于GNP以创纪录的速度增长？显然外面有人认为利率不是太高。
No one was more troubled by the explosion of debt than the Fed governors themselves. They knew it was like a gun pointed at the future—threatening corporate balance sheets and household solvency alike, inviting widespread failures down the road or, alternately, producing irresistible pressure to bail out debtors by returning to inflation.
尽管有继承下来的民间传说，积累新债务本身不是不健康的。相反，它是活力的标志。毕竟，没有持续的借贷，现代美国经济无法运作。总的来说，如果家庭不借钱，那意味着他们不买东西。如果公司不负债，那么它们也不建造新工厂和为未来扩大生产和就业。政府债务如果刺激了垂死的私营经济，如果它购买了使社会在未来更有生产力的真实经济资源——道路和运河、学校和大学、基础研究实验室甚至新工厂——也是有创造性的。按照这个标准，二战是历史上最有建设性的借贷狂欢。
Interest rates are the device by which you ration the demand for credit so it won’t be excessive [Governor Partee said]. Yet, if you look at the demand for credit now, it’s extraordinarily large—it’s huge. Government debt is going up, household debt, corporate debt. How can you look at that and conclude that interest rates are too high? I’d say interest rates aren’t high enough. Yet I have to admit the economy isn’t doing very well. It’s a conundrum—extraordinary.
在每一个层面，从家庭到政府，新债务都必须通过两个基本质量问题的判断，任何谨慎的家庭在接受新贷款前都会问自己同样的问题。借来的钱要去买什么——有价值的东西还是不值得为之负债的轻浮之物？其次，家庭能够偿还贷款的可能性有多大？这些是规划债券发行的公司经理，或（至少在理论上）规划赤字预算的政府经济学家所应用的相同基本测试。
The Federal Reserve chairman worried over the dilemma too. His strategy of restraining the economy on its upswing had required a price for credit that was outrageously high by past standards, real interest rates unmatched in the central bank’s own history. Yet borrowers of every kind were not deterred. For 1984, the Federal Open Market Committee set a goal of 8 to 11 percent growth in total domestic debt, but the economy grossly surpassed the Fed’s target. The nation’s outstanding debt grew by 14.1 percent—the fastest year of new borrowing on record—creating at least $300 billion more in new obligations than the Fed had thought prudent.49
在1980年代膨胀的债务中，正是这两个问题引起了警觉。美国新增债务的相当一部分并没有为未来购买什么有生产力的东西。其中很大一部分是为即时消费融资，只有消费者能跟上还款才有活力。而且很多借贷被用于盈利但空洞的金融投机。例如，在1984年，美国企业积累了惊人的1400亿美元新债务，完全用于公司并购的资金。它没有建造任何新工厂。
This tremendous debt creation worries me [Volcker said]. Why are people making all these bad loans? People can say interest rates are too high and I might agree with that, certainly by historic standards and by the conditions in the economy. But if interest rates are too high, why is debt expanding so fast? Why is debt growing at a record rate relative to GNP? Apparently somebody out there doesn’t think interest rates are too high.
随着债务的积累，第二个问题变得更加令人担忧。全国作为一个整体，承担债务的速度快于其增强还款能力的速度。任何借款人都可以这样做一段时间，指望他们的投资最终能收回，或假设他们的收入会赶上他们的债务义务。但当美国经济趋于平稳并失去其强劲扩张时，全国集体赶上其不断增加的贷款的前景似乎越来越有问题。
Despite the inherited folklore, accumulating new debt was not in itself unhealthy. On the contrary, it was a sign of vigor. After all, the modern American economy would not function without the constant lending and borrowing. In general, if families weren’t borrowing, it meant they weren’t buying. If corporations did not go into debt, then they also did not build new factories and expand production and employment for the future. Government debt was creative too, if it stimulated a moribund private economy, if it purchased real economic resources that would make the society more productive in the future —roads and canals, schools and universities, basic research labs and even new factories. By that standard, World War II had been the most constructive borrowing binge in history.
规模是惊人的。美国的总未偿债务——政府和私人——在1984年底为7.1万亿美元，自1977年以来几乎翻番。令人担忧的一点是国内债务仅在1983年和1984年就增加了超过25%。显然，债务的增长速度远快于整体经济的增长速度——而经济是最终必须偿还这些贷款的收入、利润和税收收入的来源。
At every level, from households to government, new debt had to be judged by two basic questions of quality, the same questions any careful family would ask itself before taking on a new loan. What was the borrowed money going to buy—something worthwhile or something frivolous that was not worth the price of going into debt? Secondly, what was the likelihood that the family would be able to pay off the loan? These were the same essential tests applied by corporate managers planning a bond issue or, in theory at least, by government economists planning a deficit budget.
在战后几十年里，国家的总债务，公共和私人，与扩张的经济保持着相当稳定的关系——债务通常比国民生产总值大约40%。现在，未偿债务已经比GNP大了60%以上。在这种情况下，第一个问题变得特别相关——这些借来的钱为未来买到了什么？总的来说，不多。新债务，大部分情况下，并没有提高国家的生产潜力——它赶上贷款支付的能力。
In the mushrooming debt of the 1980s, it was those two questions that aroused alarm. A sizable portion of America’s new debt was not buying anything very productive for the future. Much of it was financing immediate consumption, which was invigorating only if consumers could keep up with the payments. And much of the borrowing was used for profitable but empty financial speculation. In 1984, for instance, American business accumulated a staggering $140 billion in new debt that was devoted solely to finance corporate mergers and take-overs. It did not build any new factories.
美联储无法抑制"借贷狂欢"是Paul Volcker主席任期内的核心矛盾。如果看利率，Volcker的货币政策看起来惩罚性地紧缩。然而，如果看信贷的爆炸，Volcker看起来异常慷慨。有两个基本解释。Volcker自己的策略和中央银行自身的政策本身帮助刺激了快速的债务积累。但美联储失去控制力的另一个原因是政治决定。国会早在1980年通过金融放松管制立法时，有效地剥夺了美联储限制债务扩张的能力。
As the debt mounted, the second question became more alarming. The nation as a whole was taking on debt faster than it was enhancing its ability to repay. Any borrower could do this for a time, counting on his investment to pay off eventually or assuming his income would catch up with his debt obligations. But when the American economy flattened out and lost its robust expansion, the prospects that the nation could collectively catch up with its mounting loans seemed more and more questionable.
Volcker自己理解金融放松管制的隐藏后果，尽管可能很少有在1980年投票废除所有剩余利率上限的国会议员意识到这也将削弱美联储的货币控制。由于这项立法，1983年和1984年展开的经济复苏在现代经验中与任何其他都不同——自1920年代以来第一个在没有联邦任意限制利率的情况下进行的商业周期。市场终于自由了，而1980年代的市场产生了与"咆哮的二十年代"同样的东西——可疑贷款的大量累积。
The dimensions were awesome. The United States’s total outstanding debt—government and private—was $7.1 trillion at the end of 1984, nearly doubled since 1977. The alarming point was that domestic debt had increased by more than 25 percent in 1983 and 1984 alone. Obviously, debt was growing much faster than the economy as a whole had grown—and the economy was the source of incomes, profits and tax revenues that ultimately must pay off these loans.50
自新政时期以来，政府的各种利率上限——除了补贴借款人之外——一直像金融管道中的粗略切断阀门。一旦市场利率达到法定上限，不能再高，资金就会从金融中介机构流出，因为存款人撤出资金寻求其他更好的回报。失去存款的银行和储蓄贷款机构别无选择，只能停止放贷。新债务的扩张自然缓和了（尽管在"信贷紧缩"中被困的失望借款人和金融机构不乏痛苦和喧嚣）。如果美联储希望减缓借贷，它只需将利率推向切断点——新信贷的供应就会自动受到限制。
Over the postwar decades, the nation’s total debt, public and private, had expanded in a remarkably steady relationship to the expanding economy—debt normally ran about 40 percent larger than the Gross National Product. Now, the outstanding debt had become more than 60 percent larger than GNP. In that situation, the first question became especially relevant—what did this borrowed money buy for the future? In general, not much. The new debt, for the most part, was not enhancing the nation’s productive potential—its ability to catch up with the loan payments.
然而，一旦政府控制被拆除，贷款人可以自由收取市场能承受的任何价格。美联储只剩下一个抑制杠杆——提高利率。为了遏制借贷，它必须将利率提高到市场实际上再也无法承受的程度——通过设定过高的价格抑制贷款需求，赶走一些贷款客户，通常是最小和最弱的。
The Fed’s inability to restrain the “borrowing binge” was the central contradiction of Paul Volcker’s tenure as chairman. If one looked at interest rates, Volcker’s monetary policy seemed punishingly stringent. Yet, if one looked at the explosion of credit, Volcker seemed wildly generous. There were two basic explanations. Volcker’s own strategy and the central bank’s own policies themselves helped to stimulate the rapid debt accumulation. But the other reason for the Fed’s loss of control was a political decision. Congress had effectively stripped the Federal Reserve of its ability to constrain debt expansion when it enacted the financial deregulation legislation back in 1980.
正如Briggs Schaedle的经济学家Philip Braverman解释的那样：
Volcker himself had understood the hidden consequences of financial deregulation, though probably few members of Congress who had voted to abolish all remaining interest-rate controls in 1980 appreciated that it would also undermine the Federal Reserve’s monetary control. Because of that legislation, the economic recovery that unfolded in 1983 and 1984 was unlike any other in modern experience— the first business cycle since the 1920s that proceeded without any arbitrary federal limits on interest rates. The market was free at last and the market of the 1980s produced the same thing it had produced in the “roaring twenties”—a burgeoning accumulation of dubious loans.
重点在于使信贷贵到不想要，而不是减少对它的获取。希望是市场会理性地分配信贷。希望是借款人不会继续借贷，贷款人会抑制放贷。我们让市场成为信贷分配以及信贷是否过度的唯一仲裁者。但这适得其反——使信贷过于自由可得最终是通胀性的。
Since New Deal days, the government’s various interest-rate ceilings—in addition to subsidizing borrowers—had acted like crude cutoff valves in the plumbing of finance. Once market interest rates reached the legal limits and could go no higher, money flowed away from the financial intermediaries as depositors pulled out, looking for better returns elsewhere. Banks and savings and loans that were losing deposits had no choice but to stop lending. The expansion of new debt naturally moderated (though not without great anguish and uproar from the disappointed borrowers and financial institutions caught in the “credit crunch”). If the Fed wished to slow down borrowing, it simply pushed interest rates closer to the cutoff point—and the supply of new credit was automatically curtailed.
美联储的唯一杠杆——将利率提高到过高的水平——在Volcker任职头几年，当美联储的目标是引发衰退和打破通胀时，虽然粗暴但有效地起了作用。美联储不得不将优惠利率推高至21%才能获得想要的效果，许多受害者遭受了额外的损害，但收缩确实达到了目的。
Once the government controls were dismantled, however, the lenders were free to charge whatever the traffic would bear. And the Fed was left with only one lever of restraint—raising interest rates. To curb lending, it had to push rates up literally to a point where the traffic could no longer bear it—depressing the demand for loans by setting an exorbitant price, driving off some loan customers, usually the smallest and weakest.
然而，一旦经济复苏开始，中央银行陷入了新的困境。它保持利率非常高，但不敢将它们推得更高并冒扼杀新复苏的风险。正如美联储发现的那样，即使其非常高的利率水平仍然不足以说服贷款人和借款人放慢新债务的创造。分配新信贷的自由市场和支出超过收入的联邦政府一样缺乏纪律。
As Philip Braverman, economist at Briggs Schaedle, explained:
你剩下的唯一抑制影响力[一位美联储高级官员抱怨道]就是利率，这种抑制最终通过使客户破产来起作用。它显然更伤害人。在典型经济学家的愿景中，这是一个美妙的世界。利率上升半厘，贷款成本增加了那么多点，因此放贷放慢了。这更多是理论上的而非现实的。
The emphasis is on making credit too expensive to want, not on reducing access to it. The hope is that the marketplace will allocate credit rationally. The hope is that borrowers won’t continue to borrow and lenders will restrain lending. We make the market the only arbiter of credit allocation and whether credit is excessive. But it’s perverse—making credit too freely available is ultimately inflationary.51
现在，很多取决于人们的情绪。当我们有利率上限时，银行缺乏资金，它们不能放贷。这不取决于银行家的情绪。现在，没有任何上限，如果贷款人和银行家都感到乐观，他们可以永远继续下去。或者他们以为可以。
The Fed’s single lever—raising interest to exorbitant levels— worked brutishly but effectively during Volcker’s first years in office when the Fed’s objective was to induce a recession and break inflation. The Fed had to push the prime rate as high as 21 percent to get the desired results and many victims suffered added damage, but the contraction did accomplish its purpose.
Ronald Reagan的传染性乐观主义普遍被认同，导致信贷交易双方都高估了未来。就像总统不被联邦债务成本飙升所阻挡一样，家庭和企业（及其银行家）也不被他们所承担的真正债务负担所阻挡。在广泛的经济活动领域，债务人和债权人都对未来进行高风险赌博，赌贷款能以某种方式被偿还，尽管有高利率成本和下降的经济增长。First Boston的首席经济学家Albert Wojnilower在1980年金融放松管制立法通过时就预言了这一点。到1984年，他的警告已被证实。
Once the economic recovery began, however, the central bank was in a new dilemma. It kept interest rates very high, but it dared not push them still higher and risk killing off the new recovery. As the Fed discovered, even its very high level of interest rates still wasn’t high enough to persuade lenders and borrowers to slow down the creation of new debt. The free market distributing new credit was as undisciplined as was the federal government spending more than it took in.
"毫无疑问，"Wojnilower观察到，"存在高到足以抑制私人信贷需求的利率水平，但1980年代迄今为止的经验，包括好时光和坏时光，以及两位数通胀和零通胀的间隔，表明它们远远超出了最近的观察范围。"
The only restraining influence you have left [a senior Fed official complained] is interest rates, restraint which works ultimately by bankrupting the customer. It obviously hurts people more. In the typical economist’s vision, this is a wonderful world. Interest rates go up half a point and so many points are added to the cost of the loan and therefore lending slows. This is more theoretical than real.
然而，除了基本解释之外，美联储自己也以从未被承认的方式卷入了债务的爆炸。中央银行开始是为了建立稳定的货币并成功了，但它对价格通胀的不懈斗争也创造了基本的压力来借更多的钱——新的进入债务的激励因素。例如，通过扭转货币价值，美联储基本上将通胀投机的领域从有形资产转移到了金融资产。在1970年代，正如美联储理事们经常哀叹的那样，人们借钱是为了投机于实体资产——购买房屋和农田、艺术品和黄金及其他商品，相信通胀会提升它们的价值。在1980年代，膨胀的价值和投机性借贷转向了纸上——一种令人眼花缭乱的新金融工具的激增，由于美联储的硬通货政策，这些工具现在可以提供快速的利润。
Now, a lot depends on the mood people are in. When we had interest-rate controls, the banks were stuck for funds and they couldn’t lend. It didn’t depend on the mood the banker was in. Now, without any ceilings, if the lender and the banker are both feeling optimistic, they can go on forever. Or they think they can.
在这两种情况下，人们基本上是在货币问题上赌博，押注于通胀或其反面。1980年代的金融投机没有任何东西表明它比1970年代的商品投机更不激烈或更不浪费。当投机者在七十年代通过购买房屋、农田、石油租赁从通胀中获利时，这个过程至少刺激了实物商品的生产——更多的房屋、更多的农产品、更多的石油。除了个人利润，八十年代金融价格令人眩晕的上涨所生产的唯一东西是更多纸张。当价格通胀转移到华尔街时，美联储没有挑战它。
The infectious optimism of Ronald Reagan was shared generally, leading both sides of the credit transaction to overestimate the future. Just as the President was undeterred by the soaring cost of federal debt, so were families and businesses (and their bankers) undeterred by the real debt burdens they took on. Across a broad front of economic activity, both debtors and creditors made high-risk gambles on the future, betting that the loans could somehow be paid off, despite the high interest costs and declining economic growth. Albert Wojni-lower, the chief economist at First Boston, had predicted as much in 1980 when the financial deregulation legislation was enacted. By 1984, his warnings were confirmed.
美联储的货币稳定也是推动1980年代公司并购大战狂潮的根本原因——包括"垃圾债券"、杠杆收购和中央银行家们谴责的公司债务深化。T. Boone Pickens追逐Unocal。Carl Icahn突袭Uniroyal。Capital Cities吞下ABC，General Electric抢到RCA。Ivan Boesky成为家喻户晓的名字。投资银行家没有专注于资本形成的崇高功能，而是全神贯注于这场有利可图的鲨鱼游戏。关于公司所有权的豪赌故事一旦变得司空见惯就不再令人激动。
“No doubt,” Wojnilower observed, “there exist interest-rate levels high enough to curb private credit demand, but the experience of the 1980s to date, featuring good times and bad, and intervals of double-digit as well as zero inflation, suggests they lie well beyond the range of recent observations.”52
虽然公司袭击者正义地谈论保护股东免受懒惰的公司经理之害，但推动大多数这些"交易"的基本力量是美联储创造的变化的经济世界的直接结果——实体资产萎靡不振，金融资产飙升。公司操纵的目的是提取投资于公司利润较低的实体资产——其工厂和建筑——中的资本，以便财富可以重新部署到提供更高回报的金融资产中。这在一定程度上是通过蚕食公司——出售各种部分并拿走现金——以及使公司更深地陷入债务——现在以其未来收益借贷来实现的。尽管技术不同，八十年代正在进行中的资本重组过程在本质上与J. P. Morgan及其同代人在本世纪初的金融操作或金融家在1920年代玩的投机性股票市场游戏没有什么不同。
Beyond that basic explanation, however, the Federal Reserve itself was also implicated in the explosion of debt, in ways that were never acknowledged. The central bank had set out to establish stable money and it succeeded, but its relentless campaign against price inflation also created basic pressures to borrow more—new incentives to get into debt. By turning the tables on money values, for instance, the Fed essentially shifted the arena of inflationary speculation from tangible assets to financial assets. During the 1970s, as Fed governors often lamented, people borrowed money in order to speculate on real assets—buying houses and farmland, artworks and gold and other commodities, confident that inflation would appreciate their values. In the 1980s, the inflated values and speculative borrowing turned to paper—a bewildering proliferation of new financial instruments that could now deliver quick profits, thanks to the Fed’s hard-money policy.
几乎每个参与者都立即获利，从为其股票获得更高价格的股东到为促成交易收取可观费用的投资银行家。潜在的输家大多在未来——当被削弱的公司背负着超出其承受能力的债务而倒闭时。然而，今天的游戏在于将钱从公司资产中取出——将股权所有权转换为债务。无论是否通过并购，美国公司在1984年赎回了720亿美元的公司股票，用等量的新公司债务替代。未来被抵押了，但没有发生任何使未来更具生产力的事情。
In both instances, people were essentially gambling on the money question, betting on inflation or its opposite. There was nothing about the financial speculation of the 1980s that suggested it was less intense or wasteful than the commodity speculation of the 1970s. When speculators profited on inflation in the seventies by buying houses, farmland, oil leases, the process at least stimulated the production of real goods—more houses, more farm commodities, more oil. Aside from personal profits, the only thing produced by the dizzy run-up of financial prices in the eighties was more paper. When price inflation moved to Wall Street, the Federal Reserve did not challenge it.
"对公司来说，股权和债务之间有一个根本区别，"Partee理事解释道。"如果利润下降，你不需要向股东支付股息。但如果你不偿还债务，你就破产。所以公司比过去暴露得更多。"
The Federal Reserve’s stabilization of money was also an underlying cause driving the frenzy of corporate take-over battles in the 1980s— including the “junk bonds” and leveraged buy outs and deepening corporate debt that the central bankers deplored. T. Boone Pickens goes after Unocal. Carl Icahn raids Uniroyal. Capital Cities swallows ABC and General Electric grabs RCA. Ivan Boesky becomes a household name. Instead of the noble function of capital formation, investment bankers were preoccupied by this lucrative game of sharks. The stories of high-stakes combat over corporate ownership were no longer exciting once they became commonplace.
美联储谴责这种做法，甚至引入了法规以限制用于为许多公司收购提供资金的"垃圾债券"的使用。尽管如此，只要货币政策为金融投资保持如此高的实际回报，同时抑制实体资产的回报，聪明的投资者自然会寻找方法将他们的资本从一个转移到另一个。
While corporate raiders talked righteously about defending stockholders against slothful corporate managers, the fundamental force that motivated most of these “deals” was a direct result of the changed economic world the Fed had created—real assets languished and financial assets soared. The aim of the corporate manipulations was to extract capital invested in a company’s less profitable real assets, its factories and buildings, so the wealth could be redeployed in the financial assets that offered much higher returns. This was accomplished partly by cannibalizing a company, selling off various parts and taking the cash, and by putting the company deeper into debt—borrowing now against its future earnings. Though the techniques differed, the process of recapitalization under way in the eighties was in essence no different from the financial manipulations by J. P. Morgan and his contemporaries early in the century or the speculative stock-market games that financiers played in the 1920s.
Albert Wojnilower所谓的高风险游戏的金融市场的"赌场本能"，也受到美联储对银行业危机的保护性回应的鼓励。政府为确保Continental伊利诺伊继续运营而向债权人提供的全面担保，向投资者传递了一个信息——你的钱在任何大到美联储不能让它倒闭的银行或经纪公司都是安全的。风险将由他人承担，包括美国纳税人。
Nearly every player profited immediately, from the stockholders who got a higher price for their shares to the investment bankers who collected handsome fees for brokering the deals. The potential losers were mostly in the future—when the weakened corporation, saddled with more debt than it could sustain, failed. The game for today, however, was getting the money out of a company’s assets—converting equity ownership into debt. With and without take-overs, U.S. corporations retired $72 billion in corporate stock shares in 1984, replacing it with an equivalent amount of new corporate debt. The future was mortgaged, but nothing had happened to make the future more productive.
Salomon Brothers的Henry Kaufman得出结论说：
“For a corporation, there is one fundamental difference between equity and debt,” Governor Partee explained. “You are not required to pay a dividend to stockholders if your profits are down. But if you don’t service your debt, you go bankrupt. So corporations are much more exposed than they used to be.”
这种信贷的增长是由……我们的政府愿意在各种参与者之间传播官方安全网滋养的，这倾向于降低借贷风险。没有大型企业公司被允许倒闭。没有大型金融机构被允许倒闭。没有有存款人的机构被允许不履行其对存款人的义务，无论大小。陷入困境的联邦信贷机构不被允许倒闭。所以有一种官方安全网在扩展，并被市场所认知。
The Federal Reserve deplored the practice and even introduced regulations to curb the use of the “junk bonds” used to finance many of the corporate buy outs. Nonetheless, so long as monetary policy maintained such high real returns for financial investments and simultaneously depressed the return from real assets, smart investors would naturally seek ways to get their capital out of one and into the other.
最后，债务爆炸在很大程度上是由Paul Volcker自己的货币政策策略推动的。高实际利率虽然意在抑制，实际上迫使许多人借得越来越多，仅仅是为了维持他们的旧债务。对于负债累累的农民和石油钻井者来说，这与拉丁美洲的第三世界国家一样真实。就此而言，对于美国政府来说也是如此。实际上，许多借款人别无选择，只能借更多，即使他们知道额外的债务是不谨慎的，利息成本过高。不借就意味着完蛋。
The financial market’s “casino instincts,” as Albert Wojnilower called the high-risk games, were also encouraged by the Federal Reserve’s protective response to the banking crisis. The blanket guarantee to creditors extended by the government to keep Continental Illinois afloat sent a message to investors—your money is safe in any bank or brokerage large enough that the Fed cannot let it fail. The risk would be borne by others, including U.S. taxpayers.

Henry Kaufman of Salomon Brothers concluded:
这些异常高的利率本身——无论是由预算赤字还是由美联储造成的——矛盾地成为，即使不是主要原因，也是债务飙升的重要原因。当然，通常情况下，高利率会阻止借贷。但过高利率可能产生增加借贷的反常效果，这正是过去几年所发生的事情。
This growth in credit is nourished by . . . the willingness of our government to spread an official safety net over a variety of participants which tends to reduce the risk of borrowing. No large business corporation is allowed to fail. No large financial institution is allowed to fail. No institution that has depositors is allowed to fail in its obligation to depositors, large or small. Federal credit agencies that get into trouble are not allowed to fail. So there is the kind of official safety net that’s spreading and that is perceived by the marketplace.53
这种作用方式最明显的例子是发展中国家债务（尽管它不是美国国内债务负担的一部分）。当南美和加勒比国家在1982年达到危机顶峰时，它们已陷入3300亿美元的债务。每个人都同意它们借得太多了。两年后，根据世界银行统计，这些国家负债3740亿美元——更深的窟窿。
Finally, the debt explosion was driven substantially by Paul Volck-er’s own strategy for monetary policy. The high real interest rates, though intended to restrain, actually forced many to borrow more and more, just to keep up with their old debts. This was as true for debt-burdened farmers and oil drillers as it was for Third World countries of Latin America. For that matter, it was also true for the U.S. government. As a practical matter, many borrowers had no choice but to borrow more, even if they knew the added debt was imprudent and the interest costs were exorbitant. Not to borrow meant going under.
它们以高实际利率承担的新贷款，主要用于支付旧贷款到期的利息。它们在利率如此之高的情况下借得越多，未来就需要借更多以支付到期的利息。尽管有几个国家显示出改善，大多数国家正在加重其实际债务负担——这是判断它们能否维持借贷的关键衡量标准。1981年，这些国家欠的外国债务是其全部GNP的38%；到1984年，是61%。同样，它们的债务增长快于其出口收入的增长。Paul Volcker对国际债务危机的管理正在逐步减少美国大银行的风险敞口，但与此同时，大多数债务人的状况更糟。
Edward Sonnino, a Wall Street portfolio manager, argued:
它们能持续多久？只要银行家和国际贷款机构愿意借给它们新钱，以便它们能继续支付旧债务的利息。这确实类似于"庞氏游戏"，正如Henry Wallich所暗示的。
These abnormally high interest rates by themselves—whether caused by the budget deficits or by the Fed—have paradoxically been an important, if not the main, cause of skyrocketing debt. Normally, of course, high interest rates deter borrowing. But excessively high interest rates can have the perverse effect of increasing borrowing, which is precisely what has happened over the past few years.54
影响不那么戏剧性，但同样的侵蚀正在许多美国借款人身上发挥作用，家庭和企业被迫借更多以避免止赎或破产。平均而言，家庭资产负债表看起来并不危险——上升的收入或多或少与债务保持同步。但这忽略了收入分配不公以及很大一部分美国家庭借款更多是因为其实际收入在下降的事实。实际上，他们从上半部分家庭那里借钱——那些由于高利率和其他政府政策其收入份额更大的家庭。
The most visible example of how this worked was the LDC debt (though, of course, it was not a part of the U.S. domestic debt burden). When South American and Caribbean nations reached the peak of their crisis in 1982, they had sunk $330 billion into debt. Everyone agreed they had borrowed too much. Two years later, according to World Bank statistics, these countries were $374 billion in debt— further in the hole.
"有相当多的收入向高收入群体再分配，"Partee理事解释说。"这要求消费者积累越来越多的债务。高收入人群将其增加的收入投资于金融资产——这笔钱被其他所有人借走。"
Their new loans, obligated at the high real interest rates, were devoted mostly to paying the interest due on their old loans. The more they borrowed at the high rates, the more they would need to borrow in the future to pay interest due. Though several countries showed improvement, most were compounding their real debt burdens—the crucial measure of whether they could sustain the lending. In 1981, the foreign debt owed by these countries was 38 percent of their total GNP; by 1984, it was 61 percent. Likewise, their debts were growing faster than their export income was expanding. Paul Volcker’s management of the international debt crisis was gradually reducing the exposure of the major U.S. banks but, meanwhile, most of the debtors were in worse shape.
借款人承受着额外的风险，因为浮动利率贷款现在被普遍使用，既用于住房抵押贷款也用于主要商业贷款。协议常常给借款人一开始低利率的诱惑，但该利率将来可能急剧上升。贷款机构已经保护自己免受利率上升的风险，因此更愿意放贷。贷款人唯一会损失的方式是他们的贷款客户违约。
How long could they go on? As long as bankers and international lending agencies would lend them new money so they could keep paying the interest on the old debt. It did resemble a “Ponzi game,” as Henry Wallich had suggested.
甚至联邦政府的巨额赤字和迅速积累的债务，部分也是由美联储的货币政策驱动的。Volcker和美联储当然坚决反对赤字，并不认为自己部分有责任。但中央银行采用的利率高位策略也损害了政府的资产负债表。它减缓了经济增长，因此减少了税收收入。同时，国债的高利率极大地提高了政府借贷成本——向联邦赤字增加了高达300或400亿美元。像许多其他债务人一样，政府别无选择，只能支付更高的价格并继续借钱。
The effects were less dramatic, but the same erosion was working away on many American borrowers, families and businesses compelled to borrow more to stave off foreclosure or failure. On average, household balance sheets did not look endangered—rising incomes were more or less keeping pace with debt. But that overlooked the maldistribution of incomes and the fact that a large share of America’s families were borrowing more because their real incomes were declining. In effect, they borrowed the money from the upper half—the families whose income shares were larger, thanks to high interest rates and other government policies.
美联储从其自身角度来看，也没有太多选择。美联储应用了它唯一的可用杠杆，短期内它有效：物价保持稳定或下降。然而从长期来看，美联储的货币政策似乎反常地适得其反。没有其他对信贷供应的控制，高利率使经济越来越深地陷入债务，并创造了新的不稳定，与Volcker着手要完成的事情相反。膨胀的信贷通常是通货膨胀的前奏。在未来的某个时刻，中央银行将面临艰难的选择：是让所有债务人失败，还是通过允许价格通胀再次发生来解救他们？
“There’s been quite a lot of redistribution of income toward the higher-income groups,” Governor Partee explained. “It requires consumers to accumulate more and more debt. The high-income people invest their increased income in financial assets—money which gets borrowed by everyone else.”
在缺乏其他控制的情况下，美联储陷入了政治困境。如果它放松货币的价格，会因让步、失去控制而受到债券持有人的批评。然而，如果它继续如此强力地推动，它冒着民众愤怒的风险——大量贷款客户最终破产，大量银行的毁灭性贷款损失。到1984年底，超过七百家银行被列为"问题银行"，这个名单最终会增长到一千二百家。美国即将成为世界其他地区的净债务人。一个新的债务危机正在发展，只是这一次的中心在美国，而不是第三世界。当权者中没有人做多少事来阻止它。
The borrowers were burdened with an additional risk because adjustable-rate loans were now in general use, both for home mortgages and for major business loans. The agreement often gave the borrowers the enticement of a low rate at the start, but one that could escalate sharply in the future. The lending institutions had protected themselves from risk of rising rates and were therefore more willing to lend. The only way the lenders would lose was if their loan customers failed and defaulted.
在华盛顿最重要的参与者中，包括美联储，几乎没有对这些根本原因的公开讨论，或者如何将政治经济从这些破坏性后果中解脱出来。解决办法是显而易见的：联邦政府将不得不重新施加对银行和信贷市场的其他种类的控制，能够遏制信贷供应的法律限制，特别是最可疑的贷款，这样美联储就不必仅仅依赖高利率的极端惩罚。新的控制可以采取多种形式，从利率上限到所得税惩罚再到中央银行对银行和其他贷款机构如何分配其贷款的直接监管。控制将意味着更多公平——但也更多稳定。如果国会希望美联储降低利率，实现这一点的最可靠方法将是创造不同的机制来控制信贷扩张。
Even the federal government’s huge deficits and rapidly accumulating debt were driven, in part, by the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy. Volcker and the Fed, of course, stood foursquare against the deficits and did not think of themselves as partly responsible. But the strategy of high interest rates adopted by the central bank damaged the government’s balance sheet too. It slowed down economic growth and therefore reduced tax revenues. Meanwhile, the high rates on Treasury bonds raised the cost of government borrowing enormously —adding as much as $30 or $40 billion to the federal deficits. Like many other debtors, the government had no choice but to pay the higher price and keep on borrowing.
在Reagan时代的自由市场和少管政府的风气中，两党政治家都没有寻求这一解决方案的前景。事实上，商业银行家的政治影响力正朝着相反方向推动——朝向更大程度地免于法律限制。最大的银行寻求在全国范围内经营的权力，金融权力的集中将不可避免地将银行的政治影响力集中在一小部分非常大的机构手中。它们还游说进入其他商业领域的权利——在那里它们将比竞争对手享有巨大优势，因为它们仍将因其作为商业银行的特殊地位而受到联邦政府的保护，免于倒闭。
The Federal Reserve, from its own perspective, did not have much choice either. The Fed applied the only lever it had available and in the short term it worked: prices held steady or declined. In the long term, however, the Fed’s monetary policy appeared to be perversely self-defeating. Without other controls on the supply of credit, the high interest rates propelled the economy deeper and deeper into debt and created a new instability, the opposite of what Volcker had set out to accomplish. Bloated credit was the usual preamble to inflation. At some future point, the central bank would face a hard choice: should it let all debtors fail or should it bail them out by allowing price inflation to resume?
当Paul Volcker敦促对进一步放松管制保持谨慎时，来自货币中心银行的游说者（和Reagan政府官员）感到愤怒。他拖后腿并游说国会缓慢进行。但是，尽管他如此强大，中央银行主席并不打算挑战他自己选区的正统观念，成为重新管制金融的首要倡导者。
The Federal Reserve, given the absence of other controls, was in a political bind. If it relaxed the price of money, it would be criticized by the bondholders for yielding, for losing control. Yet if it continued to push so hard, it risked popular fury—eventual bankruptcy for lots of loan customers and devastating loan losses for lots of banks. By the end of 1984, more than seven hundred banks were listed as “problem banks,” a list that would eventually grow to twelve hundred. The United States was about to become a net debtor to the rest of the world. A new debt crisis was developing, only this one was centered in the United States, not the Third World. No one in power was doing much to stop it.
讽刺的是，唯一一贯且有说服力地提出恢复信贷控制的声音来自华尔街的主要投资银行公司——资本主义的保守堡垒。像Henry Kaufman、Albert Wojnilower、Philip Braverman这样的金融专家反复警告债务爆炸的风险，并提出了各种施加新的借贷法律限制的想法。
Among the most important players in Washington, including the Federal Reserve, there was almost no public discussion of these underlying causes or how to extricate the political economy from the debilitating consequences. The solution was obvious: the federal government would have to reimpose other kinds of controls on banking and credit markets, legal limits that could contain the supply of credit, particularly the most dubious lending, so the Fed would not have to rely solely on the extreme punishment of high interest rates. The new controls could take many forms, from interest-rate ceilings to income-tax penalties to direct regulation by the central bank of how banks and other lending institutions allocated their lending. Controls would mean more equity—but also more stability. If Congress wanted lower interest rates from the Fed, the surest way to get them would be to create different mechanisms to control the expansion of credit.
他们的提案没有一个在华盛顿得到任何认真的审查。尽管看起来奇怪，华尔街的主要大师们敦促联邦政府对金融施加新的纪律，而华盛顿的政府领导人却不愿听他们的。这是主流政治观点已经转变了多少的另一个衡量标准。在政治首都，新的神话认为联邦政府最终退出了监管金融的业务。而自由市场正在产生为其预测的所有好处。
In the Reagan era’s ethos of free markets and less government, there was no prospect that politicians of either party would pursue this solution. Indeed, the political influence of commercial bankers was pushing in the other direction—toward greater freedom from legal restraints. The largest banks sought the power to operate nationwide, a consolidation of financial power that would inevitably centralize the political influence of banking in a handful of very large institutions. They also lobbied for the right to enter into other business sectors— where they would enjoy enormous advantages over competitors since they would still be protected from failure by the federal government, thanks to their special status as commercial banks.
华尔街的经济学家知道不是这样。自由市场本身总是一种虚构。现实当然是美联储仍然监管信贷。它监管价格。然而它无法控制债务的增长。1980年代确立了一点，试图仅通过利率来控制信贷基本上是无效的，此外还如此不公平。它肯定会惩罚借款人，特别是最弱的借款人，同时过度奖励贷款人。然而它也没有防止愚蠢。
Lobbyists from money-center banks (and Reagan administration officials) were outraged when Paul Volcker urged caution on further deregulation. He dragged his feet and lobbied Congress to proceed slowly. But, powerful as he was, the chairman of the central bank was not about to challenge the orthodoxy of his own constituency and become the foremost advocate for the reregulation of finance.
当然，政治领袖有可能及时认识到美联储困境中固有的不公正和经济危险，并开始采取补救行动。但可能性不大。如此突然地反转，放弃两党在过去十年中都拥抱的放松管制逻辑，将需要在政治精英——民选官员、他们所咨询的经济专家、传达可接受政治思想的新闻媒体——之间所共享的传统智慧发生戏剧性转变。所有这些群体总体上致力于新的现状。
The only voices persistently and persuasively raised in behalf of restoring credit controls were, ironically, from major investment-banking firms of Wall Street—the conservative citadels of capitalism. Financial experts like Henry Kaufman, Albert Wojnilower, Philip Braverman and others warned repeatedly of the risks of the debt explosion and proposed various ideas for imposing new legal limits on lending.55
在美国历史上，经济正统观念的根本转变通常直到经历了一场大而痛苦的灾难之后才发生，像1929年金融崩溃或随后的大萧条那样的明显危机。这种经历导致的可怕后果使流行智慧信誉扫地，突然为新的思维方式打开了道路。不幸的是，只有在灾难之后，大多数政治家和大多数经济学家才能接受他们以前认为不可想象的想法。
None of their proposals received any serious examination in Washington. Strange as it seemed, leading gurus of Wall Street were urging the federal government to impose new discipline on finance and the leaders of government in Washington would not listen to them. It was another measure of how much the mainstream political opinion had shifted. In the political capital, the new mythology held that the federal government had at last gotten out of the business of regulating finance. And the free market was producing all the benefits predicted for it.
对于将美元考虑在内，我比委员会中任何其他人都有更多的同情。但你对此能做什么呢？看着美元，我们应该相当宽松吗？以我自己的标准，我认为直到你在通胀问题上有了更稳定的心理和对经济有了更多信心，放松将很难——特别是当困难的真正来源来自别处时。
The Wall Street economists knew it wasn’t so. The free market was always itself a fiction. The reality, of course, was that the Federal Reserve still regulated credit. It regulated the price. Yet it was unable to control the growth of debt. The 1980s established that trying to control credit by interest rates alone was fundamentally ineffective, in addition to being so inequitable. It was certain to punish the borrowers, especially the weakest ones, while it rewarded the lenders excessively. Yet it also did not prevent folly.
It was possible, of course, that political leaders might in time recognize the injustice and the economic dangers inherent in the Federal Reserve’s dilemma and begin to take remedial action. But that was not likely. To reverse fields so abruptly, to abandon the logic of deregulation that both Democrats and Republicans had embraced in the previous decade, would require a dramatic shift in the conventional wisdom shared among the political elites—the elected officials, the economic experts whom they consulted, the news media that communicated acceptable political thinking. All these groups, in general, were committed to the new status quo.
In American history, fundamental shifts in the economic orthodoxy usually did not occur until after there was a large and painful calamity, a visible crisis like the financial collapse of 1929 or the Great Depression that followed. The awful consequences from such an experience discredited the prevailing wisdom and suddenly opened the way for new thinking. It was only after a disaster, unfortunately, that most politicians and most economists were able to entertain ideas they had previously dismissed as unthinkable.
